### **SOLUTION TO THE PROBLEM**

#### **Issue**

The issue is whether A has committed the offense of **voyeurism** under **Section 77 of the Bhartiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023** (previously Section 354C IPC) by secretly recording video of B bathing and sharing it with others.

---

#### **Relevant Law**

**Section 77 BNS - Voyeurism** provides:

"Whoever watches, or captures the image of, a woman engaging in a **private act** in circumstances where she would usually have expectation of **not being observed** either by the perpetrator or by any other person at the behest of the perpetrator, or disseminates such image, shall be punished on first conviction with imprisonment which may extend to **three years**, and shall also be liable to fine, and be punished on a second or subsequent conviction, with imprisonment which may extend to **seven years**, and shall also be liable to fine."

#### **Essential Ingredients of Voyeurism**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Watching or Capturing Image of Woman**

Two prohibited acts:
- **Watching** the woman, OR
- **Capturing image** (photo, video, recording)

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Woman Engaging in Private Act**

**"Private act"** includes:
- Woman in state where she expects privacy
- Examples:
  - Bathing or undressing
  - Using toilet
  - Engaged in sexual act
  - In state of undress in private space

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Expectation of Not Being Observed**

**Requirement**: Circumstances where woman would usually have expectation of:
- Not being observed by perpetrator, OR
- Not being observed by any other person

**Key Element**: **Invasion of privacy**

---

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Dissemination (Aggravated Form)**

**Additional Act**: Disseminating (sharing, distributing) such image

**Punishment**: Enhanced on second conviction

---

#### **Application to Facts**

**Analyzing A's Actions**:

**1. Did A capture image of woman?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - A **secretly recorded video** of B
- Captured moving images (video recording)
- First element satisfied

**2. Was B engaging in private act?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - B was **bathing in her private bathroom**
- Bathing is quintessential private act
- Woman in state of undress
- Engaged in personal hygiene activity
- Second element satisfied

**3. Expectation of not being observed?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - B was in her **private bathroom**
- Reasonable expectation of privacy in own bathroom
- Would not expect to be watched or recorded
- Circumstances clearly indicate expectation of privacy
- Third element satisfied

**4. Manner of Recording?**
- A recorded **secretly** - without B's knowledge
- Without B's consent
- Invasion of privacy clear

**5. Did A disseminate?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - A **shared video with friends**
- Dissemination element also satisfied
- This is **aggravated form** of voyeurism

---

#### **Analysis**

**All Elements Satisfied**:
1. ‚úÖ A captured image (video) of woman (B)
2. ‚úÖ B was engaging in private act (bathing)
3. ‚úÖ In circumstances with expectation of privacy (private bathroom)
4. ‚úÖ A also disseminated the video (shared with friends)

**Relevant Provision**: Section 77 BNS clearly applies

---

#### **Conclusion**

**A is guilty of VOYEURISM under Section 77 BNS** because:

1. A captured video of B (woman) bathing
2. Bathing in private bathroom is private act
3. B had reasonable expectation of not being observed
4. Recording was done secretly without consent
5. A also disseminated video by sharing with friends

**Punishment**:
- **First conviction**: Imprisonment up to **3 years** + fine
- **Second/subsequent conviction**: Imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine

**Nature**: Cognizable, bailable offense

**Therefore**, A can be prosecuted and punished under Section 77 BNS for voyeurism. The dissemination of video makes it aggravated form with potential for higher punishment.
