**Variant C** (10% probability):

**üìù Answer Structure (15 Marks)**

**Part I: 4 Stages of Crime** (6 marks)

1Ô∏è‚É£ **Intention** (1.5 marks)
- First stage
- Mere intention not punishable
- Difficult to prove

2Ô∏è‚É£ **Preparation** (1.5 marks)
- Arranging means/measures
- Generally not punishable
- Exceptions: War against Govt, Dacoity

3Ô∏è‚É£ **Attempt** (1.5 marks)
- Direct movement toward commission
- Punishable under Section 62
- 3 Essentials:
  - Guilty intention
  - Act toward commission
  - Falls short of completion

4Ô∏è‚É£ **Accomplishment** (1.5 marks)
- Last stage
- Crime completed
- Full punishment

**Part II: Critical Distinction - Preparation vs Attempt** (7 marks)

**Tests to Distinguish** (4 marks):

1Ô∏è‚É£ **Proximity Test**
- How close to actual commission?
- Attempt = proximate to crime
- Preparation = remote from crime

2Ô∏è‚É£ **Equivocality Test**
- Does act unequivocally show criminal intent?
- Attempt = clear criminal intent
- Preparation = ambiguous

3Ô∏è‚É£ **Locus Poenitentiae**
- Opportunity to change mind?
- Preparation = can still retreat
- Attempt = crossed point of no return

4Ô∏è‚É£ **Overt Act Test**
- Has substantial step been taken?
- Attempt = yes
- Preparation = no

**Illustrations** (2 marks):
- **Preparation**: A buys poison intending to kill B
- **Attempt**: A mixes poison in B's food
- **Accomplishment**: B consumes and dies

- Abhayanand Mishra v. State of Bihar
