## üìù Understanding Robbery and Dacoity

Robbery and Dacoity are **aggravated forms** of property offenses where the crime against property is accompanied by **crime against person** (violence or threat). These offenses are considered more serious than simple theft or extortion because they involve dual harm:
1. Loss of property
2. Violence or fear of violence to persons

The presence of force or threat makes these offenses particularly dangerous to public safety and attracts much higher punishment than simple theft.

## üìñ ROBBERY (Section 309 BNS)

### ‚öñÔ∏è Definition (Previously Section 390 IPC)

**"Robbery** is either **theft** or **extortion** when, in order to commit the theft or extortion, or in committing the theft or extortion, or in carrying away or attempting to carry away property obtained by the theft or extortion, the offender, for that end, voluntarily causes or attempts to cause to any person death or hurt or wrongful restraint, or fear of instant death or of instant hurt or of instant wrongful restraint."

---

### üß© 5 Essential Ingredients of Robbery

---

### **1Ô∏è‚É£ THEFT OR EXTORTION MUST BE PRESENT**

**Base Requirement**: Robbery builds upon **theft** (Section 303) OR **extortion** (Section 308)

**Key Point**: Without underlying theft or extortion, there cannot be robbery

**Two Scenarios**:

**(a) Robbery based on THEFT**:
- Taking movable property without consent + Force
- Example: Snatching purse forcefully

**(b) Robbery based on EXTORTION**:
- Taking property by putting in fear + Additional force/instant threat
- Example: Threatening at gunpoint and taking property

**Formula**: 
```
ROBBERY = THEFT + FORCE/THREAT OF INSTANT HARM
OR
ROBBERY = EXTORTION + FORCE/THREAT OF INSTANT HARM
```

---

### **2Ô∏è‚É£ VOLUNTARY CAUSING OR ATTEMPT TO CAUSE HARM**

**Requirement**: Offender voluntarily causes or **attempts to cause**:
- **Death**, OR
- **Hurt**, OR
- **Wrongful restraint**

**"Voluntarily"** (Section 39 BNS):
- Causing effect intentionally
- Not by accident or without knowledge
- Deliberate use of force

**"Attempts to Cause"**: Even attempt to harm sufficient, actual harm not required

**Examples**:
- Hitting victim with weapon (causing hurt)
- Pushing victim to ground (causing hurt)
- Holding victim to prevent movement (wrongful restraint)
- Attempting to strike but missing (attempt to cause hurt)

---

### **3Ô∏è‚É£ PUTTING IN FEAR OF INSTANT HARM**

**Requirement**: Putting person in **fear of instant**:
- Death, OR
- Hurt, OR
- Wrongful restraint

**"Instant"**: Immediate, right now, not future

**Critical Distinction from Extortion**:
- **Extortion**: Fear of future harm acceptable
- **Robbery**: Fear must be of **instant** (immediate) harm

**Examples**:

‚úÖ **Instant Fear** (Robbery):
- "Give wallet now or I'll stab you" (instant threat)
- Pointing gun at person saying "money now" (instant)
- "Hand over jewelry or I'll hit you right now" (instant)

‚ùå **NOT Instant** (Extortion only, not Robbery):
- "Give money tomorrow or I'll harm you" (future)
- "Pay by next week or face consequences" (future)

---

### **4Ô∏è‚É£ FORCE/THREAT MUST BE ON PERSON**

**Requirement**: Force or threat must be against **person**, not merely against property

**"Person" Can Be**:
- Person whose property is taken (owner), OR
- Any person **present** at time of theft/extortion

**Examples**:

‚úÖ **Force on Person** (Robbery):
- Hitting owner before taking property
- Threatening cashier at gunpoint
- Restraining guard while taking goods
- Pushing bystander who tries to intervene

‚ùå **Force Only on Property** (NOT Robbery):
- Breaking lock of empty house (no person present)
- Cutting chain link fence (no person threatened)
- Breaking car window when no one inside

**Principle**: Robbery requires **personal violence** or threat thereof, not just property damage.

---

### **5Ô∏è‚É£ FORCE/THREAT FOR SPECIFIC PURPOSE**

**Requirement**: Force/threat used "for that end" - meaning:
- In order to commit theft/extortion, OR
- In committing theft/extortion, OR
- In carrying away property obtained, OR
- In attempting to carry away property

**Three Stages When Force Can Be Used**:

**Stage 1: BEFORE Taking** (In order to commit):
- Force used to facilitate theft/extortion
- Example: Hitting person, then taking wallet

**Stage 2: DURING Taking** (In committing):
- Force used while taking property
- Example: Snatching chain forcefully from neck

**Stage 3: AFTER Taking** (Carrying away):
- Force used to escape with property or retain it
- Example: Taking purse, then pushing victim who tries to stop

**All three constitute robbery** if other elements satisfied.

## üìñ DACOITY (Section 310 BNS)

### ‚öñÔ∏è Definition (Previously Section 391 IPC)

**"When five or more persons conjointly commit or attempt to commit a robbery, or where the whole number of persons conjointly committing or attempting to commit a robbery, and persons present and aiding such commission or attempt, amount to five or more, every person so committing, attempting or aiding, is said to commit dacoity."**

---

### üß© Essential Ingredients of Dacoity

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Five or More Persons**

**Minimum Number**: **5 persons** required
- Less than 5 = Robbery (not dacoity)
- Exactly 5 or more = Can be dacoity

**Counting**:
- All persons committing robbery, PLUS
- Persons present and aiding
- Total must be 5 or more

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ All Elements of Robbery Present**

**Requirement**: Must satisfy all requirements of robbery
- Theft or extortion
- Use of force or threat
- On persons
- Fear of instant harm

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Acting Conjointly**

**"Conjointly"**: Together, in concert

**Requirement**:
- Acting in **furtherance of common intention**
- Coordinated action
- All working toward same objective

**Need Not**:
- All be physically present at exact same moment
- All do same acts
- But must be part of same criminal enterprise

## üìñ HOUSE BREAKING (Section 309 BNS)

### ‚öñÔ∏è Definition

A person commits **"house-breaking"** who commits house-trespass if he effects his **entrance** into house or any part of it, or his **exit** therefrom, in any of **six ways** specified.

---

### üî¢ 6 Ways of House Breaking

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Passage Made by Breaking**

**Through any passage made by himself or by any abettor by breaking**:
- Breaking through wall, roof, floor, door, window
- Creating opening by force

**Example**: Breaking window to enter house

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Through Opening Made by Breaking**

**Through any passage made by himself or abettor**:
- Using opening created by breaking
- Even if someone else made opening earlier

**Example**: Using hole in wall made by accomplice

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Passage Made by Criminal Acts**

**Through any passage not intended by person in possession for human entrance**:
- Using passage created by criminal means
- Not meant for entry/exit

**Example**: Entering through tunnel dug under house

---

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Opening Not Intended for Entry**

**Through opening not intended by person in possession for human entrance or exit**:
- Using opening meant for other purpose
- Not meant as door or window

**Examples**:
- Entering through ventilator
- Entering through chimney
- Entering through drainage pipe

---

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Opening Lock Improperly**

**By opening any lock in order to effect entrance or exit**:
- Lock not provided by person in possession for purpose of fastening entrance/exit
- Using improper means to open lock

**Examples**:
- Lock picking
- Using duplicate key
- Breaking padlock

---

**6Ô∏è‚É£ Using Criminal Force or Threat**

**By using criminal force or committing assault, or by threatening any person with assault**:
- Threatening occupant to let one in
- Using force against guard/watchman

**Example**: Forcing watchman at gunpoint to open gate

---

### **House Breaking + Robbery**

**When house breaking becomes robbery**:
- Person commits house-breaking (enters/exits by any of 6 ways)
- Commits theft or extortion during house-breaking
- Uses force or threat on persons present

**Enhanced Seriousness**: Combining unlawful entry with theft and violence

## üìä Comparative Analysis

### **Theft vs Extortion vs Robbery vs Dacoity**

| **Offense** | **Key Element** | **Persons** | **Punishment** |
|------------|----------------|------------|---------------|
| **Theft** | Without consent, movable property | Any number | Up to 3 years |
| **Extortion** | Consent by fear, movable/immovable | Any number | Up to 3 years |
| **Robbery** | Theft/Extortion + Force/Instant threat | Any number | Up to 10 years |
| **Dacoity** | Robbery by 5+ persons conjointly | Min. 5 | Life or 10 years |

**Escalation**:
```
Theft ‚Üí Extortion ‚Üí Robbery ‚Üí Dacoity
(No force) (Fear) (Force+Instant) (5+ persons)
```

## üìä Punishment Provisions Summary

**Robbery** (Section 309 BNS):
- Imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

**Robbery with Hurt** (Section 311 BNS):
- **Life imprisonment**, OR
- Imprisonment up to 10 years + fine

**Dacoity** (Section 310 BNS):
- **Life imprisonment**, OR
- Imprisonment up to 10 years + fine

**Dacoity with Murder** (Section 313 BNS):
- **Death** penalty, OR
- Life imprisonment + fine

## üèõÔ∏è Important Case Laws

### **Jhandu Lal v. State of Punjab** (1974)

**Principle**: For robbery, force must be:
- **Voluntary** (intentional)
- Used to commit theft/extortion OR overcome resistance

**Held**: Force is essential distinguishing element between theft and robbery.

---

### **Shri Ram v. State of U.P.** (1975)

**Principle**: Distinguished theft and extortion based on consent element
- Theft: Without consent
- Extortion: Consent obtained by fear

## üìö Summary

**House Breaking**: Entering/exiting building by any of **6 illegal ways**

**Robbery**: **Theft or Extortion + Force/Threat of instant harm**
- 5 essentials must be satisfied
- Punishment: Up to 10 years (with hurt: life)

- All elements of robbery + minimum 5 persons
- Punishment: Life or up to 10 years

**Key Distinctions**:
- Number of persons (Robbery: any, Dacoity: 5+)
- Instant vs future threat (Robbery: instant, Extortion: can be future)
- Force on persons essential (not just property damage)
