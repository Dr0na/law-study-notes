## üìù Introduction

Offenses Against Public Tranquility are crimes that disturb **public peace and order**. These offenses recognize that maintaining public tranquility is essential for orderly society and that collective action with unlawful objectives poses significant threat. The law intervenes to prevent breaches of peace before they escalate into serious violence or disorder. Chapter XIII of BNS 2023 deals with these offenses.

## üìñ UNLAWFUL ASSEMBLY (Section 189 BNS)

### ‚öñÔ∏è Definition (Previously Section 141 IPC)

An assembly of **five or more persons** is designated "unlawful assembly" if the **common object** of persons composing that assembly is to achieve any of five specified unlawful purposes.

---

### üß© 5 Essential Ingredients

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Assembly of Five or More Persons**

- **Minimum 5 persons** required
- Less than 5 = NOT unlawful assembly
- Can assemble at one time or join gradually

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Common Object**

- Shared purpose or aim
- **Common object** ‚â† **Common intention**
- Can develop spontaneously

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Unlawful Common Object** (must fall under one of 5 categories)

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Actually Assembled**

- Must have physically come together
- Can be public or private place

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Members Aware of Common Object**

- Must know the unlawful purpose
- Can be inferred from conduct

---

### üìã 5 Categories of Unlawful Object

**Category (i)**: **Overawe Government/Public Servant**
- By criminal force or show of criminal force
- Overawe Central/State Government, Parliament, Legislature, or public servant

**Category (ii)**: **Resist Execution of Law**
- Resist execution of law or legal process

**Category (iii)**: **Commit Offense**
- Commit any mischief, criminal trespass, or other offense

**Category (iv)**: **Take Possession by Force**
- By criminal force, take/obtain possession of property
- Deprive person of right of way, water, incorporeal right
- Enforce right or supposed right

**Category (v)**: **Compel Person by Force**
- By criminal force, compel person to:
  - Do what NOT legally bound to do
  - Omit what legally entitled to do

- Imprisonment up to **6 months**, OR
- Fine up to Rs. 5000, OR
- Both

**With Deadly Weapons** (Section 190):
- Imprisonment up to **2 years**, OR
- Fine, OR
- Both

## üìñ RIOTING (Section 191 BNS)

### ‚öñÔ∏è Definition (Previously Section 146 IPC)

If members of unlawful assembly use **force or violence** in prosecution of common object, every member is guilty of **rioting**.

---

### üîë Formula

```
UNLAWFUL ASSEMBLY + USE OF FORCE/VIOLENCE = RIOTING
```

---

### üß© Essential Ingredients

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Members of Unlawful Assembly**
- All elements of unlawful assembly must be present
- 5+ persons with unlawful common object

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Use of Force or Violence**
- Actual use of force
- OR show of force/violence
- Not mere threat - actual force used

**3Ô∏è‚É£ In Prosecution of Common Object**
- Force used to achieve common unlawful object
- Causal connection between force and object

---

### üìä Punishment

**Rioting** (Section 192 BNS):
- Imprisonment up to **2 years**, OR
- Fine, OR
- Both

**Rioting with Deadly Weapons** (Section 193):
- Imprisonment up to **3 years**, OR
- Fine, OR
- Both

## üìñ AFFRAY (Section 194 BNS)

### ‚öñÔ∏è Definition (Previously Section 159 IPC)

When **two or more persons** fight in a **public place** and disturb **public peace**.

---

### üß© Essential Ingredients

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Two or More Persons**
- Minimum 2 persons
- No common object required (unlike unlawful assembly)

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Fighting**
- Actual physical fight
- Mutual combat
- Violence between parties

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Public Place**
- Place accessible to public
- Where public peace can be disturbed

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Disturbance of Public Peace**
- Fight disturbs tranquility
- Causes alarm or terror to public

---

### üìä Distinction: Affray vs Rioting

| **Affray** | **Rioting** |
|-----------|------------|
| 2+ persons fighting | 5+ persons in unlawful assembly |
| No common object needed | Common unlawful object essential |
| Just fighting | Force in prosecution of object |
| Up to 1 month punishment | Up to 2-3 years |

---

### üìä Punishment

**Affray** (Section 195):
- Imprisonment up to **1 month**, OR
- Fine up to Rs. 1000, OR
- Both

## üìñ OTHER OFFENSES AGAINST PUBLIC TRANQUILITY

### **Section 196 BNS: Promoting Enmity Between Groups**

**Previously Section 153A IPC**

**Definition**: Promoting enmity between different groups on grounds of religion, race, place of birth, residence, language, caste, or community

**Punishment**: Up to **3 years** + fine + both

---

### **Section 197 BNS: Assertions Prejudicial to National Integration**

**Previously Section 153B IPC**

**Definition**: Imputations, assertions prejudicial to national integration

**Punishment**: Up to **3 years** + fine + both

## üìö Summary

**Unlawful Assembly** (Section 189):
- **5+ persons** with **common unlawful object**
- 5 categories of unlawful object
- Punishment: 6 months (2 years with weapons)

**Rioting** (Section 191):
- Unlawful assembly + **Force/violence**
- Punishment: 2 years (3 years with weapons)

- **2+ persons fighting** in public place
- Disturbs public peace
- Punishment: 1 month

**Common Thread**: All threaten **public tranquility** and social order
