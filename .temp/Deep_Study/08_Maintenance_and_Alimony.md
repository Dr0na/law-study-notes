# üìò MAINTENANCE AND ALIMONY (Deep Study)

## üîç Introduction

Maintenance and alimony are financial support mechanisms provided under Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 to ensure that a spouse (typically the wife) is not left destitute during or after matrimonial proceedings. These provisions recognize the economic vulnerability that often accompanies marital breakdown.

---

## üìö TYPES OF MAINTENANCE

Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 provides **THREE types** of maintenance:

### **1. Maintenance Pendente Lite** [Section 24]
- **Temporary maintenance** during pendency of proceedings
- Includes litigation expenses

### **2. Permanent Alimony and Maintenance** [Section 25]
- **After decree** of divorce, nullity, judicial separation, or restitution
- Lump sum or monthly payments

### **3. Maintenance Under CrPC Section 125**
- **Criminal remedy** available to wife unable to maintain herself
- Independent of Hindu Marriage Act proceedings

---

## üìú PART I: MAINTENANCE PENDENTE LITE [Section 24]

### **SECTION 24: Maintenance Pendente Lite and Expenses of Proceedings**

**Complete Text:**
"Where in any proceeding under this Act it appears to the court that either the wife or the husband, as the case may be, has no independent income sufficient for her or his support and the necessary expenses of the proceeding, it may, on the application of the wife or the husband, order the respondent to pay to the petitioner the expenses of the proceeding, and monthly during the proceeding such sum as, having regard to the petitioner's own income and the income of the respondent, it may seem to the court to be reasonable.

**Provided** that the application for the payment of the expenses of the proceeding and such monthly sum during the proceeding, shall, as far as possible, be disposed of within sixty days from the date of service of notice on the wife or the husband, as the case may be."

---

### **A. CONCEPT AND PURPOSE**

**Definition:**
Maintenance pendente lite means "maintenance during litigation" - temporary financial support during pendency of matrimonial proceedings.

**Purpose:**
1. Enable financially weaker spouse to contest/defend proceedings
2. Ensure access to justice
3. Prevent economic hardship during litigation
4. Level playing field between parties

**Latin Meaning:**
*Pendente lite* = "while litigation is pending"

---

### **B. WHO CAN CLAIM?**

**Either Spouse:**
- **Wife** OR **Husband**
- Whoever lacks independent income sufficient for support

**Gender Neutral:**
Section 24 is gender-neutral. Either party can claim.

**Practical Reality:**
Predominantly claimed by **wives** due to economic dependence.

---

### **C. CONDITIONS FOR GRANT**

#### **1. Proceeding Under Hindu Marriage Act**
- Must be proceeding under HMA, 1955
- Examples: Divorce, nullity, judicial separation, restitution, maintenance

#### **2. Lack of Sufficient Income**
Petitioner must show:
- No independent income, OR
- Independent income insufficient for:
  - Support/maintenance
  - Litigation expenses

#### **3. Application by Petitioner/Respondent**
- Must be applied for
- Not granted suo motu (on court's own motion)

#### **4. Respondent's Capacity**
- Respondent must have means to pay
- Court considers respondent's income and assets

---

### **D. WHAT CAN BE CLAIMED?**

Section 24 covers **TWO components**:

#### **1. Expenses of Proceedings**
- Court fees
- Lawyer's fees
- Other litigation expenses

#### **2. Monthly Sum During Proceedings**
- Regular monthly maintenance
- For livelihood and sustenance
- Throughout pendency of case

---

### **E. FACTORS CONSIDERED BY COURT**

Court considers following factors:

#### **1. Petitioner's Own Income**
- Earning capacity
- Assets and property
- Other sources of income

#### **2. Respondent's Income**
- Salary, business income
- Assets and property
- Earning capacity

#### **3. Standard of Living**
- Lifestyle parties accustomed to
- Social status

#### **4. Needs and Obligations**
- Living expenses
- Children's needs
- Medical expenses
- Educational expenses

#### **5. Conduct of Parties**
- Relevant but not determinative
- Serious misconduct may affect quantum

#### **6. Duration of Marriage**
- Longer marriage may result in higher maintenance

---

### **F. QUANTUM (AMOUNT)**

**No Fixed Formula:**
Court has discretion to fix reasonable amount.

**General Practice:**
Courts often award **25-33%** of husband's net income as interim maintenance (guideline, not rule).

**Lump Sum:**
Court can order lump sum for litigation expenses.

**Monthly:**
Court orders monthly payment for maintenance.

---

### **G. DURATION**

**Starts:** From date of order (or as court directs)

**Ends:**
- Final disposal of main proceeding
- Death of either party
- Remarriage of recipient
- As court directs

**After Final Decree:**
Section 24 maintenance ends; Section 25 (permanent alimony) may apply.

---

### **H. TIME LIMIT FOR DISPOSAL**

**Proviso to Section 24:**
Application for maintenance pendente lite shall, **as far as possible**, be disposed of within **60 days** from date of service of notice.

**Purpose:**
Ensure quick interim relief.

**Practical Reality:**
Often delayed beyond 60 days, but courts try to expedite.

---

### **I. MODIFICATION**

Court can **modify** order:
- Increase maintenance (if circumstances warrant)
- Decrease maintenance (if payer's income reduces)
- On application showing change in circumstances

---

### **J. ENFORCEMENT**

If maintenance pendente lite not paid:
- Execution proceedings
- Attachment of salary/property
- Arrest and detention (in extreme cases)

---

### **K. IMPORTANT CASE LAWS**

#### **1. Chaturbhuj v. Sita Bai** AIR 1970 SC 259
**Held:**
- Section 24 is remedial provision
- Should be liberally construed
- Purpose is to enable weaker party to conduct proceedings

#### **2. Jasbir Kaur Sehgal v. District Judge, Dehradun** AIR 1997 SC 1796
**Held:**
- Court should consider needs of applicant and capacity of respondent
- Quantum should be reasonable and just

#### **3. Sunita Kachwaha v. Anil Kachwaha** AIR 2015 SC 366
**Held:**
- Maintenance pendente lite should be decided expeditiously
- 60-day time limit should be followed as far as possible

---

## üìú PART II: PERMANENT ALIMONY AND MAINTENANCE [Section 25]

### **SECTION 25: Permanent Alimony and Maintenance**

**Complete Text:**
"(1) Any court exercising jurisdiction under this Act may, at the time of passing any decree or at any time subsequent thereto, on application made to it for the purpose by either the wife or the husband, as the case may be, order that the respondent shall pay to the applicant for her or his maintenance and support such gross sum or such monthly or periodical sum for a term not exceeding the life of the applicant as, having regard to the respondent's own income and other property, if any, the income and other property of the applicant, the conduct of the parties and other circumstances of the case, it may seem to the court to be just, and any such payment may be secured, if necessary, by a charge on the immovable property of the respondent.

(2) If the court is satisfied that there is a change in the circumstances of either party at any time after it has made an order under sub-section (1), it may at the instance of either party, vary, modify or rescind any such order.

(3) If the court is satisfied that the party in whose favour an order has been made under this section has remarried or, if such party is the wife, that she has not remained chaste, or, if such party is the husband, that he has had sexual intercourse with any woman outside wedlock, it may at the instance of the other party vary, modify or rescind any such order:

**Provided** that if such party is the wife, any such order shall not be varied, modified or rescinded on the ground that she has not remained chaste, if she was living separately from the husband at the time of the sexual intercourse on which the application for such variation, modification or rescission is based, under circumstances beyond her control and without any fault on her part."

---

### **A. CONCEPT AND PURPOSE**

**Definition:**
Permanent alimony is financial support granted **after decree** in matrimonial proceedings to ensure ongoing maintenance of a spouse.

**Purpose:**
1. Prevent destitution after divorce/separation
2. Recognize contributions to marriage (especially homemaking)
3. Ensure financial security
4. Compensate for loss of matrimonial support

**"Permanent":**
Misnomer - not truly permanent as it can be modified/terminated.

---

### **B. WHEN AVAILABLE?**

Section 25 applies:
1. **At time of passing decree**, OR
2. **Any time subsequent** to decree

**Types of Decrees:**
- Divorce [Section 13]
- Nullity of marriage [Sections 11-12]
- Judicial separation [Section 10]
- Restitution of conjugal rights [Section 9]

---

### **C. WHO CAN CLAIM?**

**Either Spouse:**
- Wife OR Husband
- Gender-neutral provision

**Practical Reality:**
Predominantly claimed by wives.

**Note on Husband's Claim:**
Husband can claim if wife has independent income/property and husband lacks means.

---

### **D. FORMS OF ALIMONY**

Court can order:

#### **1. Lump Sum Payment**
- Single one-time payment
- **"Gross sum"** in Section 25(1)
- Final settlement

#### **2. Monthly/Periodical Payment**
- Regular payments (monthly, quarterly, etc.)
- For specified term not exceeding applicant's life
- Ongoing obligation

#### **3. Combination**
- Lump sum + monthly payment
- Partial settlement

#### **4. Secured Payment**
- Payment secured by charge on immovable property
- Ensures compliance

---

### **E. FACTORS CONSIDERED BY COURT**

Section 25(1) specifies factors:

#### **1. Respondent's Income and Property**
- Earning capacity
- Assets (movable and immovable)
- Business interests
- Investments

#### **2. Applicant's Income and Property**
- Own income sources
- Assets and property
- Earning capacity
- Employment status

#### **3. Conduct of Parties**
- Matrimonial conduct
- Fault in breakdown
- Behavior during marriage

**Note:** Conduct relevant but not sole factor. Even guilty party may get maintenance based on need.

#### **4. Other Circumstances**
- Duration of marriage
- Age of parties
- Standard of living during marriage
- Health and medical needs
- Children's needs and custody
- Contributions to marriage (homemaking, career sacrifice)
- Future prospects

---

### **F. QUANTUM (AMOUNT)**

**No Fixed Formula:**
Court exercises **judicial discretion**.

**Guiding Principles:**

#### **One-Third Rule (Guideline):**
Courts often award approximately **1/3rd of husband's net income** as permanent alimony (guideline, not rigid rule).

**Recent Trend:**
Supreme Court has cautioned against rigid formulas. Each case decided on facts.

**Case Laws on Quantum:**

#### **Jasbir Kaur Sehgal v. District Judge, Dehradun** AIR 1997 SC 1796
- No fixed formula
- Depends on facts and circumstances
- Should be fair and reasonable

#### **Shamima Farooqui v. Shahid Khan** (2015) 5 SCC 705
- Maintenance should enable wife to live with dignity
- Not penury level, not luxurious level

---

### **G. MODIFICATION [Section 25(2)]**

**Change in Circumstances:**
Court can **vary, modify, or rescind** order if satisfied there is change in circumstances.

**Examples of Changed Circumstances:**
- Increase/decrease in income of payer
- Recipient obtains employment
- Recipient inherits property
- Health deterioration requiring higher expenses
- Change in living expenses

**Who Can Apply:**
Either party can apply for modification.

**Burden of Proof:**
Party seeking modification must prove material change.

---

### **H. TERMINATION/RESCISSION [Section 25(3)]**

Court can **vary, modify, or rescind** alimony order if:

#### **1. Remarriage of Recipient**
If party receiving alimony remarries, alimony terminated.

**Rationale:** New spouse has obligation to maintain.

**Automatic or On Application:**
Court rescission required; not automatic (but usually granted on application).

#### **2. Unchastity of Wife**
If wife (receiving alimony) has not remained chaste (sexual relations outside marriage), court may rescind.

**Exception [Proviso to Section 25(3)]:**
If wife was:
- Living separately from husband at time of intercourse
- Under circumstances beyond her control
- Without fault on her part

Then alimony shall NOT be rescinded for unchastity.

#### **3. Husband's Adultery**
If husband (receiving alimony - rare) had sexual intercourse with any woman outside wedlock, court may rescind.

**Criticism:**
Concept of "unchastity" for wife and different standard for husband is criticized as discriminatory.

---

### **I. DURATION**

**Maximum Term:**
"For a term **not exceeding the life of the applicant**" [Section 25(1)]

**In Practice:**
- Often until remarriage
- Or until death of recipient
- Or as modified by court

---

### **J. ENFORCEMENT**

If permanent alimony not paid:
- Execution proceedings
- Attachment of salary
- Attachment of property
- Arrest and civil imprisonment (Section 28 read with Order XXI CPC)

---

### **K. IMPORTANT CASE LAWS**

#### **1. Bhagwan Dutt v. Kamla Devi** AIR 1975 SC 1992
**Held:**
- Section 25 is meant to prevent destitution
- Should be fair and reasonable
- Not punitive

#### **2. Kanchan Devi v. Promod Kumar Mittal** AIR 1996 SC 3188
**Held:**
- Duration of marriage relevant
- Longer marriage may justify higher alimony

#### **3. Shamima Farooqui v. Shahid Khan** (2015) 5 SCC 705
**Held:**
- Maintenance should enable dignified living
- Not calculated to penalize payer
- Balance required

#### **4. Rajnesh v. Neha** (2021) 2 SCC 324
**Held:**
- Detailed guidelines on alimony
- Factors to be considered
- Quantum should be reasonable considering all circumstances

---

## üìú PART III: MAINTENANCE UNDER SECTION 125 CRPC

### **SECTION 125 CRPC: Order for Maintenance of Wives, Children and Parents**

**Nature:**
- **Criminal remedy**
- Available under Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
- Independent of Hindu Marriage Act proceedings

---

### **A. WHO CAN CLAIM?**

Under Section 125 CrPC, following can claim:

1. **Wife** (including divorced wife in certain cases)
2. **Legitimate or illegitimate minor child**
3. **Legitimate or illegitimate major child** (if unable to maintain due to physical/mental abnormality or injury)
4. **Father or mother** unable to maintain themselves

**For Family Law I, focus on wife's claim.**

---

### **B. CONDITIONS FOR WIFE'S CLAIM**

#### **1. Legally Wedded Wife**
- Valid marriage
- Hindu, Muslim, Christian, etc. (applies to all)

#### **2. Unable to Maintain Herself**
- No sufficient means to maintain
- No independent income

#### **3. Husband Has Sufficient Means**
- Husband has income/means
- Refuses or neglects to maintain

#### **4. Wife Not Living in Adultery**
Exception: Wife living in adultery disentitled (unless condoned).

#### **5. Wife Not Refuses to Live Without Sufficient Reason**
If wife refuses to live with husband without sufficient reason, may be denied maintenance.

**Sufficient Reasons:**
- Cruelty by husband
- Husband has another wife
- Other valid grounds

---

### **C. ADVANTAGES OVER SECTION 25 HMA**

#### **1. Speedy Remedy**
- Heard by Magistrate Court
- Summary procedure
- Faster than HMA proceedings

#### **2. No Divorce Required**
- Can claim during subsistence of marriage
- No need to file divorce/separation

#### **3. Criminal Proceedings**
- Failure to pay = criminal contempt
- Arrest possible

#### **4. Accessible**
- Simple procedure
- Can be filed in Magistrate Court

---

### **D. QUANTUM**

**Old Position (Pre-2001):**
Maximum ‚Çπ500 per month.

**After 2001 Amendment:**
**No upper limit**. Magistrate can award any reasonable amount based on circumstances.

**Factors Considered:**
- Husband's income
- Wife's needs
- Standard of living
- Conduct (limited relevance)

---

### **E. ENFORCEMENT**

If order not complied:
- Warrant for arrest
- Attachment of salary
- Recovery as fine

---

### **F. DIVORCED WIFE'S CLAIM**

**Section 125(4) CrPC:**
Divorced wife can claim maintenance if:
- She has not remarried
- Has not received adequate provision (alimony) from ex-husband

**Time Limit:**
Claim must be made within reasonable time.

**Case Law:**

**Shah Bano Begum v. Mohd. Ahmed Khan** AIR 1985 SC 945
- Landmark case
- Divorced Muslim wife entitled to Section 125 maintenance
- Created controversy leading to Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986

---

### **G. INTERACTION WITH HMA SECTION 25**

**Can Both Be Claimed?**
Yes, but:
- If HMA proceeding pending, Magistrate may stay Section 125 proceedings
- Double maintenance not awarded
- Amount under Section 125 may be adjusted against Section 25 alimony

**Strategy:**
Often, wife files Section 125 for immediate interim relief while HMA divorce proceeding pending.

---

|------------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Type** | Interim | Permanent | Summary criminal |
| **When** | During proceedings | After decree | Any time |
| **Court** | District Court | District Court | Magistrate Court |

| **Nature** | Civil | Civil | Criminal |

## üéì KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

### **Three Types of Maintenance:**
1. **Pendente lite** (during proceedings) - Section 24
2. **Permanent alimony** (after decree) - Section 25
3. **CrPC maintenance** (criminal remedy) - Section 125

### **Section 24:**
- During proceedings
- Covers litigation expenses + monthly sum
- 60-day disposal target

### **Section 25:**
- After decree
- Lump sum or monthly
- Factors: income, property, conduct, circumstances
- Can be modified on change of circumstances
- Terminates on remarriage

### **Section 125 CrPC:**
- Criminal remedy
- Faster
- No divorce required
- No upper limit on quantum

### **Gender Neutral:**
Sections 24 and 25 are gender-neutral (though predominantly claimed by wives).

### **Quantum Guidelines:**
- Often **25-33%** of net income (interim)
- Often **33%** of net income (permanent)
- No rigid formula - court's discretion

### **Enforcement:**
All three provisions have enforcement mechanisms including attachment and arrest.
