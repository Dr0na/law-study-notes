## üìù Introduction

Offenses Against the State are the **gravest category** of crimes under BNS 2023, as they strike at the very foundation of state sovereignty, security, and governance. These offenses include waging war against the Government, sedition, and allied crimes that threaten the existence and stability of the State. They are treated with utmost severity, often punishable with **death or life imprisonment**, reflecting their potential to destabilize the nation and endanger public safety on a massive scale.

## üìñ WAGING WAR (Sections 147-150 BNS)

### **Section 147 BNS: Waging War or Attempting to Wage War**

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Provision (Previously Section 121 IPC)

**"Whoever wages war against the Government of India, or attempts to wage such war, or abets the waging of such war, shall be punished with death, or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine."**

---

#### üß© Essential Ingredients

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Waging War**

**"Waging War"** means:
- Actual act of war against Government
- **Organized armed insurrection**
- Use of force or violence against Government
- Intent to overthrow Government or coerce it to change measures

**Characteristics**:
- Must be **organized** and **systematic**
- Not mere riot or disturbance
- Military-like operation
- Significant force involved

**Examples**:
- Armed rebellion against Government
- Organized military attack on Government forces
- Seizure of Government property/territory by force with intent to overthrow
- Organized insurrection to subvert constitutional Government

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Against Government of India**

**Target**: Constituted Government of India

**Scope**: 
- Central Government
- State Governments
- Constitutional authorities

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Intention**

**Intent Required**: 
- Intent to **overthrow** Government by force, OR
- Intent to **coerce** Government to change its measures, OR
- Intent to **subvert** Government authority

**Must Show**: Treasonable intent, not mere criminal intent

---

#### üö´ What is NOT Waging War

**Not Waging War**:
- ‚ùå Mere riot or unlawful assembly
- ‚ùå Isolated act of violence against official
- ‚ùå Protest or demonstration (even if violent)
- ‚ùå Single act without organized effort to overthrow

**Test**: Is there organized effort to subvert or overthrow Government by force?

---

#### üìä Punishment

**Waging War, Attempt, or Abetment**:
- **DEATH penalty**, OR
- **Life imprisonment** + fine

**Nature**: Most serious offense - highest punishment

---

### **Section 148 BNS: Conspiracy to Wage War**

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Provision (Previously Section 121A IPC)

**Definition**: Conspiracy to commit waging war

**Ingredients**:
- Agreement between 2+ persons
- To wage war against Government

**Special Feature**: **No overt act required** - mere conspiracy punishable

**Punishment**: Life imprisonment OR up to **10 years** + fine

---

### **Section 149 BNS: Collecting Arms for Waging War**

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Provision (Previously Section 122 IPC)

**Definition**: Collecting men, arms, or ammunition with **intention** of waging war

**Ingredients**:
- Collecting men, arms, or ammunition
- With intention to wage war
- OR facilitating waging of war

**Punishment**: Life imprisonment OR up to **10 years** + fine

---

### **Section 150 BNS: Concealing Design to Wage War**

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Provision (Previously Section 123 IPC)

**Definition**: Concealing existence of design to wage war when knowing of such design

**Ingredients**:
- Knowledge of design to wage war
- Concealing such design
- By own act or by omission to give information

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **10 years** + fine

## üìñ SEDITION (Section 152 BNS)

### ‚öñÔ∏è Definition (Previously Section 124A IPC)

**"Whoever, by words, either spoken or written, or by signs, or by visible representation, or by electronic communication or by use of financial means, or otherwise, brings or attempts to bring into hatred or contempt, or excites or attempts to excite disaffection towards, the Government established by law shall be punished with imprisonment for life or with imprisonment which may extend to three years, and shall also be liable to fine."**

---

### üß© Essential Ingredients

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Means of Expression**

**Various Forms**:
- **Words** - spoken or written
- **Signs** - symbols, gestures
- **Visible representation** - pictures, cartoons, videos
- **Electronic communication** - social media, emails, websites
- **Financial means** - funding anti-government activities
- **Otherwise** - any other form

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Bringing Into or Exciting**

**Two Acts**:
**(a) Brings or attempts to bring into**:
- Hatred
- Contempt

**(b) Excites or attempts to excite**:
- Disaffection

**Key**: Even attempt to bring/excite sufficient

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Three Mental States Created**

**(a) HATRED**:
- Strong aversion or detestation
- Intense dislike
- Hostility toward Government

**(b) CONTEMPT**:
- Disrespect
- Ridicule or scorn
- Belittling Government

**(c) DISAFFECTION**:
- Absence of affection
- Disloyalty
- Enmity toward Government

---

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Toward Government Established by Law**

**Target**: Constitutional Government of India

**Scope**:
- Central Government
- State Governments
- Constitutional machinery

**Important**: Not against individuals or policies alone, but against Government itself

---

### üõ°Ô∏è 3 Explanations - What is NOT Sedition

**Explanation 1: Comments on Government Measures**

Disapproving of administrative or other actions of Government **with a view to obtain their alteration by lawful means** without exciting hatred, contempt, or disaffection.

**‚úÖ Protected**: Criticism of policies seeking change through lawful means

---

**Explanation 2: Criticism Without Exciting Disaffection**

Comments expressing disapprobation of actions **without exciting or attempting to excite** hatred, contempt, or disaffection.

**‚úÖ Protected**: Expressing disapproval without creating enmity

---

**Explanation 3: Comments on Public Servants**

Comments on public servants in discharge of duties **without exciting or attempting to excite** hatred, contempt, or disaffection.

**‚úÖ Protected**: Criticism of officials' conduct without targeting Government

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è Balance: Freedom of Speech vs State Security

**PERMISSIBLE (NOT Sedition)**:
- ‚úÖ Criticism of Government **policies**
- ‚úÖ Disapproval of administrative actions
- ‚úÖ Seeking change by **lawful means**
- ‚úÖ Expressing dissent peacefully
- ‚úÖ Political opposition
- ‚úÖ Constructive criticism

**SEDITION (Punishable)**:
- ‚ùå Exciting **hatred** against Government
- ‚ùå Creating **disaffection** toward constitutional Government
- ‚ùå Promoting **violence** or **public disorder**
- ‚ùå Intent to **overthrow** Government
- ‚ùå Inciting **rebellion**

---

### üèõÔ∏è Important Case Law

**Kedar Nath Singh v. State of Bihar** (1962):

**Issue**: Constitutional validity of sedition

**Held**: 
- Sedition provision **constitutionally valid**
- BUT scope **narrowed**

**Requirements for Sedition**:
1. Act creating hatred/disaffection toward Government
2. **Tendency to incite violence** or create **public disorder**
3. Mere criticism of Government NOT sedition

**Principle**: 
- **Strong words** expressing disapproval of Government measures acceptable
- But words **inciting people to violence** against Government = Sedition
- Balance between **security** and **freedom of speech**

---

### üìä Punishment

**Sedition** (Section 152):
- **Life imprisonment**, OR
- Imprisonment up to **3 years** + fine

**Nature**:
- Non-bailable
- Cognizable

## üìñ OTHER OFFENSES AGAINST STATE

### **Section 151 BNS: Assaulting President/Governor**

**Previously Section 124 IPC**

**Definition**: Assaulting President, Governor, etc., with intent to compel or restrain exercise of lawful power

**Ingredients**:
- Assault on President/Governor
- Intent to compel or restrain lawful power

**Punishment**: Life imprisonment OR up to **7 years** + fine

---

### **Section 153 BNS: Waging War Against Asiatic Power**

**Previously Section 125 IPC**

**Definition**: Waging war against any Asiatic Power in alliance with Government of India

**Punishment**: Life imprisonment OR up to **7 years** + fine

---

### **Section 154 BNS: Depredation on Territories**

**Previously Section 126 IPC**

**Definition**: Committing depredation on territories of power at peace with Government

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine + forfeiture of property

---

### **Section 155 BNS: Receiving Property Taken in War**

**Previously Section 127 IPC**

**Definition**: Receiving property taken by war or depredation, knowing it was so taken

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to **7 years** + fine + forfeiture

---

### **Section 156 BNS: Public Servant Allowing Prisoner to Escape**

**Previously Section 128 IPC**

**Definition**: Public servant voluntarily allowing prisoner of State or war to escape

**Punishment**: Life imprisonment OR up to **10 years** + fine
