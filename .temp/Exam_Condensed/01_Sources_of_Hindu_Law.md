### **3. DIGESTS & COMMENTARIES (Nibandhas)**

**2 Major Schools Created:**

#### **A. MITAKSHARA (11th Century)**
- **Author:** Vijnaneshwara
- **On:** Yajnavalkya Smriti
- **Area:** Most of India (except Bengal, Assam)

#### **B. DAYABHAGA (12th Century)**
- **Author:** Jimutavahana
- **Area:** Bengal, Assam

**Other Important:**
- Vyavahara Mayukha (Maharashtra, Gujarat)
- Vivada Ratnakara (Mithila)

---

### **4. CUSTOM (ACHARA)**

**3 Types:**
1. **Local Custom** (locality-specific)
2. **Family Custom** (family-specific)
3. **Class/Caste Custom** (community-specific)

**6 Essential Conditions for Valid Custom: "ACRCU-R"**
1. **A**ntiquity (ancient)
2. **C**ertainty (clear)
3. **R**easonableness (not against public policy)
4. **C**ontinuity (continuously observed)
5. **U**niformity (uniformly followed)
6. **R**ecognition (by community)

**Statutory Recognition:** Section 3(a) Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

**Key Case:** Collector of Madura v. Moottoo Ramalinga (1868) - Custom must be proved; burden on claimant

### **1. JUDICIAL DECISIONS (Precedents)**

**Hierarchy:**
1. **Supreme Court** - Binding on all
2. **High Courts** - Binding on subordinate courts
3. **Subordinate Courts** - Persuasive only

**Article 141 Constitution:** SC decisions binding on all courts

**Key Case:** Gowli Buddanna v. CIT (1966) - Judicial decisions = important source

---

### **2. LEGISLATION (Statutes)**

**4 Major Acts (Hindu Code):**

**Mnemonic: "MMSA"**
1. **M**arriage Act, 1955
2. **M**inority & Guardianship Act, 1956
3. **S**uccession Act, 1956
4. **A**doptions & Maintenance Act, 1956

**Supremacy:** Legislation overrides all other sources

---

### **3. EQUITY, JUSTICE & GOOD CONSCIENCE**

**When Applied:**
- Last resort
- No rule in statute, precedent, custom, or traditional sources
- Fill gaps in law

**Case:** Bai Vajia v. Thakorbhai Chelabhai (1953)
