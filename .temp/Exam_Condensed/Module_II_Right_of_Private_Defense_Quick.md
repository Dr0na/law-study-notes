## üìå Fundamental Principle

**"Self-preservation is the first law of nature"**

Right of private defense = Right to protect oneself and property when immediate help from State not available

---

## üõ°Ô∏è Two Types of Defense

### **1. Defense of BODY** (Sections 34-38)
### **2. Defense of PROPERTY** (Sections 39-44)

---

## üî¥ DEFENSE OF BODY

### **When Right Commences & Continues** (Section 35)

**Commences**: When **reasonable apprehension** of danger to body

**Continues**: Till apprehension continues

**Does NOT extend to**: Inflicting **more harm** than necessary

---

### **Against Which Offenses** (Section 36)

Can defend against:
- Assault
- Wrongful restraint/confinement
- Kidnapping/abduction
- Acid attacks
- Rape, unnatural offenses
- Death or grievous hurt attempts

---

### **6 Situations - Right to Cause DEATH** (Section 38)

Can cause death if reasonable apprehension of:

2Ô∏è‚É£ **Grievous hurt**
3Ô∏è‚É£ **Acid attack**
4Ô∏è‚É£ **Rape**
5Ô∏è‚É£ **Unnatural lust**
6Ô∏è‚É£ **Kidnapping/abduction**

**Remember**: **"DGA-RUK"** (Death, Grievous, Acid, Rape, Unnatural, Kidnapping)

---

### **When Right Does NOT Extend to Death** (Section 37)

Cannot cause death if:
- Time to seek public authority protection
- No reasonable apprehension of death/grievous hurt/rape/acid/lust/kidnapping
- Assault not reasonably grave

**Can cause**: Any harm **other than death**

---

## üîµ DEFENSE OF PROPERTY

### **When Right Commences & Continues** (Section 40)

**Commences**: When reasonable apprehension of danger to property

**Continues**: While apprehension continues

---

### **Against Which Offenses** (Section 41)

Can defend against:
- **Robbery**
- **House-breaking by night**
- **Mischief by fire** (on building, tent, vessel)
- **Theft, mischief, criminal trespass** in certain circumstances

---

### **6 Situations - Right to Cause DEATH (Property)** (Section 42)

Can cause death to defend property if:

1Ô∏è‚É£ **Robbery**
2Ô∏è‚É£ **House-breaking by night**
3Ô∏è‚É£ **Mischief by fire** on dwelling/property
4Ô∏è‚É£ **Theft, mischief, trespass** where:
   - Reasonably fear death/grievous hurt if not defended
   - OR property cannot be otherwise protected

**Remember**: **"RHM-TMT"** (Robbery, House-breaking night, Mischief fire, Theft/Mischief/Trespass with fear)

---

### **When Right Does NOT Extend to Death** (Section 43)

Cannot cause death for:
- Simple theft
- Simple mischief
- Simple trespass
- Where death/GH not reasonably apprehended

**Can cause**: Any harm **other than death**

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è 5 LIMITATIONS on Right

### **1. No Time for Public Authority**
- Cannot claim if had time to seek help from authorities

### **2. More Harm Than Necessary**
- Cannot inflict more harm than necessary for defense
- Must be proportionate

### **3. Right Ceases When Danger Passes**
- Right exists only while danger continues
- Cannot continue after threat removed

### **4. Against Innocent Person**
- Cannot harm innocent person even to save oneself
- Exception: Life vs life situation

### **5. Against Public Servant**
- Cannot exercise if you have time to seek authority
- If public servant acts in good faith

## üß† Memory Aids

### **"DGA-RUK"** (Death allowed - BODY)
- **D** - Death
- **G** - Grievous hurt
- **A** - Acid attack
- **R** - Rape
- **U** - Unnatural lust
- **K** - Kidnapping

### **"RHM-TMT"** (Death allowed - PROPERTY)
- **R** - Robbery
- **H** - House-breaking (night)
- **M** - Mischief by fire
- **T** - Theft (with fear of death/GH)
- **M** - Mischief (with fear)
- **T** - Trespass (with fear)

### **"5 L's"** for Limitations
- **L**ack of time for authority
- **L**imit to necessary harm
- **L**asts only while danger
- **L**eave innocent persons
- **L**awful public servants
