## üîµ MITAKSHARA SCHOOL

### **Basic Info (4 Points)**
1. **Author:** Vijnaneshwara (11th Century)
2. **Text:** Commentary on Yajnavalkya Smriti
3. **Meaning:** "Measured in words"
4. **Area:** Most of India (except Bengal, Assam)

### **5 SUB-SCHOOLS**

**Mnemonic: "BMBD-P"**
1. **B**enaras (UP, North India)
2. **M**ithila (Bihar)
3. **B**ombay/Maharashtra (Maharashtra, Gujarat)
4. **D**ravida/Madras (Tamil Nadu, Andhra, Kerala)
5. **P**unjab (Punjab, Haryana, Himachal)

**Quick Memory:**
- **Benaras** = Orthodox
- **Mithila** = Vivada Ratnakara
- **Bombay** = Vyavahara Mayukha
- **Dravida** = Most liberal, Smriti Chandrika
- **Punjab** = Customary law heavy

### **5 KEY PRINCIPLES**

**Mnemonic: "CBSJP"**
1. **C**oparcenary (4-degree rule)
2. **B**irth right (son gets interest by birth)
3. **S**urvivorship (not succession)
4. **J**oint family property (ancestral vs self-acquired)
5. **P**artition (any coparcener can demand)

---

## üî¥ DAYABHAGA SCHOOL

### **Basic Info (4 Points)**
1. **Author:** Jimutavahana (12th Century)
2. **Text:** Original treatise (not commentary)
3. **Meaning:** "Division of Inheritance"
4. **Area:** Bengal, Assam

### **5 KEY PRINCIPLES**

**Mnemonic: "NSSSO"**
1. **N**o birth right (father has absolute ownership)
2. **S**uccession, not survivorship
3. **S**apinda relationship (7 degrees father, 5 mother)
4. **S**apinda order of succession
5. **O**wner's absolute power (father can alienate)
