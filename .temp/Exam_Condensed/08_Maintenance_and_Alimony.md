## üéØ 3 TYPES OF MAINTENANCE

**Mnemonic: "PPC"**
1. **P**endente Lite [Sec 24 HMA] - During proceedings
2. **P**ermanent [Sec 25 HMA] - After decree
3. **C**rPC [Sec 125 CrPC] - Criminal remedy

---

## üíº PENDENTE LITE [Section 24]

### **QUICK POINTS:**

**Meaning:** "During litigation"

**When:** During pendency of HMA proceedings

**Who:** Either spouse (no sufficient income)

**Covers:**
1. Litigation expenses
2. Monthly maintenance

**Time:** **60-day** disposal target

### **FACTORS: "RICO"**
- **R**espondent's income/property
- **I**ncome of petitioner
- **C**onduct of parties
- **O**ther circumstances (needs, obligations)

### **Quantum:**
- Court's discretion
- Guideline: **25-33%** of net income

### **Duration:**
- Until final decree
- Or death/remarriage

### **Key Case:**
**Chaturbhuj v. Sita Bai (1970)** - Liberal construction; enable weaker party

### **Quick Memory:**
"Interim support during fight; 60-day target; 25-33% income"

---

## üí∞ PERMANENT ALIMONY [Section 25]

### **WHEN:**

At time of or after:
- Divorce
- Nullity
- Judicial separation
- Restitution

### **WHO:**
Either spouse (gender-neutral)

### **FORMS: "LMS"**

1. **L**ump sum (one-time)
2. **M**onthly/periodical
3. **S**ecured (by property charge)

---

### **FACTORS [Section 25(1)]: "RICO"**

Same as Section 24:
1. **R**espondent's income/property
2. **I**ncome/property of applicant
3. **C**onduct of parties
4. **O**ther circumstances

**Other Circumstances Include:**
- Duration of marriage
- Age of parties
- Standard of living
- Health needs
- Children's custody/needs
- Contributions (homemaking)
- Future prospects

---

### **QUANTUM:**

**No Fixed Formula**

**Guidelines:**
- Often **1/3 of net income**
- Should enable dignified living
- Not penury, not luxury

**Key Cases:**
- **Jasbir Kaur (1997)** - No rigid formula; fair & reasonable
- **Shamima Farooqui (2015)** - Dignified living standard
- **Rajnesh v. Neha (2021)** - Detailed guidelines

---

### **MODIFICATION [Section 25(2)]:**

**When:** Change in circumstances

**Examples:**
- Income increase/decrease
- Recipient gets job
- Health deterioration
- Living expense changes

**Who Can Apply:** Either party

---

### **TERMINATION [Section 25(3)]:**

**3 Grounds: "RAU"**

1. **R**emarriage of recipient
2. **A**dultery:
   - Wife's unchastity (with exception)
   - Husband's adultery
3. **U**nchastity exception:
   - Wife living separately
   - Beyond her control
   - Without her fault

### **Quick Memory:**
"Remarry = ends; adultery = can end (wife has exception)"

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è CrPC SECTION 125

### **NATURE:**
Criminal remedy, summary procedure

### **WHO CAN CLAIM:**

1. Wife (including divorced)
2. Children (minor/disabled major)
3. Parents

*Focus: Wife's claim*

### **CONDITIONS FOR WIFE:**

1. **Legally wedded wife**
2. **Unable to maintain herself**
3. **Husband has sufficient means**
4. **Refuses/neglects to maintain**

### **BARS:**

1. Living in **adultery** (unless condoned)
2. Refuses to live **without sufficient reason**

**Sufficient Reasons:**
- Cruelty
- Husband has another wife
- Valid matrimonial grounds

---

### **QUANTUM:**

**Old:** Maximum ‚Çπ500/month

**2001 Amendment:** **No upper limit**

**Factors:**
- Husband's income
- Wife's needs
- Standard of living

---

### **ADVANTAGES OVER HMA:**

1. **Speedy** - Magistrate court, summary
2. **No divorce required** - During marriage
3. **Criminal enforcement** - Arrest possible
4. **Accessible** - Simple procedure

### **DIVORCED WIFE [125(4)]:**

Can claim if:
- Not remarried
- Not received adequate provision

### **Key Case:**
**Shah Bano (1985)** - Divorced Muslim wife entitled; created controversy

---

### **INTERACTION WITH HMA:**

- Both can be claimed
- Double maintenance not awarded
- Section 125 amount adjusted against Section 25
- Strategy: 125 for interim; 25 for permanent

## üéì EXAMINER TRIGGER LINES

**Section 24:**
- "Pendente lite means maintenance during litigation under Section 24"
- "Ensures financially weaker spouse can contest proceedings; access to justice"
- "Covers litigation expenses and monthly maintenance during pendency"
- "Should be disposed within 60 days as per proviso"

**Section 25:**
- "Permanent alimony granted at time of or after decree under Section 25"
- "Can be lump sum, monthly, or secured by property charge"
- "Factors: respondent's income, applicant's income, conduct, other circumstances"
- "No fixed formula; often 1/3 of net income as guideline"
- "Can be modified on change of circumstances; terminated on remarriage"

**Section 125:**
- "Section 125 CrPC provides criminal remedy for maintenance"
- "Advantages: speedy, no divorce needed, accessible, arrest possible"
- "2001 Amendment removed upper limit; now unlimited based on needs"
- "Divorced wife can claim if not remarried and not adequately provided"
