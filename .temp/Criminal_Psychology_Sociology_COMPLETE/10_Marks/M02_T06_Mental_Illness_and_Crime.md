## INTRODUCTION

Not all mentally ill are criminals, and not all criminals are mentally ill. But certain mental illnesses increase risk of specific crimes.

---

## TYPES OF MENTAL ILLNESS LINKED TO CRIME (4)

**1. PSYCHOSIS** (Severe - loss of reality)
- **Types:** Schizophrenia (hallucinations), paranoia (delusions), bipolar (mood swings)
- **Crimes:** Violent crimes during episodes, acting on delusions
- **Awareness:** May not know act was wrong

**2. NEUROSIS** (Less severe - anxiety)
- **Types:** Anxiety disorders, OCD, phobias
- **Crimes:** Kleptomania (compulsive stealing), pyromania (compulsive fire-setting)
- **Nature:** Minor crimes, driven by compulsion

**3. PERSONALITY DISORDERS**
- **Antisocial PD (Psychopathy):** No conscience, no empathy, manipulative - MOST criminal type
- **Narcissistic PD:** Excessive self-importance, lacks empathy
- **Borderline PD:** Unstable emotions, impulsive

**4. MENTAL RETARDATION (Intellectual Disability)**
- Low IQ (below 70)
- Poor judgment
- Easily manipulated by others
- May not understand consequences

## LEGAL PROVISIONS

**Section 84, Indian Penal Code:**
> "Nothing is offence done by person who, by unsoundness of mind, cannot know nature of act or that it's wrong."

**Requirements for Insanity Defense:**
1. Had mental disease at time of act
2. Could not know:
   - Nature of act, OR
   - That act was wrong

**M'Naghten Rules (Common Law):** Same test - focuses on knowledge

---

## TREATMENT VS PUNISHMENT

**Modern Approach:** Treat, don't punish

**Rationale:**
- Lack mens rea (guilty mind)
- Cannot be held fully responsible
- Punishment ineffective
- Treatment addresses cause

**Facilities:**
- Psychiatric hospitals
- Forensic mental health units
- Prison mental health services
- Community treatment post-release

---

## IMPORTANT CLARIFICATIONS (4)

1. Most mentally ill people are NOT criminal
2. Most criminals are NOT mentally ill
3. Some overlap exists for certain disorders
4. Treatment and support can prevent crime

---

## CONCLUSION

Mental illness and crime have complex relationship. Certain disorders (psychosis, antisocial personality, intellectual disability) increase risk of specific crimes, but most mentally ill never offend. Section 84 IPC recognizes diminished responsibility for those unable to understand wrongfulness. Modern approach emphasizes treatment over punishment, recognizing humanitarian concerns and therapeutic effectiveness. Proper mental health services can prevent crime while ensuring appropriate legal response when it occurs.
