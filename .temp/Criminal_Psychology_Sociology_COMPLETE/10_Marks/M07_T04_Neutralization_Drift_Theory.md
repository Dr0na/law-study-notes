## INTRODUCTION

Sykes and Matza (1957) observed most delinquents feel guilt and drift in/out of crime. They use "neutralization techniques" to overcome guilt BEFORE committing crimes.

---

## KEY OBSERVATIONS (3)

1. Delinquents feel guilty (not rejecting values completely)
2. Admire law-abiding persons (don't want family victimized)
3. Delinquency is episodic (not always criminal, most age out)

**Conclusion:** NOT fully committed to deviant values

---

## CONCEPT OF DRIFT

**Definition:** Movement between conventional and criminal behavior

**Metaphor:** Like drifting on raft between two shores
- Not committed to crime
- Not committed to convention
- Move between both

**Implication:** Youth can drift IN and OUT of crime

---

## FIVE TECHNIQUES OF NEUTRALIZATION (3D-CA)

> Rationalizations used BEFORE crime to overcome guilt

---

### **1. DENIAL OF RESPONSIBILITY**
- "It wasn't my fault"
- Blame external forces
- Examples: "I was drunk," "Friends made me"

### **2. DENIAL OF INJURY**
- "Nobody really hurt"
- Minimize harm
- Examples: "Store has insurance," "He's rich"

### **3. DENIAL OF VICTIM**
- "They deserved it"
- Victim becomes wrongdoer
- Examples: "He insulted me," "Rich exploit poor"

### **4. CONDEMNATION OF CONDEMNERS**
- "Everyone corrupt, who are they to judge?"
- Attack accusers
- Examples: "Police take bribes," "Politicians steal"

### **5. APPEAL TO HIGHER LOYALTIES**
- "Had to do it for gang/family"
- Loyalty to smaller group
- Examples: "Gang needed me," "Family first"

## POLICY IMPLICATIONS (4)

1. **Challenge rationalizations** - Show logical flaws
2. **Strengthen values** - Moral education
3. **Victim awareness** - Meet real victims
4. **Accountability** - Take responsibility, no excuses
