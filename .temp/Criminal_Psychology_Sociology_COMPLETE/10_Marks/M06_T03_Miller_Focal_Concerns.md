## INTRODUCTION

Walter Miller (1958) argued lower-class culture is distinct and independent, not reactive. Gang delinquency is normal expression of lower-class values (focal concerns).

**Key Difference from Cohen:** NOT reacting to middle class; FOLLOWING their own culture

---

## SIX FOCAL CONCERNS (T-T-S-E-F-A)

**Focal Concerns = Values most important to lower-class culture**

---

### **1. TROUBLE**
- Getting into and out of trouble is major concern
- **Ambivalence:** Trouble brings status BUT also punishment
- **Crime link:** Fighting, drinking bring respect

---

### **2. TOUGHNESS**
- Physical prowess, masculinity highly valued
- Prove "real man" status
- **Why:** Often no male role models (female-headed homes)
- **Crime link:** Fighting to show toughness, cannot back down from challenges

---

### **3. SMARTNESS**
- "Street smarts" - ability to outsmart others
- Cunning, conning, hustling
- NOT academic intelligence
- **Crime link:** Fraud, hustling, outwitting police brings status

---

### **4. EXCITEMENT**
- Seeking thrills, avoiding boredom
- Gambling, drinking, fast driving
- Life otherwise monotonous
- **Crime link:** Joyriding, crimes for thrill

---

### **5. FATE**
- Life controlled by fate/luck, not effort
- "What will be, will be"
- Cannot control destiny
- **Crime link:** Don't worry about consequences, "If caught, it's fate"

---

### **6. AUTONOMY**
- Desire freedom from authority
- "Nobody tells me what to do"
- Resentment of control
- **Crime link:** Defying authority, breaking rules to assert independence

---

## GANG DELINQUENCY EXPLAINED

**Miller's Argument:**
- Boys CONFORMING to lower-class culture
- Expressing focal concerns naturally
- NOT reacting to middle class
- Behavior defined as "delinquent" by middle class, but normal for them

**Process:**
1. Grow up learning focal concerns
2. Form peer groups (gangs)
3. Act according to values (fighting, hustling, excitement)
4. Middle class calls it "crime"

---

## POLICY IMPLICATIONS (4)

1. **Accept cultural differences** - Don't try to change their culture
2. **Provide legal outlets** - Sports for toughness, business for smartness
3. **Realistic expectations** - Don't expect middle-class values
4. **Economic opportunities** - Jobs valuing practical skills
