## INTRODUCTION

Forensic Psychology assists criminal investigation through profiling, enhanced interviews, lie detection, and psychological analysis.

---

## ROLES IN INVESTIGATION (5)

**1. CRIMINAL PROFILING**
- Analyze crime scene
- Predict offender characteristics (age, gender, personality)
- Narrow suspect pool
- **Best for:** Serial murders, rapes, arson

**2. COGNITIVE INTERVIEW (Witness)**
- Enhance memory recall
- **Techniques:** Context reinstatement, report everything, different perspectives, different orders
- **Result:** More accurate information

**3. POLYGRAPH (Lie Detection)**
- Measures: Heart rate, blood pressure, sweating
- **Theory:** Lying causes stress
- **India:** NOT admissible as evidence, needs consent (Selvi case, 2010)
- **Problem:** Can be beaten, measures anxiety not lying

**4. PSYCHOLOGICAL AUTOPSY**
- Determine suicide vs homicide vs accident
- Interview family, review documents
- Reconstruct mental state before death

**5. STATEMENT VALIDITY**
- Assess witness credibility
- Distinguish real from false memories
- Content analysis

## POLICE APPLICATIONS

**Personnel Selection:**
- Psychological testing
- Assess suitability for police work

**Training:**
- Communication, de-escalation
- Stress management
- Decision-making under pressure

**Hostage Negotiation:**
- Understand hostage-taker psychology
- Build rapport
- Active listening
- Reduce emotional arousal

---

## CONCLUSION

Forensic Psychology provides vital investigation support through profiling, enhanced interview techniques, lie detection, psychological autopsies, and statement analysis. Applications in police selection, training, and negotiation further demonstrate utility. While techniques like polygraph have limitations, when used appropriately as investigative tools by trained professionals, forensic psychology significantly enhances investigation effectiveness.
