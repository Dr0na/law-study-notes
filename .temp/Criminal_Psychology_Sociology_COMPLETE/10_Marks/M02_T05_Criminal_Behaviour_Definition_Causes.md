## DEFINITIONS OF CRIMINAL BEHAVIOUR (4)

**1. Legal Definition**
> Acts prohibited by law and punished by state

**2. Moral/Religious Definition**
> Acts violating moral code, punishable by God

**3. Social Definition**
> Acts violating society's norms, punishable by community

**4. Psychological Definition**
> Acts causing serious mental harm to victim

**Comprehensive:**
> Any anti-social behavior punishable by law or community norms

---

## CAUSES OF CRIMINAL BEHAVIOUR (6)

**1. Financial Problems**
- Struggle for basic survival
- Poverty, starvation
- No legitimate income
- **Result:** Property crimes for survival

**2. Low Social Status**
- Feel looked down upon
- Resentment toward society
- Sense of injustice
- **Result:** Violent crimes expressing frustration

**3. Mental Illness**
- Schizophrenia, bipolar, psychopathy
- Impaired judgment
- Cannot distinguish right/wrong clearly
- **Result:** Impulsive, violent crimes

**4. Anti-Social Values**
- Believe crime is justified
- No moral conscience
- Rationalize criminal acts
- **Result:** Persistent offending, no remorse

**5. Family Background**
- Broken homes, parental conflict
- Child abuse, neglect
- Criminal family members
- Lack of support
- **Result:** Early delinquency, join gangs

**6. Low Self-Control**
- Cannot control impulses
- Cannot delay gratification
- Don't think before acting
- **Result:** Impulsive crimes without planning

## PSYCHODYNAMICS

**Argument:** Crime originates in personality of offenders

**Key Point:** Something in personality compels them to revolt against norms

**Summary:** Criminal is aggressive, frustrated person dominated by childhood events

---

## CONCLUSION

Criminal behavior can be defined from legal, moral, social, or psychological perspectives. Six main causes include financial problems, low status, mental illness, anti-social values, poor family background, and low self-control. Psychodynamic perspective emphasizes personality disturbances from childhood. Multiple factors interact to produce crimeâ€”no single cause explains all. Understanding varied causes enables targeted prevention and treatment addressing specific risk factors.
