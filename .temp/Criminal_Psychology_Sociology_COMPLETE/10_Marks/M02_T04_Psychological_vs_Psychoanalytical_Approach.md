## INTRODUCTION

Criminal psychology uses two main approaches: psychoanalytical (focuses on unconscious mind) and psychological (focuses on observable behavior).

---

## PSYCHOANALYTICAL APPROACH

**Founded by:** Sigmund Freud

**Focus:** Unconscious mind and childhood experiences

### Freud's Personality (3)

**ID:**
- Primitive drives (food, sex, sleep)
- Pleasure principle: "I want now!"
- Unconscious, impulsive

**EGO:**
- Reality principle: "Be practical"
- Mediator between Id and reality
- Rational

**SUPEREGO:**
- Moral conscience
- Morality principle: "That's wrong!"
- Creates guilt

### Crime from Imbalance

- Underdeveloped Superego â†’ No guilt (psychopathy)
- Overdeveloped Superego â†’ Crime for self-punishment

---

## PSYCHOLOGICAL APPROACH

**Focus:** Observable behavior

**Characteristics:**
- Scientific experiments and tests
- Studies measurable behavior
- Learning and conditioning emphasized
- Current behavior, less focus on childhood

---

## COMPARISON

| Aspect | Psychoanalytical | Psychological |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Focus** | Unconscious mind | Observable behavior |
| **Method** | Case studies, therapy | Experiments, tests |
| **Key Concept** | Id-Ego-Superego | Learning, conditioning |
| **Cause** | Personality conflict | Learned behavior |
| **Childhood** | Crucial | Important but not sole |
| **Treatment** | Psychotherapy | Behavior modification |
| **Testability** | Difficult | Easier |

---

## APPLICATIONS

**Psychoanalytical:**
- Deep personality understanding
- Long-term therapy
- Unconscious motivations

**Psychological:**
- Behavior modification programs
- Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
- Measurable, time-limited interventions
- Prison programs

---

## CONCLUSION

Psychoanalytical approach offers deep understanding through unconscious and childhood, while psychological approach provides scientific, testable methods. Modern forensic psychology integrates bothâ€”using scientific methods while recognizing personality dynamics. Both contribute valuable insights for comprehensive assessment and treatment of offenders.
