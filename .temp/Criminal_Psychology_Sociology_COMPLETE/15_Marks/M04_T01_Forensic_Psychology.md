## INTRODUCTION

Forensic Psychology is application of psychology to legal system helping in investigation, court proceedings, and prison management.

**Etymology:** "Forensic" from Latin "forensis" meaning of the forum or court.

**Definition:** Application of psychological knowledge and methods to criminal justice system encompassing criminal and civil law.

---

## SCOPE (7 Areas)

- Criminal investigation through profiling and interviews
- Courts with expert testimony and evaluations
- Prisons through treatment and classification
- Police including selection, training, stress management
- Victims providing counseling and support services
- Juvenile justice with assessment and rehabilitation
- Civil cases involving custody and competency hearings

## APPLICATION 1: INVESTIGATION (5 Roles)

**1. Criminal Profiling:**
- Analyze crime scene evidence systematically, predict offender demographics and psychology, narrow suspect pool efficiently - best for serial murders, rapes, arson.

**2. Interview Techniques:**
- Cognitive Interview enhances witness memory through context reinstatement and varied recall perspectives
- Reid Technique uses nine-phase interrogation to elicit confessions (though criticized for false confession risk)

**3. Polygraph (Lie Detection):**
- Measures heart rate, blood pressure, breathing, sweating assuming lying causes stress â†’ physiological changes
- India status: NOT admissible as evidence, requires consent (Selvi v. State 2010), can be beaten by training or drugs

**4. Psychological Autopsy:**
- Determines suicide vs homicide vs accident when unclear, interviews family and reviews documents to reconstruct mental state before death

**5. Statement Analysis:**
- Assesses witness credibility and truthfulness, distinguishes real memories from fabricated through content analysis

---

## APPLICATION 2: COURT (4 Roles)

**1. Expert Witness:**
- Testifies on mental state, competency, responsibility, risk assessment, sentencing factors - requires advanced degree and forensic training.

**2. Competency to Stand Trial:**
- Assesses accused's PRESENT ability to understand proceedings and assist defense
- Criteria (5): Understand proceedings, understand consequences, communicate with lawyer, assist in defense, make rational decisions
- Outcome: Competent (trial proceeds), Incompetent (treatment to restore), Never competent (alternative procedures)

**3. Criminal Responsibility:**
- Section 84 IPC evaluates unsoundness of mind at time of offense
- Assessment: Was accused mentally ill? Could they understand nature or wrongfulness of act?
- Psychologist evaluates retrospectively and currently with diagnosis and causal link

**4. Eyewitness Testimony:**
- Factors affecting accuracy: Stress impairs memory (weapon focus), time delay causes forgetting, leading questions create false memories, cross-race identification harder
- Key point: Witness confidence does NOT equal accuracy despite common belief

## APPLICATION 3: PRISON (6 Roles)

**1. Classification:**
- Match inmates to appropriate security level (maximum, medium, minimum, open) - assesses security risk, mental health needs, program suitability ensuring safety.

**2. Treatment Programs:**
- Cognitive-behavioral therapy targets criminal thinking, anger management for violent offenders, sex offender treatment reducing recidivism, substance abuse programs addressing addiction.

**3. Mental Health Services:**
- Prevalence 3-4 times higher than general population requires screening, individual/group therapy, medication management, crisis intervention when needed.

**4. Suicide Prevention:**
- Risk factors: mental illness especially depression, first-time incarceration shock, solitary confinement, life sentences, victimization by others
- Prevention: Screen high-risk at intake, regular monitoring, remove hazards, crisis procedures, peer support programs

**5. Managing Difficult Inmates:**
- Violent inmates need anger management and special housing, mentally ill require psychiatric treatment, gang members need separation, sexual predators protective custody

**6. Release Preparation:**
- Vocational training and life skills development, psychological preparation for freedom adjustment, family reintegration counseling, community linkages including halfway houses
