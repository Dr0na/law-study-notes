## **DEFINITION OF FORENSIC PSYCHOLOGY**

> Application of psychological knowledge and methods to the criminal justice system.

**Etymology:** "Forensic" from Latin "forensis" (of the forum/court)

---

## **HISTORICAL DEVELOPMENT ABROAD**

### Early Pioneers (1900s-1920s)

**Hugo Münsterberg (1863-1916)**
- **Title:** "Father of Forensic Psychology"
- **Book:** "On the Witness Stand" (1908)
- **Contributions:**
  - Psychology of testimony
  - Detection of deception
  - Memory reliability research
  - Eyewitness suggestibility

**William Stern (1871-1938)**
- Studied eyewitness testimony reliability
- Developed reality testing methods
- Applied psychology to law

---

### Timeline Abroad

**1910s-1920s:**
- Psychology enters courtrooms
- Expert testimony begins
- Mental testing in prisons

**1960s-1970s:**
- FBI develops criminal profiling
- Behavioral Science Unit (1972)
- Systematic research on criminals

**1980s-Present:**
- Professionalization of field
- Academic programs established
- Evidence-based practices
- Integration with neuroscience

---

## **DEVELOPMENT IN INDIA**

### Pre-Independence
- Limited application under British rule
- Lunacy Act, 1912
- Minimal mental health consideration

### Post-Independence (1950s-1990s)
- Very limited growth
- Mostly academic interest
- Few practicing forensic psychologists

### Current Status (2000s-Present)
- Gradual growth and recognition
- Some police departments consult psychologists
- Academic research increasing
- Still developing compared to West

### Key Indian Institutions (4)
- National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB)
- Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI)
- Forensic Science Laboratories
- National Institute of Criminology and Forensic Science (NICFS), Delhi

### Challenges in India
- Still developing field
- Limited formal training programs
- Few dedicated positions
- Awareness needs increase
- Resource constraints

---

## **LEGAL FRAMEWORK IN INDIA**

### Relevant Provisions
- **Section 84, IPC:** Act of insane person (criminal responsibility)
- **Section 328-330, CrPC:** Procedures for unsoundness of mind
- **Mental Healthcare Act, 2017:** Rights and treatment
- **Section 45, Evidence Act:** Expert opinion admissibility

---

## **CONCLUSION**

Forensic Psychology originated in early 20th century abroad with pioneers like Münsterberg and Stern, evolving into sophisticated professional field with academic programs and evidence-based practices. In India, field remains in developmental stage with growing recognition, institutional support, and academic interest, though facing challenges of limited training, resources, and awareness. Future growth requires increased investment in training, institutional positions, and integration into criminal justice system.
