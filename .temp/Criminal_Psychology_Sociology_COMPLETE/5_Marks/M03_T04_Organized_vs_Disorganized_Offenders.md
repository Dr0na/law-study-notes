## **FBI CLASSIFICATION**

FBI's Behavioral Science Unit classifies violent offenders into two categories based on crime scene characteristics.

---

## **ORGANIZED OFFENDER**

### Characteristics (10)
- **Above average intelligence**
- **Planned, premeditated crime**
- **Controlled crime scene** (orderly)
- **Targeted stranger victim** (selected)
- **Brings own weapon** to scene
- **Uses restraints** on victim
- **Moves or hides body**
- **Little physical evidence** left
- **Good social skills**
- **Employed or skilled worker**
- **Lives with partner**
- **Follows media coverage** after crime

---

## **DISORGANIZED OFFENDER**

### Characteristics (10)
- **Below average intelligence**
- **Spontaneous, impulsive crime**
- **Chaotic crime scene** (messy)
- **Victim of opportunity** (may be known)
- **Uses weapon at scene** (not brought)
- **Little or no restraints** used
- **Leaves body at scene** openly
- **Much physical evidence** left
- **Poor social skills** (loner)
- **Unemployed or unskilled**
- **Lives alone** or with parents
- **May change behavior** drastically after

---

## **COMPARISON TABLE**

| Feature | Organized | Disorganized |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| IQ | Above average | Below average |
| Planning | Planned | Spontaneous |
| Scene | Controlled | Chaotic |
| Victim | Targeted | Opportunity |
| Weapon | Brings own | Uses at scene |
| Evidence | Minimal | Extensive |
| Social | Good skills | Poor skills, loner |
| Work | Employed | Unemployed |

---

## **INVESTIGATIVE IMPLICATIONS**

**Organized Offender Investigation:**
- Look for intelligent, employed suspect
- May have vehicle and tools
- Will follow investigation in media
- Interview strategies need sophistication

**Disorganized Offender Investigation:**
- Look for local, socially inadequate person
- May live near crime scene
- Poor social functioning
- May have mental illness
- Less sophisticated interview approach

---

## **CONCLUSION**

FBI's organized vs disorganized classification helps investigators predict offender characteristics from crime scene analysis. Organized offenders plan carefully and leave minimal evidence, while disorganized offenders act impulsively and leave chaotic scenes. Understanding this distinction guides investigation strategy, suspect prioritization, and interview techniques for effective apprehension.
