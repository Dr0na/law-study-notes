### **HOUSE BREAKING AND ROBBERY**

#### **Introduction**

House breaking and robbery are serious offenses against property under the Bhartiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023. While house breaking (Section 309 BNS) involves entering or exiting a building through specific illegal means, robbery (Section 309 BNS) involves theft or extortion combined with violence or threat. When house breaking is committed in order to commit robbery, the offense becomes more serious. Dacoity (Section 310 BNS) is an aggravated form of robbery involving five or more persons.

---

### **I. HOUSE BREAKING (Section 309 BNS)**

#### **Definition**

A person is said to commit **"house-breaking"** who commits house-trespass if he effects his entrance into the house or any part of it, or his exit therefrom, in any of the **six ways** specified in the section.

#### **6 Ways of House Breaking**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Breaking Through Wall, Roof, Floor, Door, Window, or Other Part**
- Making opening by force
- Example: Breaking window to enter

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Through Opening Made by Breaking**
- Using opening created by breaking
- Even if someone else made the opening

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Through Passage Made by Criminal Acts**
- Using passage created by criminal means
- Example: Tunnel dug by accomplice

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Through Opening Not Intended for Entry/Exit**
- Using opening meant for other purpose
- Example: Entering through ventilator, chimney

**5Ô∏è‚É£ By Opening Lock Not Provided for Purpose**
- Opening lock by improper means
- Example: Lock picking, using duplicate key

**6Ô∏è‚É£ Using Criminal Force or Threat**
- Threatening or using force against occupant
- Example: Forcing watchman to let one in

---

### **II. WHEN HOUSE BREAKING BECOMES ROBBERY**

#### **Connection Between House Breaking and Robbery**

**House Breaking becomes Robbery when**:
- Person commits **house-breaking** (enters/exits by any of 6 ways)
- **After** house-breaking, commits theft or extortion
- OR commits theft/extortion and uses house-breaking to **exit**

**Section 311 BNS**: "If any person, in committing or in attempting to commit robbery, voluntarily causes hurt, such person and any other person jointly concerned in committing or attempting to commit such robbery, shall be punished with imprisonment for life, or with rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to ten years, and shall also be liable to fine."

**Aggravating Factors**:
- If hurt caused during robbery ‚Üí Enhanced punishment
- House breaking + Robbery = More serious offense

---

### **III. ROBBERY (Section 309 BNS)**

#### **Definition**

**Robbery** is either **theft** or **extortion** combined with:
- Violence or threat of violence, AND
- Intent to commit theft/extortion OR to overcome resistance

#### **5 Essential Ingredients of Robbery**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Theft or Extortion Must Occur**

**Base Element**: Must be theft OR extortion

**Theft** (Section 303 BNS):
- Dishonest taking of movable property
- Without consent
- With intention to take dishonestly

**Extortion** (Section 308 BNS):
- Intentionally putting person in fear
- To deliver property or valuable security

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Use of Force or Threat of Injury**

**Requirement**: Voluntary causing or attempting to cause:
- Death, OR
- Hurt, OR
- Wrongful restraint, OR
- Fear of instant death/hurt/restraint

**"Voluntarily"**: Must be intentional use of force

**Examples**:
- Hitting victim with weapon
- Threatening with knife/gun
- Restraining victim while taking property

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Force Must Be Against Person**

- Force must be used **on the person** whose property is taken
- OR on any person present at the time
- Not against inanimate objects only

**Example**:
- Breaking lock = NOT robbery (only theft)
- Breaking lock + Pushing person = Robbery

---

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Force Used in Order to Commit Theft/Extortion**

**Two Scenarios**:

**(a) Force to commit theft/extortion**:
- Force used to facilitate taking of property
- Example: Hitting person and then taking wallet

**(b) Force to retain property after theft**:
- Theft committed, force used to escape with property
- Example: Snatching purse, pushing victim who tries to stop

---

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Fear of Instant Harm**

**Timing**: Fear must be of **instant** (immediate) harm
- Not future harm
- Not delayed threat

**Degree**: Fear of:
- Death, OR
- Hurt, OR
- Wrongful restraint

---

#### **Formula for Robbery**

```
THEFT/EXTORTION + FORCE/THREAT = ROBBERY
```

**Example 1**:
- A snatches B's gold chain forcefully from neck
- Theft + Force = **ROBBERY**

**Example 2**:
- A points gun at B and demands money
- Extortion + Threat of death = **ROBBERY**

---

### **IV. DACOITY (Section 310 BNS)**

#### **Definition**

**Dacoity** is robbery committed by **five or more persons** conjointly.

#### **Essential Ingredients**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Five or More Persons**
- Minimum 5 persons required
- Acting conjointly (together)

**2Ô∏è‚É£ All Elements of Robbery**
- Must satisfy all requirements of robbery
- Theft/extortion + Force/threat

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Acting Conjointly**
- All acting in furtherance of common intention
- Coordinated action

---

### **V. DISTINCTION: Robbery vs Dacoity**

| **Aspect** | **ROBBERY** | **DACOITY** |
|------------|------------|-----------|
| **Persons** | Can be 1 or more | Minimum **5 persons** required |
| **Acting** | May be alone or together | Must act **conjointly** |
| **Punishment** | Imprisonment up to 10 years | Imprisonment for **life** or up to 10 years |
| **Seriousness** | Serious offense | More serious (higher minimum persons) |
| **Section** | Section 309 BNS | Section 310 BNS |
| **Example** | 2 persons snatch and run | 6 persons attack and loot |

**Key Difference**: **Number of persons** (Robbery: any number, Dacoity: minimum 5)

---

### **VI. DISTINCTION: Theft vs Robbery**

| **Aspect** | **THEFT** | **ROBBERY** |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| **Definition** | Dishonest taking of property | Theft + Force/threat |
| **Force/Violence** | ‚ùå No force used | ‚úÖ Force/threat essential |
| **Consent** | Without consent | Against will (by force) |
| **Seriousness** | Less serious | More serious |
| **Punishment** | Up to 3 years | Up to 10 years |
| **Example** | Pickpocketing | Snatching with force |

---

### **VII. PUNISHMENT PROVISIONS**

**House Breaking** (Section 309 BNS):
- Imprisonment up to 2 years + Fine

**Robbery** (Section 309 BNS):
- Imprisonment up to 10 years + Fine

**Robbery with Hurt** (Section 311 BNS):
- Life imprisonment OR up to 10 years + Fine

**Dacoity** (Section 310 BNS):
- Life imprisonment OR up to 10 years + Fine

---

### **Conclusion**

House breaking, robbery, and dacoity represent escalating degrees of offenses against property under BNS 2023. House breaking involves entering or exiting premises through six specified illegal ways. When house breaking is combined with theft or extortion, and force or threat is used, it becomes robbery. Robbery is fundamentally **theft or extortion plus violence or threat of violence**, requiring five essential elements including use of force on persons and fear of instant harm. When robbery is committed by five or more persons acting conjointly, it becomes dacoity, attracting even more serious punishment. The law distinguishes these offenses based on **manner of commission, number of offenders, and degree of violence**, ensuring proportionate punishment for varying levels of threat to person and property.
