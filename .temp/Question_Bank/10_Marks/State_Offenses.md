### **OFFENSES AGAINST THE STATE**

#### **Introduction**

Offenses against the State are the most serious category of crimes under the Bhartiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, covered under **Sections 147-156** (previously Sections 121-130 IPC). These offenses strike at the very foundation of state sovereignty, security, and governance. They include waging war against the Government, sedition, and allied offenses. These crimes are treated with utmost severity as they threaten the existence and stability of the State itself and are often punishable with death or life imprisonment.

---

### **I. WAGING WAR AGAINST GOVERNMENT (Sections 147-150 BNS)**

#### **Section 147 BNS: Waging War or Attempting to Wage War**

**Previously Section 121 IPC**

**Definition**: "Whoever wages war against the Government of India, or attempts to wage such war, or abets the waging of such war, shall be punished with death, or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine."

#### **Essential Ingredients**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Waging War**:
- Must be actual **act of war** against Government
- Not mere riot or disturbance
- Organized armed insurrection

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Against Government of India**:
- Target must be constituted Government
- Intent to overthrow or coerce Government

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Intention**:
- Must have intention to wage war
- Intent to subvert Government by force

#### **What Constitutes "Waging War"**

**Elements**:
- Use of **force or violence**
- **Organized** and **systematic**
- Intent to **overthrow** Government or **compel** it to change measures
- Not mere riot or unlawful assembly

**Examples**:
- Armed rebellion against Government
- Organized military attack on Government forces
- Seizure of Government property by force with intent to overthrow

**Not Waging War**:
- Mere riot or disturbance
- Protest without intent to overthrow
- Single act of violence without organized effort

---

#### **Section 148 BNS: Conspiracy to Wage War**

**Previously Section 121A IPC**

**Definition**: Conspiracy to commit waging war

**Punishment**: Life imprisonment or imprisonment up to 10 years + fine

**Key Point**: Mere conspiracy punishable - no overt act required beyond agreement

---

#### **Section 149 BNS: Collecting Arms for Waging War**

**Previously Section 122 IPC**

**Definition**: Collecting men, arms, or ammunition with intention of waging war

**Punishment**: Life imprisonment or up to 10 years + fine

---

#### **Section 150 BNS: Concealing Design to Wage War**

**Previously Section 123 IPC**

**Definition**: Concealing existence of design to wage war when knowing of design

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to 10 years + fine

### **II. SEDITION (Section 152 BNS)**

#### **Definition (Section 152 BNS)**

**Previously Section 124A IPC**

**"Whoever, by words, either spoken or written, or by signs, or by visible representation, or by electronic communication or by use of financial means, or otherwise, brings or attempts to bring into hatred or contempt, or excites or attempts to excite disaffection towards, the Government established by law shall be punished with imprisonment for life or with imprisonment which may extend to three years, and shall also be liable to fine."**

#### **Essential Ingredients**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Words, Signs, or Visible Representation**

**Means of Expression**:
- Spoken words (speeches)
- Written words (articles, posts, books)
- Signs or symbols
- Visible representation (cartoons, images, videos)
- Electronic communication (social media, emails)
- Financial means (funding anti-government activities)

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Bringing/Exciting Hatred, Contempt, or Disaffection**

**Three Mental States Created**:

**(a) Hatred**: Strong aversion or detestation

**(b) Contempt**: Disrespect, ridicule, or scorn

**(c) Disaffection**: Absence of affection, enmity, disloyalty

**Target**: Toward **Government established by law**

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Toward Government Established by Law**

**Meaning**: Constitutional Government of India

**Important**: Not against individuals or policies, but against Government itself

---

#### **3 Explanations (Exceptions) - What is NOT Sedition**

**Explanation 1**: **Comments on Government Measures**
- Disapproving of Government's measures or administrative actions
- **With a view to obtain their alteration by lawful means**
- ‚úÖ NOT sedition

**Explanation 2**: **Criticism Without Disaffection**
- Comments expressing disapprobation
- Without exciting or attempting to excite hatred, contempt, or disaffection
- ‚úÖ NOT sedition

**Explanation 3**: **Comments on Judicial Conduct**
- Comments on public servants in discharge of duties
- Without exciting or attempting to excite hatred, contempt, or disaffection
- ‚úÖ NOT sedition

#### **Balance - Freedom of Speech vs State Security**

**Permissible** (NOT Sedition):
- ‚úÖ Criticism of Government policies
- ‚úÖ Disapproval of administrative actions
- ‚úÖ Seeking change by lawful means
- ‚úÖ Expressing dissent

**Sedition** (Punishable):
- ‚ùå Exciting hatred against Government
- ‚ùå Creating disaffection toward constitutional Government
- ‚ùå Promoting violence or disorder
- ‚ùå Intent to overthrow Government

---

#### **Recent Judicial Trends**

**Kedar Nath Singh v. State of Bihar** (1962):
- Upheld constitutional validity of sedition
- But narrowed scope
- Must involve **incitement to violence** or **intention to create public disorder**
- Mere criticism not sedition

**Principle**: Sedition must involve:
1. Act creating hatred/disaffection
2. Tendency to incite violence or public disorder
3. Not mere criticism of Government

### **III. OTHER OFFENSES AGAINST STATE**

#### **1Ô∏è‚É£ Section 151 BNS: Assaulting President/Governor**

**Previously Section 124 IPC**

- Assaulting President, Governor with intent to compel or restrain exercise of lawful power
- Punishment: Life imprisonment or up to 7 years + fine

---

#### **2Ô∏è‚É£ Section 153 BNS: Waging War Against Asiatic Power**

**Previously Section 125 IPC**

- Waging war against any Asiatic Power in alliance with Government
- Punishment: Life imprisonment or up to 7 years + fine

---

#### **3Ô∏è‚É£ Section 154 BNS: Depredation on Territories**

**Previously Section 126 IPC**

- Committing depredation on territories of power at peace with Government
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to 7 years + fine + forfeiture

---

#### **4Ô∏è‚É£ Section 155 BNS: Receiving Property Taken in War**

**Previously Section 127 IPC**

- Receiving property taken by war or depredation
- Knowing it was so taken
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to 7 years + fine + forfeiture

---

#### **5Ô∏è‚É£ Section 156 BNS: Public Servant Allowing Prisoner to Escape**

**Previously Section 128 IPC**

- Public servant voluntarily allowing prisoner of State or war to escape
- Punishment: Life imprisonment or up to 10 years + fine

### **Conclusion**

Offenses against the State under BNS 2023 represent the gravest category of crimes, threatening state sovereignty and public order. **Waging war** is the most serious, involving organized armed insurrection against the Government, punishable with death or life imprisonment. **Sedition** involves bringing Government into hatred, contempt, or disaffection through words, signs, or representations, but recent judicial interpretation requires incitement to violence or public disorder, not mere criticism. 

The law carefully balances **state security** with **fundamental rights** like freedom of speech, ensuring that genuine criticism and dissent through lawful means remain protected while punishing acts that threaten state existence or incite violence. Other offenses like assaulting constitutional authorities, waging war against allied powers, and allowing prisoners to escape complete the framework protecting state security. These provisions, while severe, are essential for maintaining sovereignty, territorial integrity, and constitutional governance.
