### **DISTINCTION BETWEEN EXTORTION AND THEFT**

#### **Introduction**

Extortion and Theft are two important offenses against property under the Bhartiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023. While both involve taking another person's property dishonestly, they differ fundamentally in the **manner of taking**. Theft involves taking property **without consent**, while extortion involves taking property **with consent obtained through threat or intimidation**. Theft is defined under **Section 303 BNS** (previously Section 378 IPC), while Extortion is defined under **Section 308 BNS** (previously Section 383 IPC).

---

### **I. THEFT (Section 303 BNS)**

#### **Definition**

**"Whoever, intending to take dishonestly any movable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent, moves that property in order to such taking, is said to commit theft."**

#### **5 Essential Ingredients of Theft**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Dishonest Intention**

**Requirement**: Must be **dishonest intention** to take property

**Dishonestly** (Section 23 BNS): Doing anything with intention of causing wrongful gain to one person or wrongful loss to another.

**Test**: Did accused intend to cause wrongful gain/loss?

**Example**: Taking property knowing one has no right to it.

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Movable Property**

**Requirement**: Property must be **movable**

**Movable Property** includes:
- Corporeal property (tangible - car, jewelry, money)
- Incorporeal property (intangible - shares, bonds)

**NOT Theft**:
- ‚ùå Immovable property (land, buildings)
- ‚ùå Services
- ‚ùå Intangible rights (unless deemed property)

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ Stealing car, laptop, jewelry ‚Üí Theft
- ‚ùå Occupying someone's land ‚Üí Not theft (different offense)

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Taking Out of Possession**

**Requirement**: Taking property **out of possession** of another person

**Possession** means:
- Physical custody OR
- Legal control over property

**Key Point**: Must take from someone's possession

**Example**:
- Taking money from person's wallet = Theft
- Taking abandoned property = Not theft (no possession)

---

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Without Consent**

**Requirement**: Taking must be **without consent** of possessor

**Critical Element**: Absence of consent

**Distinctions**:
- No consent = Theft
- Consent obtained by deception = Cheating (not theft)
- Consent obtained by threat = Extortion (not theft)

**Example**:
- Secretly taking property = Without consent ‚Üí Theft
- Taking with permission = Not theft

---

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Moving the Property**

**Requirement**: Accused must **move** the property in order to take it

**"Moving"**: Any motion or change of position

**Key Point**: Even slightest movement sufficient

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ Lifting article from shelf ‚Üí Movement
- ‚úÖ Pushing car few inches ‚Üí Movement
- ‚ùå Merely touching without moving ‚Üí Not theft

**Illustration**: A cuts down tree on B's land intending to dishonestly take it. Tree falls. Not yet theft until A moves it away.

---

### **II. EXTORTION (Section 308 BNS)**

#### **Definition**

**"Whoever intentionally puts any person in fear of any injury to that person, or to any other, and thereby dishonestly induces the person so put in fear to deliver to any person any property or valuable security or anything signed or sealed which may be converted into a valuable security, commits extortion."**

#### **5 Essential Ingredients of Extortion**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Intentionally Putting Person in Fear**

**Requirement**: Accused must **intentionally** create fear in victim's mind

**Fear**: Apprehension of injury or harm

**Types of Injury** (Section 44 BNS):
- Harm illegally caused to person, body, mind, reputation, or property

**Key Point**: Must be intentional act to create fear

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Fear of Injury**

**Fear Can Be Of**:
- Injury to **that person** (victim himself), OR
- Injury to **any other person** (family member, friend, etc.)

**Types of Injury Feared**:
- Physical harm (death, hurt)
- Harm to reputation
- Harm to property
- Mental torture

**Scope**: Fear need not be of instant harm (can be future threat)

---

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Dishonestly Inducing**

**Requirement**: By putting in fear, accused **dishonestly induces** victim

**Dishonestly**: Intention to cause wrongful gain or wrongful loss

**Induces**: Causes victim to act (deliver property)

---

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Delivery of Property/Valuable Security**

**Requirement**: Victim **delivers** property or valuable security

**Delivery**: Voluntary transfer by victim (under threat)

**What Can Be Extorted**:
- Property (movable or immovable)
- Valuable security (documents, bonds, shares)
- Signed/sealed documents that can be converted to valuable security

---

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Consent Obtained by Threat**

**Critical Feature**: Victim gives **consent** but consent is **vitiated** by fear

**Difference from Theft**:
- Theft = No consent
- Extortion = Consent, but obtained by putting in fear

---

#### **Examples of Extortion**

**Example 1**:
- A threatens to publish defamatory matter about B unless B pays money
- B pays money due to fear
- This is **extortion** (fear of reputation injury)

**Example 2**:
- A threatens to kill B's child unless B hands over property
- B gives property
- This is **extortion** (fear of harm to another)

---

### **III. DISTINCTION: Extortion vs Theft**

| **Aspect** | **THEFT** | **EXTORTION** |
|------------|----------|--------------|
| **Section** | Section 303 BNS | Section 308 BNS |
| **Consent** | ‚ùå **Without consent** | ‚úÖ With consent (vitiated by fear) |
| **How Taken** | **Secretly** or **without knowledge** | By **threat/intimidation** |
| **Property** | Only **movable** property | Movable OR **immovable** property |
| **Presence** | Victim may be absent | Victim usually **present** |
| **Fear/Force** | No fear or force used | **Fear** is essential element |
| **Delivery** | Accused **takes** property | Victim **delivers** property |
| **Punishment** | Up to 3 years + fine | Up to 3 years + fine |
| **Example** | Pickpocketing wallet | Threatening person to give wallet |

---

### **IV. DISTINCTION: Robbery vs Dacoity**

| **Aspect** | **ROBBERY** | **DACOITY** |
|------------|------------|-----------|
| **Section** | Section 309 BNS | Section 310 BNS |
| **Persons** | Can be **1 or more** persons | Minimum **5 persons** required |
| **Nature** | Theft/Extortion + Force | Robbery by 5+ persons conjointly |
| **Acting** | May be alone | Must act **conjointly** (together) |
| **Punishment** | Up to 10 years | **Life** or up to 10 years |
| **Seriousness** | Serious | Very serious |
| **Example** | One person forcefully snatches purse | Gang of 6 persons attacks and loots |

---

### **V. KEY CASE LAWS**

**Shri Ram v. State of U.P.** (1975):
- Theft requires taking without consent
- Extortion requires consent obtained by putting in fear
- Held: Clear distinction based on consent element

**Jhandu Lal v. State of Punjab** (1974):
- For robbery, force must be voluntary
- Must be used to commit theft or overcome resistance
- Held: Force is essential distinguishing element

---

### **Conclusion**

The distinction between extortion and theft lies fundamentally in the **presence or absence of consent**. In **theft**, property is taken **without the owner's consent** - stealthily or secretly. In **extortion**, property is obtained **with consent**, but that consent is **vitiated by fear** induced through threats or intimidation. 

Further, **theft** is limited to **movable property** only, while **extortion** can involve both movable and immovable property. When theft or extortion is accompanied by **force or threat of instant harm**, it becomes **robbery**, and when robbery is committed by **five or more persons** acting conjointly, it becomes **dacoity**. 

Understanding these distinctions is crucial as they determine the appropriate charge and punishment, ranging from 3 years for simple theft/extortion to life imprisonment for dacoity, reflecting the escalating threat to person and property.
