### **OUTRAGING THE MODESTY OF A WOMAN**

#### **Introduction**

Outraging the modesty of a woman is a serious offense under **Section 74 of the Bhartiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023** (previously Section 354 IPC), designed to protect the dignity, honor, and modesty of women from unwanted physical contact or gestures. This offense recognizes that women have a fundamental right to bodily integrity and personal dignity, and any act that outrages or offends her sense of decency and modesty is punishable under criminal law.

---

#### **Definition (Section 74 BNS)**

**"Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment which may extend to two years, or with fine, or with both."**

---

### **ESSENTIAL INGREDIENTS**

---

### **1Ô∏è‚É£ ASSAULT OR CRIMINAL FORCE**

**Requirement**: There must be **assault** or use of **criminal force** on woman

**Assault** (Section 128 BNS):
- Making gesture or preparation intending or knowing it likely that such gesture/preparation will cause person to apprehend immediate use of criminal force

**Criminal Force** (Section 132 BNS):
- Intentionally using force to person
- Without that person's consent
- Intending or knowing it likely to cause injury, fear, or annoyance

**Key Point**: Actual physical contact OR apprehension of force

**Examples**:
- Touching woman inappropriately
- Pulling woman's clothing
- Pushing or grabbing woman
- Making gestures suggesting imminent physical contact

---

### **2Ô∏è‚É£ WOMAN AS VICTIM**

**Requirement**: Victim must be a **woman**

**Key Points**:
- Gender-specific offense
- Protects modesty of women specifically
- Age immaterial (applies to women of all ages)

---

### **3Ô∏è‚É£ INTENTION OR KNOWLEDGE**

**Two Alternative Mental States**:

**(a) Intention to Outrage Modesty**:
- Accused **intends** to outrage woman's modesty
- Positive purpose to offend her modesty
- Deliberate act

**(b) Knowledge Likely to Outrage**:
- Accused **knows** act is likely to outrage modesty
- May not intend, but aware of consequence
- Awareness sufficient

**Key Point**: Either intention OR knowledge sufficient

---

### **4Ô∏è‚É£ OUTRAGE OF MODESTY**

**"Modesty"**: 
- Not defined in BNS/IPC
- Judicial interpretation: Woman's sense of **decency, decorum, and propriety**
- What would offend a woman's sense of personal dignity

**"Outrage"**:
- To offend, violate, or insult
- Act that shocks woman's sense of decency

**Test - Objective Standard**:
- Not what particular woman feels
- What a **reasonable woman** in same circumstances would consider as offense to her modesty
- Whether act is capable of offending modesty of woman with reasonable sense of decency

---

### **WHAT CONSTITUTES OUTRAGING MODESTY**

**Judicial Interpretation**:

The act must be such that it is:
- **Capable of shocking** the sense of decency of woman
- Intrudes upon woman's **privacy**
- Involves **unwanted physical contact** or gestures
- Of **sexual nature** or with sexual overtones

**Examples of Outraging Modesty**:

‚úÖ **Physical Acts**:
- Touching private parts of woman
- Hugging or kissing woman without consent
- Pulling woman's clothing
- Groping or inappropriate touching
- Forcibly holding woman's hand

‚úÖ **Gestures/Actions**:
- Making obscene gestures toward woman
- Exposing private parts to woman
- Following woman persistently with sexual intent
- Blocking woman's path with sexual intent

**Important Cases**:

**Rupan Deol Bajaj v. KPS Gill** (1995):
- Senior police officer slapped woman's posterior at party
- Supreme Court held: Act outraged woman's modesty
- Convicted under Section 354 IPC
- **Principle**: Even single act of inappropriate touching can outrage modesty

**State of Punjab v. Major Singh** (1967):
- Accused caught hold of woman's saree
- Tried to pull it
- Held: Clear case of outraging modesty
- Physical contact with sexual overtones

---

#### **Application to Facts**

**Analyzing A's Actions**:

**1. Did A use criminal force?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - A **beats B severely** causing injuries
- Use of force without consent
- Physical violence constitutes criminal force
- First element satisfied

**2. Is B a woman?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - B is A's wife, a woman
- Second element satisfied

**3. Did A have intention or knowledge?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - A's acts of:
  - Constant harassment
  - Insults and threats
  - Mental torture
  - Severe beating
- Show **intention to humiliate** and outrage B's dignity and modesty
- A **knows** such conduct will outrage B's modesty
- Third element satisfied

**4. Was modesty outraged?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - A's conduct:
  - Insults and harassment humiliate B
  - Threats and mental torture affect her dignity
  - Severe beating violates her bodily integrity
  - All this conduct outrages B's sense of decency and modesty
- Fourth element satisfied

**Additional Consideration - Dowry Harassment**:
- A's harassment is specifically for **dowry demand**
- This also attracts **Section 85 BNS (Cruelty)**
- Harassment for dowry is one of two forms of cruelty

---

#### **Conclusion**

**YES, A can be held liable** for:

**1. Cruelty under Section 85 BNS** (primarily applicable):
- A is husband of B
- Subjected B to two forms of cruelty:
  - **(a) Physical and mental cruelty**: Beating, harassment, threats, mental torture likely to cause grave injury
  - **(b) Harassment for dowry**: Demanding additional dowry, harassing to coerce meeting demand
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to **3 years** + fine
- Non-bailable, cognizable offense

**2. Outraging Modesty under Section 74 BNS** (also applicable):
- A used criminal force (beating)
- With knowledge it would outrage B's modesty
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to **2 years** + fine

**Primary Charge**: **Section 85 BNS (Cruelty)** as it specifically covers:
- Husband subjecting wife to cruelty
- Harassment for dowry
- Both physical and mental torture

**Therefore**, A can be prosecuted under Section 85 BNS (primarily) and Section 74 BNS (additionally) and faces imprisonment up to 3 years along with fine.
