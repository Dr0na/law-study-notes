### **SOLUTION TO THE PROBLEM**

#### **Issue**

The issue is whether A can be held liable for **cruelty** under **Section 85 of the Bhartiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023** (previously Section 498A IPC) for harassing his wife B and demanding dowry.

---

#### **Relevant Law**

**Section 85 BNS - Cruelty by Husband or Relatives** provides:

"Whoever, being the **husband or relative of husband** of a woman, subjects such woman to **cruelty** shall be punished with imprisonment which may extend to **three years** and shall also be liable to fine."

#### **Definition of "Cruelty"** (Explanation)

Cruelty means:

**(a) Any willful conduct which is of such nature as is likely to drive the woman to commit suicide or to cause **grave injury or danger** to life, limb or health (whether **mental or physical**) of the woman; OR**

**(b) Harassment of woman where such harassment is with a view to **coercing her or any person related to her to meet any unlawful demand** for any **property or valuable security** or is on account of **failure by her or any person related to her** to meet such demand.**

---

#### **Essential Ingredients of Cruelty**

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Person Accused Must Be**:
- **Husband** of woman, OR
- **Relative of husband**

**"Relative of husband"** includes:
- Parents, siblings, in-laws
- Extended family members

---

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Two Forms of Cruelty**

**FORM A: Physical/Mental Cruelty**
- Willful conduct likely to:
  - Drive woman to suicide, OR
  - Cause grave injury/danger to life, limb, or health (mental or physical)

**FORM B: Harassment for Dowry/Property**
- Harassment of woman
- To coerce her or relatives to meet **unlawful demand**
- For property or valuable security
- OR on account of **failure** to meet such demand

---

#### **Application to Facts**

**Analyzing A's Actions**:

**1. Is A the husband?**
- ‚úÖ **YES** - A is the **husband** of B
- First ingredient satisfied
- A falls within scope of persons who can be prosecuted

**2. Did A subject B to cruelty?**

**Analyzing A's Conduct - Form A (Physical/Mental Cruelty)**:

**Physical Cruelty**:
- ‚úÖ A **beats B severely** causing injuries
- Willful conduct causing physical harm
- Danger to life and limb
- **Form A satisfied**

**Mental Cruelty**:
- ‚úÖ A **constantly harasses** B
- ‚úÖ A **insults** B
- ‚úÖ A **threatens** B
- ‚úÖ Subjects B to **mental torture**
- This conduct is of such nature as likely to:
  - Drive woman to commit suicide, OR
  - Cause grave mental injury
- **Form A satisfied**

**Analyzing A's Conduct - Form B (Harassment for Dowry)**:

**Demand for Property**:
- ‚úÖ A demands **additional dowry** from B's parents
- This is unlawful demand for property/valuable security

**Harassment**:
- ‚úÖ A constantly **harasses** B
- ‚úÖ With view to **coerce** her and her parents to meet demand
- ‚úÖ Harassment is **in connection with dowry demand**

**Form B Elements**:
1. ‚úÖ Harassment present (insults, threats, mental torture)
2. ‚úÖ View to coercing to meet unlawful demand (dowry)
3. ‚úÖ Demand for property (dowry from parents)
4. **Form B satisfied**

**Conclusion on Cruelty**: A's conduct satisfies **BOTH forms** of cruelty.

---

#### **Relevant Case Law**

**S.V. Bharath v. State** (2018):
- Constant harassment and demand for dowry constitutes cruelty
- Both physical and mental harassment covered

**Pinakin Mahipatray Rawal v. State of Gujarat** (2013):
- Cruelty includes both physical violence and mental torture
- Harassment for dowry is serious form of cruelty

---

#### **Conclusion**

**A can be held liable for cruelty** under **Section 85 BNS** because:

**Elements Satisfied**:
1. ‚úÖ A is the **husband** of B (person covered by section)
2. ‚úÖ A subjected B to **physical cruelty**:
   - Beat her severely causing injuries
   - Willful conduct causing danger to life and limb
3. ‚úÖ A subjected B to **mental cruelty**:
   - Constant harassment, insults, threats
   - Mental torture likely to cause grave mental injury
4. ‚úÖ A committed **harassment for dowry**:
   - Demanded additional dowry
   - Harassed B to coerce her/parents to meet demand

**Both forms of cruelty (Form A and Form B) are satisfied.**

**Punishment**:
- Imprisonment up to **3 years** + fine
- Cognizable, non-bailable offense
- Can be arrested without warrant

**Therefore**, A is guilty of **cruelty** under Section 85 BNS and can be prosecuted and punished accordingly. A can face imprisonment up to 3 years along with fine for his actions.
