## ğŸ”„ How Madrid System Works (5 Steps)

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Step 1: Basic Application/Registration**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Must have trademark application or registration in home country (called "basic mark" in "Office of Origin").

**Example:** Indian company must first file trademark in India.
</div>

<br>

**Step 2: International Application**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
File one international application through home country's trademark office - designate countries where protection wanted - pay single set of fees.
</div>

<br>

**Step 3: WIPO Examination & Registration**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
WIPO examines formalities (not substantive) - registers in International Register - publishes in WIPO Gazette.
</div>

<br>

**Step 4: Notification to Designated Countries**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
WIPO notifies each designated country's trademark office.
</div>

<br>

**Step 5: National Examination**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Each country examines per own laws - if no objections â†’ protected - if objections â†’ can refuse (but may still be protected in other countries).

## âœ… 5 Benefits of Madrid System

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Benefit 1: Cost-Effective**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
File one application instead of multiple - pay one set of fees (lower than filing separately in each country).
</div>

<br>

**Benefit 2: Efficient**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Single application process - manage all registrations through one system.
</div>

<br>

**Benefit 3: Simplified Management**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Renewals: Renew all registrations with one renewal - Changes: Record changes (address, ownership) once, effective in all countries.
</div>

<br>

**Benefit 4: Language**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
File in one language (English, French, or Spanish).
</div>

<br>

**Benefit 5: Expansion**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Can easily add more countries later by filing "subsequent designation".
</div>

</div>

## âš ï¸ Limitation: Central Attack

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

If, within 5 years of international registration, the basic application/registration in home country is refused, cancelled, or limited, then international registration automatically becomes invalid (to same extent) in all designated countries.

**However:** Applicant can "transform" international registration into national applications in designated countries, maintaining original filing date.

## ğŸ¯ Conclusion

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

The Madrid System significantly simplifies international trademark registration by allowing applicants to file one application, in one language, with one set of fees to seek protection in multiple countries. India's accession to the Madrid Protocol in 2013 enables Indian businesses to efficiently protect their brands globally and facilitates foreign brands' entry into the Indian market. The system's efficiency in terms of cost, time, and management makes it an attractive option for businesses seeking international trademark protection.
