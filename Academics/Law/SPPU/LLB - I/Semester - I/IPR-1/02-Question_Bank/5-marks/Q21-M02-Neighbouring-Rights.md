## üéØ Introduction

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

Neighbouring rights, also called related rights, are rights that are "neighboring" or related to copyright. They protect the rights of persons and entities who contribute to making works available to the public, even if they're not the original creators of the underlying work.

</div>

## üë• 3 Categories of Neighbouring Rights Holders

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Category 1: Performers (Actors, Singers, Musicians, Dancers)**

**Category 2: Producers of Phonograms (Sound Recordings)**

**Category 3: Broadcasting Organizations (Radio and Television)**

</div>

---

## üé≠ Rights of Performers (Section 38)

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Economic Rights:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**1. Right to Make Recordings:** Authorization required to record live performance

**2. Right to Reproduce:** Control over copying of recordings of performances

**3. Right to Broadcast/Communicate:** Control over broadcasting live or recorded performances

**4. Right to Sell/Rent:** Commercial distribution and rental rights

</div>

**Moral Rights (Section 38B):**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**1. Right of Attribution:** Right to be identified as performer

**2. Right of Integrity:** Object to distortion harming reputation

</div>

**Duration:** 50 years from beginning of calendar year following year of performance

**Example:** Singer performing song owns rights in their specific performance, separate from composer's copyright in musical work.

</div>

---

## üìÄ Rights of Producers of Phonograms (Section 38C)

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**"Producer"** = Person taking initiative and responsibility for first fixation of sounds

**Rights:**
- Right to make copies (reproduce sound recording)
- Right to sell or rent commercially
- Right to communicate to public
- Right to equitable remuneration when broadcast/communicated

**Duration:** 50 years from publication

**Example:** Music production company recording album owns rights in sound recording, separate from rights in underlying musical/literary works.

</div>

---

## üì° Rights of Broadcasting Organizations (Section 37)

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Rights:**
- Right to re-broadcast their broadcasts
- Right to cause broadcast to be heard/seen by public (control public viewing in restaurants, hotels)
- Right to make recordings of broadcasts
- Right to reproduce recordings

**Duration:** 25 years from beginning of calendar year following year of broadcast

</div>

---

## üÜö Distinction from Copyright

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Copyright:**
- Protects original creative work itself
- Belongs to author/creator
- Example: Songwriter owns copyright in musical composition

**Neighbouring Rights:**
- Protect performances and productions of works
- Belong to performers, producers, broadcasters
- Example: Singer owns rights in their specific performance of song

**Both can coexist:** Recorded song has multiple layers - copyright in composition (composer), copyright in lyrics (lyricist), neighbouring rights in performance (singer), neighbouring rights in sound recording (producer).

</div>

---

## üåç International Recognition

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Rome Convention (1961):** First international treaty on neighbouring rights (India not signatory)

**WIPO Performances and Phonograms Treaty (1996):** Enhanced protection for digital age (India party since 2018)

**TRIPS Article 14:** Requires minimum 50 years protection for performers and phonogram producers, 20 years for broadcasts

</div>

---

## üéØ Conclusion

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

Neighbouring rights recognize that performers, producers, and broadcasters invest time, money, and creativity in making works available to the public. They deserve protection for their contributions even though they may not be the original creators. These rights are essential in the modern entertainment industry where multiple parties contribute to bringing creative works to audiences. India's Copyright Act provides comprehensive protection for neighbouring rights, aligning with international standards.

</div>

---

> **üéØ Key Points:**
> - **3 Categories:** Performers, Producers, Broadcasters (PPB)
> - **Duration:** 50Y (performers/producers) | 25Y (broadcasts)
> - **Separate from copyright:** Can coexist with author's copyright
> - **International:** Rome Convention, WPPT, TRIPS Article 14
> - **India:** Sections 37, 38, 38B, 38C of Copyright Act
