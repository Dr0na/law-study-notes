## ğŸ“œ Legal Provision (Sections 33-35)

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

Copyright society is a registered organization under Section 33 of Copyright Act that represents multiple copyright owners and administers their rights collectively.

</div>

---

## ğŸ¯ Purpose and Function

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

### Why Copyright Societies Exist:

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

Individual copyright owners, especially music creators, cannot practically monitor and collect royalties for every use of their work. Copyright societies solve this problem.

**Example:** Restaurant plays music for customers. Instead of obtaining permission from each songwriter individually (impractical), restaurant obtains blanket license from copyright society, which distributes collected fees to members based on usage.

</div>

### 4 Main Functions:

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**1. Collective Administration:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Managing rights for numerous creators collectively.
</div>

<br>

**2. Licensing:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Issuing blanket licenses to users (radio stations, restaurants, streaming services).
</div>

<br>

**3. Collection:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Collecting license fees from users.
</div>

<br>

**4. Distribution:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Distributing royalties to members based on usage.
</div>

**5. Enforcement:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Monitoring and taking action against infringement.
</div>

</div>

</div>

---

## ğŸ“‹ Registration Requirements

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**Who Can Form:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Minimum **7 members** (authors, publishers, other copyright owners).
</div>

**Registration:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Must register with Copyright Office - provide details of objectives, members, operations - submit rules and regulations - pay prescribed fees.
</div>

**Registrar's Approval:**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Registrar examines if society can effectively administer rights - may grant with/without conditions - can refuse if requirements not met.
</div>

</div>

## âš–ï¸ Rights and Responsibilities

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

### Rights:

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

- Issue licenses for public performance, broadcasting, other uses
- Collect license fees and royalties
- Take legal action for infringement on behalf of members
- Act as collective voice for creators

</div>

### Responsibilities:

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

- Maintain proper accounts and records (transparency)
- Distribute collected fees fairly among members
- Submit annual reports to Registrar
- Follow Copyright Act and own rules
- Cannot refuse membership arbitrarily (non-discrimination)

</div>

</div>

---

## ğŸ›ï¸ Supervision by Copyright Board

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

Copyright Board has oversight powers:

- Resolve disputes between societies and users/members
- Review license fees - ensure reasonable
- Investigate complaints
- Can suspend or cancel registration for violations

</div>

## ğŸ‡®ğŸ‡³ Copyright Societies in India

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

**1. IPRS (Indian Performing Right Society):**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Represents composers, lyricists, publishers of musical works - issues licenses for public performance and broadcast of music.
</div>

<br>

**2. PPL (Phonographic Performance Limited):**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Represents producers of sound recordings - licenses broadcasting and public performance of recorded music.
</div>

<br>

**3. IRRO (Indian Reprographic Rights Organisation):**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Represents publishers, authors, visual artists - issues licenses for reprographic reproduction.
</div>

<br>

**4. ISRA (Indian Singers' Rights Association):**

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">
Represents singers - administers performers' rights.
</div>

</div>

## ğŸ¯ Conclusion

<div style="margin-left: 20px;">

Copyright societies provide essential collective management infrastructure for copyright administration, particularly for musical works and performances. By representing multiple rights holders and issuing blanket licenses, they make it practical for users to legally access copyrighted works while ensuring creators receive fair compensation. The Copyright Board's oversight ensures societies operate transparently and serve members' interests. For industries like music where works are widely used, copyright societies are indispensable for effective rights management.

</div>
