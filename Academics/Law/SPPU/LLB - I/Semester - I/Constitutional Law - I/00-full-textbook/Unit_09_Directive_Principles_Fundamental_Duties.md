## ğŸ“œ **SECTION 1: DIRECTIVE PRINCIPLES OF STATE POLICY (ARTICLES 36-51)**

### ğŸŒŸ **Introduction**

**Part IV** of Constitution contains Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs)

**Borrowed from:** Irish Constitution

**Articles:** 36 to 51

---

### ğŸ”‘ **NATURE OF DIRECTIVE PRINCIPLES**

#### Article 37 - Application of DPs

> **"The provisions contained in this Part shall not be enforceable by any court, but the principles therein laid down are nevertheless fundamental in the governance of the country and it shall be the duty of the State to apply these principles in making laws."**

---

### Key Characteristics

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Non-justiciable** | Cannot be enforced in court |
| **Fundamental in governance** | State duty to apply |
| **Guidelines** | For State policy |
| **Positive obligations** | State to do something |
| **Socio-economic rights** | Welfare state objectives |

---

### Why Non-Justiciable?

**Reasons:**

1. **Resource Constraints**
   - Require money to implement
   - State may not have resources

2. **Policy Matters**
   - Involve policy decisions
   - Courts cannot make policy

3. **Progressive Implementation**
   - To be achieved gradually
   - Cannot be immediate

4. **Flexibility**
   - State decides how and when
   - Based on circumstances

---

### ğŸ›ï¸ **Case: State of Madras v. Champakam Dorairajan, AIR 1951**

**Issue:** Can DPs override FRs?

**Held:**
- **No, Directive Principles cannot override Fundamental Rights**
- FRs are justiciable
- DPs are non-justiciable
- In case of conflict, FRs prevail

---

### Article 36 - Definition of "State"

> **"State" has the same meaning as in Part III (Article 12)**

**Includes:**
- Government and Parliament of India
- State Governments and Legislatures
- Local authorities
- Other authorities

## 1ï¸âƒ£ **SOCIALISTIC PRINCIPLES**

**Aim:** Social and economic justice

### Article 38 - Welfare State

> **State to promote welfare** by securing social order based on **justice** - social, economic, political

**Minimize inequalities:**
- In status
- In facilities
- In opportunities

---

### Article 39 - Certain Principles of Policy

**Most important DP**

**Six clauses:**

#### Article 39(a)

> Adequate means of livelihood for men and women

---

#### Article 39(b)

> Ownership and control of material resources of community to **subserve common good**

**Prevents:** Concentration of wealth

---

#### Article 39(c)

> Economic system does not result in **concentration of wealth and means of production**

---

#### Article 39(d)

> **Equal pay for equal work** for men and women

---

#### Article 39(e)

> Health and strength of workers (men, women, children) **not abused**

> Workers not forced by economic necessity to enter vocations unsuited to age/strength

---

#### Article 39(f)

> Children given opportunities to develop in **healthy manner** and in conditions of **freedom and dignity**

> Childhood and youth protected against exploitation and moral/material abandonment

---

### Article 39A - Free Legal Aid

**Added by:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

> State to ensure operation of legal system promotes **justice** on basis of **equal opportunity**

> Provide **free legal aid** to ensure no citizen denied justice due to economic or other disabilities

---

### Article 40 - Village Panchayats

> State to organize **village panchayats**

> Endow them with powers and authority for self-government

---

### Article 41 - Right to Work, Education

> State to secure, within limits of economic capacity:
> - Right to work
> - Right to education
> - Public assistance in cases of unemployment, old age, sickness, disablement

---

### Article 42 - Just and Humane Conditions of Work

> State to make provisions for:
> - Just and humane conditions of work
> - Maternity relief

---

### Article 43 - Living Wage

> State to secure:
> - Living wage
> - Decent standard of life
> - Full enjoyment of leisure and social/cultural opportunities

> Promote cottage industries on individual/co-operative basis in rural areas

---

### Article 43A - Workers' Participation in Management

**Added by:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

> State to take steps to secure participation of workers in management of undertakings

---

### Article 43B - Promotion of Co-operative Societies

**Added by:** 97th Amendment, 2011

> State to promote voluntary formation, autonomous functioning, democratic control and professional management of co-operative societies

---

### Article 47 - Public Health

> State to raise:
> - Level of nutrition
> - Standard of living
> - To improve public health

> Prohibit intoxicating drinks and drugs injurious to health (except for medicinal purposes)

## 3ï¸âƒ£ **LIBERAL-INTELLECTUAL PRINCIPLES**

**Aim:** Strengthen democratic institutions

### Article 44 - Uniform Civil Code

> State to secure for citizens a **Uniform Civil Code** throughout territory of India

**Most controversial DP**

**Current situation:**
- Different personal laws for different religions
- Debate on implementation

---

### Article 45 - Early Childhood Care and Education

**Originally:** Free and compulsory education for children up to 14 years

**After 86th Amendment, 2002:**
> State to provide early childhood care and education for all children **until they complete age of six years**

**Note:** Education 6-14 years now FR under Art. 21A

---

### Article 49 - Protection of Monuments

> State to protect monuments and places of **national importance** against:
> - Spoliation
> - Disfigurement
> - Destruction
> - Removal
> - Disposal
> - Export

---

### Article 50 - Separation of Judiciary from Executive

> State to separate **judiciary from executive** in public services of State

**Implemented** in most States

---

### Article 51 - Promotion of International Peace

> State to promote:
> - International peace and security
> - Just and honourable relations between nations
> - Respect for international law and treaty obligations
> - Settlement of international disputes by arbitration

| **Implementation** | Immediate | Progressive |

---

### Relationship Between FRs and DPs

#### Early View: Conflict

**Case:** Champakam Dorairajan (1951)
- FRs prevail over DPs
- DPs cannot override FRs

---

#### Harmonious Construction

**Need:** Balance between individual rights and social welfare

---

### ğŸ›ï¸ **Case: Minerva Mills v. Union of India, AIR 1980**

**Issue:** Relationship between Parts III and IV?

**Held:**
- FRs and DPs are **complementary**, not contradictory
- Constitution founded on **bedrock of balance**
- Harmony and balance between FRs and DPs is **basic structure**
- Neither can claim supremacy

**Justice Chandrachud:**
> *"Indian Constitution is founded on the bedrock of the balance between Parts III and IV."*

---

### Constitutional Amendments Favoring DPs

#### Article 31C

**Added by:** 25th Amendment, 1971
**Amended by:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

**Current position:**

> Laws for giving effect to **Arts. 39(b) and (c)** cannot be challenged on ground of violating Arts. 14, 19 (and Art. 31 - now deleted)

**Originally:** Applied to all DPs - struck down in Minerva Mills

**Effect:** Gives primacy to Arts. 39(b) and (c) over Arts. 14 and 19

---

### ğŸ›ï¸ **Case: Unnikrishnan v. State of AP, AIR 1993**

**Held:**
- FRs must be read with DPs
- Together provide comprehensive social order
- Right to education derived from reading Art. 21 with Art. 45

**Later:** Art. 21A added making education FR

## ğŸ“œ **SECTION 2: FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES (ARTICLE 51A)**

### ğŸŒŸ **Introduction**

**Added by:** 42nd Constitutional Amendment, 1976

**On recommendation of:** Swaran Singh Committee

**Borrowed from:** USSR Constitution (erstwhile)

**Article:** 51A

---

### Why Added?

**Reasons:**

1. **Rights without duties** incomplete
2. Inspired by **Emergency period** (1975-77)
3. Promote **patriotism** and **national integration**
4. Citizens should contribute to nation-building
5. Balance rights with responsibilities

---

### Nature of Fundamental Duties

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Non-justiciable** | Like DPs, cannot be enforced in court |
| **Moral obligations** | Ethical responsibilities |
| **Not absolute** | Cannot override FRs |
| **Reminder** | Of civic duties |
| **Educational** | Instill values |

---

### Article 51A - Text and Explanation

**Preamble:**
> **"It shall be the duty of every citizen of Indiaâ€”"**

---

#### 51A(a) - Constitution, National Symbols

> To abide by the Constitution and respect its ideals and institutions, the **National Flag** and the **National Anthem**;

**Includes:**
- Follow Constitution
- Respect National Flag
- Stand for National Anthem

---

#### 51A(b) - Freedom Struggle

> To cherish and follow the noble ideals which inspired our **national struggle for freedom**;

**Remember:**
- Sacrifice of freedom fighters
- Ideals of independence movement
- Values they fought for

---

#### 51A(c) - Sovereignty, Unity, Integrity

> To uphold and protect the **sovereignty, unity and integrity** of India;

**Duty to:**
- Defend sovereignty
- Maintain unity
- Preserve integrity

---

#### 51A(d) - Defend Country

> To **defend the country** and render **national service** when called upon to do so;

**Includes:**
- Military service if required
- Civil defence
- National service

---

#### 51A(e) - Composite Culture

> To promote **harmony** and the spirit of **common brotherhood** amongst all the people of India transcending religious, linguistic and regional or sectional diversities; to renounce practices **derogatory to the dignity of women**;

**Two parts:**
1. Promote harmony and brotherhood
2. Renounce practices derogatory to women

---

#### 51A(f) - Composite Culture

> To value and preserve the **rich heritage** of our **composite culture**;

**Includes:**
- Art, music, dance
- Literature, architecture
- Traditions, customs
- Diversity

---

#### 51A(g) - Environment

> To protect and improve the **natural environment** including forests, lakes, rivers and wildlife, and to have **compassion for living creatures**;

**Environmental duties:**
- Protect nature
- Conserve wildlife
- Compassion to animals

---

#### 51A(h) - Scientific Temper

> To develop the **scientific temper, humanism** and the spirit of **inquiry and reform**;

**Promote:**
- Rational thinking
- Question superstitions
- Spirit of inquiry
- Reform mindset

---

#### 51A(i) - Public Property

> To safeguard **public property** and to abjure **violence**;

**Two duties:**
1. Protect public property
2. Renounce violence

---

#### 51A(j) - Excellence

> To strive towards **excellence** in all spheres of individual and collective activity so that the nation constantly rises to higher levels of endeavour and achievement;

**Duty to excel** in:
- Personal life
- Professional life
- Collective activities

---

#### 51A(k) - Education to Child

**Added by:** 86th Amendment, 2002

> A parent or guardian to provide opportunities for **education** to his child or ward between the age of **six and fourteen years**.

**Corresponds to:** Art. 21A (Right to Education)

**Parent's duty** to educate child (6-14 years)

## âš–ï¸ **ENFORCEMENT OF FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES**

### Direct Enforcement

**Cannot be directly enforced** in court (non-justiciable)

---

### Indirect Enforcement

**Can be enforced indirectly through:**

1. **Legislation**
   - Parliament can make laws
   - To enforce duties
   - Example: Prevention of Insults to National Honour Act, 1971

2. **Judicial Interpretation**
   - Courts consider duties while interpreting laws
   - Used as aid to interpretation

3. **Education**
   - Include in curriculum
   - Awareness campaigns

---

### ğŸ›ï¸ **Case: M.C. Mehta v. Union of India**

**Environmental cases**

**Court relied on:**
- Art. 51A(g) (protect environment)
- Art. 48A (DP on environment)
- Art. 21 (right to clean environment)

**Directions issued** for environmental protection

### ğŸ›ï¸ **Case: Bijoe Emmanuel v. State of Kerala, AIR 1987**

**Facts:** Jehovah's Witness children refused to sing National Anthem (religious belief)

**Issue:** Violation of Art. 51A(a)?

**Held:**
- Expulsion invalid
- Stood respectfully during anthem
- Respect shown, though didn't sing
- Fundamental duties cannot override FRs
- Religious freedom protected

**Principle:**
- Art. 51A(j) - duty to strive for excellence
- Supports merit-based selections
- Cannot be used to deny reservations (as that's constitutional mandate)

## ğŸ“Š **CRITICISMS AND DEFENSES**

### Criticisms of DPs

**Critics argue:**
1. Pious declarations without teeth
2. Not enforceable
3. Depend on State's will
4. Often ignored in practice

**Defense:**
1. Progressive realization possible
2. Inspire legislation
3. Moral pressure on government
4. Many implemented through laws

---

### Criticisms of FDs

**Critics argue:**
1. One-sided (only duties, no duties of State to citizens)
2. Non-enforceable
3. Vague language
4. Can be misused to curtail rights

**Defense:**
1. Remind citizens of responsibilities
2. Complete rights-duties balance
3. Build national character
4. Cannot override FRs
