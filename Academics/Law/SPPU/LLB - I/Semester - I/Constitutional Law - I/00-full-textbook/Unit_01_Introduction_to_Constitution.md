## üèõÔ∏è **SECTION 1: CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY AND FRAMING OF THE CONSTITUTION**

### üåü **Historical Background**

**India's struggle for independence** led to the demand for self-governance and a Constitution that would reflect the aspirations of the Indian people.

---

### üìã **1. FORMATION OF THE CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY**

#### Cabinet Mission Plan (1946)

**Cabinet Mission** came to India in March 1946 to discuss the transfer of power from British to Indian leadership.

**Key Provisions:**
- Constituent Assembly to frame Constitution
- Members elected by Provincial Legislative Assemblies
- Indirect election process

---

#### Composition

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Total Members** | 389 |
| **From British India** | 296 |
| **From Princely States** | 93 |
| **After Partition** | 299 (Pakistan members left) |

**First Meeting:** December 9, 1946

**Temporary President:** Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha (eldest member)

**Permanent President:** Dr. Rajendra Prasad (elected December 11, 1946)

---

### üéØ **2. THE ISSUES BEFORE THE CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY**

#### Major Questions to be Decided

```
Constitutional Framework
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 1. Form of Government (Federal/Unitary)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 2. Nature of Federation (if federal)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 3. Fundamental Rights (which to include)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 4. Minorities Protection (how to ensure)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 5. Language Policy
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 6. Center-State Relations
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 7. Judiciary Structure
    ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ 8. Emergency Provisions
```

---

#### Key Debates

**Federal vs Unitary:**
- Should India be truly federal or strong center?
- Result: **Quasi-federal** - federal in structure, unitary in spirit

**Separate Electorates:**
- Demand from minorities for separate electorates
- Result: **Joint electorates** with reservations

**Language Issue:**
- Hindi vs English vs Regional languages
- Result: **Hindi as official language**, English for 15 years (continues)

**Property Rights:**
- Individual rights vs socialistic goals
- Result: Included as FR (later deleted in 1978)

### üéØ **4. DR. AMBEDKAR'S WARNING AND ANXIETY**

#### Final Speech (November 25, 1949)

**Dr. Ambedkar's three warnings:**

---

#### Warning 1: Democracy Not Just Political

> "Political democracy cannot last unless there lies at the base of it social democracy."

**Meaning:**
- Formal equality not enough
- Social and economic equality essential
- Without social democracy, political democracy will fail

---

#### Warning 2: Contradictions in Life

> "On the 26th of January 1950, we are going to enter into a life of contradictions."

**Contradictions identified:**
- **In politics:** One person, one vote, one value
- **In social and economic life:** Denial of principle of one person, one value
- Deep inequalities continue

**Warning:** These contradictions must be resolved quickly, or those suffering will **"blow up the structure of political democracy"**.

---

#### Warning 3: Grammar of Anarchy

> "However good a Constitution may be, if those who are implementing it are not good, it will prove to be bad. However bad a Constitution may be, if those implementing it are good, it will prove to be good."

**Emphasized:**
- Constitution is not everything
- **People must avoid:**
  - Hero worship
  - Bhakti (blind devotion) in politics
- **People should use:**
  - Constitutional methods
  - Not grammar of anarchy

## üìñ **SECTION 2: CONCEPTS OF CONSTITUTIONAL LAW AND CONSTITUTIONALISM**

### üîë **What is a Constitution?**

**Definition:** 
> A Constitution is the **fundamental law** of a country that establishes:
> - Structure of government
> - Powers of government organs
> - Rights of citizens
> - Relationship between State and citizens

---

### üìö **Classification of Constitutions**

#### 1. Written vs Unwritten

| Written Constitution | Unwritten Constitution |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Codified in single document | Not in single document |
| Example: India, USA | Example: UK |

**Note:** UK has constitutional documents but not codified.

---

#### 2. Rigid vs Flexible

| Rigid Constitution | Flexible Constitution |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Special procedure for amendment | Same procedure as ordinary law |
| Example: USA | Example: UK |
| **India:** Partly rigid, partly flexible | |

---

#### 3. Federal vs Unitary

| Federal | Unitary |
|---------|---------|
| Division of powers between Centre and States | All powers with Centre |
| Two governments | One government |
| Written Constitution | May be written or unwritten |
| Example: USA, India | Example: UK, France |

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è **CONSTITUTIONALISM**

#### Definition

> **Constitutionalism** = Limited government under law

**Core Principles:**

```
Constitutionalism
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 1. Rule of Law (not rule by law)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 2. Limited Government (constitutional restraints)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 3. Separation of Powers
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 4. Independent Judiciary
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 5. Fundamental Rights Protection
    ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ 6. Democratic Accountability
```

---

#### Difference: Constitution vs Constitutionalism

| Constitution | Constitutionalism |
|--------------|------------------|
| Document | Philosophy/Approach |
| Creates structure | Limits power |
| Exists in all countries | Not in all countries |
| Example: USSR had Constitution | Example: USSR lacked constitutionalism |

**Key Point:**
> Having a Constitution ‚â† Having Constitutionalism

---

#### Rule of Law

**Three meanings (A.V. Dicey):**

1. **Supremacy of law** over arbitrary power
2. **Equality before law** - no one above law
3. **Rights determined by courts**, not abstract declarations

**In India:**
- Arts. 14, 21 embody Rule of Law
- Judicial review ensures Rule of Law

---

#### Separation of Powers

**Three organs of government:**

```
Government
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ Legislature (makes law)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ Executive (implements law)
    ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Judiciary (interprets law)
```

**In India:**
- Not strict separation (parliamentary system)
- Checks and balances exist
- Judicial review by Judiciary
- Parliamentary control over Executive

## üåü **SECTION 3: SALIENT FEATURES OF THE CONSTITUTION**

### üìã **Overview**

**The Indian Constitution is:**
- Longest written Constitution in world
- Originally: 395 Articles, 8 Schedules
- Currently: 470+ Articles, 12 Schedules
- Most amended Constitution (105+ amendments)

---

### üîë **KEY FEATURES**

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **Lengthiest Written Constitution**

**Why so long?**
- Diverse country (religions, languages, cultures)
- Single Constitution for entire country
- Detailed provisions needed
- Includes both justiciable and non-justiciable rights
- Administrative provisions included

**Comparison:**
- USA Constitution: 7 Articles
- India: 470+ Articles

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **Parliamentary System of Government**

**Adopted from:** Britain

**Features:**
- Nominal Head (President) vs Real Head (Prime Minister)
- PM and Council responsible to Lok Sabha
- Collective responsibility
- Dissolution of Lok Sabha possible

**Why chosen over Presidential?**
- Familiarity (worked under British rule)
- Greater accountability
- Flexibility

---

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ **Quasi-Federal Structure**

**India described as:** "Federation with strong unitary bias"

#### Federal Features:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Written Constitution** | ‚úÖ Yes |
| **Division of Powers** | ‚úÖ Union List, State List, Concurrent List |
| **Independent Judiciary** | ‚úÖ Yes |
| **Bicameralism** | ‚úÖ Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha |
| **Rigid Constitution** | ‚úÖ Art. 368 |

#### Unitary Features:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Single Constitution** | For Centre and States |
| **Single Citizenship** | No state citizenship |
| **Flexible Constitution** | Many provisions amended by simple majority |
| **Strong Centre** | Residuary powers, emergency provisions |
| **Appointment of Governors** | By Centre |
| **All-India Services** | IAS, IPS, IFS |
| **Integrated Judiciary** | Single judicial system |

---

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ **Fundamental Rights**

**Part III (Articles 12-35)**

**Borrowed from:** USA Constitution

**Six Categories:**
1. Right to Equality (14-18)
2. Right to Freedom (19-22)
3. Right against Exploitation (23-24)
4. Right to Freedom of Religion (25-28)
5. Cultural and Educational Rights (29-30)
6. Right to Constitutional Remedies (32)

**Features:**
- Justiciable (enforceable in court)
- Not absolute (reasonable restrictions)
- Can be suspended during emergency
- Basic Structure doctrine protects

---

### 5Ô∏è‚É£ **Directive Principles of State Policy**

**Part IV (Articles 36-51)**

**Borrowed from:** Irish Constitution

**Nature:**
- Non-justiciable (not enforceable in court)
- Guidelines for State policy
- Socio-economic rights

**Classification:**
1. Socialistic Principles
2. Gandhian Principles
3. Liberal-Intellectual Principles

**Relationship with FRs:**
- Complementary, not contradictory
- Together provide comprehensive framework

---

### 6Ô∏è‚É£ **Fundamental Duties**

**Article 51-A**

**Added by:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

**Originally:** 10 duties
**Currently:** 11 duties (11th added 2002)

**Nature:** Non-justiciable (like DPs)

---

### 7Ô∏è‚É£ **Secular State**

**Added explicitly:** 42nd Amendment, 1976 (to Preamble)

**Meaning in Indian context:**
- No state religion
- Equal respect to all religions
- Freedom of religion (Arts. 25-28)
- State treats all religions equally

**Different from Western secularism:**
- Not separation of religion and state
- Principled distance approach

---

### 8Ô∏è‚É£ **Independent Judiciary**

**Features:**
- Single integrated judicial system
- Appointment by President (with consultation)
- Fixed tenure
- Salaries not subject to vote
- Cannot practice after retirement (SC judges)
- Difficult removal process (impeachment)
- Contempt powers

**Powers:**
- Judicial review (most important)
- Writ jurisdiction (Art. 32, 226)
- Advisory opinion (Art. 143)

---

### 9Ô∏è‚É£ **Judicial Review**

**Borrowed from:** USA

**Meaning:** Power of courts to examine validity of:
- Legislative enactments
- Executive actions

**Can strike down if:**
- Violates Constitution
- Beyond legislative competence

**Forms:**
1. Review of Constitutional amendments
2. Review of laws (Central and State)
3. Review of administrative actions

---

### üîü **Single Citizenship**

**Unlike USA** (dual citizenship), **India has single citizenship**

**Reasons:**
- Promote national unity
- Prevent discrimination
- India as one nation

---

### 1Ô∏è‚É£1Ô∏è‚É£ **Universal Adult Franchise**

**Article 326**

**All citizens 18+ years** can vote

**Originally:** 21 years (reduced to 18 by 61st Amendment, 1989)

**One person, one vote, one value**

---

### 1Ô∏è‚É£2Ô∏è‚É£ **Emergency Provisions**

**Three types:**

| Article | Type | Grounds |
|---------|------|---------|
| **352** | National Emergency | War, external aggression, armed rebellion |
| **356** | President's Rule | Failure of constitutional machinery in State |
| **360** | Financial Emergency | Threat to financial stability |

**Criticized as:** "Centralizing and unitary"

---

### 1Ô∏è‚É£3Ô∏è‚É£ **Special Provisions for Certain States**

**Articles 370-371** (now only 371 onwards after 370 repeal)

**Special provisions for:**
- Jammu & Kashmir (Art. 370) - **Repealed 2019**
- Maharashtra and Gujarat (Art. 371)
- Nagaland (Art. 371A)
- Assam (Art. 371B)
- And others

**Reason:** Unique historical/cultural circumstances

---

### 1Ô∏è‚É£4Ô∏è‚É£ **Three-Tier Government**

**Unique feature after 73rd & 74th Amendments (1992):**

```
Government Structure
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 1. Union Government (Centre)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 2. State Governments
    ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ 3. Local Governments
        ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ Panchayati Raj (Rural)
        ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Municipalities (Urban)
```

**Constitutional status** to local bodies

---

### 1Ô∏è‚É£5Ô∏è‚É£ **Co-operative Societies**

**97th Amendment, 2011:** Added Part IX-B

**Article 43-B:** State to promote co-operative societies

**Article 19(1)(c):** Right to form co-operatives

## üéØ **SECTION 4: PREAMBLE TO THE CONSTITUTION**

### üìú **TEXT OF THE PREAMBLE**

> **"WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC and to secure to all its citizens:**
>
> **JUSTICE, social, economic and political;**
>
> **LIBERTY of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship;**
>
> **EQUALITY of status and of opportunity; and to promote among them all**
>
> **FRATERNITY assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation;**
>
> **IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949, do HEREBY ADOPT, ENACT AND GIVE TO OURSELVES THIS CONSTITUTION."**

---

### üîë **KEY WORDS IN PREAMBLE**

#### **1. WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA**

**Meaning:**
- Constitution derives authority from people
- Popular sovereignty
- Not granted by external authority

---

#### **2. SOVEREIGN**

**Meaning:**
- India is free from external control
- Supreme authority within territory
- Not subordinate to any foreign power

**Since 1950:** Complete sovereignty (earlier Dominion)

---

#### **3. SOCIALIST**

**Added by:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

**Meaning:**
- Democratic socialism (not communist socialism)
- Mixed economy
- Welfare state
- Reduce inequalities

**Not rigid socialism:** Private property allowed, but with social obligations

---

#### **4. SECULAR**

**Added by:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

**Meaning:**
- No state religion
- Equal respect to all religions
- Freedom of religion guaranteed
- State treats all religions equally

**Indian secularism:**
- Positive engagement (not strict separation)
- Principled distance approach
- Reform of all religions allowed

---

#### **5. DEMOCRATIC**

**Meaning:**
- Government by the people
- Representative democracy
- Universal adult franchise
- Periodic elections
- Rule of law

**Forms of democracy:**
- Political democracy
- Social democracy
- Economic democracy

---

#### **6. REPUBLIC**

**Meaning:**
- Head of State elected
- Not hereditary monarch
- President elected for fixed term

**Difference from Monarchy:**
- UK: Queen is hereditary head
- India: President is elected head

---

### üéØ **OBJECTIVES OF PREAMBLE**

#### **JUSTICE - Social, Economic, Political**

**Social Justice:**
- Absence of discrimination
- Equal opportunity
- Special protection for weaker sections

**Economic Justice:**
- No exploitation
- Equal pay for equal work
- Distribution of wealth

**Political Justice:**
- Equal political rights
- One person one vote
- Right to hold office

---

#### **LIBERTY - Thought, Expression, Belief, Faith, Worship**

**Essential for:**
- Human development
- Democratic functioning
- Individual dignity

**Guaranteed through:**
- Part III (Fundamental Rights)
- Especially Articles 19, 21, 25

---

#### **EQUALITY - Status and Opportunity**

**Equality of Status:**
- No discrimination
- Abolition of untouchability
- Abolition of titles

**Equality of Opportunity:**
- Especially in public employment
- Equal access to education

**Guaranteed through:**
- Articles 14-18

---

#### **FRATERNITY - Dignity and Unity**

**Fraternity means:** Brotherhood, fellow-feeling

**Two aspects:**

1. **Dignity of Individual**
   - Each person has inherent worth
   - Human dignity to be preserved

2. **Unity and Integrity of Nation**
   - "Unity" - added originally
   - "Integrity" - added by 42nd Amendment, 1976
   - After threats to territorial integrity

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è **LEGAL STATUS OF PREAMBLE**

#### **Is Preamble Part of Constitution?**

**Debate settled in two landmark cases:**

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case 1: Berubari Union Case, 1960**

**Held:** Preamble is **NOT part** of Constitution.

**Reasoning:**
- Not enacted by Constituent Assembly under Art. 394
- Introductory statement only
- Not source of powers or prohibitions

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case 2: Kesavananda Bharati Case, 1973**

**Held:** Preamble **IS part** of Constitution.

**Reasoning:**
- Integral part
- Preamble + Constitution = one document
- Overruled Berubari view

**But:** Preamble not enforceable in court (not source of rights/powers)

---

### üéØ **ROLE OF PREAMBLE**

#### 1. Key to Open the Mind of Makers

Shows intentions and aspirations of framers.

---

#### 2. Aid to Interpretation

**When Constitution ambiguous:**
- Preamble helps interpretation
- Interpretation in harmony with Preamble

---

#### 3. Basic Structure

**Kesavananda Bharati:**
- Ideals in Preamble are **basic structure**
- Cannot be destroyed by amendments

**Example:**
- Secular character cannot be amended away
- Democratic form cannot be changed to dictatorship

---

### üîÑ **CAN PREAMBLE BE AMENDED?**

**Yes, Preamble can be amended.**

**Case:** 42nd Amendment added "Socialist" and "Secular"

**But:** Amendment cannot destroy basic structure.
