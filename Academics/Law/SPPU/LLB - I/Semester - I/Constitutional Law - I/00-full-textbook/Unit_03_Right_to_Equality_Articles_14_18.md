## üåü **INTRODUCTION TO RIGHT TO EQUALITY**

### Constitutional Significance

> ‚öñÔ∏è **"Equality is one of the magnificent cornerstones of Indian democracy."**

**Articles 14-18** guarantee the Right to Equality, which is:
- A **basic feature** of the Constitution
- Necessary corollary of **Rule of Law**
- Pervades the entire Indian Constitution

---

### The Equality Guarantee Framework

```
Right to Equality (Arts. 14-18)
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ Art. 14: General equality clause
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ Art. 15: Specific grounds of discrimination prohibited
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ Art. 16: Equality in public employment
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ Art. 17: Abolition of untouchability
    ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Art. 18: Abolition of titles
```

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Right to Equality as Basic Feature**

#### Case: Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, AIR 1973

**Held:**
- Right to Equality is a **basic feature** of Constitution
- Constitution wedded to concept of equality
- Preamble emphasizes equality as basic principle

#### Implication

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Even constitutional amendment** offending right to equality will be **invalid**

**Neither Parliament nor State Legislature can transgress this principle.**

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è **ARTICLE 14: EQUALITY BEFORE LAW**

### üìú Text of Article 14

> **"The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India."**

### Constitutional Position

> üìå **"Articles 21 and 14 are the heart of the chapter on Fundamental Rights."**

---

### üîç **TWO CONCEPTS IN ARTICLE 14**

```
Article 14
    ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ 1. Equality Before Law (Negative)
    ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ 2. Equal Protection of Laws (Positive)
```

---

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **EQUALITY BEFORE LAW**

#### Nature: **NEGATIVE CONCEPT**

**Ensures:**
- ‚úÖ No special privilege for anyone
- ‚úÖ All equally subject to ordinary law
- ‚úÖ No person above the law

**Source:** Second corollary of **DICEAN Rule of Law** (Britain)

---

#### Exceptions to Equality Before Law

| Exception | Provision | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Foreign Diplomats** | International Law | Diplomatic immunity |
| **President** | Art. 361 | Office needs protection |
| **State Governors** | Art. 361 | Office needs protection |
| **Judges** | Various laws | Judicial independence |
| **Public Officers** | Various laws | Official duties protection |
| **Trade Unions** | Labour laws | Collective bargaining |

> üìå These exceptions are **reasonable** and serve **public interest**.

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **EQUAL PROTECTION OF LAWS**

#### Nature: **POSITIVE CONCEPT**

**Does NOT mean:**
- ‚ùå Identical law for all persons
- ‚ùå Universal application irrespective of circumstances

**MEANS:**
> ‚úÖ **"Equality of treatment in equal circumstances"**

**Principle:**
- Like should be treated alike
- Without distinction of race, religion, wealth, social status

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Judicial Interpretation**

#### Case: Sri Srinivasa Theatre v. Govt. of Tamil Nadu

**Held:**
The two expressions have **different meanings** even with much in common.

#### Two Facets of "Equality Before Law"

| Facet | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **First** | No privileged person/class; none above law |
| **Second** | State obligation to bring about more equal society through law |

#### Conclusion

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Article 14 means: "Equals should be treated alike"**

Legislature entitled to make **reasonable classification** for legislation.

---

## üìä **REASONABLE CLASSIFICATION**

### Fundamental Principle

> üîë **Art. 14 forbids CLASS LEGISLATION; does NOT forbid REASONABLE CLASSIFICATION**

---

### ‚úÖ **TWO-FOLD TEST FOR VALID CLASSIFICATION**

#### Test 1: Intelligible Differentia

**Classification must be based on:**
- ‚úÖ Real and substantial distinction
- ‚úÖ Intelligible differentia
- ‚úÖ Distinguishes persons/things in class from those outside

**Should NOT be:**
- ‚ùå Arbitrary
- ‚ùå Artificial
- ‚ùå Evasive

#### Test 2: Rational Nexus

**The differentia must have:**
- ‚úÖ Rational/reasonable nexus
- ‚úÖ With object sought to be achieved by statute

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è **Who Decides Reasonableness?**

> **Courts decide** whether classification is reasonable.

---

## üéØ **BURDEN OF PROOF**

### General Rule

**Person challenging law has burden to prove:**
- Initial presumption: Law is **valid**
- Challenger must show **unequal treatment**
- Must place sufficient material before court

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: G.K. Krishnan v. State of Tamil Nadu, AIR 1975

**Held:** Initial presumption favors validity of law.

---

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: A.V. Nachane v. Union of India, AIR 1982

**Facts:** LIC employees exempted from IDA provisions

**Held:** Burden on petitioners to establish **hostile discrimination**.

---

### Exception to General Rule

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: Dalmia v. Tendolkar, AIR 1958

**Principle:**
> ‚ö†Ô∏è If discrimination **writ large on face** of legislation, **onus shifts to State** to sustain validity.

---

## üìã **VALID CLASSIFICATIONS - CASE LAW**

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **GEOGRAPHICAL CLASSIFICATION**

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: State of Bombay v. Balsara, AIR 1951

**Facts:** Prohibition law differentiated Indians and short-staying foreigners

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid** - Reasonable basis for classification

---

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: Joshi v. Madhya Bharat, AIR 1955

**Facts:** Capitation fee for non-residents for medical college admission

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid** - Residence-based classification reasonable

---

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: Kishan Singh v. State of Rajasthan, AIR 1955

**Facts:** Rajasthan Act for Marwar region only (rent control)

**Challenge:** Act doesn't apply to whole State

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid**
- Act not bad merely because limited to region
- Invalid only if similar conditions exist elsewhere

> üìå **Principle:** Art. 14 prohibits unequal treatment of **similarly situated** persons.

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **PROCEDURAL DIFFERENTIATION**

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: State of UP v. Deoman Upadhyaya, AIR 1960

**Facts:** S. 27 Evidence Act - statement to police officer admissible if leads to discovery

**Challenge:** Distinction between persons in/not in custody

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid** - Classification not arbitrary in context of admissibility

---

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ **SINGLE ENTITY CLASSIFICATION**

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: Charanjit Lal v. Union of India, AIR 1951

**Facts:**
- Large cotton mill closed due to mismanagement
- Parliament enacted Act for this **single company**
- Government to appoint directors

**Challenge:** Act singles out one company - discriminatory

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid** (by majority)

---

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ **UNIQUE ENTITY CLASSIFICATION**

| Entity | Principle | Valid? |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Shri Jagannath Temple** | Unique position among Hindu temples | ‚úÖ Yes |
| **Each University** | Each university is a class by itself | ‚úÖ Yes |
| **Prime Minister** | P.M. is a class by himself (duties/status) | ‚úÖ Yes |

**Example:** P.M. allowed IAF aircraft for election; other leaders not allowed.

**Held:** ‚úÖ Not discriminatory - P.M.'s status & duties make him unique class.

---

### 5Ô∏è‚É£ **DISASTER-SPECIFIC LEGISLATION**

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: Charan Lal Sahu v. Union of India (Bhopal Gas Disaster)

**Facts:**
- Parliament enacted **Bhopal Gas Disaster Act, 1985**
- Central Government took over **exclusive right** to represent victims
- Against multinational corporation

**Challenges:**
1. Can Parliament enact such law?
2. Is it discriminatory under Art. 14?

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid on both grounds**

---

## ‚ùå **INVALID CLASSIFICATION**

### üèõÔ∏è Case: State of West Bengal v. Anwar Ali Sarkar

**Facts:**
- Bengal law for special courts for **"speedier trial"**
- State Government to direct which offences/cases
- Procedure **less advantageous** to accused than ordinary courts

**Held:** ‚ùå **Invalid**
- Made **no reasonable classification**
- Arbitrary power to pick offences/cases

---

## ‚öôÔ∏è **ADMINISTRATIVE DISCRETION & ARTICLE 14**

### Problem

Modern statutes often confer **discretionary power** on administrators:
- Many times **without norms/principles**
- Creates danger of **official arbitrariness**
- Subversive of equality doctrine

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Landmark: Maneka Gandhi Case**

**Principle:**
> ‚ö†Ô∏è When statute vests **unguided and unrestricted power** without policy/principle, it's affected by **vice of discrimination**.

Authority left free to discriminate between persons similarly situated.

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Air India v. Nergesh Meerza, AIR 1981**

**Facts:**
- Regulation: Normal retirement age for air hostesses = 35 years
- Managing Director can extend to 45 years **at his option**

**Held:** ‚ùå **Invalid**
- **Uncanalized and unguided discretion**
- No guidelines/principles/norms
- No procedural safeguards

---

### ‚úÖ **Valid Discretion**

#### üèõÔ∏è Case: Re Kerala Education Bill, AIR 1958

**Facts:** Bill gave broad control to government over private schools

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid**
- General policy **deducible** from preamble and title
- Reinforced by definite policy statements in clauses

> üìå **Principle:** Discretion valid if **policy/principles** laid down in statute.

---

## üö´ **ADMINISTRATIVE DISCRIMINATION**

### Two Situations

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Type 1** | Statute itself is discriminatory ‚Üí **Statute void** |
| **Type 2** | Statute valid, but **implementation discriminatory** ‚Üí **Action void** |

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Vellore Educational Trust v. State of AP**

**Facts:**
- Tamil Trust applied to establish engineering college for Tamil minority in AP
- Government **refused** permission to Trust
- **Granted** permission to two other societies at same time

**Held:** 
- Refusal **"not at all tenable"**
- Order **quashed** as discriminatory governmental action
- Government directed to **reconsider**

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Ajay Hasia v. Khalid Mujib, AIR 1981**

**Facts:**
- Many candidates admitted to engineering college with high interview marks
- But low written exam marks

**Held:**
- Gives rise to suspicion of manipulation
- But **not proved** mala fides
- Admissions **not quashed**
- College agreed to take 50 best students next year
- Court laid down **guidelines** for fair interviews

---

## üö® **ARBITRARY STATE ACTION**

### Fundamental Principle

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Art. 14 outlaws arbitrary administrative action.**

**When state action is arbitrary:**
- Art. 14 activated
- Courts strike down action
- Arbitrary action infringes Art. 14

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: E.P. Royappa v. State of Tamil Nadu, AIR 1974**

**Principle:**
> **"Any action that is arbitrary must necessarily involve negation of equality."**

Abuse of power hit by Art. 14.

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab, AIR 1982**

**Principle:**
> **"Rule of law excludes arbitrariness."**
> **"Wherever we find arbitrariness or unreasonableness there is denial of rule of law."**

**Art. 14 enacts primarily a guarantee against arbitrariness.**

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Maneka Gandhi Case**

**Principle:**
> **Principles of natural justice are integral part of guarantee of equality** assured by Art. 14.

**Order passed without hearing:**
- Violates natural justice
- Arbitrary order
- Violates Art. 14

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Ramana Dayaram Shetty v. International Airport Authority, AIR 1979**

**Facts:**
- Airport Authority floated tender for restaurant at Bombay Airport
- Eligibility conditions laid down
- Contract awarded to person **not fulfilling** conditions

**Held:**
- Authority should have stuck to conditions
- Action **discriminatory**
- Did not give equal chance to similarly situated persons
- **"Plainly arbitrary and without reason"**

---

## üìÖ **CUT-OFF DATES & ARTICLE 14**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: D.S. Nakara v. Union of India, AIR 1983**

**Facts:**
- Government issued liberalized pension scheme
- Applicable only to those retired **after March 31, 1979**

**Held:** ‚ùå Cut-off date **discriminatory** - violates Art. 14

---

## üíº **COMPASSIONATE APPOINTMENT**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: V. Sivamurthy v. State of A.P., (2008)**

**Principles:**
1. **NOT a right** of person
2. **Violative** of rule of equality (Art. 14)
3. But considered **inevitable** in certain circumstances
4. To prevent family from starvation

#### Object

> üéØ **Save bereaved family from sudden financial crisis** due to death of sole breadwinner.

**It is an exception to general rule of equality.**

## üè¢ **SPECIAL CLASSIFICATIONS - OTHER CASE LAW**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Sarbananda Sonowal v. Union of India, (2005)**

**Principles:**
- Government power to **expel foreigners** is absolute and unlimited
- No Constitutional fetter on this discretion
- Unrestricted right to expel foreigner
- Foreigners Act procedure is just, fair and reasonable
- Does not offend any Constitutional provision

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Mohan Kumar Singhania v. Union of India**

**Principle:**
Each civil service is **"separate and determinate"** service:
- I.A.S.
- I.F.S.
- I.P.S.
- Group A Services
- Group B Services

**Each forms distinct cadre founded on intelligible differentia.**

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Hindusthan Paper Corp. Ltd. v. Govt. of Kerala, AIR 1986**

**Principle:**
- Favourable treatment to **public sector undertaking** not discriminatory
- These undertakings stand in **different class altogether**
- Valid classification

## üìú **ARTICLE 15: PROHIBITION OF DISCRIMINATION**

### Text of Article 15(1)

> **"State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth or any of them."**

---

### Article 15(2)

> **No citizen shall be subjected to any disability, restriction or condition with regard to:**
> - Access to shops
> - Public restaurants
> - Hotels
> - Places of public entertainment
> - Use of wells, tanks, bathing ghats, roads
> - Places of public resort maintained wholly or partly out of State funds or dedicated to use of general public
>
> **on grounds of** religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth.

---

### üîë **Key Features**

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Beneficiary** | Only **citizens** (not all persons) |
| **Prohibited Grounds** | Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Place of Birth |
| **Scope** | Access to public places, facilities |
| **Horizontal Application** | Applies to **State and private persons** (Art. 15(2)) |

---

### Exceptions: Article 15(3), 15(4), 15(5)

#### Article 15(3)

> State may make **special provisions for women and children**.

#### Article 15(4) - Added 1951

> State may make **special provisions for:**
> - Advancement of **socially and educationally backward classes**
> - Scheduled Castes
> - Scheduled Tribes

**Purpose:** Enable **protective discrimination** and **affirmative action**.

---

#### Article 15(5) - Added 2005

> Enables reservations in **educational institutions** (including private unaided) for:
> - Socially and educationally backward classes
> - SC/ST
>
> **Except minority educational institutions**

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è **ARTICLE 16: EQUALITY IN PUBLIC EMPLOYMENT**

### Article 16(1)

> **"Equality of opportunity for all citizens in matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State."**

---

### Article 16(2)

> **"No citizen shall be discriminated against or be ineligible for any employment or office under the State on grounds only of:**
> - Religion
> - Race
> - Caste
> - Sex
> - Descent
> - Place of birth
> - Residence
> - Any of them"

---

### üîç **ARTICLE 14 vs ARTICLE 16**

| Feature | Article 14 | Article 16 |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Beneficiary** | All persons | Only citizens |
| **Scope** | All state activities | Public employment only |
| **Grounds** | General equality | Specific grounds listed |
| **Relationship** | General provision | Specific application of Art. 14 |

> üìå **Articles 16(1) and 16(2) give effect to Arts. 14 and 15.**

---

### üîß **SCOPE AND APPLICATION**

#### Available To

- ‚úÖ Even **temporary government servants**
- ‚úÖ If arbitrarily discriminated against
- ‚úÖ Singled out for harsh treatment

#### Classification

- ‚úÖ **Reasonable classification** permissible (like Art. 14)
- ‚öñÔ∏è **Onus** on person asserting discrimination

## üìù **SERVICE MATTERS UNDER ARTICLE 16**

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **SENIORITY**

#### Important Principles

| Principle | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Governed by** | Service rules |
| **Nature** | Civil right, NOT Fundamental Right |
| **Vested Right** | No vested right, but interest in seniority |
| **Can be taken away** | Only by valid law |
| **Fixation** | Art. 16 applies to appointment, not seniority fixation |
| **Unilateral change** | Arbitrary, violates Arts. 14 & 16 |

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Reserve Bank of India v. N.C. Paliwal, AIR 1976**

**Principle:**
- Rule-making authority can determine seniority rules with **objectivity and fairness**
- Rules ought not be **arbitrary and irrational**
- Should not result in **inequality of opportunity** among same class employees

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **APPOINTMENT**

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Nagpur Improvement Trust v. Yadaorao, (1999)**

**Principle:** Appointments can be made under **executive power** if no statutory service rules.

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: State of Orissa v. S. Mohapatra, (1993)**

**Principle:** Appointments in **violation of rules** infringe Arts. 14 & 16.
- Government **cannot regularize** them later.

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: State of Maharashtra v. Raj Kumar**

**Principle:** Should be **reasonable relation** between prescribed test and post.

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: State of J&K v. Shiv Ram Sharma, AIR 1999**

**Principle:**
- Permissible to prescribe **appropriate qualifications**
- Qualifications can be **"rational differentia"** under Art. 16
- Example: Stenographic ability 100 wpm is relevant qualification

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Javed Niaz Beg v. Union of India, AIR 1981**

**Facts:** Civil services exam 1979 - candidates from Eastern States (Mizoram, Meghalaya, Nagaland) given **option** to take/not take Indian languages paper.

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Not discriminatory** - these States have handicaps in language.

---

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ **COMPASSIONATE APPOINTMENT**

**Valid vis-a-vis Arts. 16(1) & 16(2).**

#### Principles

| Principle | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Relieve economic distress from sudden demise of govt servant |
| **Nature** | Exception carved out in interests of justice |
| **Procedure** | According to rules and guidelines |
| **Pre-requisite** | ‚ö†Ô∏è **Vacancy must be available** |

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Indian Bank v. K. Usha, AIR 1998**

**Principle:**
> ‚è±Ô∏è **"If vacancy available, appointment should be made as soon as possible. Improper to keep case pending for years."**

---

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ **PROMOTION**

#### Principles

| Principle | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Vested Right** | ‚ùå No vested right to promotion |
| **Right to Consideration** | ‚úÖ Right to be considered according to rules |
| **Chances of Promotion** | Not conditions of service; defeasible by law |
| **Policy Change** | Government can formulate/change, but must conform to equality |

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: All India Station-Masters' Ass. v. Gen. Manager, C. Rly., AIR 1960**

**Facts:** Guards given better promotion channel than roadside station-masters.

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Not discriminatory**
- Guards and station-masters are **separate categories**
- Separate avenues of promotion
- Equality means equality within **same class**, not between separate classes

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: T.R. Kothandaraman v. TN Water Supply Board, (1994)**

**Principle:**
> **Higher educational qualification** is permissible basis of classification.
> Acceptability depends on facts and circumstances of each case.

---

### 5Ô∏è‚É£ **COMPULSORY RETIREMENT**

**Principle:**
- Provision for compulsory retirement in **public interest** does not infringe Arts. 14 & 16
- These Articles do not prohibit reasonable rules for compulsory retirement
- Neither involves civil consequences, nor stigma

---

### 6Ô∏è‚É£ **RETIREMENT AGE**

**Expression "conditions of service"** includes age of superannuation.

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: K. Nagaraj v. State of AP, AIR 1985**

**Facts:** Age of superannuation reduced from 58 to 55 years.

**Held:** ‚úÖ Service rules can be amended under Art. 309.

---

### 7Ô∏è‚É£ **TERMINATION**

**No discrimination in termination.**

**Example:** Dismissal **solely** on ground of being **'non-Andhra'** held **void** as discrimination based on place of birth (prohibited by Art. 16(2)).

---

### 8Ô∏è‚É£ **TRANSFER**

**Transfer order** is part of service conditions.

#### Court Interference

Court interferes **only if:**
- ‚ùå Mala fide
- ‚ùå Service rules prohibit
- ‚ùå Authority not competent

#### No Legal Right

> üìå **Government employee has NO legal right to insist on posting at particular place** (unless service conditions provide).

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Arvind Dattatraya Dhande v. State of Maharashtra, AIR 1997**

**Facts:** Honest officer transferred due to pressure from vested interests.

**Held:**
- Transfer **quashed** as **mala fide**
- "Victimisation of honest officer"
- **Not in public interest**

---

## üéØ **EXCEPTIONS TO ARTICLES 16(1) & 16(2)**

### Exception 1: Article 16(3) - Residential Qualification

**Parliament** may prescribe **residence requirement** for specified appointments/posts in a State/UT.

#### Act Enacted

**Public Employment (Requirement as to Residence) Act, 1957**

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: A.V.S. Narasimha Rao v. State of AP**

**Principle:**
- Parliament can impose residential qualification for services in **whole State**
- **Cannot** impose for **part of State** (e.g., Telangana region)
- Provision for Telangana declared **unconstitutional**

---

### Exception 2: Article 16(4) - Reservations for Backward Classes

> üîë **Most significant exception to Art. 16(1)**

---

## üéì **RESERVATIONS IN SERVICES: ARTICLE 16(4)**

### Text

> **"State may make any provision for reservation of appointments or posts in favour of any backward class of citizens which, in the opinion of the State, is not adequately represented in the services under the State."**

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Mohan Kumar Singhania v. UOI, AIR 1992**

**Principle:**
> Art. 16(4) is an **"enabling provision"** conferring **discretionary power**.

#### Key Points

| Point | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Nature** | Enabling, not mandatory |
| **No Duty** | ‚ùå Does not impose constitutional duty |
| **No Right** | ‚ùå Does not confer Fundamental Right to claim reservation |
| **Discretionary** | State has discretion whether to reserve or not |

---

### Definition: 'Backward Class'

**Article 16(4):** "any backward class of citizens"

**Article 15(4):** "socially and educationally backward classes of citizens"

#### Judicial Interpretation

**Both mean the SAME thing.**

> ‚úÖ To qualify as 'backward class' under Art. 16(4), must be member of **"socially and educationally backward class"**.

**Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes** fall within "backward class".

---

## üìà **RESERVATION IN PROMOTIONS**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: General Manager, Southern Rly. v. Rangachari, AIR 1962**

**Facts:** Circulars providing reservation for SC/ST in promotions (by selection).

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid**

**Principle:**
> Reservation under Art. 16(4) can be made:
> - ‚úÖ At initial recruitment stage
> - ‚úÖ In matter of promotion to higher post/cadre

**Selection posts can also be reserved for backward classes.**

---

## üìä **QUANTUM OF RESERVATION**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Balaji v. State of Mysore, AIR 1963**

**Facts:** Government order reserved **68% seats** in educational institutions for SC/ST/OBC.

**Held:** ‚ùå **Unconstitutional** - excessive reservation, fraud on Constitution.

**Principle:**
> üìå **"Special provision should be less than 50%; how much less depends on circumstances."**

---

### 50% Rule

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **General Rule: Reservation should NOT exceed 50%**

---

## ‚ôªÔ∏è **CARRY FORWARD RULE**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: T. Devadasan v. Union of India, AIR 1964**

**Facts:**
- 17¬Ω% posts reserved for SC/ST annually
- Unfilled reserved posts carried forward for 2 years
- Result: Year 3 had **29 out of 45 vacancies** (65%) reserved

**Held:** ‚ùå **Invalid**
- Based on Balaji principle
- **More than 50% reservation unconstitutional** in single year

---

### Post-Thomas Era Change

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: State of Kerala v. N.M. Thomas, AIR 1976**

**Facts:** Kerala rules - promotion from lower to upper division clerk requires passing test within 2 years. SC/ST given **2 extra years** exemption.

**Challenge:** Discriminatory under Art. 16(1)

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid**
- **Preferential treatment** to SC/ST permissible under Art. 16(1) **outside Art. 16(4)**

> **Thomas marks beginning of new judicial thinking on Art. 16.**

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: A.B.S.K. Sangh (Rly.) v. Union of India, AIR 1981**

**Facts:** Reservation in selection posts in railways for SC/ST.

**Held:** ‚úÖ **Valid**

**Key Holdings:**
- Quantum (**17¬Ω%**) not excessive
- Field of eligibility not unreasonable
- ‚úÖ **Carry forward rule for 3 years NOT bad**
- Could go up to maximum **66%** - upheld

#### Distinction from Devadasan

| Case | Reservation % | Validity |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Devadasan** | Exceeded 50% | ‚ùå Invalid |
| **ABSK Sangh** | Up to 66% | ‚úÖ Valid |

#### Rationale for Different Treatment

**Art. 16(2) expressly forbids discrimination on basis of 'caste'.**

**BUT:**
- SC/ST are **not castes** in ordinary meaning
- They are **backward human groups**
- Great divide between them and rest of community
- Suffer from **socio-economic backwardness**

---

## üîç **WHAT ARE BACKWARD CLASSES?**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: K.C. Vasanth Kumar v. State of Karnataka, AIR 1985**

**Five judges wrote five separate opinions:**

| Judge | View on Backwardness |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Chandrachud, C.J.** | Economic backwardness |
| **Desai, J.** | **Only** economic backwardness criterion |
| **Chinnappa Reddy, J.** | Poverty, caste, occupation, habitation |
| **Sen, J.** | **Poverty predominant**; caste for identification only |
| **Venkataramiah, J.** | Stressed **relevance of caste** factor |

**No single clear test emerged.**

---

## üìã **THE MANDAL COMMISSION CASE**

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Indra Sawhney v. Union of India, AIR 1993** ‚≠ê

**Most significant pronouncement on reservations.**

---

### Background

| Event | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **1979** | Mandal Commission appointed under Art. 340 |
| **Purpose** | Investigate conditions of socially & educationally backward classes |
| **Finding** | OBCs constitute ~52% population |
| **Recommendation** | **27% government jobs** reserved for OBCs (total 50% with SC/ST) |
| **1982, 1983** | Report discussed in Parliament |
| **Aug 13, 1990** | V.P. Singh Govt accepted recommendation via office memorandum |
| **Result** | Widespread disturbances |
| **1991** | Narasimha Rao Govt modified: (1) Total not to exceed 50% (2) Preference to poorer within OBC quota |

---

### Caste Factor After Indra Sawhney

> üîë **Caste can be ONE factor, but NOT SOLE factor to assess backwardness.**

---

## ü•õ **CREAMY LAYER**

### Principle from Mandal Case

> ‚öñÔ∏è **"Socially advanced members of backward class - the 'creamy layer' - must be EXCLUDED."**

**Benefit of reservation under Art. 16(4) only to the "class" after exclusion of creamy layer.**

### Rationale

Would more appropriately serve purpose and object of Art. 16(4).

---

## üö´ **ARTICLE 17: ABOLITION OF UNTOUCHABILITY**

### Text

> **"Untouchability" is abolished and its practice in any form is forbidden. The enforcement of any disability arising out of "untouchability" shall be an offence punishable in accordance with law.**

---

### Main Object

> üéØ **Ban practice of untouchability in ANY form.**

**Complete abolition:**
- All-pervading
- Applicable to **state action AND private acts**

---

### Legislative Framework

#### Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955

**Originally:** Untouchability (Offences) Act, 1955
**Renamed 1976:** Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955

#### Definition

**"Untouchability"** not defined (not capable of precise definition).

---

### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Pavadai v. State of Madras, AIR 1973**

**Facts:** State law for acquisition of land to construct colony for untouchables.

**Challenge:** Construction of colony not in conformity with Art. 17.

**Held:** ‚úÖ Argument rejected. Court referred to **Art. 15(4)**.

---

### Constitutional Amendment: Article 15(4)

**Added 1951** to enable:
- Special provisions for advancement of backward classes
- Special provisions for SC/ST

---

### SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989

#### Objectives

1. **Prevent** commission of atrocities against SC/ST members
2. **Provide** for special Courts for trial
3. **Provide** for relief and rehabilitation of victims

---

#### üèõÔ∏è **Case: Jai Singh v. Union of India, AIR 1993**

**Supreme Court observed:**
> *"Despite various measures, SC/ST remain vulnerable. Denied civil rights. Subjected to offences, indignities, humiliations, harassment. Deprived of life and property in brutal incidents."*

---

### Article 15(2) & Untouchability

**Art. 15(2) also helps** eradicate untouchability.

**Prohibits denial of access** to:
- Shops
- Public restaurants
- Hotels
- Public entertainment
- Wells, tanks, ghats
- Roads
- Places of public resort

**On grounds of:** religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth.

---

### Horizontal Application

**Arts. 17 and 15(2) protect individual:**
- ‚úÖ From State action
- ‚úÖ From private persons

**When private individual violates Art. 17:**
> **Constitutional obligation of State** to take necessary steps for interdiction and ensuring observance.

---

### Directive Principles

**Arts. 38 and 46** obligate State to:
- Render socio-economic and political justice to Dalits
- Improve quality of their life

---

## üèÖ **ARTICLE 18: ABOLITION OF TITLES**

### Article 18(1)

> **State prohibited from conferring any 'title' except a military or academic distinction.**

### Article 18(2)

> **Citizens cannot accept title from foreign government.**

### Article 18(3)

> **Foreigner holding office under State** cannot accept title from foreign state without President's consent.

### Article 18(4)

> **Person holding office of profit under State** cannot accept present, emolument, or office from foreign state without President's consent.

---

### Legal Implications

**Unclear:** What happens if citizen accepts title contrary to Art. 18(2)?

**Parliament can legislate** under residuary powers to prescribe consequences.

---

### üèõÔ∏è **National Awards - Case: Balaji Raghavan v. UOI, AIR 1996**

**Issue:** Are national awards like Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan "titles" under Art. 18(1)?

**Held:** ‚ùå **NOT titles**
- Not violative of Arts. 14 and 18
- **Valid**

---

### Theory Behind Article 18

#### Principle

> üéØ **Equality does NOT mandate that merit should not be recognized.**

#### Art. 51A(j)

Exhorts every citizen:
> *"To strive towards excellence in all spheres of individual and collective activity."*

**Therefore:** System of awards and decorations necessary to recognize excellence.

---

### Guidelines for National Awards

#### Usage

**Awards NOT to be used as suffixes or prefixes.**

#### Selection

Prime Minister in consultation with President should appoint **high-level committee** to lay down criteria for selection.
