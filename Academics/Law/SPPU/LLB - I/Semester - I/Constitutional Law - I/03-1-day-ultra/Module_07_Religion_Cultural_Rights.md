## üìú **ARTICLE 25 - INDIVIDUAL**

### Three Ps

1. **Profess** - declare belief
2. **Practice** - perform rituals
3. **Propagate** - spread (NOT forced conversion)

### Essential Practices Doctrine

**Commissioner HRE v. Lakshmindra 1954:**
- Only **essential** practices protected
- Non-essential can be regulated

**Examples:**
- ‚úÖ Namaz essential (protected)
- ‚ùå Mosque not essential (Ismail Faruqui 1994)
- ‚ùå Polygamy not essential (Ratilal Panachand 1954)

## üìú **ARTICLE 26 - DENOMINATION**

### Four Rights - EMOA

1. **Establish** institutions
2. **Manage** affairs in religion
3. **Own** property
4. **Administer** property (per law)

**Durgah Committee:** Property administration = secular, can be regulated

## üîë **SECULARISM**

**Added to Preamble:** 1976 (42nd Amendment)

**S.R. Bommai 1994:** Secularism is **basic feature**

**Indian secularism:** Equal respect to all religions, positive engagement

---

## üéì **ARTICLES 29-30: MINORITIES**

### Article 29

**29(1):** Any section can **conserve** culture (language, script, culture)

**29(2):** No discrimination in admission (State/aided institutions)

---

### Article 30 - Most Important

**30(1):** Minorities can **establish and administer** educational institutions

**Minority:** Religious or Linguistic (determined at **State level**)

---

### T.M.A. Pai Foundation 2005 ‚≠ê

**Most comprehensive judgment**

**Holdings:**
- Extensive **autonomy** for minority institutions
- Can give preference to minority students
- Must maintain excellence
- No capitation fee
- Transparent admissions

**St. Stephen's:** 50% preference to Christians valid
