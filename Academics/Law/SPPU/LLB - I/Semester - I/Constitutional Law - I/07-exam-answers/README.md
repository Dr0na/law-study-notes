## ðŸ“ SPPU-SPECIFIC WRITING TIPS

### What SPPU Examiners Look For

**âœ“ DO's:**
1. **Article Numbers** - Always cite (e.g., "Article 19(1)(a)", "Article 14")
2. **Case Laws** - Minimum 4-5 for 15M, 2-3 for 10M, 1 for 5M
3. **AIR Year** - Write case name with year (e.g., "Maneka Gandhi AIR 1978")
4. **Headings** - Clear, numbered headings and subheadings
5. **Tables** - Use tables for comparisons
6. **Underline** - Underline important terms, case names, Article numbers
7. **Structure** - Follow Introduction â†’ Body â†’ Conclusion format
8. **Legible Writing** - Clear handwriting with proper spacing
9. **Constitutional Language** - Use terms like "petitioner," "detenu," "ratio decidendi"
10. **Balance** - Equal treatment of all aspects

**âœ— DON'Ts:**
1. Long paragraphs without breaks
2. Forgetting Article numbers
3. No case laws or wrong case names
4. Irrelevant content (stick to question)
5. Exceeding page limit
6. Colloquial language
7. Political opinions
8. Copying question in answer
9. Starting without reading question properly
10. Poor time management

## ðŸŽ¨ PRESENTATION TECHNIQUES

### Use of Headings

**Good Example:**
```
I. INTRODUCTION
   1.1 Definition of Public Interest Litigation
   1.2 Constitutional Basis

II. EVOLUTION OF PIL IN INDIA
   2.1 Pre-1980 Position
   2.2 S.P. Gupta Case and Liberalization
   
III. LANDMARK CASES
   3.1 Bandhua Mukti Morcha (1984)
   3.2 M.C. Mehta Cases
   
IV. CONCLUSION
```

**Bad Example:**
```
Public Interest Litigation
PIL is important
Some cases
Conclusion
```

---

### Use of Tables

**When to Use Tables:**
- Comparisons (Article 32 vs 226, FR vs DPSP)
- Classifications (Types of writs, Categories of DPSP)
- Before/After scenarios (Pre-1978 property rights vs current)
- Multiple elements (Salient features, Types of citizenship)

**Example:**

| Article 32 | Article 226 |
|------------|-------------|
| Only Supreme Court | High Courts |
| Only for FR | FR + other rights |
| Part of FR | Not FR |

---

### Citation Format

**Case Law Citation:**

**Full Citation (First mention):**
"In **Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala (AIR 1973 SC 1461)**, the Supreme Court held..."

**Short Citation (Subsequent mentions):**
"The **Kesavananda Bharati** case established..."

**Very Short (If already discussed):**
"As held in **Kesavananda Bharati**..."

**Article Citation:**

**With Clause:**
"Article 19(1)(a)" or "Article 19(2)"

**Without Clause:**
"Article 14" or "Article 21"

**Multiple Articles:**
"Articles 14, 19, and 21" or "Articles 25-28"
