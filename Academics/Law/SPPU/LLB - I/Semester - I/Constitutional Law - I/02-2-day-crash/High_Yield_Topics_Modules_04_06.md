### Article 21 - Most Important FR

**Text:** "No person deprived of life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law"

### Evolution in 2 Stages

| Stage | Case | Year | View |
|-------|------|------|------|
| **Stage 1** | A.K. Gopalan | 1950 | Narrow - only physical restraint |
| **Stage 2** | Maneka Gandhi | 1978 | Broad - numerous implied rights |

---

### Maneka Gandhi Revolution (1978)

**5 Key Principles:**

1. ‚öñÔ∏è **Arts. 14, 19, 21 NOT mutually exclusive** - interconnected
2. ‚öñÔ∏è **Procedure must be reasonable, fair, just** - due process introduced

4. ‚öñÔ∏è **Anti-arbitrariness** - Art. 14 outlaws arbitrariness
5. ‚öñÔ∏è **Expand reach** of FRs, not attenuate

### Article 21A - Right to Education

**Added:** 86th Amendment, 2002

**Age:** 6-14 years

**Free and compulsory**

**Duty:** Art. 51A(k) - parent to provide education

**Case:** Unnikrishnan (before amendment implied from Art. 21)

---

### Article 22 - Arrest & Detention

**Four Safeguards - ICLP:**
1. **I** - Information about grounds
2. **C** - Consult lawyer
3. **L** - Lawyer (right to be defended)
4. **P** - Production before magistrate (24 hours)

**NOT applicable to:** Enemy aliens + Preventive detention

---

### Preventive Detention

**Definition:** Detention without trial to prevent future act

**Difference from Conviction:**

| Conviction | Preventive Detention |
|------------|---------------------|
| Past act | Future act |
| Trial + proof | Subjective satisfaction |
| Punishment | Prevention |

**Laws - NCP:**
- **N** - NSA (1980)
- **C** - COFEPOSA (1974)
- **P** - PBMSECA (1980)

**Rights of Detenu:**
- Communication of grounds (Art. 22(5))
- Make representation
- Advisory Board (within 3 months)

## üéØ **MODULE 06: RIGHT AGAINST EXPLOITATION (ARTICLES 23-24)**

### Article 23 - Three Prohibitions: TBF

| Prohibition | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **T** - Traffic | Slavery, buying/selling humans |
| **B** - Begar | Work without payment |
| **F** - Forced Labour | Any forced work |

---

### Revolutionary Change (1982)

**Before 1982:** Narrow interpretation, rarely used

**After 1982 (Asiad case):** **Every form of forced labour prohibited**

**Key Principle:** Wages below minimum = Forced labour

---

### Acts Under Art. 23 - BIMEC

| Act | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **B** - Bonded Labour (Abolition) Act, 1976 | Abolish bonded labour |
| **I** - Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956 | Prevent trafficking |
| **M** - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 | Ensure minimum wages |
| **E** - Equal Remuneration Act, 1976 | Equal pay for equal work |
| **C** - Contract Labour Act | Regulate contract labour |

---

### Key Cases - Art. 23

| Case | Principle |
|------|-----------|
| **Asiad (1982)** | Expansive interpretation started |
| **Bandhua Mukti Morcha** | PIL for bonded labour, links Arts. 23 & 21 |
| **Sanjit Roy** | Wages below minimum = forced labour |
| **Neeraja Choudhary** | Rehabilitation essential |

---

### Article 24 - Child Labour

**Age:** Below 14 years

**Prohibited in - FMH:**
- **F** - Factory
- **M** - Mine
- **H** - Hazardous employment

**Horizontal application** - enforceable against everyone

---

### Acts - FMAC

- **F** - Factories Act, 1948
- **M** - Mines Act, 1952
- **A** - Apprentice Act, 1961
- **C** - Child Labour (Prohibition & Regulation) Act, 1986

---

### Key Case - Art. 24

**M.C. Mehta v. Tamil Nadu:** Sivakasi match industry

**Directions:**
- Eradicate child labour in hazardous industry
- Employers comply with Child Labour Act
- Children to school, free education

---

### Link with Art. 21A

**Children have FR to:**
- Education (Art. 21A)
- Life with dignity (Art. 21)
- No exploitation (Arts. 23-24)
