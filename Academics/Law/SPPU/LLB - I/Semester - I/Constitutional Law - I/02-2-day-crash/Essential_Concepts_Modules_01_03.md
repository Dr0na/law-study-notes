### Dr. Ambedkar's 3 Warnings

1. **Social democracy needed** for political democracy
2. **Contradictions** - political equality vs social inequality
3. **Avoid hero worship** and grammar of anarchy

---

### Preamble - Key Words

> **SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC**
> **JUSTICE LIBERTY EQUALITY FRATERNITY**

**Mnemonic:** "We The People - SSSDR - JLEF"

**Legal Status:** Part of Constitution (Kesavananda 1973) but not enforceable

---

### Salient Features - Top 10

**Mnemonic: "PLUS-DIIF-JE"**

1. **P** - Parliamentary system
2. **L** - Lengthiest written
3. **U** - Universal adult franchise (18+)
4. **S** - Secular
5. **D** - Directive Principles
6. **I** - Independent Judiciary
7. **I** - Integrated (single citizenship)
8. **F** - Fundamental Rights
9. **J** - Judicial Review
10. **E** - Emergency provisions

**Quasi-Federal:** Federal structure + Unitary bias

## üéØ **MODULE 02: CITIZENSHIP & ARTICLES 12-13**

### Citizenship - BDRNI

**5 Ways to Acquire:**
1. **B** - Birth (born in India)
2. **D** - Descent (parent Indian)
3. **R** - Registration (overseas Indians)
4. **N** - Naturalisation (foreigners)
5. **I** - Incorporation (territory joins India)

**3 Ways to Lose: RCD**
1. **R** - Renunciation (voluntary)
2. **C** - Cessation (acquiring foreign citizenship)
3. **D** - Deprivation (govt order, 5 grounds)

### Key Principles

‚úì **Single citizenship** (no state citizenship)
‚úì **No dual citizenship** (Art. 9)
‚úì **Overseas citizenship** available (2003)

---

### Article 12 - Definition of "State"

**For Part III (Fundamental Rights)**

**Includes:**
- Govt + Parliament (Centre)
- State Govts + Legislatures
- Local authorities
- **Other authorities**

**Test (Ajay Hasia):** Statutory creation + State control + Public function + Financial assistance

---

### Article 13 - Laws Inconsistent with FRs

**Pre-Constitution laws:** Void to extent of inconsistency

**Post-Constitution laws:** Cannot abridge FRs

**Key Doctrines:**
- **Eclipse** - Pre-Constitution law eclipsed, not dead
- **Severability** - Invalid part severed if possible

---

### Basic Structure Doctrine

**Kesavananda Bharati (1973):**

**Parliament can amend Constitution BUT cannot destroy basic structure**

**Basic Features:** SURFS-JRP
- Supremacy, Unity, Rule of law, Federal, Secular
- Judicial review, Republican, Parliamentary

**Evolution:**
1. **Shankari Prasad (1951):** Amendment not law ‚úì
2. **Golak Nath (1967):** Amendment is law ‚úó (overruled)
3. **Kesavananda (1973):** Basic Structure ‚úì (current law)

### Indra Sawhney (1993) - MUST KNOW

**The Mandal Case:**

**Background:** 27% reservation for OBC announced by V.P. Singh govt (1990)

**Key Holdings (Memorize):**
1. ‚úÖ 27% for OBC **valid**
2. ‚úÖ **Creamy layer must be excluded** (OBC only)
3. ‚úÖ **50% rule reaffirmed**
4. ‚úÖ Caste one factor, not sole factor
5. ‚ùå No reservation in promotions (as per this judgment)

**Why Important:** Most comprehensive judgment on reservations

---
