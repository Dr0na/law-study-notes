## üéØ **MODULE 07: FREEDOM OF RELIGION & CULTURAL RIGHTS**

### Articles 25-28: Religion

**Mnemonic: "PaManSciNo"**

| Article | Subject | Key Point |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **25** | **Pa**rsonal freedom | PPP: Profess, Practice, Propagate |
| **26** | **Man**age affairs | EMOA: Establish, Manage, Own, Administer |
| **27** | (Fi)**Sci**al - No taxes | No tax for religion |
| **28** | **No** instruction | No religious instruction in State schools |

---

### Article 25 - Individual Freedom

**Three Components - PPP:**
1. **Profess** - declare beliefs
2. **Practice** - perform rituals
3. **Propagate** - spread religion (NOT forced conversion)

**Subject to:** Public order, morality, health

**Essential Practices Doctrine:**
- Only **essential** practices protected
- Non-essential can be regulated

---

### Top 3 Cases - Religion

| Case | Year | Principle |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Commissioner HRE v. Lakshmindra** | 1954 | Essential practices doctrine |
| **Ratilal Panachand** | 1954 | Polygamy not essential |
| **M. Ismail Faruqui** | 1994 | Mosque not essential for namaz |

---

### Article 26 - Denominational Freedom

**Four Rights - EMOA:**
1. **E** - Establish institutions
2. **M** - Manage affairs in religion
3. **O** - Own property
4. **A** - Administer property (per law)

**Durgah Committee case:** Property administration is **secular** activity - State can regulate

---

### Secularism - Indian Style

**S.R. Bommai (1994):** Secularism is **basic feature**

**Indian vs Western:**
- Western: Separation
- Indian: **Principled distance**, positive engagement

---

### Articles 29-30: Minorities

| Article | Right | Beneficiary |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| **29(1)** | Conserve culture | Any section |
| **29(2)** | No discrimination | In admission |
| **30(1)** | Establish & administer | Minorities only |
| **30(2)** | No discrimination in aid | By State |

---

### Who is Minority?

**Determined at:** State level (not national)

**Types:** Religious + Linguistic

---

### Article 30 - Top 2 Cases

| Case | Year | Principle |
|------|------|-----------|
| **T.M.A. Pai Foundation** | 2005 | Extensive autonomy for minority institutions |
| **St. Stephen's College** | 1992 | 50% preference to Christians valid |

**Exam Tip:** T.M.A. Pai most comprehensive judgment on minority rights!

## üéØ **MODULE 08: CONSTITUTIONAL REMEDIES (ARTICLE 32)**

### Article 32 - Soul of Constitution

> üí¨ **Dr. Ambedkar:** "Very soul and very heart of Constitution"

**Nature:** Itself a **Fundamental Right**

**Scope:** Only for enforcement of FRs

**Cannot be suspended:** Except during Emergency (but Arts. 20, 21 cannot be suspended)

---

### Five Writs - CHAMP

| Writ | Purpose | Stage |
|------|---------|-------|
| **C** - Certiorari | Quash order | After decision |
| **H** - Habeas Corpus | Produce body | Any time |
| **A** - (Quo) Warranto | Question authority | Any time |
| **M** - Mandamus | Command duty | Any time |
| **P** - Prohibition | Stop proceedings | Before decision |

---

### Quick Memory: Writ Purposes

**Mnemonic: "P-D-S-P-A"**
- **P** - Person (Habeas Corpus - personal liberty)
- **D** - Duty (Mandamus - do your duty)
- **S** - Stop (Prohibition - stop proceedings)
- **P** - Past (Certiorari - quash past order)
- **A** - Authority (Quo Warranto - show authority)

---

### Article 32 vs Article 226

| Feature | Art. 32 (SC) | Art. 226 (HC) |
|---------|--------------|---------------|
| **Nature** | Fundamental Right | Power of HC |
| **Scope** | Only FRs | FRs + other rights |
| **Territorial** | All India | HC jurisdiction area |
| **Suspend** | Yes (Emergency) | No |

**Choice:** Petitioner can choose either (but not both simultaneously)

---

### Top Cases - Writs

| Case | Writ | Principle |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Rudul Shah** | Habeas Corpus | Compensation for 14 years illegal detention |
| **A.D.M. Jabalpur** | Habeas Corpus | Infamous Emergency case |
| **Minerva Mills** | Art. 32 | Art. 32 is basic feature |

---

### Public Interest Litigation (PIL)

**Pioneer:** S.P. Gupta (1982) - Judges Transfer Case

**Principle:** **Liberalized locus standi** - any public-spirited person can file

**For:** Poor and disadvantaged who cannot approach court

---

### Top 5 PIL Cases

| Case | Subject |
|------|---------|
| **Bandhua Mukti Morcha** | Bonded labour |
| **M.C. Mehta** | Environment (multiple cases) |
| **Vishaka** | Sexual harassment guidelines |
| **Sheela Barse** | Women/children in custody |
| **Olga Tellis** | Pavement dwellers |

**Exam Tip:** PIL is favorite exam topic - prepare well!

## üéØ **MODULE 09: DIRECTIVE PRINCIPLES & FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES**

### Directive Principles - Nature

**Article 37:** Non-justiciable but fundamental in governance

**Three Categories - SGL:**
1. **S** - Socialistic (38, 39, 41-43, 47)
2. **G** - Gandhian (40, 43, 46-48)
3. **L** - Liberal-Intellectual (44, 45, 49-51)

---

### Article 39 - Most Important DP

**Six Clauses - LWWCH:**

| Clause | Subject |
|--------|---------|
| **(a)** | **L**ivelihood - adequate means |
| **(b)** | **W**ealth - material resources for common good |
| **(c)** | **W**ealth - no concentration |
| **(d)** | **Women** - equal pay |
| **(e)** | **C**hildren - health/strength not abused |
| **(f)** | **H**ealthy - children opportunities |

**Exam Tip:** Art. 39 is most frequently asked DP!

---

### Other Key DPs

| Article | Subject | Importance |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **39A** | Free legal aid | Implemented |
| **40** | Panchayats | Implemented (73rd Amendment) |
| **44** | Uniform Civil Code | **NOT implemented** (controversial) |
| **45** | Early childhood care | Modified after Art. 21A |
| **46** | SC/ST welfare | Basis for reservations |
| **48A** | Environment | Used in PIL |

---

### FRs vs DPs - Quick Table

| Aspect | FRs | DPs |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Nature** | Justiciable | Non-justiciable |
| **Enforcement** | Courts | Legislature |
| **Aim** | Political democracy | Economic democracy |
| **Implementation** | Immediate | Progressive |

---

### Relationship Cases

| Case | Year | Principle |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Champakam** | 1951 | FRs prevail over DPs |
| **Minerva Mills** | 1980 | **Complementary**, balance is basic structure |
| **Unnikrishnan** | 1993 | Read FRs with DPs |

---

### Article 31C - Primacy to DPs

**Laws for Arts. 39(b) and (c)** cannot be challenged under Arts. 14, 19

**Effect:** Social welfare over individual rights (limited to these 2 clauses)

---

### Fundamental Duties - Article 51A

**Added:** 1976 (42nd Amendment) - 10 duties
**Added:** 2002 (86th Amendment) - 11th duty (education)

**Mnemonic: CHAIRS-VIP-ED**

**Quick List:**
1. Constitution, Flag, Anthem
2. Freedom struggle ideals
3. Sovereignty, unity, integrity
4. Defend country
5. Harmony, women's dignity
6. Composite culture
7. Environment, wildlife
8. Scientific temper
9. Public property, non-violence
10. Excellence
11. **Education to child (6-14)** [2002]

---

### Nature

**Non-justiciable** - moral obligations

**Cannot override FRs** (Bijoe Emmanuel)
