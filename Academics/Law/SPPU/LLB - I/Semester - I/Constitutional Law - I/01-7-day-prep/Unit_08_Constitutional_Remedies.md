## ‚öñÔ∏è **ARTICLE 32: SOUL OF CONSTITUTION**

### Dr. Ambedkar's Quote

> üí¨ **"Article 32 is the very soul of the Constitution and the very heart of it."**

**Why?**
- Gives teeth to Fundamental Rights
- Without remedy, rights are meaningless
- Ensures judicial protection

---

### Key Features

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Nature** | **Itself a Fundamental Right** |
| **Guaranteed** | Right to approach SC |
| **Scope** | Only for enforcement of FRs |
| **Power** | SC can issue writs/directions |
| **Basic Feature** | Yes (Minerva Mills 1980) |
| **Suspension** | Possible during Emergency (but Arts. 20,21 protected) |

## üîê **THE FIVE WRITS - CHAMP**

### Quick Reference

| Writ | Meaning | Purpose | Stage |
|------|---------|---------|-------|
| **C** - Certiorari | To be certified | Quash order | After |
| **H** - Habeas Corpus | Produce body | Release from detention | Anytime |
| **A** - (Quo) Warranto | By what authority | Question office | Anytime |
| **M** - Mandamus | We command | Command duty | Anytime |
| **P** - Prohibition | Forbid | Stop proceedings | Before |

**Memory: P-D-S-P-A**
- **P**erson (Habeas - liberty)
- **D**uty (Mandamus - do duty)
- **S**top (Prohibition - stop)
- **P**ast (Certiorari - quash)
- **A**uthority (Warranto - show)

---

### 1. Habeas Corpus - Most Important

**For:** Release from illegal detention

**Against:** Public authorities + Private persons

**Key Case: Rudul Shah v. Bihar (1983)**
- Detained 14 years after acquittal
- Released + **Compensation ‚Çπ35,000**
- Precedent for compensatory relief

**A.D.M. Jabalpur (1976):**
- Infamous Emergency case
- No habeas corpus during Emergency (held)
- **Overruled by 44th Amendment** (Arts. 20,21 cannot be suspended)

---

### 2. Mandamus - Most Common

**For:** Command to perform public duty

**Against:** Public authorities (not private persons)

**Requirements:**
- Legal right in petitioner
- Legal duty on respondent
- Duty must be public, clear, enforceable

**NOT issued:** Against President/Governor (Art. 361), for discretionary duty

---

### 3 & 4. Prohibition & Certiorari

**Both for:** Jurisdictional errors of lower courts/tribunals

| Feature | Prohibition | Certiorari |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Stage** | Before decision | After decision |
| **Nature** | Preventive | Curative |
| **Effect** | Stops | Quashes |

---

### 5. Quo Warranto

**For:** Question authority to hold public office

**Requirements:**
- Public office
- Substantive (not honorary)
- Created by statute/Constitution

**Example:** University of Mysore (VC appointment challenged)

## üìä **ARTICLE 32 vs ARTICLE 226**

| Aspect | Art. 32 (SC) | Art. 226 (HC) |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| **Nature** | FR itself | Power of HC |
| **Scope** | Only FRs | FRs + other rights |
| **Territorial** | All India | HC jurisdiction |
| **Discretion** | Cannot refuse | Can refuse (discretionary) |
| **Suspend** | Yes (Emergency) | No |

**Petitioner's choice:** Can choose either (not both)

**Romesh Thappar:** Choice of forum with petitioner

## üèõÔ∏è **PUBLIC INTEREST LITIGATION (PIL)**

### Definition

**Litigation for public interest** by any public-spirited person

**Traditional rule:** Only aggrieved person (locus standi required)

**PIL:** Liberalized locus standi

---

### Pioneer Case

**S.P. Gupta v. Union of India, AIR 1982** (Judges Transfer Case)

**Held:**
- **Liberalized locus standi**
- Any public-spirited person can file
- For public injury or public duty
- Democratize judicial process

**Justice Bhagwati:** Access to justice for poor and disadvantaged

---

### When PIL Filed

‚úì FR violation of poor/disadvantaged
‚úì Public duty not performed
‚úì Victims cannot approach court
‚úì Public interest affected

---

### Top 5 PIL Cases

| Case | Subject | Impact |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Bandhua Mukti Morcha** | Bonded labour | Release + rehabilitation |
| **M.C. Mehta** (Multiple) | Environment, child labour | Numerous directions |
| **Vishaka** | Sexual harassment | Guidelines issued |
| **Sheela Barse** | Women/children custody | Protection guidelines |
| **Olga Tellis** | Pavement dwellers | Right to livelihood |

---

### Limitations

**PIL NOT for:**
- Private interest disguised as public
- Publicity seeking
- Political vendetta
- Settling personal scores

**Ashok Kumar Pandey:** Court can impose costs for frivolous PIL

## üö® **EMERGENCY & FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS**

### Article 359 - Suspension

**During National Emergency (Art. 352):**
- President can suspend right to move court
- **Originally:** All FRs including Art. 21
- **After 44th Amendment 1978:** **Arts. 20, 21 CANNOT be suspended**

**A.D.M. Jabalpur (1976):**
- Right to life suspended during Emergency
- Infamous judgment
- Led to 44th Amendment

**Current position:** Right to life always available, even during Emergency
