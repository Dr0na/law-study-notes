## üîÑ **INTERCONNECTION WITH ARTS. 14 & 21**

**Maneka Gandhi Principle:**
- Arts. 14, 19, 21 NOT mutually exclusive
- Must be read together
- Restriction on Art. 19 must satisfy Arts. 14 and 21 also

**Implications:**
- Restriction must not be arbitrary (Art. 14)
- Must be reasonable, fair, just (Art. 21)
- Greater protection to freedoms

## üè† **PROPERTY RIGHTS - TRANSFORMATION**

### Historical Evolution

**1950-1978:**
- **Art. 19(1)(f):** Right to property
- **Art. 31:** Compulsory acquisition with compensation
- **Status:** Fundamental Right

**Problem:** Conflict with land reforms and socialism

**Special Provisions Added:**
- **Art. 31A:** Saving of land reform laws
- **Art. 31B:** Ninth Schedule (protected laws)
- **Art. 31C:** Laws for Arts. 39(b),(c) protected

**Many amendments:** 1st, 4th, 17th, 25th

---

### 44th Amendment, 1978

**Deleted:**
- Art. 19(1)(f)
- Art. 31

**Added:**
- **Art. 300A:** Property as constitutional right

---

### Current Position (Post-1978)

| Aspect | Pre-1978 | Post-1978 |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Status** | FR | Constitutional right (NOT FR) |
| **Article** | 19(1)(f) + 31 | 300A only |
| **Enforceable** | Art. 32 | Not Art. 32 |
| **Compensation** | Required | Not constitutionally required |
| **Review** | Arts. 14,19,31 | Arts. 14,21 only |

**Art. 300A Text:** "No person deprived of property save by authority of law"

**Special provisions continue:** Arts. 31A, 31B, 31C still exist
