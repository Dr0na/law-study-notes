## üìú **DIRECTIVE PRINCIPLES (PART IV, ARTS. 36-51)**

### Nature - Article 37

> **"Non-justiciable but fundamental in governance of country"**

**Key Points:**
- Cannot be enforced in court
- State's duty to apply in making laws
- Guidelines for policy
- Socio-economic rights

**Why non-justiciable?**
- Resource constraints
- Policy matters
- Progressive implementation
- Flexibility needed

---

## üìä **THREE CATEGORIES - SGL**

### 1. Socialistic Principles

**Articles:** 38, 39, 39A, 41, 42, 43, 43A, 43B, 47

**Aim:** Social and economic justice

**Key provisions:**
- Art. 38: Welfare state
- **Art. 39: Most important** (6 clauses)
- Art. 39A: Free legal aid
- Art. 41: Right to work, education
- Art. 42: Just conditions of work
- Art. 43: Living wage

---

### 2. Gandhian Principles

**Articles:** 40, 43, 46, 47, 48, 48A

**Based on:** Gandhi's philosophy

**Key provisions:**
- Art. 40: Panchayats (implemented by 73rd Amendment)
- Art. 43: Village industries
- Art. 46: SC/ST welfare
- Art. 48: Cattle protection
- Art. 48A: Environment (added 1976)

---

### 3. Liberal-Intellectual Principles

**Articles:** 44, 45, 49, 50, 51

**Key provisions:**
- **Art. 44: Uniform Civil Code** (NOT implemented - controversial)
- Art. 45: Early childhood care (modified after Art. 21A)
- Art. 50: Separation of judiciary from executive
- Art. 51: International peace

## ‚öñÔ∏è **FRs vs DPs - COMPARISON**

| Aspect | FRs (Part III) | DPs (Part IV) |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| **Nature** | Justiciable | Non-justiciable |
| **Enforcement** | Through courts | Through legislature |
| **Binding** | Legally binding | Morally/politically binding |

## üîÑ **RELATIONSHIP: FRs & DPs**

### Evolution of Judicial View

**Phase 1: Champakam Dorairajan (1951)**
- FRs prevail over DPs
- In conflict, FRs win
- DPs cannot override FRs

---

**Phase 2: Minerva Mills (1980)** ‚≠ê

**Most important case on relationship**

**Held:**
- FRs and DPs are **COMPLEMENTARY**, not contradictory
- Not in conflict
- **Balance between them is BASIC STRUCTURE**
- Neither can claim supremacy
- Constitution founded on "bedrock of balance"

**Justice Chandrachud:** Indian Constitution is founded on balance between Parts III and IV

---

**Phase 3: Unnikrishnan (1993)**
- Read FRs with DPs together
- Comprehensive social order
- DPs can enrich and expand FRs
- Right to education derived from Art. 21 + Art. 45

---

### Article 31C - Primacy to Some DPs

**Laws for Arts. 39(b) and (c):**
- Cannot be challenged under Arts. 14, 19
- Limited primacy to these 2 clauses only

**Originally:** All DPs (struck down in Minerva Mills)

## üèõÔ∏è **FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES (ART. 51A)**

### Background

**Added by:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

**On recommendation of:** Swaran Singh Committee

**Why added:**
- Rights without duties incomplete
- Emergency period (1975-77)
- Promote patriotism
- Nation-building

**Further addition:** 86th Amendment, 2002 (11th duty - education)

---

---

### Nature

**Non-justiciable** - like DPs

**Cannot override FRs:**
- Bijoe Emmanuel v. Kerala (1987)
- Jehovah's Witness children
- Refused to sing anthem (religious belief)
- Stood respectfully
- Expulsion invalid - FRs protected

---

### Enforcement

**Direct:** Cannot be enforced in court

**Indirect:**
1. Through legislation (e.g., Prevention of Insults to National Honour Act)
2. Judicial interpretation (M.C. Mehta - environment duty)
3. Education (include in curriculum)
