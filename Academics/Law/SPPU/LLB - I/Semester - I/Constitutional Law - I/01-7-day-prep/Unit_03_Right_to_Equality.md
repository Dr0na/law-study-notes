## ‚öñÔ∏è **ARTICLE 14: EQUALITY BEFORE LAW**

### üìú Text

> **"State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or equal protection of laws within territory of India."**

### Two Concepts

| Concept | Meaning | Nature |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Equality Before Law** | No one above law | Negative |
| **Equal Protection** | Equals treated equally | Positive |

### Basic Feature

‚≠ê **Kesavananda Bharati (1973):** Right to equality is **basic feature** - even amendments cannot destroy it.

---

## üìä **REASONABLE CLASSIFICATION**

### Principle

> Art. 14 forbids **CLASS LEGISLATION** but allows **REASONABLE CLASSIFICATION**

### Two-Fold Test: **"I-R"**

| Test | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **I** - Intelligible Differentia | Real & substantial distinction |
| **R** - Rational Nexus | Between classification and object |

### Burden of Proof

**On challenger** - but shifts to State if discrimination obvious (Dalmia v. Tendolkar)

---

### Valid Classifications - Cases

| Classification Type | Case | Principle |
|-------------------|------|-----------|
| **Geographical** | Kishan Singh v. Rajasthan | Regional law valid if conditions different |
| **Single Entity** | Charanjit Lal | One company can be class |
| **Disaster-Specific** | Charan Lal Sahu | Bhopal Gas Act valid |

### Invalid Classification

**Anwar Ali Sarkar:** Special courts without reasonable classification - **Invalid**

---

## üö® **ARBITRARINESS & ARTICLE 14**

### Revolutionary Principle

> ‚öñÔ∏è **E.P. Royappa (1974):** "Arbitrary action = negation of equality"

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Bachan Singh (1982):** "Art. 14 strikes at arbitrariness"

### Administrative Discretion

**Unguided discretion = Unconstitutional**

**Air India v. Nergesh Meerza:** Unguided power to extend retirement age - **Invalid**

**Re Kerala Education Bill:** Discretion valid if policy deducible from statute

## üìú **ARTICLE 15: PROHIBITION OF DISCRIMINATION**

### Five Prohibited Grounds: **"RRC-SPB"**

Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Place of Birth

### Three Exceptions

| Exception | Provision | Added |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **Women & Children** | Art. 15(3) | 1950 |
| **Backward Classes** | Art. 15(4) | 1951 |
| **Educational Reservations** | Art. 15(5) | 2005 |

---

## üìú **ARTICLE 16: EQUALITY IN PUBLIC EMPLOYMENT**

### Seven Grounds: **"RRC-SDPR"**

Adds **Descent** and **Residence** to Art. 15 grounds

### Key Difference from Art. 14

| Article | Beneficiary | Scope |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **14** | All persons | All activities |
| **16** | Citizens only | Public employment |

---

## üéì **ARTICLE 16(4): RESERVATIONS**

### Text

> State may reserve appointments/posts for **backward class** not adequately represented

### Nature

**Mohan Kumar Singhania:** **Enabling provision** - not mandatory, no right to claim

---

### Quantum - 50% Rule

**Balaji v. Mysore (1963):** Reservation should be **less than 50%**

**Reason:** Otherwise becomes rule, not exception

---

### Carry Forward

**T. Devadasan (1964):** Carry forward exceeding 50% - **Invalid**

**A.B.S.K. Sangh (1981):** Carry forward valid for SC/ST even if reaches 66% (different reasoning)

---

### Promotion Reservations

**Rangachari (1962):** Reservations **valid in promotions** also, not just recruitment

---

## üìã **MANDAL COMMISSION CASE**

### üèõÔ∏è Indra Sawhney v. Union of India, AIR 1993

**Background:**
- Mandal Commission (1979) investigated OBC conditions
- Recommended **27% reservation** for OBCs
- V.P. Singh Govt (1990) accepted
- Nationwide agitation

### Key Holdings

| Principle | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **27% for OBC** | Valid (total 50% with SC/ST) |
| **Creamy Layer** | **Must be excluded** from OBC quota |
| **50% Rule** | Reaffirmed (except extraordinary cases) |
| **Caste Factor** | One factor, not sole factor |
| **Promotions** | No reservation in promotions to higher posts |
| **Backward Class** | = Socially + educationally backward |

### Creamy Layer

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Most important contribution:** Socially advanced members must be excluded

**Applicable to:** OBC only (not SC/ST per Mandal judgment)

---

### Evolution - N.M. Thomas Era (1976)

**N.M. Thomas:** Preferential treatment to SC/ST permissible under Art. 16(1) **outside Art. 16(4)**

**Marked:** New judicial thinking, greater concessions

---

## üìú **ARTICLE 17: ABOLITION OF UNTOUCHABILITY**

### Complete Abolition

- Practice in any form forbidden
- Contravention is offence
- **Horizontal application** - against State AND private parties

### Legislative Framework

| Act | Year | Purpose |
|-----|------|---------|
| **Protection of Civil Rights Act** | 1955 | Implement Art. 17 |
| **SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act** | 1989 | Prevent atrocities, special courts |

### Jai Singh v. UOI (1993)

> "Despite measures, SC/ST remain vulnerable to crimes, indignities, harassment"

---

## üìú **ARTICLE 18: ABOLITION OF TITLES**

### Four Provisions

| Clause | Prohibition |
|--------|-------------|
| **18(1)** | State cannot confer title (except military/academic) |
| **18(2)** | Citizens cannot accept from foreign state |
| **18(3)** | Foreigners holding office need President's consent |
| **18(4)** | Office holders cannot accept present/office from foreign state |

### National Awards

**Balaji Raghavan v. UOI (1996):** Bharat Ratna, Padma awards are **NOT titles** - Valid

**Rationale:** Merit should be recognized (Art. 51A(j) - strive for excellence)
