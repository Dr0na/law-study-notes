### Maneka Gandhi Revolution (1978)

**Five Key Principles:**

1. ‚öñÔ∏è **Arts. 14, 19, 21 NOT mutually exclusive** - interconnected
2. ‚öñÔ∏è **Procedure must be reasonable, fair, just** - not any procedure
3. ‚öñÔ∏è **Law must meet Art. 19 requirements**
4. ‚öñÔ∏è **Procedure must answer Art. 14** (not arbitrary)
5. ‚öñÔ∏è **Court can strike down law itself**

**Result:** **Due process introduced** (though word "due" absent)

### Criminal Justice Protections

**Fair Trial:**
- Free and fair trial sine qua non
- Investigation, trial, appeal all fair

**Speedy Trial:**
- Right under Art. 21
- Reasonable promptitude required
- No fixed time limit

**Legal Aid:**
- Khatri v. Bihar (II)
- State cannot plead inability

**Protection from Violence:**
- D.K. Basu (custodial violence)
- Sheela Barse (women in lock-up)
- Citizens for Democracy (no handcuffing)

**Compensation:**
- Rudul Shah (‚Çπ35,000 for 14 years)
- For illegal detention

## üìú **ARTICLE 22: ARREST & DETENTION**

### Four Safeguards - ICLP

| Safeguard | Article | Details |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **I** - Information | 22(1) | About grounds, as soon as may be |
| **C** - Consult | 22(2) | Right to consult lawyer |
| **L** - Lawyer | 22(2) | Right to be defended by lawyer |
| **P** - Production | 22(2) | Before magistrate within 24 hours |

**NOT applicable to:** Enemy aliens + Preventive detention

**Cases:** Shobharam (information), Joginder Kumar (justification), Gunpati (24 hours)

---

### Preventive Detention

**Definition:** Detention without trial to prevent future act

**Different from Conviction:**

| Aspect | Conviction | PD |
|--------|------------|-----|
| For | Past act | Future act |
| Trial | Yes | No |
| Proof | Beyond doubt | Subjective satisfaction |
| Purpose | Punishment | Prevention |

---

### Constitutional Framework

**Powers:**
- Entry 9, List I (Parliament exclusive) - defence, foreign affairs, security of India
- Entry 3, List III (Concurrent) - state security, public order, supplies

**Safeguards:**
- Art. 22(5): Communication of grounds, representation
- Art. 22(4): **Advisory Board within 3 months** (mandatory)

---

### PD Laws - NCP

1. **N** - NSA (National Security Act, 1980) - Current main law
2. **C** - COFEPOSA (1974) - Smuggling, foreign exchange
3. **P** - PBMSECA (1980) - Black marketing

**Plus State laws**

---

### Judicial Review of PD

**Limited scope:**
- Mala fides
- Non-existent grounds
- Procedural violations (Art. 22(4)-(7))

**Cannot examine:** Subjective satisfaction generally
