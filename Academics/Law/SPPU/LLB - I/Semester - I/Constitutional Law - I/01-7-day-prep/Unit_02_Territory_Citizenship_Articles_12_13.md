## üáÆüá≥ **TERRITORY OF INDIA (Arts. 1-4)**

**Article 1:** India = Union of States (not Federation)

**Articles 2-4:** Parliament can alter boundaries, admit new states

**Key:** States have no right to secede - Union is permanent

---

## üë• **CITIZENSHIP**

### Constitutional Provisions (At Commencement)

**Three types at Jan 26, 1950:**

| Type | Article | Requirements |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Domicile** | 5 | Domicile + (born OR parent born OR 5 years resident) |
| **Migration** | 6 | From Pakistan (before/after July 19, 1948) |
| **Registration** | 8 | Overseas Indians |

**Termination:** Art. 7 (migration to Pakistan)
**No Dual:** Art. 9 (voluntary foreign citizenship bars Indian)

---

### Citizenship Act, 1955

**5 Ways to Acquire - BDRNI:**
1. **Birth** (S. 3) - Born in India
2. **Descent** (S. 4) - Parent Indian
3. **Registration** (S. 5) - Specified categories
4. **Naturalisation** (S. 6) - Foreign nationals (conditions)
5. **Incorporation** (S. 7) - Territory joins India

**3 Ways to Lose - RCD:**
1. **Renunciation** (S. 8) - Voluntary
2. **Cessation** (S. 9) - Foreign citizenship (Central Govt determines)
3. **Deprivation** (S. 10) - 5 grounds with Committee

---

### Key Principles

‚úì **Single citizenship** (no state citizenship)
‚úì **No dual citizenship** (Art. 9)
‚úì **Overseas citizenship** (2003) - limited rights

**Case:** S. Nalini Srikaran (2007) - Born in India = citizen till Central Govt determines otherwise

## üìú **ARTICLE 12: DEFINITION OF "STATE"**

**For Part III (Fundamental Rights)**

**Includes:**
- Govt + Parliament of India
- State Govts + Legislatures
- Local authorities (municipalities, panchayats)
- **Other authorities**

---

### "Other Authority" Test

**Ajay Hasia v. Khalid Mujib, AIR 1981**

**Four Factors - SPFM:**
1. **S**tatutory creation
2. **P**ervasive state control
3. **F**unction - public
4. **M**oney - financial assistance

**Holistic approach** - totality of facts

**Examples held as State:**
- LIC, ONGC
- Statutory universities
- Airport Authority

---

## üìú **ARTICLE 13: LAWS INCONSISTENT WITH FRs**

**Principle:** Laws violating FRs are **void** to extent of inconsistency

### Two Types

| Type | Rule |
|------|------|
| **Pre-Constitution** | Art. 13(1) - Void to extent |
| **Post-Constitution** | Art. 13(2) - Cannot take away/abridge FRs |

**"Law" includes:** Acts, ordinances, rules, orders, customs

---

## üèõÔ∏è **BASIC STRUCTURE DOCTRINE**

**Most Important Constitutional Doctrine**

### Evolution - Three Key Cases

**1. Shankari Prasad (1951):**
- Amendment NOT "law" under Art. 13
- Parliament can amend FRs ‚úì

**2. Golak Nath (1967):**
- Amendment IS "law" under Art. 13
- Parliament CANNOT amend FRs ‚úó (overruled)

**3. Kesavananda Bharati (1973):**
- Amendment NOT "law"
- **BUT cannot destroy BASIC STRUCTURE** ‚úì (current law)

---

### Basic Features - SURFS-JRP

**Remember these 8:**
1. **S**upremacy of Constitution
2. **U**nity and integrity
3. **R**ule of Law
4. **F**ederal character
5. **S**ecular character
6. **J**udicial review
7. **R**epublican democracy
8. **P**arliamentary system

**Others:** Independence of judiciary, Separation of powers, FRs (especially 14, 19, 21), Balance between FRs and DPs

---

### Implications

‚úì Even constitutional amendments can be struck down
‚úì Judicial review of amendments
‚úì Core values protected
‚úì Flexibility with stability

**Significance:** Greatest contribution of Indian judiciary

## üîÑ **IMPORTANT DOCTRINES**

### 1. Doctrine of Eclipse

**For:** Pre-Constitution laws only

**Principle:**
- Not dead, just eclipsed
- Can revive if Constitution amended
- Bhikaji case

---

### 2. Doctrine of Severability

**Principle:**
- Invalid part severed
- Valid part survives
- If separable

**Test:** Can parts be separated? Is remainder complete?

---

### 3. Waiver of FRs

**General Rule:** FRs cannot be waived

**Exception:** Some can (Arts. 32, 226 - right to approach court)

**Cannot waive:** Rights with public element

**Case:** Basheshar Nath v. CIT
