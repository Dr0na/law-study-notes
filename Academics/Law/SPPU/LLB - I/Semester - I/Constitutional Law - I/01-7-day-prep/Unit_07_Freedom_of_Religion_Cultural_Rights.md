## üìú **ARTICLE 25: INDIVIDUAL FREEDOM**

**Text:** "All persons equally entitled to freedom of conscience and right to profess, practice and propagate religion"

### Three Ps

1. **Profess** - Declare religious beliefs outwardly
2. **Practice** - Perform religious worship, rituals
3. **Propagate** - Spread religion, convince others (NOT forced conversion)

**Subject to:** Public order, morality, health

---

### Essential Religious Practices Doctrine

**Commissioner HRE v. Lakshmindra, AIR 1954:**

**Principle:**
- Religion has essential and non-essential practices
- Only **essential** practices protected
- Non-essential can be regulated/prohibited

**Test:**
- Is practice essential and integral to religion?
- Can religion exist without it?

**Applications:**

| Practice | Essential? | Case | Result |
|----------|-----------|------|--------|
| Namaz (prayer) | ‚úì Yes | - | Protected |
| Mosque building | ‚úó No | Ismail Faruqui 1994 | Can be regulated |
| Polygamy | ‚úó No | Ratilal Panachand 1954 | Can be prohibited |
| Excommunication | ‚úì Yes | Sardar Syedna 1962 | Protected (Bohras) |

**Purpose:** Enable social reform while protecting religion

## üìú **ARTICLE 26: DENOMINATIONAL RIGHTS**

**For:** Religious denominations (group with common beliefs, organization, name)

### Four Rights - EMOA

1. **Establish** institutions (religious/charitable)
2. **Manage** own affairs in matters of religion
3. **Own** and acquire property
4. **Administer** property (per law)

**Subject to:** Public order, morality, health

---

### Key Case: Durgah Committee, Ajmer (1961)

**Principle:**
- Property administration is **secular** activity
- State can regulate financial management
- But cannot interfere with religious practices

**Balance:** Regulation of secular aspects ‚úì | Interference in religion ‚úó

---

## üìú **ARTICLES 27-28: TAX & INSTRUCTION**

**Article 27:** No compulsion to pay tax specifically for promoting particular religion

**Article 28(1):** No religious instruction in **wholly State-funded** institutions

**Article 28(2):** Exception - State-administered but **endowment/trust** established

**Article 28(3):** State-recognized/aided - religious instruction allowed with **consent**

---

## üîë **SECULARISM**

**Added to Preamble:** 42nd Amendment, 1976

**S.R. Bommai v. UOI (1994):** Secularism is **basic feature**

### Indian vs Western Secularism

| Aspect | Western | Indian |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Approach** | Separation of Church-State | Principled distance |
| **State Role** | Passive | Active (for reform) |
| **Concept** | Negative | Positive |

**Indian secularism:**
- No state religion
- Equal respect to all
- State can regulate secular aspects
- Positive engagement for social reform

## üéì **CULTURAL & EDUCATIONAL RIGHTS (ARTS. 29-30)**

### Article 29 - Any Section

**29(1):** Any section with distinct language/script/culture ‚Üí Right to **conserve**

**29(2):** No discrimination in admission (State/aided institutions) on religion, race, caste, language

**Beneficiary:** ANY section (not just minorities)

---

### Article 30 - Minorities Only

**30(1):** Minorities can **establish and administer** educational institutions

**30(2):** State cannot discriminate in granting aid

---

### Who is Minority?

**Two types:**
1. Religious minority
2. Linguistic minority

**Determined at:** **State level** (not national level)

**Example:**
- Muslims minority in most States
- Hindus minority in Punjab, Meghalaya
- Linguistic minorities vary by State

---

## üèõÔ∏è **T.M.A. PAI FOUNDATION v. KARNATAKA (2005)**

**Most comprehensive judgment on minority rights**

### Key Holdings

**1. Minority Status:**
- Determined at State level

**2. Autonomy:**
- Extensive autonomy for minority institutions
- Can admit students
- Can set reasonable fees
- Can appoint staff

**3. Limitations:**
- Must maintain educational excellence
- No capitation fee
- Transparent admissions
- Cannot be exploited for profit

**4. State Regulation:**
- Can ensure academic standards
- Can prescribe teacher qualifications
- Cannot take over management
- Cannot destroy minority character

**5. Private Unaided:**
- Greater autonomy than aided
- Must still be fair and transparent

---

### Other Important Cases

**St. Stephen's College (1992):**
- 50% preference to Christians valid
- Admission based on merit within community

**Pa. Inamdar (2005):**
- Unaided minority institutions have autonomy
- But transparent procedure needed
- No capitation fee
