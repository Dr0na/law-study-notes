### 1. Constituent Assembly

**Outline:**
- Formation: Cabinet Mission 1946
- President: Dr. Rajendra Prasad
- Drafting Chairman: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar
- Time: 2 years, 11 months, 18 days
- Adopted: Nov 26, 1949
- Commenced: Jan 26, 1950

### 2. Preamble

**Outline:**
- Text (SSSDR + JLEF)
- Legal status: Part of Constitution (Kesavananda)
- Not enforceable but aids interpretation
- Can be amended (42nd Amendment)
- Reflects basic structure

---

### 3. Salient Features of Constitution

**Outline:**
- Lengthiest written
- Parliamentary system
- Quasi-federal
- Fundamental Rights + Directive Principles
- Independent Judiciary
- Judicial Review
- Single citizenship
- Universal adult franchise

---

### 4. Dr. Ambedkar's Warnings

**Outline:**
- Final speech (Nov 25, 1949)
- Three warnings:
  1. Social democracy needed
  2. Life of contradictions
  3. Avoid hero worship, grammar of anarchy
- Relevance today

---

### 5. Constitutionalism

**Outline:**
- Limited government under law
- Rule of Law
- Separation of Powers
- Judicial Review
- FR protection
- Different from just having Constitution

---

### 6. Quasi-Federal Structure

**Outline:**
- Federal features (5-6 points)
- Unitary features (5-6 points)
- Why called quasi-federal
- "Federation with strong unitary bias"

- Unlike USA (dual)
- Only Indian citizenship
- No state citizenship
- Reasons: Unity, no discrimination
- Arts. 5-11

### 8. Ways to Acquire Citizenship (BDRNI)

### 9. Loss of Citizenship (RCD)

- Deprivation (S. 10) - 5 grounds
- Procedure for deprivation

### 10. Overseas Citizenship

**Outline:**
- Introduced 2003
- Ss. 7A, 7B
- For persons of Indian origin
- Except Pakistan, Bangladesh
- Can be cancelled (S. 7D)
- Different from full citizenship

---

### 11. Article 12 - Definition of State

**Outline:**
- For Part III
- Components
- "Other authorities"
- Ajay Hasia test (SPFM)
- Why important

---

### 12. Basic Structure Doctrine

**Outline:**
- Kesavananda Bharati 1973
- Parliament can amend but cannot destroy basic structure
- Basic features: SURFS-JRP
- Evolution: Shankari Prasad ‚Üí Golak Nath ‚Üí Kesavananda
- Significance: Greatest contribution of judiciary

---

### 13. Doctrine of Eclipse

**Outline:**
- Pre-Constitution laws
- Eclipsed, not dead
- Can be revived if Constitution amended
- Bhikaji case
- Applicability only to pre-1950 laws

---

### 14. Doctrine of Severability

**Outline:**
- Invalid part severed
- Valid part survives
- If separable
- A.K. Gopalan case
- Purpose: Save valid portions

## üìö **MODULE 03: RIGHT TO EQUALITY**

### 15. Reasonable Classification

**Outline:**
- Art. 14 permits
- I-R test (two-fold)
- Intelligible differentia
- Rational nexus with object
- Examples: Geographical, procedural, single entity
- Burden on challenger

---

### 16. Article 14 Strikes at Arbitrariness

**Outline:**
- E.P. Royappa case
- Bachan Singh
- Arbitrary = negation of equality
- Modern interpretation of Art. 14
- Administrative discretion (Air India v. Nergesh)

---

### 17. Administrative Discrimination

**Outline:**
- Statute valid but implementation discriminatory
- Vellore Trust case
- Ajay Hasia case
- Ramana Shetty case
- Arbitrary action quashed

---

### 18. Compassionate Appointment

**Outline:**
- V. Sivamurthy case
- Not a right
- Exception to equality
- To relieve sudden financial crisis
- Vacancy must exist
- Indian Bank v. K. Usha (don't delay)

---

### 19. Article 15(4)

**Outline:**
- Added 1951
- Special provisions for backward classes
- SC, ST
- Protective discrimination
- Affirmative action

---

### 20. 50% Rule in Reservations

**Outline:**
- Balaji v. Mysore, 1963
- Reservation should be < 50%
- Otherwise rule becomes exception
- Reaffirmed in Indra Sawhney
- Extraordinary circumstances exception

---

### 21. Creamy Layer

**Outline:**
- Indra Sawhney 1993
- Socially advanced members excluded
- Only for OBC (not SC/ST)
- Government frames criteria
- Economic + social factors
- Purpose: Benefit truly backward

---

### 22. Mandal Commission

**Outline:**
- Appointed 1979 (Art. 340)
- Investigate OBC conditions
- Found 52% population
- Recommended 27% reservation
- V.P. Singh govt accepted (1990)
- Indra Sawhney case (1993)

---

### 23. Carry Forward Rule

**Outline:**
- Unfilled reserved posts
- Carried to next year
- T. Devadasan (>50% invalid)
- A.B.S.K. Sangh (for SC/ST valid)
- With 50% ceiling in any year

---

### 24. N.M. Thomas Case

**Outline:**
- Kerala case (1976)
- Preferential treatment to SC/ST
- Outside Art. 16(4) also valid
- New judicial thinking
- Greater concessions era began

---

### 25. Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955

**Outline:**
- Originally: Untouchability (Offences) Act
- Renamed 1976
- Implements Art. 17
- Prescribes punishments
- Horizontal application

---

### 26. SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989

**Outline:**
- Objectives: Prevent atrocities, special courts, rehabilitation
- Jai Singh v. UOI
- SC/ST remain vulnerable
- Despite constitutional provisions
- Special protection needed

## üìö **MODULE 04: RIGHT TO FREEDOM I**

### 27. Freedom of Press

**Outline:**
- Not expressly mentioned
- Implied in Art. 19(1)(a)
- Romesh Thappar case
- Bennett Coleman case
- Prior restraint generally not allowed
- Exceptions

---

### 28. Reasonable Restrictions (Art. 19)

**Outline:**
- Each freedom has restriction clause
- Two-part test: Procedural + Substantive
- Must be reasonable
- Proportionality
- Courts decide
- Chintaman Rao case

---

### 29. Section 66A of IT Act

**Outline:**
- Criminalized offensive messages
- Vague terms
- Shreya Singhal v. UOI, 2015
- Struck down as unconstitutional
- Violated Art. 19(1)(a)
- Chilling effect on free speech

---

### 30. Right to Property (Present Position)

**Outline:**
- Originally Art. 19(1)(f) + 31
- Deleted by 44th Amendment, 1978
- Now Art. 300A (constitutional right, not FR)
- Not enforceable under Art. 32
- Compensation not constitutionally required
- Special provisions: 31A, 31B, 31C exist

---

### 31. Article 31A

**Outline:**
- Saving of laws
- Estates, tenancy abolition
- Land reforms
- Cannot be challenged under Arts. 14, 19
- Added 1951

---

### 32. Ninth Schedule (Article 31B)

**Outline:**
- Laws in Ninth Schedule protected
- From judicial review
- Originally 13 laws
- Now 280+ laws
- Purpose: Protect land reforms
- Basic Structure limits (Kesavananda)

---

### 33. Article 31C

**Outline:**
- Laws for Arts. 39(b), (c)
- Cannot be challenged under Arts. 14, 19
- Originally all DPs (struck down)
- Now limited to 39(b), (c)
- Minerva Mills case

---

## üìö **MODULE 05: RIGHT TO FREEDOM II**

- Gopalan rejected (1950)
- Maneka Gandhi introduced (1978)
- Through backdoor
- Procedure must be reasonable, fair, just
- Similar to US now

### 35. Personal Liberty (Art. 21)

**Outline:**
- Gopalan: Narrow (physical restraint)
- Maneka: Broad (expansive)
- Multiple implied rights (LEPSH-VE)
- Most expansive FR
- "Heart of FRs"

### 36. Right to Livelihood

**Outline:**
- Olga Tellis case
- Implied from Art. 21
- No life without means of living
- Pavement dwellers
- Street vendors protection
- Link with Art. 39(a)

---

### 37. Right to Privacy

**Outline:**
- Not express
- Implied from Art. 21
- Constitutional status
- Telephone tapping regulated
- Puttaswamy case (recent)

---

### 38. Speedy Trial

**Outline:**
- Abdul Rehman Antulay case
- Part of Art. 21
- No fixed time limit
- Reasonable promptitude
- Covers all stages
- Long pendency violates Art. 21

---

### 39. Legal Aid

**Outline:**
- State must provide
- Khatri v. Bihar (II)
- Cannot plead financial inability
- Constitutional obligation
- Art. 39A (DP)
- Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987

---

### 40. Custodial Violence

**Outline:**
- D.K. Basu v. West Bengal
- Strikes at Rule of Law
- Guidelines issued
- Memorandum of arrest
- Inform relative
- Medical examination
- Art. 21 violated

---

### 41. Preventive Detention

**Outline:**
- Detention without trial
- Prevent future act
- Different from conviction
- Arts. 22(3) to (7)
- Entry 9, List I; Entry 3, List III
- Limited judicial review

---

### 42. National Security Act (NSA), 1980

**Outline:**
- Preventive detention law
- Grounds: Defence, security, public order, supplies
- Safeguards: Art. 22(4), (5)
- Advisory Board within 3 months
- Strict compliance required

---

### 43. Advisory Board (Art. 22(4))

**Outline:**
- For preventive detention beyond 3 months
- Must report within 3 months
- If not, detention illegal
- Composition
- Quasi-judicial function

## üìö **MODULE 08: REMEDIES**

### 53. Habeas Corpus

**Outline:**
- "Produce the body"
- Release from illegal detention
- Most important writ
- Against public and private
- Rudul Shah (compensation)
- A.D.M. Jabalpur (Emergency)

---

### 54. Mandamus

**Outline:**
- "We command"
- Command to perform public duty
- Against public authorities
- Requirements: Legal right, legal duty, public duty
- Cannot against President/Governor
- Examples

---

### 55. Certiorari

**Outline:**
- "To be certified"
- Quash order
- After decision
- Against lower courts/tribunals
- Grounds: Jurisdiction, natural justice
- Province of Bombay case

---

### 56. Prohibition

**Outline:**
- "Forbid"
- Prevent from acting
- Before decision
- Against lower courts/tribunals
- Preventive in nature
- vs Certiorari (curative)

---

### 57. Quo Warranto

**Outline:**
- "By what authority"
- Question authority to hold office
- Public office only
- Substantive, not temporary
- University of Mysore case

---

### 58. Public Interest Litigation

**Outline:**
- S.P. Gupta 1982
- Liberalized locus standi
- Any public-spirited person
- For poor/disadvantaged
- Key cases: Bandhua, M.C. Mehta, Vishaka
- Limitations: Not for private interest

---

### 59. Article 32 as Basic Feature

**Outline:**
- Minerva Mills v. UOI, 1980
- Cannot be taken away
- Even by constitutional amendment
- Judicial review essential
- Right to have rights enforced
