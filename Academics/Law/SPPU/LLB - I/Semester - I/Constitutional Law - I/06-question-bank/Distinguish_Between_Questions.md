## ‚öñÔ∏è **EQUALITY & DISCRIMINATION**

### Q1. Distinguish between Articles 14 and 15

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Article 14 | Article 15 |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Beneficiary** | All persons | Citizens only |
| **Scope** | All state activities | Specific discrimination situations |
| **Nature** | General equality clause | Specific anti-discrimination provision |
| **Grounds** | No specific grounds | 5 specific grounds (RRC-SPB) |
| **Test** | Reasonable classification (I-R) | Prohibited grounds listed |
| **Exceptions** | Reasonable classification | Arts. 15(3), (4), (5) |
| **Application** | Universal | Narrows down Art. 14 for specific situations |

**Relationship:** Art. 15 is specific application of Art. 14 principle.

---

### Q2. Distinguish between Articles 14 and 16

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Article 14 | Article 16 |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Beneficiary** | All persons | Citizens only |
| **Scope** | All activities | Public employment only |
| **Grounds** | General | 7 specific (RRC-SDPR) |
| **Nature** | Broad | Narrow (specific to jobs) |
| **Exceptions** | Reasonable classification | Arts. 16(3), (4), (4A), (4B) |
| **Application** | Horizontal | Vertical |

**Case:** Articles 16(1) and 16(2) give effect to Arts. 14 and 15. They form part of same constitutional code.

---

### Q3. Distinguish between Articles 15 and 16

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Article 15 | Article 16 |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Scope** | General (all state activities) | Public employment only |
| **Grounds** | 5 (RRC-SPB) | 7 (adds Descent, Residence) |
| **Reservations** | Art. 15(4), (5) for education | Art. 16(4) for jobs |
| **Application** | Wider in scope | Narrower (jobs only) |

---

### Q4. Distinguish between "Equality before law" and "Equal protection of laws"

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Equality Before Law | Equal Protection of Laws |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Nature** | Negative concept | Positive concept |
| **Meaning** | No special privilege | Equals treated equally |
| **Principle** | No one above law | Like treated alike |
| **Origin** | British (Dicey's Rule of Law) | American |
| **Action** | Prohibits discrimination | Requires affirmative action |

**Sri Srinivasa Theatre case:** Both have different meanings though much in common.

## üó£Ô∏è **FREEDOM PROVISIONS**

### Q5. Distinguish between Articles 19 and 21

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Article 19 | Article 21 |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Beneficiary** | Citizens only | All persons |
| **Rights** | Six specific freedoms | Life and personal liberty |
| **Scope** | Specific freedoms listed | Broad, expansive |
| **Restrictions** | Reasonable restrictions (grounds specified) | Procedure established by law |
| **Originally** | Independent of Art. 21 (Gopalan) | Independent of Art. 19 (Gopalan) |
| **Currently** | Interconnected (Maneka Gandhi) | Interconnected (Maneka Gandhi) |

**Maneka Gandhi:** Arts. 19 and 21 must be read together. Procedure under Art. 21 must satisfy Art. 19.

---

### Q6. Distinguish between Gopalan and Maneka Gandhi cases

**Answer:**

| Aspect | A.K. Gopalan (1950) | Maneka Gandhi (1978) |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Article 21 Scope** | Narrow - physical restraint only | Broad - numerous rights |
| **Arts. 19,21,22** | Mutually exclusive | Interconnected (NOT exclusive) |
| **Procedure** | Any procedure sufficient | Must be reasonable, fair, just |
| **Due Process** | Rejected | **Introduced** |
| **Natural Justice** | Not required in law | Integral part of Art. 14 & 21 |
| **Judicial Review** | Limited | Expanded |
| **Status** | **OVERRULED** | **Current law** |

**Significance:** Maneka practically overruled Gopalan. Revolutionary change in interpretation.

### Q7. Distinguish between Arrest and Preventive Detention

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Arrest | Preventive Detention |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| **For** | Past act (alleged offence) | Prevent future act |
| **Trial** | Yes, trial follows | **No trial** |
| **Proof** | Must be proved in court | Subjective satisfaction |
| **Purpose** | Prosecution and punishment | Prevention |
| **Safeguards** | Art. 22(1) & (2) - ICLP | Art. 22(3) to (7) |
| **Production** | Before magistrate (24 hours) | Not required |
| **Duration** | Pending trial | Up to maximum in law |
| **Advisory Board** | Not required | Within 3 months mandatory |

## üö´ **EXPLOITATION PROVISIONS**

### Q8. Distinguish between Articles 23 and 24

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Article 23 | Article 24 |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Age** | All ages | Below 14 years only |
| **Prohibition** | TBF (Traffic, Begar, Forced) | FMH (Factory, Mine, Hazardous) |
| **Nature** | Complete ban | Partial ban |
| **Scope** | Any forced labour | Only specified employments |
| **Exception** | Art. 23(2) - compulsory service for public purpose | None |
| **Acts** | BIMEC (5 acts) | FMAC (4 acts) |
| **Watershed** | 1982 (Asiad) | 1982 (Asiad) |
| **Key Case** | Bandhua Mukti Morcha | M.C. Mehta (Sivakasi) |

**Common:** Both have horizontal application (enforceable against everyone)

### Q9. Distinguish between Conviction and Preventive Detention

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Conviction | Preventive Detention |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| **Basis** | Past act (offence committed) | Prevent future act |
| **Trial** | Court trial required | No trial |
| **Evidence** | Proof beyond reasonable doubt | Subjective satisfaction of authority |
| **Purpose** | Punishment for crime | Prevention of undesirable act |
| **Duration** | Fixed by court (after conviction) | Maximum period in law |
| **Rights** | Full trial rights | Limited safeguards (Art. 22(4)-(7)) |
| **Review** | Appeal to higher courts | Limited judicial review |

**Quote:** "In preventive detention, past act is merely material for inference about future probable conduct."

## ‚öñÔ∏è **FUNDAMENTAL PROVISIONS**

### Q12. Distinguish between Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles

**Answer:**

| Aspect | FRs (Part III) | DPs (Part IV) |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Nature** | Justiciable | Non-justiciable |
| **Enforcement** | Through courts | Through legislature |
| **Binding** | Legally binding on State | Morally and politically binding |
| **Purpose** | Political democracy | Economic & social democracy |
| **Character** | Negative (State not to do) | Positive (State to do) |
| **Source** | USA Constitution | Irish Constitution |
| **Implementation** | Immediate | Progressive |
| **Resources** | No resource constraint | Require resources |

**Relationship:**
- **Champakam (1951):** FRs prevail
- **Minerva Mills (1980):** **Complementary**, balance is basic structure

---

### Q13. Distinguish between Fundamental Rights and Fundamental Duties

**Answer:**

| Aspect | FRs | FDs |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Nature** | Justiciable | Non-justiciable |
| **Binding** | State bound | Citizens' moral obligation |
| **Part** | Part III | Part IVA (Art. 51A) |
| **Added** | 1950 | 1976 (10) + 2002 (1) |
| **Count** | Multiple articles | 11 duties |
| **Override** | Can override ordinary law | Cannot override FRs |

**Purpose:** Both together create balanced framework

---

### Q14. Distinguish between Article 32 and Article 226

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Art. 32 (SC) | Art. 226 (HC) |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| **Nature** | Fundamental Right itself | Power of High Court |
| **Scope** | Only Fundamental Rights | FRs + any other legal right |
| **Territorial** | Throughout India | Within HC jurisdiction |
| **Beneficiary** | Citizens (guaranteed right) | Anyone (but discretionary) |
| **Suspension** | Can be suspended (Art. 359) | Cannot be suspended |
| **Discretion** | Cannot refuse if FR violated | Can refuse (discretionary) |
| **Alternative Remedy** | No bar | Court may refuse if alternative available |
| **Appeals** | To same court (review) | To Supreme Court |

**Romesh Thappar:** Petitioner can choose either, but not both simultaneously.

## üèõÔ∏è **LANDMARK CASES**

### Q15. Distinguish between Kesavananda Bharati and Golak Nath cases

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Golak Nath (1967) | Kesavananda Bharati (1973) |
|--------|------------------|--------------------------|
| **Issue** | Can Parliament amend FRs? | Can Parliament amend FRs? |
| **Held** | Amendment is "law" under Art. 13 | Amendment NOT "law" under Art. 13 |
| **Conclusion** | Parliament CANNOT amend FRs | Parliament CAN amend FRs |
| **New Doctrine** | None | **Basic Structure Doctrine** |
| **Limitation** | Complete prohibition | Cannot destroy basic structure |
| **Status** | **Overruled** by Kesavananda | **Current law** |

**Significance:** Kesavananda more balanced - allows amendment but protects core.

---

### Q16. Distinguish between Balaji and Indra Sawhney cases

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Balaji (1963) | Indra Sawhney (1993) |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Issue** | Reservation quantum | OBC reservations |
| **Facts** | 68% reservation in education | 27% reservation for OBCs |
| **Holding** | 68% invalid, should be < 50% | 27% valid |
| **Principle Laid** | **50% rule** | **Creamy layer exclusion** |
| **Applied to** | Reservations generally | Specifically OBCs |
| **Result** | Struck down | Upheld with conditions |

**Both establish:** Reservation must be reasonable and not excessive.

## üîê **WRITS**

### Q18. Distinguish between Habeas Corpus and Mandamus

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Habeas Corpus | Mandamus |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| **Meaning** | Produce the body | We command |
| **Purpose** | Release from illegal detention | Command to perform duty |
| **Against** | Public or private persons | Public authorities only |
| **Subject** | Personal liberty | Public duty |
| **When** | Any time during detention | When duty not performed |
| **Case** | Rudul Shah | Rajasthan v. UOI |

### Q19. Distinguish between Prohibition and Certiorari

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Prohibition | Certiorari |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Purpose** | Prevent excess | Quash order |
| **Stage** | **Before decision** | **After decision** |
| **Nature** | Preventive | Curative |
| **Effect** | Stops proceedings | Quashes completed order |
| **Against** | Lower courts/tribunals | Lower courts/tribunals |
| **Grounds** | Excess/lack of jurisdiction | Jurisdiction + natural justice |

**Both** issued to inferior courts/tribunals for jurisdictional issues.

---

### Q20. Distinguish between Mandamus and Prohibition

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Mandamus | Prohibition |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Nature** | Command to do | Command not to do |
| **Purpose** | Performance of duty | Stop from acting |
| **Direction** | Positive (do it) | Negative (don't do it) |
| **Against** | Any public authority | Only courts/tribunals |
| **Duty** | Must be clear public duty | Jurisdictional excess |

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Constitution | Constitutionalism |
|--------|--------------|------------------|
| **Nature** | Document | Philosophy/Approach |
| **Meaning** | Written law of land | Limited government under law |
| **Presence** | Every country has | Not every country has |
| **Function** | Creates structure | Limits power |
| **Example** | USSR had Constitution | USSR lacked constitutionalism |

### Q22. Distinguish between Federal and Unitary

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Federal | Unitary |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Powers** | Divided (Centre + States) | All with Centre |
| **Constitution** | Written | May be written/unwritten |
| **Governments** | Two or more | One |
| **Rigidity** | Generally rigid | Generally flexible |
| **Example** | USA | UK |

**India:** **Quasi-federal** - federal structure with unitary spirit

### Q23. Distinguish between Parliamentary and Presidential systems

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Parliamentary (India) | Presidential (USA) |
|--------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Executive** | PM (real) + President (nominal) | President (real + nominal) |
| **Responsibility** | To Legislature | To electorate |
| **Tenure** | Indefinite (pleasure of House) | Fixed (4 years) |
| **Dissolution** | House can be dissolved | No dissolution |
| **Separation** | Executive from legislature | Complete separation |
| **Ministers** | From legislature | Not from legislature |

## üìú **CLASSIFICATION & TESTS**

### Q24. Distinguish between Class Legislation and Classification

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Class Legislation | Classification |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| **Nature** | Arbitrary discrimination | Reasonable differentiation |
| **Basis** | No intelligible differentia | Intelligible differentia exists |
| **Nexus** | No rational nexus | Rational nexus with object |
| **Validity** | Prohibited by Art. 14 | Permitted by Art. 14 |

**Art. 14 forbids class legislation but permits reasonable classification.**

### Q25. Distinguish between Fundamental Right and Legal Right

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Fundamental Right | Legal Right |
|--------|------------------|-------------|
| **Source** | Constitution (Part III) | Statute or common law |
| **Amendment** | Only by constitutional amendment | By ordinary law |
| **Protection** | Against State mainly | Against all |
| **Enforcement** | Art. 32 (SC), Art. 226 (HC) | Ordinary courts |
| **Nature** | Higher status | Ordinary status |
| **Example** | Arts. 14, 19, 21 | Contract rights, property rights |

**After 1978:** Property is legal right, not FR.

## üìä **RIGHTS COMPARISON**

### Q28. Distinguish between Right to Life (Art. 21) and Right to Livelihood

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Right to Life | Right to Livelihood |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Source** | Express in Art. 21 | **Implied** from Art. 21 |
| **Scope** | Life and personal liberty | Means of living |
| **Protection** | Direct constitutional | Derived protection |
| **Case** | Maneka Gandhi | Olga Tellis |

**Olga Tellis:** Right to livelihood **born out of** right to life. No person can live without means of living.

### Q29. Distinguish between Right to Education under Art. 45 and Art. 21A

**Answer:**

| Aspect | Art. 45 (Original) | Art. 21A (Current) |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Nature** | Directive Principle | Fundamental Right |
| **Status** | Non-justiciable | Justiciable |
| **Age** | Up to 14 years | **6 to 14 years** |
| **Current** | Modified (now up to 6 years) | Added 2002 |
| **Enforceable** | Through legislation | Through court (Art. 32) |

**Connection:** Art. 21A fulfills Art. 45's objective.
