### 1. Development and Nature of Muslim Law

**ðŸ“œ Historical Development**
- **Pre-British**: Mughal Emperors (Hanafis) administered Hanafi law
- **British Rule**: Applied as personal law according to school
- **Modern**: Shariat Act, 1937 made it uniform

**ðŸ‘¤ Who is a Muslim?**
- Professes: "La ilahi ill lil lah Mohammad ur Rasul Allah"
- Belief in unity of God + mission of Muhammad
- **Sufficient**: Profession alone (no rites required)

**ðŸ‘¥ Application**
- **Personal law** (not territorial)
- Applies only to **Muslims** (by birth or conversion)
- **Matters**: Marriage, Divorce, Dower, Inheritance, Wakf, etc.

**ðŸ“œ Shariat Application Act, 1937**

**Section 2 (Mandatory - Coercive)**
- Applies to: Intestate succession, Marriage, Divorce, Maintenance, Dower, Guardianship, Gift, Wakf
- **Abrogates custom** in favor of Shariat
- **Excluded**: Agricultural land, Pre-emption

**Section 3 (Optional - Persuasive)**
- Declaration by competent Muslim
- Applies to: Adoption, Wills, Legacies
- Effect: Applies to declarant + minor children + descendants

**Key Differences: Section 2 vs Section 3**

| Aspect | Section 2 | Section 3 |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Nature** | Coercive (mandatory) | Persuasive (optional) |
| **Purpose** | Abrogate customs | Provide option to abandon customs |
| **Effect on Customs** | Invalidates customs | Does not invalidate, only provides option |
| **Appeal** | Not applicable | Available if authority refuses |

**Important Case Laws:**
- **Noor Jahan v. E Tiscenko (AIR 1941 Cal 582)**: Shariat Act applies only if **both parties are Muslims**
- **Ashraf Ali v. Mohammed Ali (AIR 1947 Bom 122)**: Testamentary trusts covered by Section 2

**ðŸ”„ Effect of Conversion**

**From Islam (Apostasy)**:
- **Succession**: Freedom of Religion Act, 1850 - convert does NOT lose inheritance
- **Marriage**: 
  - Husband's conversion = dissolved **ipso facto**
  - Wife's conversion = depends (born Muslim vs converted)

**To Islam**:
- Renounces former religion
- Substitutes Muslim law
- **Condition**: Must be **bonafide** (not colorable)

### 2. Schools of Muslim Law

**â˜ªï¸ Sunni Schools**

| School | Founder | Period (AH) | Status in India |
|--------|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Hanafi** | Abu Hanafee | 80-150 | **Most important** (majority) |

**Key Points**:
- **Presumption**: Sunni = Hanafi (unless shown otherwise)
- **Case**: Baftun v. Bilaiti Khanun (1903) - presumption that parties are Sunnis
- **Maliki**: Shorter waiting period (2 years vs 7 years for divorce)
- **Authoritative Book (Hanafi)**: Hedaya (by Ali bin Abu Baker al Marghinani)

**â˜ªï¸ Shia Schools**

| School | Status | Area |
|--------|--------|------|
| **Ithana Ashari** | **Most dominant** | Iraq, Iran, India |
| **Ismailis** | Two groups | Mumbai (Khojas, Bohoras) |
| **Zaiday** | None in India | South Arabia (Yemen) |

**Key Points**:
- **Presumption**: Shia = Ithana Ashari
- **Case**: Akbar ali v. Mohammad Ali (1932) - presumption that Shia is Ithana Ashari
- **Ithana Ashari**: Most Shia Muslims in India

**âš–ï¸ Applicability Rules**

| Situation | Law Applicable |
|-----------|----------------|
| Same school | Law of that school |
| Different schools | **Law of defendant** |
| Change of school | Law changes with school |
| Succession | Law at time of death |

**ðŸ›ï¸ Other Schools**

**Ibadi School**:
- Neither Shia nor Sunni
- Geographic Spread: Oman
- Key Feature: Provides principal consideration to **Ijtihad** (personal reasoning)

**Ahmadiya School**:
- **NOT recognized as Muslims** by any Muslim government
- Do NOT follow Prophet Muhammad
- Recent origin (19th century)
- Founder: Mirza Ghulam Khadiani

**ðŸ“– Quran**
- **Period**: 610-632 AD (23 years)
- **Structure**: 114 suras, 6,236 ayahs
- **Legal verses**: 200 total
  - 70 family/inheritance
  - 70 obligations
  - 30 criminal
  - 20 procedure
- **Periods**: Meccan (theological) and Medinan (legal, political, social)

**ðŸ“– Sunna (Traditions)**
- **Meaning**: "Path" (practice of Prophet)
- **Hadith**: Report of Prophet's Sunna
- **First Collection**: Mu-atha of Imam Malik (2,000 traditions from 10,000)
- **Relationship with Quran**: Explains and implements Quranic principles

**ðŸ¤ Ijma'a**
- **Meaning**: Consensus of all jurists
- **Authority**: 
  - Hadith: "My people will never agree on what is wrong"
  - Quran: "To be followed is the path of the men of faith"
- **Key**: **Cannot be repealed** once established
- **First Instance**: Election of first Khaleefa Abu Bakr
- **According to Imam Shafie**: Legal knowledge derived from Ijma'a

**ðŸ” Quiyas**
- **Meaning**: Analogy (known to unknown)
- **Nature**: Does NOT create new law, only applies existing principles
- **Function**: Principle of interpretation
- **Other Principles**: Ijithihad (Personal reasoning), Isthihsan (Juristic preference), Ikhthilaf (Disagreement)

**âš–ï¸ Judicial Decisions (Fatawas)**
- Opinions of judges and Muftis
- **Status**: **NOT binding** (only persuasive)
- **Case**: Vishwa Lochan Madan v. Union of India (2014) - Fatawas are NOT binding
- **Famous Collection**: Fatawa-i-Alamgiri (compiled during Aurangzeb's time)

**ðŸ“œ Legislation**
- Key Acts:
  - Kazis Act, 1880
  - Guardian and Wards Act, 1890
  - Musalman Wakf Validating Act, 1923, 1930
  - **Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937**
  - **Dissolution of Muslim Marriage Act, 1939**
  - Wakf Act, 1954
  - **Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986**
  - **Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019** (Triple Talaq)

**âš–ï¸ Justice, Equity and Good Conscience**
- Applied when conflict of opinion or no specific rule
- **Case**: Aziz Bano v. Muhammad (1925) - rules of equity could be applied
- **Historical**: Abu Hanifa called this **"Isthihsan"** (Juristic Preference)
