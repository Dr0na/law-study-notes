## üìå UNIT 1: INDIAN CHRISTIAN MARRIAGE ACT, 1872

### üß† MASTER STRUCTURED NOTES

#### Essentials of Valid Marriage (Section 60)

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Parties** | Indian Christians |
| **Age** | Male: 21, Female: 18 |
| **Bigamy** | Neither party has spouse living |
| **Mental Capacity** | Sound mind |
| **Prohibited Relationship** | Not within prohibited degrees |
| **Solemnization** | Licensed person + 2 credible witnesses |

**üìã Procedural Requirements**

**Section 5: Notice of Intended Marriage**
- Parties give notice to Marriage Officer
- At least one party must have resided in district for 30 days

**Section 6: Marriage Notice Book and Publication**
- Marriage Officer maintains notice book
- Affixes copy to conspicuous place
- Transmits to other district if party not permanently residing

**Section 7: Objection to Marriage**
- Any person can object
- Within **30 days** from date of publication
- Grounds: Would contravene Section 4 conditions

**Section 8: Procedure on Receipt of Objection**
- Marriage Officer makes inquiry (max 30 days)
- Cannot solemnize until inquiry completed
- Appeal to District Court (30 days)

**Section 9: Powers of Marriage Officers**
- Same as Civil Court under CPC, 1908
- Can summon witnesses, order discovery, receive evidence on affidavits
- Can impose costs (max ‚Çπ1,000) if objection not reasonable

**Section 11: Declaration by Parties and Witnesses**
- Parties + **3 witnesses** sign declaration
- In presence of Marriage Officer
- Form in Third Schedule

**Section 12: Place and Form of Solemnization**
- Office of Marriage Officer OR other place
- Each party says: "I (A) take thee (B), to be my lawful wife (or husband)"

**Section 13: Certificate of Marriage**
- Entered in Marriage Certificate Book
- **Conclusive evidence** of marriage

**Section 14: New Notice When Marriage Not Solemnized Within Three Months**
- Notice lapses if not solemnized within 3 months
- New notice required

**üìã Registration of Existing Marriages (Section 15)**

**Conditions:**
- Ceremony performed + parties living together
- No bigamy
- Sound mind
- Both parties completed **21 years** (not 18 for female)
- Not within prohibited degrees (exception: custom if before Act)
- Residence 30 days

**Section 16: Procedure for Registration**
- Application by both parties
- Public notice
- 30 days for objections
- Enter certificate in Marriage Certificate Book

**Section 17: Appeals**
- 30 days from date of order
- District Court (final)

**Section 18: Effect of Registration**
- Marriage deemed solemnized under Act from date of entry
- Children legitimate from date of ceremony
- Does NOT confer property rights on persons other than parents

---

#### Consequences of Marriage

**Section 19: Effect on Member of Undivided Family**
- Marriage of Hindu/Buddhist/Sikh/Jain member
- **Deemed to effect severance** from undivided family

**Section 20: Rights and Disabilities**
- Same rights as person to whom Caste Disabilities Removal Act, 1850 applies
- Same disabilities in regard to succession

**Section 21: Succession**
- Regulated by Indian Succession Act, 1925
- As if Chapter III of Part V (Parsi Intestates) omitted

**Section 21-A: Special Provision**
- If both parties are Hindu/Buddhist/Sikh/Jain:
  - Section 19 (severance) does NOT apply
  - Section 21 (succession) does NOT apply
  - Section 20 (disabilities) - only part creating disability does NOT apply

## üìå UNIT 6: RELATIONSHIP IN THE NATURE OF MARRIAGE

### üß† MASTER STRUCTURED NOTES

#### Live-in Relationships

**üìã Definition**
- Cohabitation without formal marriage
- Not recognized as marriage
- But certain rights recognized

**‚öñÔ∏è Legal Status**
- **Not marriage** under any personal law
- But maintenance rights may be available under:
  - Section 125 CrPC (if "wife" includes live-in partner)
  - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

**üìú Important Cases**
- **D. Velusamy v. D. Patchaiammal (2010)**: Live-in relationship recognized for maintenance
- **Indra Sarma v. V.K.V. Sarma (2013)**: Guidelines for live-in relationships

**üìã Requirements for Recognition**
- Must be of legal age
- Must be voluntarily cohabited
- Must have lived together for significant period
- Must have held themselves out as spouses

---

#### Same-Sex Relationships

**üìã Current Status**
- **Not recognized** as marriage in India
- **Navtej Singh Johar v. UOI (2018)**: Decriminalized consensual same-sex relations
- **Supriyo v. Union of India (2023)**: SC refused to recognize same-sex marriage

**üìú Key Cases**
- **NALSA v. Union of India (2014)**: Transgender rights
- **Navtej Singh Johar v. UOI (2018)**: Section 377 IPC void for consensual same-sex relations
- **Shafin Jahan v. Asokan K.M. (2018)**: Right to marry person of one's choice
