#### 1. Definition and Nature of Nikah

**ğŸ“Œ Definition**

#### 2. Essentials of Valid Marriage

**âœ… Five Essentials**

1. **Proposal and Acceptance (Ijab and Qubul)**
   - Both at **one meeting** (essential)
   - Two male OR one male + two female witnesses
   - Witnesses must be sane, adult Muslims
   - Usual form: "I have married myself to you" / "I have consented"

2. **Capacity to Contract**
   - Sound mind + Puberty
   - **Puberty = Majority** (for marriage matters)
   - **Indian Majority Act, 1875**: Does NOT apply to marriage, dower, divorce
   - Hanafi: Presumed at 15 years
   - Shia Female: Begins with menstruation
   - **Case**: Shafin Jahan v. Asokan K.M. (2018) - 16-year-old Muslim girl's marriage held valid

3. **Guardianship (Jabar)**
   - **Sunni Order**: Father â†’ Paternal Grandfather â†’ Brother â†’ Mother â†’ Others â†’ Government
   - **Shia**: Only Father + Paternal Grandfather (others ineffective unless ratified)
   - **Option of Puberty**: Minor can ratify/repudiate on attaining puberty
   - **Dissolution of Muslim Marriage Act, 1939 Section 2(vii)**: Wife entitled to dissolution if married before 15, repudiated before 18, not consummated

4. **Absence of Impediments**
   - No absolute impediments (void)
   - No relative impediments (invalid)

5. **Formalities**
   - No prescribed ceremonies
   - Customary practices (not essential): Nikah by Kazi, Exchange of gifts, Prayers
   - Registration NOT compulsory

---

#### 3. Impediments to Marriage

**ğŸš« Absolute Impediments (Batil - Void)**

1. **Polyandry**: Married woman cannot have second husband
2. **Consanguinity**: Blood relationship
   - Ascendants (mother, grandmother)
   - Descendants (daughter, granddaughter)
   - Second rank (brothers, sisters, descendants)
   - Uncles and aunts (paternal/maternal)
3. **Affinity**: Relationship by marriage
   - Wife's mother/grandmother (how highsoever)
   - Wife's daughter/granddaughter (if consummated)
   - Father's wife (or ascendant's wife)
   - Son's wife (or descendant's wife)
4. **Fosterage**: Relationship by suckling
   - Act of suckling = Act of procreation
   - Same effect as consanguinity

**âš ï¸ Relative Impediments (Fasid - Invalid)**

1. **Fifth wife**: Marriage with 5th wife (invalid)
   - Remedy: Divorce one of the four wives

2. **Absence of witnesses**: No proper witnesses
   - Shia: NOT required
   - Remedy: Subsequent confirmation in presence of witnesses

3. **Difference of religion**:
   - **Shia**: Both must be Muslim
   - **Sunni**: 
     - Muslim male + Kitabia (Christian/Jewish) = Valid
     - Muslim male + Idolatress = Irregular (can be removed by conversion)
     - Muslim female + Non-Muslim = **VOID**

4. **Unlawful conjunction**: Two wives related (e.g., sisters)
   - Remedy: Divorce one OR death of either

5. **Woman in iddat**: Marriage during iddat period

**âš–ï¸ Difference: Batil vs Fasid**

| Aspect | Batil (Void) | Fasid (Invalid) |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Nature** | Altogether illegal | Good foundation, unlawful attributes |
| **Rights Before Consummation** | None | None |
| **Rights After Consummation** | None (except customary dower) | Some rights |
| **Dower** | No dower (or customary if consummated) | Proper or specified (whichever less) |
| **Children** | Illegitimate | Legitimate |
| **Iddat** | No iddat | Wife bound to observe iddat |
| **Mutual Inheritance** | No | No (same as Batil) |

#### 4. Muta Marriage

**ğŸ“Œ Definition**
- Temporary marriage with fixed duration
- **Recognition**: Only **Ithna Ashari (Shia)** school
- **Requirements**: Period + Dower must be specified

**âš–ï¸ Legal Incidents**

- âŒ No maintenance under personal law
- âŒ NOT restricted to four (unlike permanent marriage)
- âœ… Dissolved by expiry of term OR husband's gift of term

**ğŸ“‹ Who Can Contract Muta?**
- Shia male + Muslim/Christian/Jewish/Fire-worshiper = Valid
- Shia male + Other religion = Not valid
- Shia female + Muslim only = Valid

---

#### 5. Stipulations in Marriage Contract

**ğŸ“‹ Valid Stipulations**
- Legal and reasonable conditions may be appended
- If not opposed to Islam
- Examples:
  - Wife gets separate residence if husband treats with cruelty
  - First wife gets maintenance if husband brings second wife
  - Wife can reside at father's home if husband brings other wife

**âŒ Void Stipulations**
- Agreement that wife would not be entitled to maintenance = **VOID**
- Agreement opposed to public policy or Muslim Law = **VOID**

---

#### 6. Legal Effects of Valid Marriage

**Rights Conferred Upon Wife:**
- Dower (mahr)
- Maintenance from husband
- Suitable matrimonial residence
- Equal affection and impartiality (if co-wife exists)
- Child custody (society and upbringing of infant children)

**Obligations Imposed Upon Wife:**
- Faithfulness and obedience
- Sexual intercourse (with regard to health and decency)
- Suckling her own children
- Observance of iddat

## ğŸ“Œ UNIT 2: TALAQ (DIVORCE)

### ğŸ§  MASTER STRUCTURED NOTES

#### 1. Nature and Kinds of Talaq

**ğŸ“Š Superior Right of Husband**
- Can divorce without cause
- Can have 4 wives at a time

**ğŸ“Š Disabilities of Wife**
- Cannot divorce herself (except by court decree)
- Monogamy (cannot have more than one husband)

**ğŸ“– Meaning of Talaq**
- **Religious View**: Sin which is unforgivable
- **Quranic Position**: Allah discourages divorce, encourages continuation
- **Exception**: If disputes cannot be resolved, Quran provides procedure
- **Revocation**: Procedure allows possibility of revocation

**ğŸ“Š Three Kinds**

1. **Talaq-ul-Sunnat** (Revocable)
   - **Talaq-Ahsan**: Single pronouncement in Tuhr, no revocation during iddat
   - **Talaq-Hasan**: Three pronouncements in successive Tuhrs
   - **Status**: Most approved form

2. **Talaq-ul-Bidaat** (Irrevocable)
   - Triple pronouncement in one Tuhr
   - Single declaration expressing irrevocable intention
   - **Status**: Disapproved, **NOW BANNED** (Muslim Women Act 2019)

3. **Ila and Zihar**
   - **Ila**: Oath not to have intercourse (4 months)
   - **Zihar**: Comparing wife to prohibited relationship (4 months)

---

#### 2. Talaq-ul-Sunnat (Revocable)

**ğŸ“‹ Talaq-Ahsan (Most Proper)**

**Why Most Proper:**
1. Possibility of revocation before expiry of iddat
2. Evil words uttered only **once**

**Formalities:**
- Single pronouncement during **Tuhr** (period of purity)
- Wife observes iddat of **3 monthly courses**
- No revocation during iddat
- **Revocation**: Express OR implied (cohabitation = implied)
- **Finality**: When iddat expires without revocation = irrevocable

**ğŸ“‹ Talaq-Hasan (Proper)**

**Formalities:**
- First pronouncement in Tuhr
- Second pronouncement in **next Tuhr**
- Third pronouncement in **third Tuhr**
- First and second may be **revoked**
- Third makes it **irrevocable**

---

#### 3. Talaq-ul-Bidaat (Irrevocable - BANNED)

**ğŸ“‹ Methods**
1. Triple pronouncement in one Tuhr
2. Single declaration expressing irrevocable intention

**âš ï¸ Status**: **BANNED** by Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019

**Punishment**: Imprisonment up to 3 years + fine

---

#### 4. Ila and Zihar

**ğŸ”„ Ila**
- Husband takes oath not to have intercourse
- No consummation for **4 months**
- After 4 months = marriage dissolves irrevocably
- **Revocation**: Resume cohabitation within 4 months
- **Shia Law**: Does NOT operate as divorce without court order

**ğŸ”„ Zihar**
- Husband compares wife to prohibited relationship
- Does not cohabit for **4 months**
- **Wife's Rights After 4 Months**:
  - Judicial divorce, OR
  - Restitution of conjugal rights
- **Revocation**: Resume cohabitation within 4 months
- **Methods of Revocation**:
  - Fast for 2 months, OR
  - Feed 60 people, OR
  - Free a slave
- **Shia Law**: Must be performed in presence of 2 witnesses

---

#### 5. Divorce by Mutual Agreement

**ğŸ’° Khula**
- Agreement to dissolve in lieu of compensation (usually mahr)
- **Consideration essential**
- Once husband consents = **irrevocable divorce**
- Husband cannot cancel on ground that consideration not paid

**ğŸ’° Mubarat**
- Both parties desire divorce
- Proposal may emanate from either side
- **Effect (Sunni)**: All mutual rights and obligations come to an end

---

#### 6. Divorce by Wife

**Methods**:
1. **Talaaq-i-Tafweez**: Delegated divorce
   - Husband delegates power to wife or any other person
   - **Permanently** = Revocable
   - **Temporarily** = Not revocable
   - "Perhaps the most potent weapon in the hands of a Muslim wife" (Faizee)

2. **Lian**: False charge of adultery
   - Must be voluntary and **aggressive**
   - **Exception**: If husband responds to wife's bad behavior = cannot be used
   - **Case**: Nurjahan v. Kazim Ali - husband's response to bad behavior cannot be used

3. **Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939**: 8 grounds

#### 7. Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939

**ğŸ“… Historical Background**
- Bill introduced: 17th April 1936 (Qazi Mohammad Ahmad Kazmi)
- Act became law: 17th March 1939

**ğŸ“‹ Section 2 - 8 Grounds for Divorce**

1. **Whereabouts Unknown** (Section 2(i))
   - Whereabouts of husband not known
   - For **4 years**

2. **Neglect of Maintenance** (Section 2(ii))
   - Husband has neglected or failed to provide maintenance
   - For **2 years**

3. **Imprisonment** (Section 2(iii))
   - Husband sentenced to imprisonment
   - For **7 years or upwards**

4. **Failure to Perform Marital Obligations** (Section 2(iv))
   - Husband failed to perform marital obligations
   - Without reasonable cause
   - For **3 years**

5. **Impotency** (Section 2(v))
   - Husband was impotent at time of marriage
   - Continues to be so

6. **Insanity/Leprosy/Venereal Disease** (Section 2(vi))
   - Insanity for **2 years**
   - Leprosy
   - Virulent venereal disease

7. **Option of Puberty** (Section 2(vii))
   - Given in marriage by father or other guardian
   - Before attaining **15 years**
   - Repudiated before attaining **18 years**
   - Marriage has **not been consummated**

8. **Cruelty** (Section 2(viii))
   - Husband treats her with cruelty
   - **Includes**:
     - Habitually assaults or makes life miserable
     - Associates with women of ill-repute
     - Attempts to force immoral life
     - Disposes of property or prevents exercising legal rights
     - Obstructs in observance of religious profession
     - If more than one wife, does not treat equitably

**ğŸ”„ Irretrievable Breakdown**
- Developed through judicial interpretation
- **Case**: Neorbibi v. Pir Bux (1970) - divorce granted on breakdown
- **Two Breakdown Grounds**:
  1. Non-payment of maintenance (even if failure due to wife's conduct)
  2. Total irreconcilability between spouses

#### 8. Iddat

**ğŸ“Œ Definition**
- Period of chastity after dissolution of marriage
- **Purpose**: Certainty of paternity, Avoid confusion, Restraint

**â° Duration**

| Situation | Duration |
|-----------|----------|

**ğŸ“‹ Important Points**

- **Divorce**: Commences from date of divorce

**ğŸ‘© Women's Rights During Iddat**

**ğŸ“œ Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986**

- **Pregnant**: Period between divorce and delivery/termination

## ğŸ“Œ UNIT 3: REMARRIAGE

### ğŸ§  MASTER STRUCTURED NOTES

#### Remarriage with Divorced Wife

**ğŸ“‹ General Rule**
- After iddat, parties can remarry each other
- **EXCEPT**: When divorce is by **triple pronouncement of talaq**

**ğŸ“‹ Nikah Halala**
- **Requirement**: Before remarriage after triple talaq:
  1. Wife must marry another person
  2. Marriage must be **consummated**
  3. Thereafter, she must be divorced by him
  4. Then original parties can remarry

**ğŸ“œ Case Law: Rashid Ahmed v. Anisha Khatun (1932)**
- Re-marriage after triple talaq **not valid** unless Nikah Halala performed
- Children born after pronouncement held **illegitimate**

**âš ï¸ Present Status**
- **Sameena Begum v. Union of India** (WP No. 222 of 2018): Challenged practice
- **Ashwini Kumar Upadhyay v. Union of India**: Challenged before SC
- Triple Talaq (Talaq-e-Biddat) **banned** by Act 2019
