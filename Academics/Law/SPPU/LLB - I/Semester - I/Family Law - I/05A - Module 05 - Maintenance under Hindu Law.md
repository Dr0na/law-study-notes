### 1. Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

**ğŸ’° Section 24: Interim Maintenance (Pendente Lite)**

**Who Can Claim**: Either wife or husband

**Conditions**:
- Petitioner has no independent income sufficient for support
- Petitioner needs money for expenses of proceeding
- Respondent has means to pay

**What Can Be Ordered**:
- Expenses of proceeding (court fees, lawyer fees)
- Maintenance (monthly/weekly sum)
- Amount: Reasonable, considering both parties' income

**Key Points**:
- âœ… Temporary in nature (only during pendency)
- âœ… Ceases automatically when proceedings end
- âœ… Can be modified if circumstances change
- âœ… **Both parties eligible** (not just wife)

---

**ğŸ’ Section 25: Permanent Maintenance**

**Who Can Claim**: Either wife or husband

**When Available**:
- At the time of passing decree, OR
- At any time subsequent

**Nature**:
- Gross sum, OR
- Monthly/periodical payment

**Duration**: Not exceeding the life of the applicant

**Factors Considered**:
- Respondent's income and property
- Applicant's property
- Conduct of parties
- Other circumstances

**Modification/Cancellation (Sub-section 2 & 3)**:
- Change in circumstances (either party can apply)
- Remarriage (order can be rescinded)
- Wife not chaste (order can be modified/rescinded)
- Husband has extramarital relations (order can be modified/rescinded)

**Securing Payment**:
- Can be secured by **charge on immovable property** of respondent

### 2. Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956

**ğŸ“Œ Section 3(b): Definition of Maintenance**

**Includes**:
1. **In all cases**:
   - Food
   - Clothing
   - Residence

**ğŸ‘° Section 18: Maintenance of Wife**

**Sub-section (1)**: 
- Hindu wife entitled to be maintained by husband **during her lifetime**
- Applies to marriages before or after commencement of Act

**Sub-section (2)**: Wife can live separately **WITHOUT forfeiting maintenance** if:

| Ground | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **(a) Desertion** | Abandoning without reasonable cause and without consent |
| **(b) Cruelty** | Treatment causing reasonable apprehension of harm |
| **(c) Virulent leprosy** | Husband suffering from virulent form |
| **(d) Another wife living** | Husband has other wife |
| **(e) Concubine** | Keeps concubine in same house or habitually resides with her |
| **(f) Conversion** | Husband ceased to be Hindu |
| **(g) Other cause** | Any other cause justifying separate residence |

**Sub-section (3)**: Wife **LOSES right** to maintenance if:
- âŒ She is **unchaste**, OR
- âŒ She **ceases to be Hindu** by conversion

**ğŸ‘µ Section 19: Maintenance of Widowed Daughter-in-Law**

**Sub-section (1)**: 
- Widowed daughter-in-law entitled to maintenance from **father-in-law**
- **Conditions**:
  - âœ… Unable to maintain herself from own earnings/property
  - âœ… Unable to obtain maintenance from:
    - Estate of husband
    - Father or mother
    - Son or daughter (if any) or their estate

**Sub-section (2)**: 
- **Not enforceable** if father-in-law has no means from coparcenary property
- **Ceases** on remarriage of daughter-in-law

**ğŸ‘¶ Section 20: Maintenance of Children and Aged Parents**

**Sub-section (1)**: 
- Hindu bound to maintain during lifetime:

**Sub-section (3)**:

**Explanation**: "Parent" includes **childless stepmother**

|---------|------------|

**ğŸ’¼ Section 22: Maintenance of Dependents**

**Sub-section (1)**: 
- Heirs of deceased Hindu bound to maintain dependents
- **Source**: Out of estate inherited from deceased

**Sub-section (2)**: 
- If dependent has **NOT obtained** any share by testamentary or intestate succession
- Dependent entitled to maintenance from those who take the estate

**Sub-section (3)**: 
- Liability of each heir = **Proportionate** to value of share taken

**Sub-section (4)**: 
- **Exception**: If dependent has obtained share, but its value is less than what would be awarded as maintenance
- Such dependent **not liable** to contribute to maintenance of others

**ğŸ’µ Section 23: Amount of Maintenance**

**Factors for Wife, Children, Aged Parents (Sub-section 2)**:
- Position and status of parties
- Reasonable wants of claimant
- Justification for separate living (if living separately)
- Claimant's property and income
- Number of persons entitled to maintenance

**Factors for Dependents (Sub-section 3)**:
- Net value of estate (after payment of debts)
- Provision in will (if deceased made provision)
- Degree of relationship
- Reasonable wants
- Past relations with deceased during lifetime
- Dependent's property and income
- Number of dependents entitled

**ğŸ”„ Section 25: Alteration of Maintenance**
- Amount of maintenance (whether fixed by court decree or agreement) may be **altered**
- **Condition**: Material change in circumstances justifying alteration

**âš–ï¸ Section 26: Priority of Debts**
- **Debts of deceased have priority** over claims of dependents for maintenance
- **Exception**: Subject to provisions of Section 27 (maintenance as charge)

**ğŸ“‹ Section 27: Maintenance as Charge**
- Dependent's claim for maintenance is **NOT automatically a charge** on estate
- **Becomes charge only if**:
  - Created by will of deceased, OR
  - Created by decree of court, OR
  - Created by agreement, OR
  - Created otherwise

**ğŸ  Section 28: Effect of Transfer of Property**
- If estate (or part) is **transferred**, right to maintenance can be enforced against transferee if:
  - âœ… Transferee has **notice** of the right, OR
  - âœ… Transfer is **gratuitous** (without consideration)
- **Cannot be enforced** against transferee for consideration and without notice
