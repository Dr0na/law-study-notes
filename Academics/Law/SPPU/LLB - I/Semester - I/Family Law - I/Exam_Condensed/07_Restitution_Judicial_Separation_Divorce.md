## ðŸ” KEY GROUNDS DETAILED

### **ADULTERY [13(1)(i)]:**

**Elements:**
- Voluntary
- Sexual intercourse
- With person other than spouse
- After marriage

**Proof:** Circumstantial evidence sufficient

**Single Act:** Sufficient

**Case:** Jorden Diengdeh (1985) - Inference from circumstances

---

### **CRUELTY [13(1)(ia)]:**

**Types:**
1. Physical cruelty
2. Mental cruelty

**Mental Cruelty Includes:**
- False accusations
- Denial of sexual relations
- Public humiliation
- Unreasonable jealousy
- Dowry harassment
- Persistent nagging
- [Many more - see Samar Ghosh case]

**Test:**
- Subjective + Objective
- Reasonable apprehension of harm

**Key Cases:**
- **Dastane (1975)** - Not limited to physical
- **Samar Ghosh (2007)** - Illustrative list
- **Naveen Kohli (2006)** - Irretrievable breakdown = cruelty

---

### **DESERTION [13(1)(ib)]:**

**5 Elements: "FASAC"**

1. **F**actual separation
2. **A**nimus deserendi (intention)
3. **S**ans consent (without)
4. **A**bsence of reasonable cause
5. **C**ontinuous 2 years

**Types:**
- Actual desertion
- Constructive desertion

**Case:** Moonshee Buzloor Ruheem (1867)

---

### **CONVERSION [13(1)(ii)]:**

**Rule:** Ceased to be Hindu by conversion

**Impact:**
- Sarla Mudgal (1995) - Conversion to remarry = fraud
- Lily Thomas (2000) - First marriage continues

---

### **PRESUMPTION OF DEATH [13(1)(vii)]:**

**Requirements:**
- Not heard of **7 years** or more
- By persons who would naturally have heard
- If alive

**Effect:** Divorce granted (not death declaration)

## ðŸ’š MUTUAL CONSENT [13B]

### **REQUIREMENTS (4 Points):**

1. **Living separately** - 1 year or more
2. **Unable to live together**
3. **Mutually agreed** to divorce
4. **Joint petition** - Both parties together

---

### **TWO-MOTION PROCESS:**

**FIRST MOTION:**
- File joint petition
- After 1 year separation

**COOLING PERIOD:**
- Minimum **6 months**
- Maximum **18 months**

**SECOND MOTION:**
- Confirm desire to divorce
- Court passes decree

**WITHDRAWAL:**
- Possible **until second motion**
- After that, irrevocable

---

### **ADVANTAGES:**

1. No fault-finding
2. Dignified exit
3. Faster process
4. Less acrimonious
5. Privacy maintained

### **Key Case:**

**Vishnu Dutt Sharma (2009):**
- No need to prove traditional grounds
- Mutual consent sufficient
- Court should grant if conditions met

### **Quick Memory:**
"1 year apart â†’ 6-month wait â†’ both agree = divorce"
