## ‚ùì QUESTION 1

**Q1-MAR-5M:** Explain the significance of Saptapadi in Hindu marriage under Section 7 of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955.

### **üìã MODEL ANSWER:**

**INTRODUCTION:**
Saptapadi (seven steps) is the most sacred ritual in Hindu marriage ceremony, recognized under Section 7(2) of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 as the completion point of marriage when included in customary ceremony.

**SECTION 7(2) PROVISION:**
"Where such rites and ceremonies include the Saptapadi (that is, the taking of seven steps by the bridegroom and the bride jointly before the sacred fire), the marriage becomes complete and binding when the seventh step is taken."

**THE SEVEN STEPS:**

Each step represents a sacred vow:
1. **First Step:** Nourishment and food
2. **Second Step:** Strength and vitality
3. **Third Step:** Prosperity and wealth
4. **Fourth Step:** Happiness and harmony
5. **Fifth Step:** Progeny and children
6. **Sixth Step:** Longevity and seasons
7. **Seventh Step:** Friendship and companionship

**SIGNIFICANCE:**

**1. Completion Point:**
- Marriage becomes **complete and binding** at 7th step
- Before 7th step, marriage not complete
- Legal binding occurs at this moment

**2. Religious Sanctity:**
- Performed before sacred fire (Agni)
- Fire as divine witness
- Most sacred part of ceremony

**3. Mutual Vows:**
- Both parties take steps together
- Symbolizes equal partnership
- Joint commitment

**4. Legal Recognition:**
- Statutory recognition in Section 7(2)
- Completion point legally defined
- No ambiguity about when marriage complete

**VARIATIONS:**
- Some communities perform Saptapadi with variations
- Some don't perform Saptapadi at all
- All valid if according to custom

**CASE LAW:**
**Chhaganlal Govindlal v. Municipal Corporation of Delhi (AIR 1964 SC 1411):** Saptapadi is completion point where it forms part of ceremony.

**CONSEQUENCE:**
If Saptapadi is essential custom and not performed, marriage may be void.

**CONCLUSION:**
Saptapadi represents the culmination of Hindu marriage ceremony, combining religious sanctity with legal recognition, and serves as definitive completion point when included in customary rites.

## ‚ùì QUESTION 2

**Q2-VVM-5M:** Distinguish between void and voidable marriages.

### **üìã MODEL ANSWER:**

**INTRODUCTION:**
Hindu Marriage Act makes crucial distinction between void marriages (Section 11) and voidable marriages (Section 12), determining legal status, rights, and remedies.

**DEFINITIONS:**

**Void Marriage:**
- Null and void ab initio (from beginning)
- No legal existence
- Decree merely declaratory

**Voidable Marriage:**
- Valid until annulled by decree
- Has legal existence until decree
- Decree is constitutive

**KEY DIFFERENCES:**

| **Aspect** | **Void** | **Voidable** |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| **Status** | Null from start | Valid until annulled |
| **Grounds** | 3 (LiSPro) | 5 (IMPUF) |
| **Who Can** | Anyone | Only parties |
| **Time Limit** | None | Yes (specific) |
| **After Death** | Can declare | Cannot annul |
| **Decree Nature** | Declaratory | Constitutive |
| **Bars** | None | Yes [Sec 12(2)] |

**VOID GROUNDS (3):**
**Mnemonic: "LiSPro"**
- Living spouse (bigamy)
- Sapinda relationship
- Prohibited relationship (Schedule I)

**VOIDABLE GROUNDS (5):**
**Mnemonic: "IMPUF"**
- Impotence (1 yr limit)
- Mental incapacity
- Pregnancy by another (1 yr)
- Underage (2 yrs after majority)
- Force/Fraud (1 yr)

**PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS:**

**Void:**
- No rights as spouses
- No need for decree (advisable though)
- Can challenge anytime

**Voidable:**
- Full rights until decree
- Must obtain decree
- Time limits apply
- Approbation may bar relief

**SECTION 16:**
Children of both void and voidable marriages are legitimate (with conditions).

**CASE LAW:**
**Satya v. Teja Singh (AIR 1975 SC 105):** Clear distinction between void and voidable essential.

**CONCLUSION:**
Void marriages involve serious violations (bigamy, incest) warranting absolute nullity, while voidable marriages involve personal capacity issues allowing aggrieved party to choose continuation or annulment, reflecting different policy considerations.

## ‚ùì QUESTION 3

**Q3-MAR-5M:** Explain the provisions of Section 16 regarding legitimacy of children of void and voidable marriages.

### **üìã MODEL ANSWER:**

**INTRODUCTION:**
Section 16 of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 provides humanitarian protection to children born of void or voidable marriages by declaring them legitimate despite invalidity of parents' marriage, subject to certain conditions.

**SECTION 16: STATUTORY PROVISION:**

**Text:**
Children of void or voidable marriages are legitimate if:
1. Marriage was null and void OR annulled by decree of nullity, AND
2. Child was **conceived before decree** of nullity, AND
3. At time of intercourse leading to conception:
   - Parties did not know of facts making marriage void, OR
   - (In case of voidable marriage) Before decree of nullity

**RATIONALE:**

**Protection of Innocent Children:**
- Children should not suffer for parents' invalid marriage
- Not responsible for marriage defects
- Right to dignity and rights

**Social Justice:**
- Prevent stigma of illegitimacy
- Ensure children's welfare
- Humanitarian approach

**RIGHTS OF LEGITIMATE CHILDREN:**

Children declared legitimate under Section 16 have right to:
1. **Maintenance** from parents
2. **Succession** to parents' property
3. Social recognition
4. Benefits under other laws

**IMPORTANT LIMITATION:**
Children have rights only to **parents' property**, NOT to property of other relatives (grandparents, uncles, etc.).

**RATIONALE FOR LIMITATION:**
- Other relatives not party to invalid marriage
- Should not be burdened
- Protection extends only to immediate parents

**ILLUSTRATION:**
A marries B (marriage void - A had living spouse). Child C born. C is legitimate under Section 16 and can inherit from A and B, but NOT from A's or B's other relatives.

**CASE LAW:**
Courts liberally interpret Section 16 to protect children's interests.

**CONDITIONS ANALYZED:**

**"Conceived before decree":**
If child conceived after decree of nullity, not covered by Section 16.

**"Did not know facts":**
Protects parties who entered marriage in good faith, unaware of impediment.

**Voidable Exception:**
For voidable marriages, protection applies if child conceived before decree (knowledge less relevant as marriage was valid until decree).

**CONCLUSION:**
Section 16 represents legislative compassion, ensuring children of void/voidable marriages are not penalized for circumstances beyond their control. By granting legitimacy with limited rights to parents' property, law balances protection of children with rights of other family members, demonstrating humane approach to complex matrimonial situations.

## ‚ùì QUESTION 4

**Q4-MAR-5M:** Explain the prohibited relationships under Schedule I of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955.

### **üìã MODEL ANSWER:**

**INTRODUCTION:**
Schedule I to Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, read with Section 5(v), lists specific relationships within which marriage is absolutely prohibited, ensuring prevention of incestuous and socially unacceptable marriages.

**SECTION 5(v) PROVISION:**
"The parties are not within the degrees of prohibited relationship unless the custom or usage governing each of them permits of a marriage between the two"

**SCHEDULE I: PROHIBITED RELATIONSHIPS**

**FOR A MAN - CANNOT MARRY:**

**Direct Lineage:**
- Mother, daughter
- Grandmother, granddaughter

**Collateral Relations:**
- Sister (full blood, half blood, uterine)
- Aunt (father's sister, mother's sister)
- Niece (brother's daughter, sister's daughter)
- Cousins (father's brother's daughter, mother's brother's daughter, father's sister's daughter, mother's sister's daughter)
- Son's daughter, daughter's daughter

**FOR A WOMAN - CANNOT MARRY:**

**Direct Lineage:**
- Father, son
- Grandfather, grandson

**Collateral Relations:**
- Brother (full, half, uterine)
- Uncle (father's brother, mother's brother)
- Nephew (brother's son, sister's son)
- Cousins (parallel and cross)
- Son's son, daughter's son

**ADOPTION:**
Relationships through valid adoption treated as blood relationships for prohibition purposes.

**RATIONALE:**

**1. Genetic:**
- Prevent genetic disorders from consanguineous marriages
- Ensure genetic diversity
- Health of offspring

**2. Social/Moral:**
- Maintain family structure
- Prevent confusion in family roles
- Uphold social values

**3. Religious:**
- Based on ancient Hindu texts
- Dharmashastra principles

**CONSEQUENCE:**
Marriage within prohibited relationship is **VOID** under Section 11.

**EXCEPTION:**
Custom may permit (extremely rare; burden of proof on party claiming).

**ILLUSTRATION:**
A (male) cannot marry his sister, daughter, mother, aunt, or niece. Marriage if performed would be void under Section 11.

**CONCLUSION:**
Schedule I provides comprehensive list of prohibited relationships ensuring prevention of incestuous marriages, based on genetic, social, and moral considerations, while allowing narrow exception for established customs.
