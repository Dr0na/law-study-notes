## üìù ANSWER FORMAT (2 MARKS)

**Structure:**
1. **Direct Definition/Answer** (1-2 lines)
2. **Brief Explanation** (3-4 lines)
3. **Section/Source mention** (if applicable)
4. **Example** (if space permits) OR **Case** (if relevant)

**Example:**

**Q: Define Sapinda relationship.**

**A:** Sapinda relationship is defined under Section 3(f) of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955. Two persons are sapindas of each other if one is a lineal ascendant of the other within three degrees through father and within five degrees through mother. The first generation is excluded while counting. For example, a person and his/her grandfather are sapindas (within 3 degrees through father). Marriage between sapindas is prohibited unless custom permits [Section 5(iv)].

*[Approximately 150-180 words]*
