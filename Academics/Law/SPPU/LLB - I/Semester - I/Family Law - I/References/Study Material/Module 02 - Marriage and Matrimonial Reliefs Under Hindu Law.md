## Unit 1: Marriage

### 1.1 Evolution and Concept of Marriage

> üìå **Why this matters**: Understanding the conceptual foundation helps in answering questions about the nature and purpose of marriage in Hindu law.

#### üìñ Definition and Purpose

- **Primary Function**: Regulates sexual relationships
- **Key Purpose**: Determines **paternity** of children (if unregulated, only maternity would be known)
- **Legal Effect**: Confers **status** on parties to the marriage

---

### 1.2 Nature and Forms Under Ancient Hindu Law

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions often ask about the nature of Hindu marriage (sacrament vs contract) and the eight ancient forms. This is a favorite topic for comparative analysis.

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Hindu Marriage: A Sacrament or a Civil Contract?

**Hindu Marriage as a Sacrament (Samskara)**

- Marriage is the **last of the ten sacraments** (Samskara)
- Performed to **purify the body** from inherited taint
- More of a **religious necessity** than physical luxury
- Described as: *"the union of flesh with flesh and bone with bone"*
- **Indissoluble union** - as long as husband is alive, wife regards him as her God
- Wife is **Ardhangini** (half the body of husband)
  - Shares equally the fruits of all his acts (good or bad)

**Hindu Marriage as a Contract**

- The ceremony of **Kanyadan** fulfills all requirements of a gift (as per Manu)
- Concept continues even after Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

> ‚úÖ **Key Legal Position**: **Hindu marriage is BOTH a sacrament AND a contract**

**Supporting Case Laws:**
- Purshottamadas v. Purshottamadas
- Bhagwati Saran Singh v. Parmeshwari Nandan Singh
- Muthuswami v. Masilamani
- Anjona Dasi v. Ghose

---

#### üìú Ancient Forms of Marriage

The ancient Hindu law recognized **eight forms of marriage**:

| Category | Form | Description | Status |
|----------|------|-------------|--------|
| **Approved Forms** | **1. Brahma** | Father gives bride away without receiving consideration | ‚úÖ Approved |
| | **2. Daiva** | Girl given to priest who officiated at sacrifice (in lieu of dakshina) | ‚úÖ Approved |
| | **3. Arsha** | Bridegroom delivers 1-2 pairs of cows to bride's father (returned at marriage) | ‚úÖ Approved |
| | **4. Prajapatya** | Similar to Brahma, but bridegroom need not be a bachelor | ‚úÖ Approved |
| **Unapproved Forms** | **5. Asura** | Father receives monetary consideration (marriage by sale) | ‚ùå Unapproved |
| | **6. Gandharva** | Union by mutual consent and amorous desires (love marriage) | ‚ùå Unapproved |
| | **7. Rakshasha** | Preceded by rape/abduction of virgin in war | ‚ùå Unapproved |
| | **8. Paishacha** | Man ravished girl while asleep/intoxicated, then obliged to marry | ‚ùå Unapproved |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: These forms are **NOT recognized** by Hindu Marriage Act, 1955. The Act only lays down essential conditions for valid marriage.

### 1.4.1 Both Parties Must Be Hindus (Section 5)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions often test understanding of who is a "Hindu" under the Act. This is crucial for determining applicability of the Act.

#### üìñ Condition

A marriage may be solemnized between **any two Hindus**.

#### üë• Who is a Hindu? (Section 2)

**The Act applies to:**

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Any person who is Hindu by religion in any of its forms, including:<br>‚Ä¢ Virashaiva<br>‚Ä¢ Lingayat<br>‚Ä¢ Follower of Brahmo, Prarthana or Arya Samaj |
| **(b)** | Any person who is **Buddhist, Jaina or Sikh** by religion |
| **(c)** | Any other person domiciled in India who is **not** Muslim, Christian, Parsi or Jew by religion<br>(unless proved they would not have been governed by Hindu law) |

#### üë∂ Explanation - Children

The following persons are Hindus, Buddhists, Jainas or Sikhs by religion:

- **(a)** Any child (legitimate or illegitimate) **both of whose parents** are Hindus/Buddhists/Jains/Sikhs
- **(b)** Any child (legitimate or illegitimate) **one of whose parents** is Hindu/Buddhist/Jaina/Sikh, and who is **brought up** as member of that community
- **(c)** Any person who is a **convert or re-convert** to Hindu/Buddhist/Jain/Sikh religion

#### üö´ Exception: Scheduled Tribes (Section 2(2))

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: The Act **does NOT apply** to members of Scheduled Tribes (Article 366(25) of Constitution), **unless** Central Government directs otherwise by notification.

#### üìù The Expression "Hindu"

The expression "Hindu" in any portion of this Act includes a person who, though not Hindu by religion, is nevertheless a person to whom this Act applies by virtue of Section 2.

> ‚ùå **Legal Effect**: **Contravention makes marriage VOID** (Section 11)<br>A marriage between a Hindu and Non-Hindu under this Act is void.

**Case Law:**
- **G.S. Raj v. Bandaru Pavani** (AIR 2009 SC 1085)

### 1.4.2 Bigamy (Monogamy) (Section 5(i))

> üìå **Why this matters**: Bigamy is a common exam topic. Questions test understanding of monogamy rule, void marriage consequences, and IPC provisions. Often combined with Section 11 and Section 17.

#### üìñ Condition

**(i)** Neither party has a **spouse living** at the time of the marriage.

#### üéØ Key Concepts

**Monogamy Rule:**
- Establishes **rule of monogamy**
- Prohibits **polygamy** (permitted before Act)
- Prohibits **polyandry** (also prohibited by ancient Hindu law)

**Meaning:**
- One is permitted to have **only one wife or one husband** at a time
- Parties must **not be already married**, OR
- If married, spouse must **not be living**, OR
- There must be a **valid divorce** between them

> ‚ùå **Legal Effect**: **Contravention makes marriage VOID** (Section 11)

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Void Marriages (Section 11)

Any marriage solemnized after commencement of this Act shall be **null and void** and may be declared so by decree of nullity if it contravenes:
- Clause (i) - Bigamy
- Clause (iv) - Prohibited relationship
- Clause (v) - Sapinda relationship

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Also Results in Penal Offence** (Section 17)

---

#### üî® Punishment of Bigamy (Section 17)

Any marriage between two Hindus is **void** if either party had a husband/wife living at the date of marriage.

**IPC Provisions Apply:**
- Section 494 IPC
- Section 495 IPC

---

#### üìú Section 494 IPC - Marrying Again During Lifetime

**Offence**: Whoever, having a husband/wife living, marries again (where such marriage is void)

**Punishment**: 
- Imprisonment up to **7 years**
- **Fine**

**Exception:**
1. Marriage with former spouse declared **void by competent court**, OR
2. Former spouse **continually absent for 7 years** and not heard of as alive
   - **Condition**: Person must **inform** the other party before subsequent marriage

---

#### üìú Section 495 IPC - Same Offence with Concealment

**Offence**: Same as Section 494, but with **concealment of former marriage** from person with whom subsequent marriage is contracted

**Punishment**:
- Imprisonment up to **10 years**
- **Fine**

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Procedure (Section 198 Cr.P.C., 1973)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: No court shall take cognizance except upon **complaint by aggrieved person**

**If aggrieved person is wife**, complaint can be made by:
- Wife herself, OR
- Her father, mother, brother, sister, son, daughter, OR
- Father's/mother's brother or sister, OR
- With court's leave, any other person related by blood, marriage or adoption

### 1.4.3 Mental Capacity (Section 5(ii))

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of mental capacity conditions and when marriage becomes voidable. Often combined with Section 12(1)(b).

#### üìñ Condition

**(ii)** At the time of marriage, neither party:

| **(a)** | Is **incapable of giving valid consent** due to unsoundness of mind<br>‚Ä¢ Note: Not every kind of unsoundness covered<br>‚Ä¢ May not be persistent or continuous |

### 1.4.5 Prohibited Relationship (Section 5(iv))

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of prohibited degrees and exceptions. Often asked to identify whether a relationship is prohibited or explain the concept.

#### üìñ Condition

**(iv)** The parties are **not within degrees of prohibited relationship**, unless **custom or usage** governing each of them permits such marriage.

---

#### üìã Custom and Usages (Section 3(a))

**Definition:**
- Any rule which, having been **continuously and uniformly observed for a long time**, has obtained force of law among Hindus in any:
  - Local area
  - Tribe
  - Community
  - Group
  - Family

**Requirements:**
1. Rule must be **certain**
2. Must **not be unreasonable**
3. Must **not be opposed to public policy**
4. If applicable only to family, must **not have been discontinued** by family

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: Custom must satisfy criteria in Section 3(a) and must govern **both parties**

---

#### üö´ Degrees of Prohibited Relationship (Section 3(g))

Two persons are within "degrees of prohibited relationship" if:

| Clause | Relationship |
|--------|--------------|
| **(i)** | One is a **lineal ascendant** of the other<br>‚Ä¢ No limit of degrees<br>‚Ä¢ Ancestor in unbroken line of ascent |
| **(ii)** | One was the **wife/husband of lineal ascendant or descendant** of the other<br>‚Ä¢ **Example**: Cannot marry father's wife (stepmother), grandfather's wife, son's wife, daughter's husband |
| **(iii)** | One was the **wife of brother**, or of **father's/mother's brother**, or of **grandfather's/grandmother's brother** of the other |
| **(iv)** | The two are:<br>‚Ä¢ Brother and sister<br>‚Ä¢ Uncle and niece<br>‚Ä¢ Aunt and nephew<br>‚Ä¢ Children of brother and sister<br>‚Ä¢ Children of two brothers<br>‚Ä¢ Children of two sisters |

---

#### üìù Explanation - Types of Relationship

For purposes of prohibited relationship, "relationship" includes:

- **(I)** Relationship by **half or uterine blood** as well as **full blood**
- **(ii)** **Illegitimate** blood relationship as well as **legitimate**
- **(iii)** Relationship by **adoption** as well as by **blood**

**Definitions:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Full blood** | Descended from common ancestor by **same wife** |
| **Half blood** | Descended from common ancestor but by **different wives** |
| **Uterine blood** | Descended from common ancestor but by **different husbands** |

> üìå **Note**: "Ancestor" includes father; "ancestress" includes mother

### 1.4.6 Sapinda Relationship (Section 5(v))

> üìå **Why this matters**: Sapinda relationship is a complex but important topic. Questions test understanding of limits (3 generations maternal, 5 generations paternal) and the theories behind it.

#### üìñ Condition

**(v)** The parties are **not sapindas** of each other, unless **custom or usage** governing each of them permits such marriage.

---

#### üìã Sapinda Relationship (Section 3(f))

**(i)** "Sapinda relationship" with reference to any person extends:
- **Third generation (inclusive)** in line of ascent through **mother**
- **Fifth generation (inclusive)** in line of ascent through **father**
- Line traced **upwards** from person concerned
- Person concerned counted as **first generation**

**(ii)** Two persons are "sapinda" of each other if:
- One is a **lineal ascendant** of the other within limits of sapinda relationship, OR
- They have a **common lineal ascendant** who is within limits of sapinda relationship with reference to each of them

**Notation:**
- X = person concerned
- F = Father
- M = Mother
- MF = Mother's Father
- FFFF = Father's Father's Father's Father

**Example:**
- Sapinda relationship of X extends up to **MF (3 generations)** on mother's side
- Sapinda relationship of X extends up to **FFFF (5 generations)** on father's side
- In both cases, **X is counted as first generation**

---

#### üß† Theories of Sapinda Relationship

**Two theories propounded in ancient Hindu Law:**

##### 1. Oblation Theory

- Hindus believe in **ancestor worship**
- Offer **pinda-dan** (rice balls) to departed ancestors during shraddha
- **Rule**: 
  - One full pinda each to paternal ancestors
  - One full pinda each to two maternal ancestors
  - One divided pinda each to three next paternal ancestors
  - One divided pinda each to two next maternal ancestors
- Connected by pinda-dan to:
  - **6 ancestors** on paternal side
  - **4 ancestors** on maternal side
- When two persons offer pindas to **same ancestor**, they are sapindas to each other

##### 2. Particles of the Same Body Theory (Vijnaneshwara)

- Changed meaning of "pinda" from "ball" to **"particles of same body"**
- Sapinda relationship arises from being connected by **particles of one body**
- All those of **same blood** are related
- **Problem**: Too wide - could exist for 10, 20, 100+ generations
- **Solution**: Limited to:
  - **7th degree** on father's side
  - **5th degree** on mother's side

### 1.4.7 Ceremonies of Marriage (Section 7)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of required ceremonies, saptapadi, and special provisions. Often combined with validity of marriage.

#### üìñ Ceremonies for a Hindu Marriage

**(1)** A Hindu marriage may be solemnized in accordance with **customary rites and ceremonies of either party** thereto.

**(2)** Where such rites and ceremonies include **saptapadi** (taking of seven steps by bridegroom and bride jointly before sacred fire), the marriage becomes **complete and binding when the seventh step is taken**.

---

#### ‚úÖ Key Points

- Act gives **statutory recognition** to Saptapadi
- Saptapadi is **NOT obligatory**
- Ceremony means **customary rites and ceremonies of either party**
- Consists of:
  - **Kanyadan** (formal giving away)
  - **Acceptance of bride** before sacred fire
  - **Recitation of Vedic mantras**
  - **Saptapadi** (seven steps)

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Case Law: Sitabai v. Vithabai (1958 NLJ, 10)

**Principle:**
- Where marriage has in fact taken place, there is **dual presumption**:
  1. All **legal formalities** have been complied with
  2. All **necessary ceremonies** have been performed
- **Burden**: On person challenging validity to **rebut these presumptions**

---

#### üÜï Innovation of New Ceremonies

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **General Rule**: Ordinarily, **no one** (not even community/organization/movement) is free to alter, vary or create ceremony at pleasure

**Special Acts:**
- Arya Marriage Validation Act, 1937
- Vedic Marriage
- Anand Marriage Act, 1909 (Sikh Marriage)

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Devani v. Chindavaram (AIR 1954 Mad 657)

**Facts:**
- Tamil Nadu: Anti-Purohit Organization / Self-Respectors cult (50+ years)
- Objective: Do away with traditional rites and ceremonies
- Innovated simple rites: **Surya-mariyathai or Seerthiruththa marriages**
- Ceremony: Exchange of garlands and rings in presence of relatives/friends

**Holding:**
- Marriage held **void** - no one can alter personal law

**Result:**
- **Section 7-A** inserted by amendment (came into force 17/1/68)
- Applies **only to marriages in Tamil Nadu**

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è S. Naglingam v. Sivagami (2001) 7 SCC 487

**Principle:**
- Mere **execution of document** by spouses that they have become husband and wife, OR
- **Declaration** in presence of friends and other persons
- Will confer status of husband and wife (Section 7-A(1)(a))
- **Presence of priest is NOT necessary**

## Unit 2: Divorce

### 2.1 Theories of Divorce

> üìå **Why this matters**: Understanding the three theories helps in answering questions about grounds of divorce and their rationale. Often tested in essay questions.

#### üìä Three Theories Recognized by the Act

| Theory | Rationale | Section | Drawback |
|--------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **1. Fault Theory**<br>(Offences/Guilt Theory) | Marriage dissolved only when either party commits matrimonial wrong<br>Requires guilty party and innocent party<br>Only innocent party can seek divorce | Section 13(1) & 13(2) | If **both parties at fault**, no remedy available |
| **2. Mutual Consent Theory** | Since parties can marry by free will, they should be allowed to dissolve by free will | Section 13-B | Critics: Promotes immorality, leads to hasty divorces |
| **3. Irretrievable Breakdown Theory** | Marriage breakdown = failure in matrimonial relationship with no reasonable probability of spouses living together again<br>Should be dissolved with maximum fairness, minimum bitterness | Section 13(1-A) | - |

---

### 2.2 Grounds of Divorce

> üìå **Why this matters**: This is the MOST CRITICAL topic. Questions frequently list grounds, ask to explain specific grounds, or analyze whether divorce can be granted. Master Section 13 thoroughly.

#### üìú Historical Development

- **1955 (Original Act)**: Based on **fault theory**
  - Section 13(1): **9 fault grounds** (both spouses)
  - Section 13(2): **2 fault grounds** (wife only)
- **1964 Amendment**: Added Section 13(1-A) - **2 breakdown grounds**
- **1976 Amendment**: 
  - Added **2 additional fault grounds** for wife
  - Added **Section 13-B** (mutual consent)

### 2.2.1 Grounds Available to Both Husband and Wife (Section 13(1))

> üìå **Why this matters**: These 9 grounds are frequently tested. Questions ask to list, explain, or apply them to fact situations.

#### üìã Section 13(1) - Divorce Grounds

Any marriage may be dissolved by decree of divorce on petition by either husband or wife, on ground that other party:

---

#### 1. Adultery (Section 13(1)(i))

**Ground**: Has, after solemnization of marriage, had **voluntary sexual intercourse** with any person other than his/her spouse.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: Wife living in adultery is **NOT entitled to maintenance** under:
- Section 125(4) of Cr.P.C.
- Section 18(2) of HAMA, 1956

**Case Law:**
- **Joseph Shine v. Union of India** (27th Sep. 2018 SC)
  - Adultery **NOT an offence** under Section 497 IPC
  - **Still a ground of divorce**

---

#### 2. Cruelty (Section 13(1)(ia))

**Ground**: Has, after solemnization of marriage, **treated the petitioner with cruelty**.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Cruelty is a broad ground - can be physical or mental. Courts interpret liberally.

---

#### 3. Desertion (Section 13(1)(ib))

**Ground**: Has **deserted the petitioner** for a **continuous period of not less than 2 years** immediately preceding presentation of petition.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Must be continuous, must be 2 years, must be immediately before petition.

---

#### 4. Conversion (Section 13(1)(ii))

**Ground**: Has **ceased to be a Hindu** by conversion to another religion.

---

#### 5. Mental Capacity (Section 13(1)(iii))

**Ground**: Has been **incurably of unsound mind**, OR has been suffering **continuously or intermittently** from mental disorder of such kind and extent that petitioner **cannot reasonably be expected to live** with respondent.

**Explanation:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Mental disorder** | Mental illness, arrested/incomplete development of mind, psychopathic disorder, or any other disorder/disability of mind<br>**Includes**: Schizophrenia |
| **Psychopathic disorder** | Persistent disorder/disability of mind (whether or not including sub-normality of intelligence) which results in:<br>‚Ä¢ Abnormally aggressive conduct, OR<br>‚Ä¢ Seriously irresponsible conduct<br>Whether or not requires/susceptible to medical treatment |

---

#### 6. Leprosy (Section 13(1)(iv))

**Ground**: Has been suffering from **virulent and incurable form of leprosy**.

---

#### 7. Venereal Disease (Section 13(1)(v))

**Ground**: Has been suffering from **venereal disease in communicable form**.

---

#### 8. Renunciation of World (Section 13(1)(vi))

**Ground**: Has **renounced the world** by entering any religious order.

---

#### 9. Not Been Heard Of (Section 13(1)(vii))

**Ground**: Has **not been heard of as being alive** for period of **7 years or more** by those persons who would naturally have heard of it, had that party been alive.

---

### 2.2.2 Breakdown Theory Grounds (Section 13(1-A))

> üìå **Why this matters**: These grounds represent the breakdown theory. Questions test understanding of when these grounds become available (after 1 year of decree).

#### üìã Section 13(1-A) - Breakdown Grounds

Either party may present petition for divorce on ground:

---

#### 1. Failure of Judicial Separation (Section 13(1-A)(i))

**Ground**: There has been **no resumption of cohabitation** for period of **1 year or upwards** after passing of decree for judicial separation in proceeding to which they were parties.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Must have decree of judicial separation first, then wait 1 year.

---

#### 2. Failure of Restitution of Conjugal Rights (Section 13(1-A)(ii))

**Ground**: There has been **no restitution of conjugal rights** for period of **1 year or upwards** after passing of decree of restitution of conjugal rights in proceeding to which they were parties.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Must have decree of restitution first, then wait 1 year.

---

### 2.2.3 Grounds Available to Wife Only (Section 13(2))

> üìå **Why this matters**: These are special protections for wives. Questions test understanding of why these are wife-only grounds and their requirements.

#### üìã Section 13(2) - Wife Only Grounds

A wife may present petition for divorce on ground:

---

#### 1. Bigamy (Section 13(2)(i))

**Ground**: In case of marriage solemnized **before commencement of this Act**, that:
- Husband had **married again before such commencement**, OR
- Any other wife of husband married before such commencement was **alive at time of solemnization** of petitioner's marriage

**Condition**: In either case, **other wife must be alive** at time of presentation of petition.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: This applies only to marriages before 1955. For post-1955 bigamy, see Section 11 (void marriage).

---

#### 2. Rape, Sodomy or Bestiality (Section 13(2)(ii))

**Ground**: Husband has, since solemnization of marriage, been guilty of:
- **Rape**
- **Sodomy**
- **Bestiality**

---

#### 3. Non-Resumption of Cohabitation After Order of Maintenance (Section 13(2)(iii))

**Ground**: 
- Order for maintenance (civil or criminal) passed against husband
- Since passing of such decree/order, **cohabitation has not been resumed** for **1 year or upwards**

---

#### 4. Repudiation of Marriage (Section 13(2)(iv))

**Ground**: 
- Marriage (whether consummated or not) was solemnized **before she attained age of 15 years**
- She has **repudiated the marriage** after attaining that age but **before attaining age of 18 years**

---

### üìã Alternate Relief in Divorce Proceedings (Section 13-A)

If in any proceeding for divorce (except grounds in clauses (ii), (vi), (vii) of Section 13(1)), court may, if just, pass **decree for judicial separation** instead of divorce.

**Exceptions (where alternate relief NOT available):**
- Clause (ii) - Conversion
- Clause (vi) - Renunciation of World
- Clause (vii) - Not Heard Of

---

### 2.3 Divorce by Mutual Consent (Section 13-B)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Mutual consent divorce is frequently tested. Questions ask about requirements, cooling period, withdrawal of consent, and recent developments.

#### üìñ Section 13-B - Divorce by Mutual Consent

**(1)** Subject to provisions of this Act, petition for dissolution by decree of divorce may be presented to District Court by **both parties together**, whether marriage was solemnized before or after commencement of Marriage Laws (Amendment) Act, 1976, on ground that:
- They have been **living separately for 1 year or more**
- They have **not been able to live together**
- They have **mutually agreed** that marriage should be dissolved

**(2)** On motion of both parties made:
- **Earlier than 6 months** after date of presentation of petition, AND
- **Not later than 18 months** after said date

If petition not withdrawn in meantime, court shall, on being satisfied (after hearing parties and inquiry), that:
- Marriage has been solemnized
- Averments in petition are true

Pass decree of divorce declaring marriage dissolved with effect from date of decree.

---

#### ‚úÖ Requirements (Section 13-B)

1. ‚úÖ **Joint petition by both parties**
2. ‚úÖ **Wait at least 6 months** after petition
3. ‚úÖ **Make joint motion before expiry of 18 months** from petition
4. ‚úÖ **May withdraw petition** during this time
5. ‚úÖ **After hearing and inquiry**, court shall pass decree

---

#### üìã Grounds of Petition (Section 13-B)

1. ‚úÖ Parties are **living separately for 1 year or more** before petition filed
2. ‚úÖ They have **not been able to live together**
3. ‚úÖ They have **mutually agreed** that marriage should be dissolved

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Important Case Laws

**Sureshta Devi v. Om Prakash** (1991) 2 SCC 25

**Principles:**
- If one party **withdraws consent**, court gets **no jurisdiction** to pass decree
- **Mutual consent must continue** till passing of decree
- **Not necessary** that parties live under separate roofs
- They might live in **same house but not as husband and wife**
- "Not been able to live together" = **complete breakdown of marriage**

---

**D.S. Narula v. Meenakshi Nangia** (AIR 2012 SC 2890)

**Principle**: If circumstances so warrant, **cooling period of 6 months can be waived**.

---

**Amardeep Singh v. Harveen Kaur** (Sep. 12, 2017)

**Principle**: Minimum period of **6 months is NOT mandatory** and can be relaxed in **exceptional situations**.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Recent trend is to waive cooling period in appropriate cases. Courts have discretion.

---

### 2.4 Customary Divorce

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of how custom operates alongside statutory law. Important for understanding Section 29(2) savings.

#### üìñ Savings (Section 29(2))

Nothing in this Act shall be deemed to affect:
- Any **right recognized by custom** to obtain dissolution of Hindu Marriage, OR
- Any right **conferred by special enactment** to obtain dissolution

**Applies to**: Marriages solemnized before or after commencement of this Act.

**Key Points:**
1. **Customary grounds and modes** of divorce are continued
2. **Grounds under special enactments** are also continued

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Divorce Under Custom

**General Rule**: Divorce under custom is available **unless** particular ground/mode found to be **contrary to public policy or morality**.

---

#### üìú Important Case Laws

| Case | Principle |
|------|-----------|
| **Shakuntalabai v. Kulkarni** (1989 SC 1309) | Ancient and unbroken custom of divorce recognized<br>Valid marriage can be dissolved by customary mode<br>**No provisions of HMA, 1955 apply** to such divorces |
| **Gurdit v. Angrej** (1968 SC 142) | Custom recognizing divorce must **fulfill all tests of valid custom** |
| **Chukna v. Lacuma** (1969 SC 605) | Customary divorce may be obtained through:<br>‚Ä¢ Gram panchayat<br>‚Ä¢ Caste tribunal/panchayat<br>‚Ä¢ Private act of parties (oral/written)<br>‚Ä¢ Agreement (tyag-patra, farkatnama, sulenama) |
| **Subramani v. Chandralekha** (AIR 2005 SC 485) | Dissolution by custom must be **specifically pleaded and established**<br>Custom prevailing in **community of parties** is must |

### 2.5 Restrictions on Divorce

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of time restrictions and when exceptions apply. Important for procedural aspects.

#### üö´ No Divorce Within One Year of Marriage (Section 14)

**(1)** Notwithstanding anything in this Act, **no court shall entertain** petition for dissolution by decree of divorce, unless **1 year has elapsed** since date of marriage.

---

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Exception to Section 14(1)

Court may, upon application, **allow petition before 1 year** if:
- Case is one of **exceptional hardship** to petitioner, OR
- Case is one of **exceptional depravity** on part of respondent

**Rules**: In accordance with rules made by High Court.

**Consequences of Misrepresentation:**
- If petitioner obtained leave by **misrepresentation or concealment**, decree shall **not have effect** until after expiry of 1 year, OR
- Court may **dismiss the petition**

**Proviso**: Court may dismiss petition **without prejudice** to any petition which may be brought after expiration of 1 year upon same or substantially same facts.

---

#### üìã Section 14(2) - Court's Duty

Court shall have regard to:
- **Interests of any children** of the marriage
- **Question whether there is reasonable probability of reconciliation** before expiration of 1 year

---

#### üíí When Divorced Persons Remarry (Section 15)

**When a marriage has been dissolved by decree of divorce**, it shall be lawful for either party to marry again when:
- There is **no right of appeal** against decree, OR
- If right of appeal exists:
  - **Time for appealing has expired** without appeal being presented, OR
  - **Appeal has been presented but dismissed**

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Must wait for appeal period or dismissal before remarriage.

## Unit 3: Nullity of Marriage

### 3.1 Void Marriage (Section 11)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Void marriages are frequently tested. Questions ask to identify void marriages, explain consequences, and distinguish from voidable. Critical distinction.

#### üìñ The Law of Nullity

**Concept:**
- Relates to **pre-marriage impediments** to marriage
- Such impediments relate to **capacity to marry**
- If they exist, parties **cannot marry each other**
- Still, if they marry, marriage may not be valid

**Two Types of Impediments:**

| Type | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Absolute Impediments** | Marriage is **void ab initio** |
| **Relative Impediments** | Marriage is **voidable** |

---

#### ‚ùå Characteristics of Void Marriage

1. **Void marriage is NO marriage**
2. **Does not exist from beginning**
3. **No legal consequences** flow from it:
   - No status
   - No rights and obligations
   - No inheritance
   - etc.

---

#### üìã Void Marriages (Section 11)

Any marriage solemnized **after commencement of this Act** shall be **null and void** and may be declared so by decree of nullity if it contravenes **any one** of conditions in:
- **Clause (i)** - Bigamy (Section 5(i))
- **Clause (iv)** - Prohibited relationship (Section 5(iv))
- **Clause (v)** - Sapinda relationship (Section 5(v))

---

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Important Points

- **No decree necessary**: In respect of void marriages, no decree of court is necessary
- **Judicial declaration**: Even if petition filed, court merely makes **judicial declaration** of existing fact
- **Who can petition**: Petition can only be made by **either party to the marriage**

**Special Case - Bigamy:**
- Where marriage is void because person has taken second wife (first wife alive):
  - **Second wife** can petition under Section 11 (she was party to void marriage)
  - **First wife** has no such right under Section 11
  - **First wife** may bring **declaratory suit** under Section 9 CPC read with Section 34 of Specific Relief Act, 1963

---

#### üìã Grounds of Void Marriage (Section 11)

If marriage solemnized in contravention of:

1. **Bigamous Marriage** - Section 5(i)
2. **Prohibited Relationship** - Section 5(iv)
3. **Sapinda Relationship** - Section 5(v)

---

#### üìã Other Grounds of Void Marriage (Apart from Section 11)

1. **Both parties not Hindus** - Section 1(2) read with Section 5
2. **Proper ceremonies not performed** - Section 7
3. **Marriage in violation of Section 15** - Divorced person marrying before appeal period over

---

### 3.2 Voidable Marriage (Section 12)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Voidable marriages are crucial. Questions test understanding of when marriage becomes voidable, who can avoid it, and consequences. Must distinguish from void.

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Characteristics of Voidable Marriage

1. **Valid until avoided**: Voidable marriage is **perfectly valid** so long as not avoided
2. **Can be avoided only by party**: Can be avoided only on **petition of one of parties** to marriage
3. **Remains valid if not avoided**: If parties do not petition, marriage **remains valid**
4. **Cannot challenge after death**: If one party dies before annulment, **no one can challenge** marriage
5. **Legal consequences flow**: So long as not avoided, **all legal consequences** of valid marriage flow from it
6. **Retrospective effect**: Once challenged and declared void, decree has **retrospective effect** from date of marriage
7. **Deemed never married**: Marriage deemed to have been **void for all purposes from inception**
8. **Children illegitimate**: Parties deemed to have **never been husband and wife**, children deemed **illegitimate**

---

#### üìã Voidable Marriages (Section 12)

**(1)** Any marriage solemnized (before or after commencement of this Act) shall be **voidable** and may be **annulled by decree of nullity** on any of following grounds:

---

#### 1. Impotency (Section 12(1)(a))

**Ground**: Marriage has **not been consummated** owing to **impotency of respondent**.

**Definitions:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Consummation** | Full and normal **sexual intercourse** between married persons after marriage |
| **Impotency** | Lack or absence of capacity to consummate marriage by act of **normal, natural and complete intercourse**<br>State of mental or physical condition making consummation **practical impossibility**<br>Synonymous with incapacity for sexual intercourse |

**Case Laws:**
- **Digvijay Singh v. Pratap Kumari** (AIR 1970 SC 137)
  - Party must be held impotent if mental/physical condition makes consummation **impossible**
- **Laxmi v. Babulal** (AIR 1973 Raj 39)
- **Shevanti v. Bhaurav** (AIR 1971 MP 168)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: **Sterility is different from impotency**. Sterile person is NOT necessarily impotent.

---

#### 2. Mental Capacity (Section 12(1)(b))

**Ground**: Marriage is in contravention of condition specified in **clause (ii) of Section 5**.

**Section 5(ii)** - At time of marriage, neither party:
- **(a)** Is incapable of giving valid consent due to unsoundness of mind, OR
- **(b)** Though capable of giving valid consent, has been suffering from mental disorder of such kind/extent as to be unfit for marriage and procreation of children, OR
- **(c)** Has been subject to recurrent attacks of insanity or epilepsy

---

#### 3. Consent by Force or Fraud (Section 12(1)(c))

**Ground**: Consent of petitioner (or guardian in marriage of petitioner where required under Section 5 as it stood before Child Marriage Restraint (Amendment) Act, 1978) was obtained by:
- **Force**, OR
- **Fraud** as to:
  - Nature of ceremony, OR
  - Any material fact or circumstance concerning respondent

---

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Restrictions on Section 12(1)(c)

**(2)(a)** No petition for annulling marriage on ground in clause (c) shall be entertained if:

- **(i)** Petition presented **more than 1 year** after:
  - Force had ceased to operate, OR
  - Fraud had been discovered, OR
- **(ii)** Petitioner has, with **full consent, lived with other party** as husband/wife after:
  - Force had ceased to operate, OR
  - Fraud had been discovered

**Requirements:**
1. Consent obtained by **force or fraud**
2. Petition must be presented **within 1 year** of discovery of force/fraud
3. Petitioner must **not have lived** with respondent (with consent) as husband/wife after discovery

---

#### 4. Pre-Marriage Pregnancy (Section 12(1)(d))

**Ground**: Respondent was at time of marriage **pregnant by some person other than petitioner**.

---

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Restrictions on Section 12(1)(d)

**(2)(b)** No petition for annulling marriage on ground in clause (d) shall be entertained **unless** court satisfied:

- **(i)** Petitioner was at time of marriage **ignorant of facts** alleged
- **(ii)** Proceedings instituted:
  - In case of marriage before commencement of Act: **Within 1 year of such commencement**
  - In case of marriage after commencement: **Within 1 year from date of marriage**
- **(iii)** **Marital intercourse** (with consent of petitioner) has **not taken place** since discovery by petitioner of existence of said ground

---

### 3.3 Legitimacy of Children (Section 16)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of legitimacy of children from void/voidable marriages. Important for inheritance and status issues.

#### üìñ Section 16 - Legitimacy of Children

**(1)** Notwithstanding that marriage is **null and void under Section 11**, any child of such marriage who would have been legitimate if marriage had been valid, shall be **legitimate**, whether:
- Child born **before or after** commencement of Marriage Laws (Amendment) Act, 1976
- **Decree of nullity granted** or not
- Marriage held void **otherwise than on petition** under this Act

**(2)** Where decree of nullity granted in respect of **voidable marriage under Section 12**, any child **begotten or conceived before decree** is made, who would have been legitimate child of parties if at date of decree it had been dissolved instead of annulled, shall be **deemed to be their legitimate child** notwithstanding decree of nullity.

**(3)** Nothing in sub-section (1) or (2) shall be construed as conferring upon any child of marriage which is null and void or annulled by decree of nullity under Section 12, any **rights in or to property of any person other than parents**, in any case where, but for passing of this Act, such child would have been incapable of possessing or acquiring any such rights by reason of not being legitimate child of parents.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Children are legitimate, but property rights limited to parents only (not extended family).

## Unit 4: Other Matrimonial Reliefs

### 4.1 Restitution of Conjugal Rights (Section 9)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Restitution is frequently tested. Questions ask about requirements, constitutional validity, and when it becomes ground for divorce. Important for understanding Section 13(1-A)(ii).

#### üìñ Section 9 - Restitution of Conjugal Rights

When either husband or wife has, **without reasonable excuse**:
1. **Withdrawn from society** of the other
2. The **aggrieved party** may apply by petition to district court
3. For **restitution of conjugal rights**
4. Court, on being satisfied of truth of statements and that there is **no legal ground** why application should not be granted
5. May **decree restitution** accordingly

---

#### üìñ Meaning of Conjugal Rights

- **Conjugal** = Matrimonial or Marital
- **Restitution** = Restoring to party through decree what has been lost
- **Restoration** of marital rights lost to party

**Two Ideas:**
1. **Right of couple** to have each other's society
2. **Right to marital intercourse**

---

#### ‚úÖ Requirements of Section 9

1. ‚úÖ **Valid marriage** between husband and wife (or voidable but not rescinded)
2. ‚úÖ **Withdrawal from society**
3. ‚úÖ **Without reasonable excuse**
4. ‚úÖ **Court's satisfaction** as to truth of statements
5. ‚úÖ **No legal ground** exists for refusing decree

---

#### üìã Withdrawal from Society

**Meaning:**
- **Cessation of cohabitation** by voluntary act of respondent
- Withdrawal means not from company but from **conjugal relationship**
- **Refusal to stay together**, to have marital intercourse

**Case Laws on Wife's Employment:**

| Case | Holding |
|------|---------|
| **Tirath Kaur v. Kripal Singh** (1964) | - |
| **Surinder v. Gurdeep** (1973) | - |
| **Kailashwati v. Ayodhya Prakash** (1977 P&H) | Wife's refusal to give up job at instance of husband = withdrawal from society |
| **Shanti Nigam v. R.C. Nigam** (1971 ALJ 67 All. HC) | Mere refusal to resign job is **NOT sufficient ground**<br>Wife taking up job contrary to husband's wishes does **NOT amount to withdrawal** |
| **Pravinben v. Sureshbhai** (1975 Guj)<br>**N.R. Radhakrishnan v. Dhanlaxmi** (1975 Mad)<br>**Mirchumal v. Devi** (1977 Raj) | Proposition that wife must always stay under husband's roof was right in past, **no longer true**<br>Wife working at different place does **NOT amount to withdrawal** |

---

#### üìã Without Reasonable Excuse

- **Not laid down** by Section 9
- To be decided by court on basis of **facts and circumstances** of each case
- On basis of **discretionary powers**
- One cause may be reasonable in one case, not in another

**Case Laws:**

| Case | Principle |
|------|-----------|
| **Chand Narain v. Saroj** (AIR 1975 Raj 88) | Husband insisting wife take non-vegetarian food and drinking = cruelty |
| **Gurdial Kaur v. Mukund Singh** (AIR 1967 Pun) | Husband contracting second marriage in violation of law = sufficient cause |
| **Mohinder Singh v. Preet Kaur** (1981 HLR 321) | Husband became blind after 6 months of marriage = reasonable |
| **Vijay Kumar v. Suman** (1996 HLR 24) | Persistent demand for dowry causing physical/mental torture = reasonable |

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Burden of Proof

**Explanation**: Where question arises whether there has been reasonable excuse for withdrawal, **burden of proving reasonable excuse** shall be on person who has withdrawn from society.

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Execution of Decree (Order 21 Rule 32 of CPC)

If respondent has **willfully failed to obey** decree after being given opportunity:

**General Rule:**
- Court may, in execution, **attach property** of respondent
- If within **1 year after attachment**, decree not complied with
- **Attached property may be sold**
- Out of sale proceeds, court may award **compensation** to petitioner

**Special Rule (Wife as Petitioner):**
- If petitioner is **wife** (not husband)
- Court may make order that if decree not obeyed within specified time
- Respondent shall make such **periodical payments** to petitioner as court thinks reasonable

---

#### üìã Legal Effect of Decree

If cohabitation **not resumed** within period of **1 year or more** after passing of decree of restitution of conjugal rights, it becomes **distinct ground of divorce** under **Section 13(1-A)(ii)**.

---

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Constitutional Validity of Section 9

**T. Sareetaha v. T. Venkatta Subbaiah** (AIR 1983 AP 356)
- Andhra Pradesh High Court: **Violates right to privacy**
- Constitutes grossest form of violation of individual's right to privacy
- Denies woman free choice whether, when and how to become vehicle for procreation
- Deprives woman of control over her choice

**Harvinder Kaur v. Harmander Singh** (AIR 1984 Del 66)
- Delhi High Court: **Constitutional**
- Object is cohabitation, not merely sexual intercourse
- Aims at stabilizing marriage and encouraging reconciliation
- Parties can seek divorce if they do not come together

**Saroj Rani v. Sudershan Kumar** (AIR 1984 SC 1562)
- **Supreme Court**: **NOT violative of Constitution**
- Settled the controversy
- Serves social purpose as aid to prevention of break-up in marriage
- If cohabitation not resumed within 1 year, becomes ground for divorce

**Ojaswa Pathak & Mayank Gupta v. Union of India**
- PIL filed by two students of Gujarat National Law University
- Challenged constitutional validity on ground it violates **Article 15(1)**
- Based on feudal English law, treats women as chattel
- **Pending in Supreme Court**

### 4.2 Judicial Separation (Section 10)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Judicial separation is important. Questions test understanding of grounds, effect, and when it becomes ground for divorce. Often combined with Section 13(1-A)(i).

#### üìñ Section 10 - Judicial Separation

**(1)** Either party to marriage (whether solemnized before or after commencement of Act) may present petition praying for decree for judicial separation on:
- Any of grounds specified in **sub-section (1) of Section 13** (both spouses), AND
- In case of wife, also on any of grounds specified in **sub-section (2) of Section 13** (wife only)

**(2)** Where decree for judicial separation passed:
- It shall **no longer be obligatory** for petitioner to cohabit with respondent
- Court may, on application by petition of either party and on being satisfied of truth of statements, **rescind the decree** if it considers it just and reasonable to do so

---

#### üìñ Meaning of Judicial Separation

- Parties to marriage are at **liberty to live separately** by order of court
- **Rights and obligations remain suspended** during period of separation
- Judicial Separation is **step prior to divorce**
- Purpose: Provide **opportunity to parties to reconcile** differences
- **Severance of connection** among spouses by order of Court
- Severance is **temporary**
- **Does not affect status** of parties as Husband and wife
- Relief to aggrieved spouse to live separately from spouse guilty of matrimonial offence
- By orders of Court **without dissolving marriage** completely and forever

---

#### ‚úÖ Requirements of Section 10

- Either party to marriage can file petition
- Whether marriage solemnized before or after commencement of HMA, 1955
- After decree, they are **not bound to cohabit** with each other
- Some matrimonial rights and obligations, however, **continue to subsist**

---

#### üìã Grounds of Judicial Separation

**Same as grounds for divorce:**
- Grounds on which petition for divorce might have been presented
- **Section 13(1)** - Grounds available to both husband and wife
- **Section 13(2)** - Grounds available to wife only

---

#### üìã Grounds Available to Both Husband and Wife (Section 13(1))

1. **Adultery** - Voluntary sexual intercourse with person other than spouse
2. **Cruelty** - Treated with cruelty after marriage
3. **Desertion** - Deserted for continuous period of 2 years immediately preceding petition
4. **Conversion** - Ceased to be Hindu
5. **Insanity** - Unsound mind or mental disorder of such kind/extent that petitioner cannot live with other party
6. **Leprosy** - Virulent and incurable form
7. **Venereal disease** - In communicable form
8. **Renounced the world** - Entered religious order
9. **Not been heard alive for 7 years**

---

#### üìã Grounds Available to Wife Only (Section 13(2))

1. **Husband has more than one wife living** - Husband married before commencement of Act and remarried after, either wife can present suit (provided other wife alive at time of petition)
2. **Rape, Sodomy or Bestiality** - Husband guilty of such offence
3. **Marriage before age of 15** - Marriage solemnized before she attained 15 years, she repudiated after 15 but before 18

---

#### üìã Rescission of Decree

Court may, on application by petition of either party and on being satisfied of truth of statements, **rescind the decree** if it considers it just and reasonable to do so.

---

#### üìã Legal Effect of Decree

1. **Ground for divorce**: If cohabitation not resumed within **1 year or more**, becomes distinct ground of divorce under **Section 13(1-A)(i)**
2. **Cannot contract another marriage**: Neither party can contract another marriage
3. **Bigamy**: Marriage after separation and before divorce amounts to **bigamy**
4. **Rape**: Intercourse by man with his wife during separation is offence of **rape (IPC Section 376-B)**
5. **Maintenance and custody**: Wife's right to maintenance, custody of children may be decided by court
6. **Succession**: Right to succession **continues to exist**

## Unit 5: Jurisdiction and Procedure

### 5.1 Jurisdiction Under Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of which court has jurisdiction and procedural requirements. Important for practical application.

#### üìã Jurisdiction and Procedure - Key Sections

1. Court to which petition shall be presented (Section 19)
2. Contents and verification of Petitions (Section 20)
3. Application of Code of Civil Procedure 1908 (Section 21)
4. Power to transfer petitions in certain cases (Section 21-A)
5. Special provision relating to trial and disposal of petitions (Section 21-B)
6. Documentary evidence (Section 21-C)
7. Proceedings to be in camera and may not be printed or published (Section 22)

---

#### üìã Court to Which Petition Shall be Presented (Section 19)

Every petition under this Act shall be presented to **District Court** within local limits of whose ordinary original civil jurisdiction:

| Clause | Place |
|--------|-------|
| **(i)** | **Marriage was solemnized**, OR |
| **(ii)** | **Respondent resides** at time of presentation of petition, OR |
| **(iii)** | **Parties last resided together**, OR |
| **(iv)** | **Petitioner is residing** at time of presentation (in case where respondent is residing outside territories to which Act extends, OR has not been heard of as alive for 7 years or more) |

---

#### üìã District Court (Section 1(b))

"District Court" means:
- In any area for which there is **City Civil Court**: That Court
- In any other area: **Principal Civil Court** of original jurisdiction
- **Includes**: Any other civil court specified by State Government by notification in Official Gazette as having jurisdiction

---

#### üìã Contents and Verification of Petitions (Section 20)

**(1)** Every petition shall state:
- As **distinctly as nature of case permits**, the **facts** on which claim to relief is founded
- **Except in petition under Section 11**, shall also state that there is **no collusion** between petitioner and other party

**(2)** Statements contained in every petition shall be **verified** by petitioner or some other competent person in manner required by law for verification of plaints
- May, at hearing, be **referred to as evidence**

---

#### üìã Application of Code of Civil Procedure 1908 (Section 21)

Subject to other provisions in this Act and to such rules as High Court may make, **all proceedings** under this Act shall be regulated, as far as may be, by **Code of Civil Procedure, 1908**.

**Example**: Bombay High Court Hindu Marriage Rules, 1960

---

#### üìã Power to Transfer Petitions (Section 21-A)

**(1)** Where:
- **(a)** Petition under this Act presented to District Court having jurisdiction by party praying for decree for judicial separation (Section 10) or divorce (Section 13), AND
- **(b)** Another petition under this Act presented thereafter by other party praying for decree for judicial separation (Section 10) or divorce (Section 13) on any ground, whether in same District Court or different District Court, in same State or different State

Petitions shall be dealt with as specified in sub-section (2).

**(2)** In case where sub-section (1) applies:
- **(a)** If petitions presented to **same District Court**: Both petitions shall be **tried and heard together** by that District Court
- **(b)** If petitions presented to **different District Courts**: Petition presented later shall be **transferred** to District Court in which earlier petition was presented, and both petitions shall be **heard and disposed of together** by district court in which earlier petition was presented

---

#### üìã Special Provision Relating to Trial (Section 21-B)

**(1)** Trial of petition shall, so far as practicable consistently with interests of justice, be **continued from day to day** until conclusion, unless Court finds adjournment beyond following day necessary for reasons to be recorded.

**(2)** Every petition shall be **tried as expeditiously as possible**
- Endeavour shall be made to **conclude trial within 6 months** from date of service of notice on respondent

**(3)** Every appeal shall be **heard as expeditiously as possible**
- Endeavour shall be made to **conclude hearing within 3 months** from date of service of notice of appeal on respondent

---

#### üìã Documentary Evidence (Section 21-C)

Notwithstanding anything in any enactment to contrary, **no document** shall be inadmissible in evidence in any proceeding at trial of petition on ground that it is **not duly stamped or registered**.

---

#### üìã Proceedings to be in Camera (Section 22)

**(1)** Every proceeding under this Act shall be conducted **in camera**
- It shall **not be lawful** for any person to print or publish any matter in relation to any such proceeding
- **Exception**: Judgment of High Court or Supreme Court printed or published with **previous permission** of Court

**(2)** If any person prints or publishes any matter in contravention, he shall be **punishable with fine** which may extend to **one thousand rupees**

---

### 5.2 Jurisdiction Under Family Courts Act, 1984

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of Family Courts jurisdiction and when they have exclusive jurisdiction.

#### üìã Section 7 - Jurisdiction of Family Court

Family Court exercises **all jurisdiction** exercisable by any district court established under any law in respect of following matters:

**A. Suit or proceeding between parties to marriage for:**
1. **Decree of nullity**
2. **Restitution of conjugal rights**
3. **Judicial separation**
4. **Dissolution of marriage**

**B. Suit or proceeding for:**
- Declaration as to **validity of marriage**
- Declaration as to **matrimonial status** of any person

**C. Suit or proceeding between parties to marriage:**
- With respect to **property of parties** or of either of them

**D. Suit or proceeding for:**
- **Order or injunction** in circumstances arising out of matrimonial relationship

**E. Suit or proceeding for:**
- Declaration for **legitimacy of any person**

**F. Suit or proceeding in relation to:**
- **Guardianship** of any person
- **Custody of or access to** any minor

**G. Jurisdiction exercisable by Judicial Magistrate First Class under Cr.P.C. Chapter-IX:**
- Relating to **maintenance of children, parents and wives**

**H. Such other jurisdiction:**
- Ordered by any other statute

## Unit 6: Bars to Matrimonial Reliefs

### 6.1 Section 23 - General Bars to Matrimonial Reliefs

> üìå **Why this matters**: Section 23 bars are frequently tested. Questions ask about taking advantage of own wrong, condonation, collusion, delay, etc. Critical for understanding when relief may be denied.

#### üìñ Section 23 - Bar to Matrimonial Relief

**(1)** In any proceeding under this Act (whether defended or not), if Court is satisfied that:

---

#### ‚úÖ (a) Taking Advantage of One's Own Wrong or Disability

**Requirement**: Petitioner (except in cases where relief sought on grounds in Section 5(ii)(a)(b)(c)) is **not in any way taking advantage** of his/her own wrong or disability for purpose of such relief.

**Principles:**
- **One who comes to equity must come with clean hands**
- **One should not take advantage of his own wrong**
- **Burden of proof**: On petitioner to show he is not taking advantage of own wrong

**Examples:**
- If respondent contracted venereal disease **from petitioner**, petitioner cannot get divorce on this ground
- If cruelty of husband results in withdrawal of society by wife, husband cannot get divorce on ground of desertion

**Case Law:**
- **Ashok v. Shabanam** (1989 Del 121)
  - Where husband harassed wife for dowry and abandoned her, he **cannot be granted decree of divorce** on ground of desertion
  - Would amount to taking advantage of own wrong

**Exception:**
- By virtue of 1976 amendment, this section **does not apply** to petitions for nullity of marriage on ground of respondent's insanity (Section 5(ii)(a)(b)(c))

---

#### ‚úÖ (b) Accessory, Connivance and Condonation

**Requirement**: Where ground of petition is:
- Ground in **clause (i) of Section 13(1)** (Adultery), OR
- Ground is **Cruelty**

Petitioner has **not in any manner** been:
- **Accessory to**, OR
- **Connived at**, OR
- **Condoned** the act/acts complained of

**Definitions:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **ACCESSORY** | Person who helps in commission of wrongful act |
| **CONNIVANCE** | Active consent |
| **CONDONATION** | To condone the wrongful act (in case of cruelty) |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: This bar applies **only when petition filed on ground of Adultery or Cruelty**

---

#### ‚úÖ (bb) Free Consent (Divorce by Mutual Consent)

**Requirement**: When divorce sought on ground of mutual consent, such consent has **not been obtained by**:
- **Force**
- **Fraud**
- **Undue influence**

---

#### ‚úÖ (c) Collusion

**Requirement**: Petition (not being petition under Section 11) is **not presented or prosecuted in collusion** with respondent.

**Meaning**: Arrangement or understanding between parties

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: **Not applicable to nullity** (Section 11)

---

#### ‚úÖ (d) Delay

**Requirement**: There has **not been any unnecessary or improper delay** in instituting proceeding.

---

#### ‚úÖ (e) No Other Legal Ground

**Requirement**: There is **no other legal ground** why relief should not be granted

---

#### üìã Then and Only Then

If all above conditions satisfied, **then, and in such case, but not otherwise**, court shall decree such relief accordingly.

---

### 6.2 Reconciliation Provisions (Section 23(2))

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of court's duty to attempt reconciliation and when it doesn't apply.

#### üìñ Section 23(2) - Duty to Attempt Reconciliation

**(2)** Before proceeding to grant any relief under this Act, it shall be **duty of Court** in first instance, in every case where it is possible so to do consistently with nature and circumstances of case, to **make every endeavour to bring about reconciliation** between parties.

**Exception (Proviso):**
- Nothing in this sub-section shall apply to proceeding wherein relief is sought on any of grounds in:
  - Clause (ii) - Conversion
  - Clause (iii) - Unsound mind
  - Clause (iv) - Leprosy
  - Clause (v) - Venereal disease
  - Clause (vi) - Renounced the world
  - Clause (vii) - Not been heard of

---

#### üìñ Section 23(3) - Adjournment for Reconciliation

**(3)** For purpose of aiding Court in bringing about reconciliation, court may:
- If parties so desire, OR
- If Court thinks it just and proper

**Adjourn proceedings** for reasonable period **not exceeding 15 days** and:
- **Refer matter** to any person named by parties, OR
- **Refer matter** to any person nominated by Court (if parties fail to name any person)

**With directions** to report to Court as to:
- Whether reconciliation **can be** effected
- Whether reconciliation **has been** effected

Court shall in disposing of proceeding have **due regard to the report**.

---

#### üìñ Section 23(4) - Copy of Decree

**(4)** In every case where marriage is dissolved by decree of divorce, court passing decree shall give **copy thereof free of cost** to each of parties.

---

#### üìñ Section 23-A - Relief for Respondent

In any proceeding for:
- Divorce, OR
- Judicial separation, OR
- Restitution of conjugal rights

**Respondent may:**
- Not only **oppose the relief** sought on ground of petitioner's adultery, cruelty or desertion, BUT ALSO
- **Make counter-claim** for any relief under this Act on that ground

**If petitioner's adultery, cruelty or desertion is proved**, Court may give to respondent **any relief under this Act** to which he/she would have been entitled if he/she had presented petition seeking such relief on that ground.

## Unit 7: Other Provisions

### 7.1 Disposal of Property (Section 27)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of court's power to dispose of property in matrimonial proceedings.

#### üìñ Section 27 - Disposal of Property

In any proceeding under this Act, Court may make such **provisions in the decree** as it deems just and proper with respect to any **property presented at or about the time of marriage**, which may belong **jointly to both husband and wife**.

---

### 7.2 Appeals and Enforcement (Sections 28, 28-A)

> üìå **Why this matters**: Questions test understanding of appeal provisions and enforcement mechanisms.

#### üìñ Section 28 - Appeals from Decrees and Orders

**(1)** All decrees made by Court in any proceeding under this Act shall, subject to provisions of sub-section (3), be **appealable** as decrees of Court made in exercise of its original civil jurisdiction.

Every such appeal shall lie to Court to which appeals ordinarily lie from decisions of Court given in exercise of its original civil jurisdiction.

**(2)** Orders made by Court in any proceeding under this Act, under **Section 25 or Section 26**, shall, subject to provisions of sub-section (3), be **appealable** if they are **not interim orders**.

Every such appeal shall lie to Court to which appeals ordinarily lie from decisions of Court given in exercise of its original civil jurisdiction.

**(3)** There shall be **no appeal** under this section on subject of **costs only**.

**(4)** Every appeal under this section shall be preferred within period of **30 days** from date of decree or order.

---

#### üìñ Section 28-A - Enforcement of Decrees and Orders

All decrees and orders made by Court in any proceeding under this Act shall be **enforced in like manner** as decrees and orders of Court made in exercise of its original civil jurisdiction for time being enforced.

### ‚úÖ Essentials of Valid Marriage (Section 5)

1. Both parties must be Hindus
2. No bigamy (monogamy)
3. Mental capacity
4. Age: 21 (bridegroom), 18 (bride)
5. Not within prohibited relationship (unless custom permits)
6. Not sapindas (unless custom permits)
7. Proper ceremonies (Section 7)
8. Registration (Section 8 - optional)
9. Valid consent (Section 12(1)(c))

---

### ‚úÖ Grounds of Divorce - Both Spouses (Section 13(1))

1. Adultery
2. Cruelty
3. Desertion (2 years)
4. Conversion
5. Mental disorder
6. Leprosy (virulent, incurable)
7. Venereal disease (communicable)
8. Renunciation of world
9. Not heard of (7 years)

---

### ‚úÖ Breakdown Grounds (Section 13(1-A))

1. No resumption after judicial separation (1 year)
2. No restitution after decree of restitution (1 year)

---

### ‚úÖ Grounds - Wife Only (Section 13(2))

1. Bigamy (pre-1955 marriages)
2. Rape, sodomy, bestiality
3. Non-resumption after maintenance order (1 year)
4. Repudiation (married before 15, repudiated 15-18)

---

### ‚úÖ Void Marriage Grounds (Section 11)

1. Bigamy (Section 5(i))
2. Prohibited relationship (Section 5(iv))
3. Sapinda relationship (Section 5(v))

---

### ‚úÖ Voidable Marriage Grounds (Section 12)

1. Impotency
2. Mental capacity (Section 5(ii))
3. Consent by force/fraud (within 1 year)
4. Pre-marriage pregnancy (within 1 year, no intercourse)

---

### ‚úÖ Bars to Relief (Section 23)

1. Not taking advantage of own wrong
2. No accessory/connivance/condonation (adultery/cruelty)
3. Free consent (mutual consent)
4. No collusion
5. No unnecessary delay
6. No other legal ground

### üìä Comparison Table: Void vs Voidable

| Aspect | Void Marriage | Voidable Marriage |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Nature** | No marriage from beginning | Valid until avoided |
| **Who can avoid** | Either party (or declaratory suit) | Only parties to marriage |
| **Effect if not avoided** | Always void | Remains valid |
| **Effect after death** | Can be challenged | Cannot be challenged |
| **Legal consequences** | None | All consequences flow until avoided |
| **Retrospective effect** | Always void | Void from date of marriage if avoided |
| **Children** | Legitimate (Section 16) | Legitimate if begotten/conceived before decree |
| **Grounds** | Section 5(i), (iv), (v) | Section 12(1)(a), (b), (c), (d) |
