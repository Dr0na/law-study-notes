# ‚öñÔ∏è Unit 1: Indian Christian Marriage Act, 1872

> üìå **Why this matters in exam**: The Act governs marriages between Indian Christians. Questions often test the essentials, licensing requirements, and the procedure for solemnization.

---

## üìã Essentials of Valid Marriage Under Christian Law

### üéØ Definition

A marriage between Indian Christians is valid if it fulfills the conditions specified in the Act and is certified under Part IV of the Indian Christian Marriage Act, 1872.

### ‚úÖ Essential Conditions (Sec. 60)

For a marriage between Indian Christians to be certified, the following conditions must be fulfilled:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Parties** | Any two persons (Indian Christians) |
| **Age** | Male: 21 years minimum<br>Female: 18 years minimum |
| **Bigamy** | Neither party has a spouse living |
| **Mental Capacity** | Both parties must be of sound mind |
| **Prohibited Relationship** | Parties must not be within prohibited degrees |
| **Solemnization** | Must be in presence of a licensed person and at least 2 credible witnesses |

---

### üìå Age Requirement

**Legal Provision**: 
- **Male**: Must be at least 21 years old
- **Female**: Must be at least 18 years old

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: Age requirement is mandatory. Marriage below the prescribed age is void.

---

### üö´ Prohibition of Bigamy

**Legal Provision**: Neither party shall have a wife or husband still living.

**Key Points**:
- Bigamy is strictly prohibited
- Previous marriage must be legally dissolved
- If either party has a living spouse, the marriage is void

---

### üß† Mental Capacity

**Requirement**: Both parties must be of sound mind and capable of giving valid consent.

**Elements**:
- Understanding the nature of marriage
- Capacity to consent
- No unsoundness of mind at the time of marriage

---

### üë• Prohibited Relationship

**Rule**: Parties must not be within the degrees of prohibited relationship as defined by law.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: The Act follows general principles of prohibited relationships (consanguinity and affinity).

---

## üîê Procedural Requirements

### üìù Licensing of Persons [Sec. 9]

**Who can solemnize**:
- Any Christian licensed by the State Government
- License may be granted:
  - By name, OR
  - By office held

**Procedure**:
1. State Government grants license
2. License may be revoked by the granting authority
3. Grant/revocation must be notified in Official Gazette

**Requirements for solemnization**:
- Must be in presence of licensed person
- At least 2 credible witnesses (in addition to licensed person)
- Each party must say: *"I call upon these persons here present to witness that I, A.B., in the presence of Almighty God, and in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, do take thee, C.D., to be my lawful wedded wife [or husband]"* or words to similar effect

---

### üìñ Marriage Notice Book and Publication [Sec. 6]

**Duties of Marriage Officer**:

1. **Maintenance of Records**:
   - Keep all notices given under Sec. 5
   - Maintain records in office

2. **Marriage Notice Book**:
   - Enter true copy of every notice
   - Book must be open for inspection
   - Inspection: Free of charge, at all reasonable times

3. **Publication**:
   - Affix copy of notice to conspicuous place in office
   - If either party not permanently residing in district:
     - Transmit copy to Marriage Officer of that district
     - That Officer must also affix copy in his office

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Objection to Marriage [Sec. 7]

**Who can object**:
- Any person

**Time limit**:
- Within 30 days from date of publication of notice

**Grounds for objection**:
- Marriage would contravene one or more conditions specified in Sec. 4

**Procedure**:
1. Objection must be made before expiration of 30 days
2. After 30 days, marriage may be solemnized (unless previously objected)
3. Nature of objection:
   - Recorded in writing in Marriage Notice Book
   - Read over and explained to objector
   - Signed by objector or on his behalf

---

### üîç Procedure on Receipt of Objection [Sec. 8]

**Steps**:

1. **Inquiry by Marriage Officer**:
   - Cannot solemnize until inquiry completed
   - Must be satisfied that objection should not prevent marriage
   - OR objection is withdrawn
   - **Time limit**: Maximum 30 days from date of objection

2. **Appeal** (if objection upheld):
   - Either party may appeal
   - **Time limit**: 30 days from date of refusal
   - **Forum**: District Court (within local limits of Marriage Officer's office)
   - Decision of District Court is **final**
   - Marriage Officer must act in conformity with Court's decision

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è Powers of Marriage Officers in Respect of Inquiries [Sec. 9]

**Powers** (same as Civil Court under CPC, 1908):

| Power | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Summoning witnesses** | Enforce attendance and examine on oath |
| **Discovery and inspection** | Order discovery and inspection of documents |
| **Production of documents** | Compel production of documents |
| **Evidence on affidavits** | Receive evidence on affidavits |
| **Commissions** | Issue commissions for examination of witnesses |

**Legal Status**:
- Proceedings before Marriage Officer = **judicial proceeding**
- Within meaning of Sec. 193, Indian Penal Code

**Costs**:
- If objection not reasonable or not in good faith:
  - Marriage Officer may impose costs (compensation)
  - Maximum: ‚Çπ1,000
  - May award whole or part to parties
  - Executable as decree of District Court

---

### üåç Marriages Abroad [Sec. 10]

**Special Provision for Jammu & Kashmir**:

**Procedure**:
1. Objection made to Marriage Officer in J&K
2. Marriage Officer makes inquiry
3. If doubt entertained:
   - **Cannot solemnize** marriage
   - Transmit record to **Central Government**
   - Central Government:
     - Makes inquiry
     - Obtains advice
     - Gives decision in writing
4. Marriage Officer acts in conformity with Central Government's decision

---

### ‚úçÔ∏è Declaration by Parties and Witnesses [Sec. 11]

**Requirement**:
- Before solemnization
- Parties + **3 witnesses** must sign declaration
- In presence of Marriage Officer
- Declaration in Form specified in Third Schedule
- Countersigned by Marriage Officer

---

### üèõÔ∏è Place and Form of Solemnization [Sec. 12]

**Place**:
- Office of Marriage Officer, OR
- Other place within reasonable distance
- Subject to conditions and additional fees (as prescribed)

**Form**:
- Parties may choose any form
- **BUT** marriage not complete unless:
  - Each party says to the other
  - In presence of Marriage Officer and 3 witnesses
  - In language understood by parties
  - Words: *"I (A) take thee (B), to be my lawful wife (or husband)"*

---

### üìú Certificate of Marriage [Sec. 13]

**Entry of Certificate**:
- After solemnization
- Marriage Officer enters certificate in **Marriage Certificate Book**
- Form: As specified in Fourth Schedule
- Signed by:
  - Parties to marriage
  - Three witnesses

**Legal Effect**:
- Certificate = **conclusive evidence** that:
  - Marriage under this Act has been solemnized
  - All formalities respecting signatures of witnesses complied with

---

### ‚è∞ New Notice When Marriage Not Solemnized Within Three Months [Sec. 14]

**When notice lapses**:

Marriage not solemnized within **3 calendar months** from:
- Date of notice (Sec. 5), OR
- Date of District Court decision (if appeal filed under Sec. 8), OR
- Date of Central Government decision (if record transmitted under Sec. 10)

**Consequence**:
- Notice and all proceedings **deemed to have lapsed**
- New notice required before solemnization

---

### üìã Registration of Marriage Celebrated in Other Forms [Sec. 15]

**Scope**:
- Any marriage celebrated (before or after commencement of Act)
- Other than:
  - Marriage under Special Marriage Act, 1872, OR
  - Marriage under this Act

**Conditions for Registration**:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Ceremony performed** | Marriage ceremony performed + parties living together as husband and wife since |
| **No bigamy** | Neither party has more than one spouse living at time of registration |
| **Mental capacity** | Neither party is idiot or lunatic at time of registration |
| **Age** | Both parties completed 21 years at time of registration |
| **Prohibited relationship** | Parties not within degrees of prohibited relationship<br>*Exception*: If marriage before Act, subject to law/custom/usage permitting such marriage |
| **Residence** | Parties residing in district of Marriage Officer for at least 30 days immediately preceding application |

---

### üìù Procedure for Registration [Sec. 16]

**Steps**:

1. **Application**:
   - Signed by both parties
   - For registration under this Chapter

2. **Public Notice**:
   - Marriage Officer gives public notice
   - In prescribed manner

3. **Objection Period**:
   - 30 days allowed for objections
   - Hear any objections received

4. **Registration** (if satisfied):
   - Enter certificate in Marriage Certificate Book
   - Form: As specified in Fifth Schedule
   - Signed by:
     - Parties to marriage
     - Three witnesses

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è Appeals from Orders Under Sec. 16 [Sec. 17]

**Who can appeal**:
- Any person aggrieved by order refusing registration

**Procedure**:
- **Time limit**: 30 days from date of order
- **Forum**: District Court (within local limits of Marriage Officer's office)
- **Decision**: Final
- Marriage Officer acts in conformity with Court's decision

---

### ‚úÖ Effect of Registration of Marriage Under This Chapter [Sec. 18]

**Legal Effect**:

1. **Status of Marriage**:
   - From date of entry in Marriage Certificate Book
   - Marriage **deemed to be solemnized under this Act**

2. **Legitimacy of Children**:
   - All children born after date of ceremony
   - Deemed to be and always to have been **legitimate children**
   - Names entered in Marriage Certificate Book

3. **Property Rights** (Limitation):
   - Does NOT confer rights in property of persons other than parents
   - Only if children would have been incapable of such rights but for this Act

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Registration under Sec. 15-18 gives retrospective validity to marriages celebrated in other forms.

---

## üèõÔ∏è Consequences of Marriage Under This Act [Sec. 19 to 21-A]

### üîÑ Effect of Marriage on Member of Undivided Family [Sec. 19]

**Rule**:
- Marriage under this Act of any member of undivided family
- Who professes **Hindu, Buddhist, Sikh or Jain** religion
- **Deemed to effect severance** from such family

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: This applies only to members of undivided families professing specified religions.

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è Rights and Disabilities Not Affected by Act [Sec. 20]

**Rule**:
- Subject to Sec. 19
- Person whose marriage solemnized under this Act:
  - **Same rights** as person to whom Caste Disabilities Removal Act, 1850 applies
  - **Same disabilities** in regard to succession to property

**Reference**: Caste Disabilities Removal Act, 1850 (Act 21 of 1850)

---

### üìú Succession to Property of Parties Married Under Act [Sec. 21]

**Rule**:
- Notwithstanding restrictions in Indian Succession Act, 1925
- Succession to property:
  - Of person whose marriage solemnized under this Act
  - Of issue of such marriage
- **Regulated by Indian Succession Act, 1925**

**Modification**:
- Indian Succession Act, 1925 applies
- **As if** Chapter III of Part V (Special Rules for Parsi Intestates) **omitted**

---

### üéØ Special Provision in Certain Cases [Sec. 21-A]

**When applicable**:
- Marriage under this Act
- Between persons both professing:
  - Hindu, OR
  - Buddhist, OR
  - Sikh, OR
  - Jain religion

**Effect**:
- **Sec. 19** (severance from undivided family) **does NOT apply**
- **Sec. 21** (succession) **does NOT apply**
- **Sec. 20** (disabilities) - only the part creating disability does NOT apply

> üìå **Exam Tip**: Sec. 21-A is an exception for intra-community marriages (Hindu-Hindu, etc.). Remember: Sec. 19 and 21 are excluded, but rights under Sec. 20 remain.

# ‚öñÔ∏è Unit 2: Special Marriage Act, 1954

> üìå **Why this matters in exam**: This Act enables inter-religious and inter-caste marriages. Frequently tested: (1) Essentials, (2) Procedure, (3) Effect on succession and family status, and (4) Registration of existing marriages.

---

## üìã Essentials of Valid Marriage Under Special Marriage Act

### üéØ Definition [Sec. 4]

**Scope**: Notwithstanding anything in any other law, a marriage between **any two persons** may be solemnized under this Act if conditions are fulfilled.

> üìå **Exam Tip**: "Any two persons" means the Act applies regardless of religion, caste, or community.

---

### ‚úÖ Essential Conditions [Sec. 4]

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Parties** | Any two persons |
| **Bigamy** | Neither party has a spouse living |
| **Mental Capacity** | Neither party:<br>‚Ä¢ Incapable of valid consent (unsoundness of mind), OR<br>‚Ä¢ Suffering from mental disorder (unfit for marriage/procreation), OR<br>‚Ä¢ Subject to recurrent attacks of insanity or epilepsy |
| **Age** | Male: 21 years minimum<br>Female: 18 years minimum |
| **Prohibited Relationship** | Parties not within degrees of prohibited relationship<br>*Exception*: Custom governing at least one party permits such marriage |

---

### üö´ Prohibition of Bigamy

**Rule**: Neither party shall have a spouse living at the time of marriage.

**Legal Effect**: Bigamous marriage is void.

---

### üß† Mental Capacity

**Three Categories of Incapacity**:

1. **Incapable of valid consent**:
   - Due to unsoundness of mind

2. **Mental disorder**:
   - Capable of consent BUT
   - Suffering from mental disorder
   - Of such kind/extent as to be unfit for:
     - Marriage, AND
     - Procreation of children

3. **Recurrent attacks**:
   - Subject to recurrent attacks of:
     - Insanity, OR
     - Epilepsy

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: Any one of these conditions makes the party incapable of valid marriage.

---

### üë• Prohibited Relationship [Sec. 2(b)]

**Definition**:
- A man and any person mentioned in **Part I** of First Schedule
- A woman and any person mentioned in **Part II** of First Schedule

**Explanation I**:
- Relationship includes:
  - By **half blood** OR **uterine blood** OR **full blood**
  - **Illegitimate** OR **legitimate**
  - By **adoption**

**Exception**:
- If custom governing **at least one party** permits marriage
- Marriage may be solemnized despite prohibited relationship

> üìå **Exam Tip**: The First Schedule contains the detailed list. Remember: includes half-blood, uterine blood, and adoption relationships.

- **Sec. 20**: Rights/disabilities (Caste Disabilities Removal Act)
- **Sec. 21**: Succession (Indian Succession Act, 1925)
- **Sec. 21-A**: Exception for intra-community marriages
- **Mnemonic**: "19-sever, 20-rights, 21-succession, 21A-exception"

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Exam Pitfalls

1. **Confusing witness requirements**: Christian Act = 2, Special Marriage Act = 3
2. **Age at registration**: For registration of existing marriages, both parties must be 21 (not 18 for female)
3. **Sec. 21-A exception**: Only applies when BOTH parties are Hindu/Buddhist/Sikh/Jain
4. **Mental capacity**: Special Marriage Act has three specific categories; Christian Act has general requirement
5. **Prohibited relationship**: Custom exception applies only if at least one party is governed by such custom
6. **Effect of registration**: Children become legitimate from date of ceremony, not date of registration
7. **Severance from HUF**: Only applies to Hindu/Buddhist/Sikh/Jain members of undivided families
