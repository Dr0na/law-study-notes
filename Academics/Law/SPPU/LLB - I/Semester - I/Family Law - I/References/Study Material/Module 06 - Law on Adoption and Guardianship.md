> ðŸ“Œ **Why this matters in exam**: Understanding the concept and objects of adoption is fundamental. Questions often test the distinction between adoption and guardianship, and the legal effects of adoption.

#### ðŸ“Œ Definition

### ðŸ‘¨â€ðŸ‘©â€ðŸ‘§ Persons Capable of Giving in Adoption (Section 9)

- Mother has ceased to be a Hindu
- Mother has been declared by court to be of unsound mind

### ðŸ“‹ Other Conditions for Valid Adoption (Section 11)

> ðŸ“Œ **Why this matters in exam**: This section has **six critical conditions**. Questions often test conditions (i) and (ii) about existing children, and condition (vi) about actual giving and taking.

- **Same child** may **not** be adopted simultaneously by **two or more persons**

  - Under their authority

### âš¡ Effect of Adoption (Section 12)

> ðŸ“Œ **Why this matters in exam**: This is a **highly tested section**. Questions ask about legal effects, especially the three provisos. Know what rights are severed and what are preserved.

#### ðŸ“‹ General Rule

An adopted child shall be **deemed to be the child** of his/her adoptive father or mother:
- **For all purposes**
- **With effect from the date of adoption**
- **From such date**:
  - All ties in family of birth â†’ **severed**
  - Ties in adoptive family â†’ **created**

#### âš ï¸ Three Provisos (Exceptions to General Rule)

##### Proviso (a): Marriage Restrictions

- Child **cannot marry** any person whom he/she could **not have married** if continued in family of birth
- **Prohibited relationships** from birth family continue to apply

##### Proviso (b): Property Vested Before Adoption

- Any property which **vested in adopted child before adoption**:
  - **Continues to vest** in such person
  - Subject to obligations (if any) attaching to ownership
  - Including obligation to **maintain relatives** in family of birth

##### Proviso (c): No Divesting of Estate

- Adopted child **shall not divest** any person of any estate which **vested in him/her before adoption**

> ðŸ§  **Memory Aid**: **S**evered ties + **M**arriage restrictions + **P**roperty preserved + **N**o divesting

> ðŸ“Œ **Exam Tip**: Always mention "deemed to be child for all purposes" + list the three provisos

#### ðŸ“‹ Definition (Section 4(c))

**Natural guardian** refers to:
- **Father** and after him the **mother** of the minor
- **Natural guardian of wife** is her husband

#### ðŸ“‹ Section 6: Natural Guardians

Natural guardian consists of **three types** of persons:

##### 1. ðŸ‘¨ Father: Section 6(a)

- **Natural guardian** in case of:
  - **Boy**, OR
  - **Unmarried girl**
- **Priority**: Firstly father, later mother
- **Exception**: Up to age of **5 years**, mother is **generally** the natural guardian

##### 2. ðŸ‘© Mother: Section 6(b)

- **Natural guardian** of:
  - **Illegitimate boy**
  - **Illegitimate unmarried girl**
- **Priority**: Even if father is alive, **mother first**, then father
- **Special cases**:
  - If mother ceases to be a Hindu â†’ right of natural guardianship **remains the same**
  - Position remains same in case of **adopted child** (not natural born child)

##### 3. ðŸ‘¨ Husband

- **Husband** is the guardian of his **minor wife**

#### ðŸ“‹ Case Law: Essakkayal Nadder v. Sreedharan Babu (AIR 1992 Ker 200)

**Facts**:
- Mother of minor was dead
- Father was not residing with children
- Father still alive, not ceased to be Hindu, not renounced world, not declared unfit

**Held**:
- This does **not authorize** any other person to assume role of natural guardian
- Cannot alienate minor's property

> âš ï¸ **Important**: Mere non-residence does not deprive father of natural guardianship rights

#### ðŸ“‹ Case Law: Jajabhai v. Pathankhan (AIR 1971 SC 315)

**Facts**:
- Mother and father had fallen out and were living separately
- Minor daughter was under care and protection of mother

**Held**:
- Mother could be considered as **natural guardian** of minor girl

#### ðŸ“‹ Case Law: Gita Hariharan v. Reserve Bank of India (AIR 1999 SC 1149)

> ðŸ“Œ **Landmark Case**: Constitutional validity of Section 6(a) challenged

**Issue**: 
- Whether giving father preference over mother was discrimination on basis of sex under Article 14

**Held**:
- Supreme Court looked into scheme of statute
- Term **"after"** must **not** be understood as "after the lifetime of father"
- Must be understood as **"in the absence of father"**
- **Welfare of child** is of supreme importance
- Word "absence" can be understood as:
  - **Total apathy**, OR
  - **Negligence** by father towards child
- **Mother could be natural guardian** even during lifetime of father

> ðŸ§  **Memory Aid**: **"After" = "In absence of"** (not after death)

> ðŸ“Œ **Exam Tip**: Always cite Gita Hariharan case when discussing mother's right to be natural guardian

### ðŸ“ Testamentary Guardians

> ðŸ“Œ **Why this matters in exam**: Questions test appointment, acceptance, and powers. Know Section 9 provisions.

#### ðŸ“‹ Section 9: Testamentary Guardians

- Testamentary guardian can be appointed **only by a will**
- **Guardian of minor girl** will **cease** to be guardian of her person:
  - **On her marriage**
  - Guardianship **cannot revive** even if she becomes widow while minor

#### ðŸ“‹ Acceptance of Guardianship

- **Necessary** for testamentary guardian to **accept** the guardianship
- Acceptance may be:
  - **Express**, OR
  - **Implied**
- Testamentary guardian may:
  - **Refuse** to accept appointment, OR
  - **Disclaim** it
- **Once accepted**:
  - Cannot refuse to act, OR
  - Resign
  - **Except with permission of court**

#### ðŸ“‹ Testamentary Power: Both Parents

- Under Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, **testamentary power** of appointing guardian has been conferred on **both parents**

#### ðŸ“‹ Priority Rules

| Scenario | Result |
|----------|--------|
| **Father appoints, mother survives** | Father's appointment **ineffective**; mother becomes natural guardian |
| **Mother appoints** | Mother's appointee becomes testamentary guardian; father's appointment continues to be ineffective |
| **Mother does not appoint** | Father's appointee becomes guardian |

#### ðŸ“‹ Illegitimate Children (Section 9(4))

- Hindu father **cannot appoint** guardian of his minor illegitimate children
- Even when entitled to act as their natural guardian
- Section 9(1) confers testamentary power in respect of **legitimate children only**
- In respect of illegitimate children, **Section 9(4)** confers such power on **mother alone**

> ðŸ§  **Memory Aid**: **W**ill required + **A**cceptance necessary + **M**arriage ends (for girl) + **M**other priority

### âš–ï¸ Guardians Appointed by the Court

> ðŸ“Œ **Why this matters in exam**: Questions test appointment criteria, powers, and relationship with Guardians and Wards Act. Know the paramount consideration.

#### ðŸ“‹ Appointment Under Guardians and Wards Act, 1890

- Where court is satisfied that it is **for welfare of minor** that order should be made
- Court may make order under **Guardians and Wards Act, 1890**
- Appointing guardian of:
  - Person of minor, OR
  - Property of minor, OR
  - Both

#### ðŸ“‹ Paramount Consideration

- **Welfare of minor** shall be the **paramount consideration**
- In appointing or declaring person as guardian

#### ðŸ“‹ Case Law: Mohini v. Virendra (AIR 1977 SC 1359)

**Held**:
- Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act is **supplementary** to and **not in derogation** of Guardians and Wards Act

#### ðŸ“‹ Powers of Certificated Guardian

- Guardian appointed by court is known as **certificated guardian**
- Powers are **controlled by Guardians and Wards Act, 1890**
- **Very few acts** can be performed **without prior permission of court**
- Powers are **co-extensive with powers of sovereign**
- May do all things (though with permission of court) which sovereign has power to do
- From date of appointment, certificated guardian is under:
  - **Supervision**
  - **Guidance**
  - **Control** of court

> ðŸ§  **Memory Aid**: **W**elfare paramount + **C**ertificated guardian + **C**ourt supervision + **L**imited powers

### ðŸ‘¤ De Facto Guardian

> ðŸ“Œ **Why this matters in exam**: Questions test definition, powers, and restrictions. Know Section 11 prohibition.

#### ðŸ“‹ Section 11: De Facto Guardian

**Prohibition**: No person shall be entitled to:
- Dispose of, OR
- Deal with
- Property of Hindu minor
- **Merely on the ground** of being de facto guardian

#### ðŸ“‹ Definition

**De facto guardian** is:
- Person who is **not a legal guardian**
- Has **no authority in law** to act as such
- **Nonetheless** has assumed management of property of child
- **As though** he were a guardian

#### ðŸ“‹ Concept

- **De facto guardianship** is concept where **past acts result in present status**
- Term literally means **"from that which has been done"**
- Recognized in Hindu law as early as **1856**

#### ðŸ“‹ Case Law: Hanuman Prasad Singh v. Bhaguati Prasad Singh (1897) ILR 19 All 357

**Privy Council held**:
- Under Hindu law, right of **bona fide incumbrancer**:
  - Who has taken from de facto guardian
  - Charge of land
  - Created **honestly**
  - For purpose of **saving the estate**, OR
  - For **benefit of the estate**
- Is **not affected** by want of union of de facto with de jure title

#### ðŸ“‹ Restrictions on De Facto Guardian

**Well settled** that de facto guardian **does not have right** to:
- âœ… Assume debt
- âœ… Gift minor's property
- âœ… Make reference to arbitration

> âš ï¸ **Important**: De facto guardian has **no legal authority** to deal with minor's property

> ðŸ§  **Memory Aid**: **D**e facto = **N**ot legal + **N**o authority + **P**rohibited from dealing with property

### ðŸ¤± Custody of a Minor

> ðŸ“Œ **Why this matters in exam**: This is a **highly tested topic**. Questions test custody principles, welfare doctrine, and landmark cases. Know Section 13 and Section 26 of Hindu Marriage Act.

#### ðŸ“‹ General Rule (Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956)

- **Father** to be natural guardian of minor
- **After him**, mother shall be guardian

**Two provisions**:

1. **Minor boy or unmarried minor girl**:
   - Natural guardian is **father**
   - **"After"** him, the mother

2. **Minor below 5 years**:
   - Custody shall **"ordinarily"** be with **mother**

#### ðŸ“‹ Case Law: Gita Hariharan v. Reserve Bank of India (1999) 2 SCC 228

> ðŸ“Œ **Landmark Case**: Constitutional validity of Section 6(a) challenged

**Issue**:
- Whether giving father preference over mother was discrimination on basis of sex under Article 14

**Held**:
- Supreme Court looked into scheme of statute
- Term **"after"** must **not** be understood as "after the lifetime of father"
- Must be understood as **"in the absence of father"**
- **Welfare of child** is of supreme importance
- Word "absence" can be understood as:
  - **Total apathy**, OR
  - **Negligence** by father towards child
- **Mother could be natural guardian** even during lifetime of father

> ðŸ§  **Memory Aid**: **"After" = "In absence of"** (not after death)

#### ðŸ“‹ Section 13: Welfare of Child (Paramount Consideration)

**Section 13** of Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956:

- In giving child in custody, OR
- While appointing guardian for him/her
- **Welfare of child** should be given **supreme importance**

**If court finds**:
- Giving child under someone's custody will be **detrimental to child**
- Court will **refrain from doing so**
- **No person** shall be appointed as guardian to minor

#### ðŸ“‹ Underlying Principle

- **Welfare of child** is underlying principle in:
  - Cases of custody
  - Cases of guardianship

#### ðŸ“‹ Non-Hindu Children

- For **non-Hindu children**:
  - Court's authority to intervene in furtherance of welfare principle
  - Is **subordinated** to that of father as natural guardian

#### ðŸ“‹ Section 26: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

**Section 26** of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 provides for custody of children:

- Court may give **interim orders** in cases related to:
  - **Custody** of minor children
  - **Education** of minor children
  - Other matters
- **With their consonance** (consent/consideration)
- Court is also authorized to **revoke** any such order previously passed

> ðŸ§  **Memory Aid**: **W**elfare paramount + **M**other (below 5) + **A**bsence of father + **I**nterim orders (HMA)

> ðŸ“Œ **Exam Tip**: Always emphasize "welfare of child is paramount" in custody questions
