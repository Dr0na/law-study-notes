#### 1. Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956

**ğŸ“Œ Definition of Adoption**
- Process by which a person takes someone into custody
- All legal rights of a legitimate biological child are transferred
- Relationship severed from biological parents
- Child becomes lawful child of adoptive parents

**ğŸ¯ Objects**
- **Religious**: Offers an heir to fulfill funeral obligations
- **Secular**: To secure an heir and perpetuate the adopter's name

**ğŸ“‹ Section 5: Adoptions to be Regulated**
- No adoption shall be made except in accordance with this Chapter
- Any adoption made in contravention = **VOID**
- Void adoption creates no rights, destroys no rights

---

**âœ… Section 6: Essentials (4 Requisites)**

1. Person adopting has:
   - Capacity to take in adoption
   - Right to take in adoption

2. Person giving in adoption has:
   - Capacity to do so

3. Person adopted is:
   - Capable of being taken in adoption

4. Adoption is made in:
   - Compliance with other conditions mentioned in this Chapter

---

**ğŸ‘¨ Section 7: Capacity of Male Hindu**

**Requirements**:
- Sound mind
- Not a minor
- **Wife's consent** required (unless exceptions)

**Exceptions to Consent Requirement**:
- Wife has completely and finally renounced the world
- Wife has ceased to be a Hindu
- Wife has been declared by court to be of unsound mind

**Multiple Wives**:
- If person has more than one wife living
- **Consent of all wives** necessary
- Unless consent of any one is unnecessary for reasons specified

---

**ğŸ‘© Section 8: Capacity of Female Hindu**

**Requirements (All Must Be Met)**:
1. Sound mind
2. Not a minor
3. **Marital status** (any one of the following):
   - Not married, OR
   - Married but marriage has been dissolved, OR
   - Husband is dead, OR
   - Husband has completely and finally renounced the world, OR
   - Husband has ceased to be a Hindu, OR
   - Husband has been declared by court to be of unsound mind

---

**ğŸ‘¨â€ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ‘§ Section 9: Persons Capable of Giving in Adoption**

**Priority**:
1. **Father** (if alive, has exclusive right with mother's consent)
2. **Mother** (if father dead/renounced/ceased to be Hindu/declared unsound)
3. **Guardian** (if both parents dead/renounced/abandoned/declared unsound/unknown parentage)

**Father's Right (Sub-section 2)**:
- Father, if alive, shall **alone** have the right
- **Cannot exercise** without mother's consent (unless exceptions)

**Mother's Right (Sub-section 3)**:
- Mother may give child in adoption if father is:
  - Dead, OR
  - Has completely and finally renounced the world, OR
  - Has ceased to be a Hindu, OR
  - Has been declared by court to be of unsound mind

**Guardian's Right (Sub-section 4)**:
- Guardian may give child in adoption if:
  - Both parents are dead/renounced/abandoned/declared unsound, OR
  - Parentage of child is not known
- **Requirements**:
  - Must obtain **previous permission of court**
  - Can give to any person (including guardian himself)

**Sub-section (5): Court's Duty**:
- Court must be satisfied that:
  1. Adoption will be **for the welfare of the child**
  2. **Wishes of child** considered (having regard to age and understanding)
  3. **No payment or reward** received/agreed (except court-sanctioned)

---

**ğŸ‘¶ Section 10: Persons Who May Be Adopted**

**Four Conditions (All Must Be Fulfilled)**:
1. âœ… Must be a Hindu
2. âœ… Not already adopted
3. âœ… Not married (unless custom/usage permits)
4. âœ… Not completed 15 years (unless custom/usage permits)

---

**ğŸ“‹ Section 11: Other Conditions**

**Six Conditions (All Must Be Complied With)**:

1. **No Existing Son/Daughter**:
   - Adoptive father or mother must not have:
     - Hindu son, OR
     - Son's son, OR
     - Son's son's son (for son adoption)
     - Hindu daughter, OR
     - Son's daughter (for daughter adoption)
   - Living at the time of adoption
   - Whether by legitimate blood relationship or by adoption

2. **Age Difference**:
   - If male adopting female: Adoptive father must be **at least 21 years older**
   - If female adopting male: Adoptive mother must be **at least 21 years older**

3. **Simultaneous Adoption Prohibited**:
   - Same child may **not** be adopted simultaneously by two or more persons

4. **Actual Giving and Taking**:
   - Child must be **actually given and taken** in adoption
   - By parents or guardian concerned, OR
   - Under their authority
   - **Intent**: To transfer child from family of birth to family of adoption

**Proviso**: Performance of datta homan is **not essential** to validity of adoption

---

**âš¡ Section 12: Effect of Adoption**

**General Rule**:
- Adopted child shall be **deemed to be the child** of his/her adoptive father or mother
- **For all purposes**
- **With effect from the date of adoption**
- **From such date**:
  - All ties in family of birth â†’ **severed**
  - Ties in adoptive family â†’ **created**

**âš ï¸ Three Provisos (Exceptions)**

**Proviso (a): Marriage Restrictions**
- Child **cannot marry** any person whom he/she could **not have married** if continued in family of birth
- Prohibited relationships from birth family continue to apply

**Proviso (b): Property Vested Before Adoption**
- Any property which **vested in adopted child before adoption**:
  - **Continues to vest** in such person
  - Subject to obligations (if any) attaching to ownership
  - Including obligation to **maintain relatives** in family of birth

**Proviso (c): No Divesting of Estate**
- Adopted child **shall not divest** any person of any estate which **vested in him/her before adoption**

---

**ğŸ’¼ Section 13: Right of Adoptive Parents to Dispose of Properties**
- **Subject to any agreement to the contrary**
- Adoption **does not deprive** adoptive father or mother of power to dispose of property
- By **transfer inter vivos**, OR
- By **will**

---

**ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ‘§ Section 14: Determination of Adoptive Mother**

| Scenario | Adoptive Mother | Others |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| **Hindu with wife living** | Wife shall be deemed adoptive mother | - |
| **Adoption with consent of multiple wives** | **Senior most in marriage** = adoptive mother | Others = stepmothers |
| **Widower or bachelor adopts, then marries** | - | Subsequent wife = stepmother |
| **Widow or unmarried woman adopts, then marries** | - | Subsequent husband = stepfather |

---

**ğŸ”’ Section 15: Valid Adoption Not to Be Cancelled**
- **No adoption** which has been **validly made** can be:
  - Cancelled by adoptive father or mother, OR
  - Cancelled by any other person
- **Adopted child cannot**:
  - Renounce status as adopted child, OR
  - Return to family of birth

**âš ï¸ Important**: Once valid, adoption is **irrevocable** and **permanent**

---

**ğŸ“„ Section 16: Presumption as to Registered Documents**
- **Whenever** any document:
  - Registered under any law
  - Produced before any court
  - Purporting to record an adoption made
  - Signed by person giving and person taking child in adoption
- **Court shall presume**:
  - Adoption has been made in compliance with provisions of this Act
  - **Unless and until it is disproved**

---

**ğŸš« Section 17: Prohibition of Certain Payments**
- **No person shall**:
  - Receive or agree to receive any payment or reward in consideration of adoption, OR
  - Make or give or agree to make or give any payment or reward (receipt of which is prohibited)

#### 2. Inter-Country Adoption

**ğŸ“‹ Hague Convention, 1993**
- **India signatory**: 2003
- Protects against illegal/irregular/premature/ill-prepared adoptions
- Operates through system of **national Central Authorities**
- Reinforces **UN Convention on the Rights of the Child** (Art. 21)

**ğŸ“œ Case Law: Re Rasiklal Chhaganlal Mehta (AIR 1982 Guj 193)**
- Inter-country adoptions should be **legally valid** under laws of **both countries**
- Adoptive parents must fulfill requirements of adoption law in their country

**ğŸ“œ Case Law: Laxmi Kant Pandey v. Union of India**
- Supreme Court framed **guidelines** governing inter-country adoptions
- **Regulatory body**: CARA (Central Adoption Resource Agency) recommended and set up

---

**ğŸ¢ CARA (Central Adoption Resource Authority)**

**Nature and Role**:
- **Autonomous Body** under Ministry of Women and Child Development
- Responsible for:
  - **In-country adoptions**
  - **Inter-country adoptions** in India

**Key Requirements**:
- Foreign applications must be sponsored by recognized/licensed agency
- Agency should be recognized by CARA
- **Priority**: Encourages **in-country adoption** rather than inter-country
- Inter-country adoption only where child finds **no suitable home in country**
- All Child Care Institutions (CCI) must be registered

**Priority Ratio**: **80:20** (in-country:inter-country) excluding special needs children

---

**ğŸ‘¶ Who Are Adoptable for Inter-Country Adoption**

**Three Types**:
1. **Orphans** (already under care of specialized adoption agency)
2. **Abandoned children**
3. **Surrendered children**

**Time Limits for Declaration**:
- **Abandoned (Below 2 years)**: Declaration within **60 days**
- **Abandoned (Above 2 years)**: Declaration within **4 months**
- **Surrendered**: **2 months reconsideration time** to biological parent(s)

**Order of Priority for Inter-Country Adoptions**:
1. **Non Resident Indian (NRI)**
2. **Overseas Citizen of India (OCI)**
3. **Persons of Indian Origin (PIO)**
4. **Foreign Nationals**

## ğŸ“Œ UNIT 2: GUARDIANSHIP

### ğŸ§  MASTER STRUCTURED NOTES

#### 1. Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956

**ğŸ“‹ Scope of the Act**
- Guardianship of person of minors
- Guardianship of property of minors
- De facto guardians
- Guardians by affinity

**ğŸ“‹ Definition of Guardian (Section 4)**
- Person having the care of:
  - Person of a minor, OR
  - Property of a minor, OR
  - Both person and property

**Includes**:
1. âœ… **Natural guardian**
2. âœ… **Guardian appointed by will** (testamentary guardian)
3. âœ… **Guardian appointed or declared by court**
4. âœ… **Person empowered to act** by order of Court of Wards

**ğŸ“‹ Definition of Minor (Section 4(a))**
- **Minor** means a person who has **not completed the age of 18 years**

**Need for Guardianship**:
- Minor is considered physically and intellectually imperfect and immature
- Hence needs someone's protection

---

#### 2. Natural Guardians

**ğŸ“‹ Definition (Section 4(c))**
- **Natural guardian** refers to:
  - **Father** and after him the **mother** of the minor
  - **Natural guardian of wife** is her husband

**ğŸ“‹ Section 6: Natural Guardians**

**Three Types**:

1. **ğŸ‘¨ Father: Section 6(a)**
   - **Natural guardian** in case of:
     - **Boy**, OR
     - **Unmarried girl**
   - **Priority**: Firstly father, later mother
   - **Exception**: Up to age of **5 years**, mother is **generally** the natural guardian

2. **ğŸ‘© Mother: Section 6(b)**
   - **Natural guardian** of:
     - **Illegitimate boy**
     - **Illegitimate unmarried girl**
   - **Priority**: Even if father is alive, **mother first**, then father
   - **Special cases**:
     - If mother ceases to be a Hindu â†’ right of natural guardianship **remains the same**
     - Position remains same in case of **adopted child**

3. **ğŸ‘¨ Husband**
   - **Husband** is the guardian of his **minor wife**

**ğŸ“œ Key Case: Gita Hariharan v. Reserve Bank of India (AIR 1999 SC 1149)**

**Issue**: 
- Whether giving father preference over mother was discrimination on basis of sex under Article 14

**Held**:
- Term **"after"** must **not** be understood as "after the lifetime of father"
- Must be understood as **"in the absence of father"**
- **Welfare of child** is of supreme importance
- Word "absence" can be understood as:
  - **Total apathy**, OR
  - **Negligence** by father towards child
- **Mother could be natural guardian** even during lifetime of father

**ğŸ“œ Other Important Cases**:
- **Essakkayal Nadder v. Sreedharan Babu (AIR 1992 Ker 200)**: Mere non-residence does not deprive father of natural guardianship rights
- **Jajabhai v. Pathankhan (AIR 1971 SC 315)**: Mother could be considered as natural guardian when parents living separately

---

#### 3. Testamentary Guardians

**ğŸ“ Section 9: Testamentary Guardians**

**Appointment**:
- Testamentary guardian can be appointed **only by a will**

**Guardian of Minor Girl**:
- Will **cease** to be guardian of her person:
  - **On her marriage**
  - Guardianship **cannot revive** even if she becomes widow while minor

**Acceptance of Guardianship**:
- **Necessary** for testamentary guardian to **accept** the guardianship
- Acceptance may be **Express** OR **Implied**
- Testamentary guardian may:
  - **Refuse** to accept appointment, OR
  - **Disclaim** it
- **Once accepted**:
  - Cannot refuse to act, OR
  - Resign
  - **Except with permission of court**

**Testamentary Power: Both Parents**:
- Under Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, **testamentary power** of appointing guardian has been conferred on **both parents**

**Priority Rules**:

| Scenario | Result |
|----------|--------|
| **Father appoints, mother survives** | Father's appointment **ineffective**; mother becomes natural guardian |
| **Mother appoints** | Mother's appointee becomes testamentary guardian; father's appointment continues to be ineffective |
| **Mother does not appoint** | Father's appointee becomes guardian |

**Illegitimate Children (Section 9(4))**:
- Hindu father **cannot appoint** guardian of his minor illegitimate children
- Section 9(1) confers testamentary power in respect of **legitimate children only**
- In respect of illegitimate children, **Section 9(4)** confers such power on **mother alone**

---

#### 4. Guardians Appointed by the Court

**âš–ï¸ Appointment Under Guardians and Wards Act, 1890**
- Where court is satisfied that it is **for welfare of minor** that order should be made
- Court may make order appointing guardian of:
  - Person of minor, OR
  - Property of minor, OR
  - Both

**Paramount Consideration**:
- **Welfare of minor** shall be the **paramount consideration**
- In appointing or declaring person as guardian

**ğŸ“œ Case Law: Mohini v. Virendra (AIR 1977 SC 1359)**
- Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act is **supplementary** to and **not in derogation** of Guardians and Wards Act

**Powers of Certificated Guardian**:
- Guardian appointed by court is known as **certificated guardian**
- Powers are **controlled by Guardians and Wards Act, 1890**
- **Very few acts** can be performed **without prior permission of court**
- Powers are **co-extensive with powers of sovereign**
- From date of appointment, certificated guardian is under:
  - **Supervision**
  - **Guidance**
  - **Control** of court

---

#### 5. De Facto Guardian

**ğŸ‘¤ Section 11: De Facto Guardian**

**Prohibition**: 
- No person shall be entitled to:
  - Dispose of, OR
  - Deal with
  - Property of Hindu minor
  - **Merely on the ground** of being de facto guardian

**Definition**:
- Person who is **not a legal guardian**
- Has **no authority in law** to act as such
- **Nonetheless** has assumed management of property of child
- **As though** he were a guardian

**Concept**:
- **De facto guardianship** is concept where **past acts result in present status**
- Term literally means **"from that which has been done"**
- Recognized in Hindu law as early as **1856**

**ğŸ“œ Case Law: Hanuman Prasad Singh v. Bhaguati Prasad Singh (1897)**
- Privy Council held: Bona fide incumbrancer from de facto guardian (for saving estate or benefit of estate) is **not affected** by want of union of de facto with de jure title

**Restrictions on De Facto Guardian**:
- **Does not have right** to:
  - Assume debt
  - Gift minor's property
  - Make reference to arbitration

---

#### 6. Custody of a Minor

**ğŸ¤± Section 13: Welfare of Child (Paramount Consideration)**

**Section 13** of Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956:
- In giving child in custody, OR
- While appointing guardian for him/her
- **Welfare of child** should be given **supreme importance**

**If court finds**:
- Giving child under someone's custody will be **detrimental to child**
- Court will **refrain from doing so**
- **No person** shall be appointed as guardian to minor

**Underlying Principle**:
- **Welfare of child** is underlying principle in:
  - Cases of custody
  - Cases of guardianship

**Non-Hindu Children**:
- For **non-Hindu children**:
  - Court's authority to intervene in furtherance of welfare principle
  - Is **subordinated** to that of father as natural guardian

**ğŸ“œ Section 26: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955**
- Court may give **interim orders** in cases related to:
  - **Custody** of minor children
  - **Education** of minor children
  - Other matters
- **With their consonance** (consent/consideration)
- Court is also authorized to **revoke** any such order previously passed
