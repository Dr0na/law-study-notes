# üìò VOID AND VOIDABLE MARRIAGES (Deep Study)

## üîç Introduction

Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 makes a clear distinction between **void marriages** (Section 11) and **voidable marriages** (Section 12). Understanding this distinction is crucial as it determines legal consequences, rights of parties, and legitimacy of children.

---

## üìö FUNDAMENTAL DISTINCTION

### **Void Marriage:**
- Marriage is **null and void ab initio** (from the beginning)
- No legal existence in eyes of law
- Creates no rights or obligations
- Decree of nullity merely declares existing position
- No formal decree necessary for marriage to be void

### **Voidable Marriage:**
- Marriage is **valid until annulled** by court
- Has legal existence until decree of nullity
- Creates rights and obligations until annulled
- Requires decree of court to set aside
- Cannot be questioned after death of either party (generally)

---

## üö´ VOID MARRIAGES (Section 11)

### **Section 11: Void Marriages**

**Complete Text:**
"Any marriage solemnized after the commencement of this Act shall be null and void and may, on a petition presented by either party thereto against the other party, be so declared by a decree of nullity if it contravenes any one of the conditions specified in clauses (i), (iv) and (v) of section 5."

---

## üìã GROUNDS FOR VOID MARRIAGE (3 Grounds)

### **Ground 1: LIVING SPOUSE [Section 5(i) violation]**

**Provision:**
"Neither party has a spouse living at the time of the marriage"

**Explanation:**
If at time of marriage, either party has a living spouse from a subsisting previous marriage, the marriage is **void**.

**Requirements for Void Marriage:**
1. Previous marriage exists
2. Previous marriage has not been dissolved by:
   - Death, OR
   - Divorce, OR
   - Decree of nullity
3. Second marriage solemnized during subsistence of first

**Consequence:**
- Marriage is **bigamous**
- Void ab initio
- Second spouse has no rights
- Children of second marriage are legitimate under Section 16

**Criminal Liability:**
- **IPC Section 494:** Bigamy - Imprisonment up to **7 years** and/or fine
- **IPC Section 495:** With concealment - Imprisonment up to **10 years** and fine

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male) marries B in 2015. Without divorcing B, A marries C in 2020. Marriage with C is **void** under Section 11 as B is still living and marriage subsisting.

**Illustration 2:**
A marries B in 2015. B dies in 2018. A marries C in 2020. Marriage with C is **valid** as B is no longer living.

**Illustration 3:**
A marries B in 2015. They get divorce decree in 2019. A marries C in 2020. Marriage with C is **valid** as first marriage dissolved.

---

#### **Important Case Laws:**

**1. Sarla Mudgal v. Union of India** (1995) 3 SCC 635
**Facts:** Hindu husband converted to Islam and contracted second marriage without dissolving first.
**Held:** 
- Conversion solely to remarry is fraud on law
- First marriage under Hindu Law continues
- Second marriage is void
- Recommended Uniform Civil Code

**2. Lily Thomas v. Union of India** (2000) 6 SCC 224
**Held:**
- Hindu Marriage Act continues to apply even after conversion
- Cannot escape personal law obligations through conversion
- Second marriage void

**3. Priya v. Suresh** (2011)
**Held:**
- Burden of proving dissolution of first marriage on person contracting second marriage
- Mere claim of divorce insufficient without proof

---

### **Ground 2: SAPINDA RELATIONSHIP [Section 5(iv) violation]**

**Provision:**
"The parties are not sapindas of each other, unless the custom or usage governing each of them permits of a marriage between the two"

**Explanation:**
Marriage between parties within prohibited degrees of sapinda relationship is void.

**Sapinda Definition [Section 3(f)]:**
Two persons are sapindas if one is lineal ascendant of other within:
- **3 degrees** (through father)
- **5 degrees** (through mother)

**Calculation:**
Exclude first generation, then count upward.

**Exception:**
Custom may permit such marriage if:
- Valid custom exists
- Applies to both parties
- Custom is proved

**Consequence:**
Marriage is **void** unless saved by custom.

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male) marries his father's sister (paternal aunt). Within 3 degrees through father. Marriage **void**.

**Illustration 2:**
A (male) marries his mother's mother (maternal grandmother). Within 5 degrees through mother. Marriage **void**.

**Illustration 3:**
A (male from Tamil Nadu) marries his mother's brother's daughter (cross-cousin). If Tamil custom permits, marriage **valid**.

---

#### **Important Case Laws:**

**1. Madhavrao v. Raghavendrarao** AIR 1946 SC 33
**Held:**
- Sapinda relationship strictly calculated as per Section 3(f)
- Marriage within prohibited degrees void unless custom proved

**2. Veerappa Setty v. Veeramma** AIR 1955 Mad 291
**Held:**
- Custom permitting sapinda marriage must be strictly established
- Burden on party claiming benefit of custom

---

### **Ground 3: PROHIBITED RELATIONSHIP [Section 5(v) + Schedule I violation]**

**Provision:**
"The parties are not within the degrees of prohibited relationship unless the custom or usage governing each of them permits of a marriage between the two"

**Explanation:**
Marriage between parties listed in **Schedule I** as prohibited relationships is void.

**Schedule I Prohibited Relationships:**

#### **For Males - Cannot Marry:**
- Mother
- Daughter
- Sister
- Father's sister
- Mother's sister
- Sister's daughter
- Brother's daughter
- Father's brother's daughter
- Mother's brother's daughter
- Father's sister's daughter
- Mother's sister's daughter

#### **For Females - Cannot Marry:**
- Father
- Son
- Brother
- Father's brother
- Mother's brother
- Sister's son
- Brother's son
- Father's brother's son
- Mother's brother's son
- Father's sister's son
- Mother's sister's son

**Note:** Relationships through adoption also included.

**Exception:**
Custom may permit (rarely applies in practice).

**Consequence:**
Marriage is **void ab initio**.

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male) marries his sister B. Prohibited under Schedule I. Marriage **void**.

**Illustration 2:**
A (male) marries his brother's daughter (niece). Prohibited under Schedule I. Marriage **void**.

---

#### **Case Law:**

**State of Bombay v. Narasu Appa Mali** AIR 1952 Bom 84
**Held:**
- Marriages within prohibited degrees void
- Personal laws don't violate fundamental rights

---

## üìä SUMMARY: VOID MARRIAGES

| **Ground** | **Section Violated** | **Explanation** |
|------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Living Spouse | 5(i) | Bigamy - previous marriage subsisting |
| Sapinda | 5(iv) | Within 3/5 degrees of relationship |
| Prohibited Relationship | 5(v) + Schedule I | Listed prohibited relatives |

## ‚öñÔ∏è VOIDABLE MARRIAGES (Section 12)

### **Section 12: Voidable Marriages**

**Complete Text:**
"Any marriage solemnized, whether before or after the commencement of this Act, shall be voidable and may be annulled by a decree of nullity on any of the following grounds, namely:‚Äî"

---

## üìã GROUNDS FOR VOIDABLE MARRIAGE (5 Grounds)

### **Ground 1: NON-CONSUMMATION DUE TO IMPOTENCE [Section 12(1)(a)]**

**Provision:**
"That the marriage has not been consummated owing to the impotence of the respondent"

**Requirements:**
1. Marriage not consummated
2. Due to **impotence** of respondent (other party)
3. Impotence must be:
   - **Incurable**, OR
   - **Of such nature** that petitioner cannot reasonably be expected to consummate

**Meaning of Impotence:**
Incapacity to perform sexual intercourse.

**Types:**
- **Physical impotence:** Anatomical defects
- **Psychological impotence:** Mental inability

**Proof Required:**
Medical examination may be ordered by court.

**Time Limit:**
Within **ONE YEAR** from date of marriage [Section 12(2)(a)].

**Bars to Relief [Section 12(2)]:**
Petition dismissed if:
- Petitioner knew of impotence and consented to marriage
- Parties cohabited with free consent after petitioner knew of impotence

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (wife) marries B (husband). B suffers from incurable impotence. Marriage cannot be consummated. A can get decree of nullity within 1 year.

**Illustration 2:**
A marries B knowing B is impotent. A consents to marriage despite knowledge. A cannot get relief.

**Illustration 3:**
A discovers B's impotence after marriage. They continue living together for 2 years. A may be barred from relief due to approbation.

---

#### **Important Case Laws:**

**1. Ramanika v. Kushalya** AIR 1965 J&K 34
**Held:**
- Impotence means incapacity to perform sexual intercourse
- Must be established through medical evidence

**2. Smt. Bani Sarkar v. Jitendra Nath Sarkar** AIR 1974 Cal 139
**Held:**
- Impotence must exist at time of marriage
- Supervening impotence not ground for annulment

---

### **Ground 2: MENTAL INCAPACITY [Section 12(1)(b)]**

**Provision:**
"That the marriage is in contravention of the condition specified in clause (ii) of section 5"

**Section 5(ii) Violations:**

#### **2(a): Incapable of Giving Valid Consent**
- Due to unsoundness of mind
- Cannot understand nature of marriage
- Cannot comprehend duties and responsibilities

#### **2(b): Mental Disorder**
- Suffering from mental disorder
- Of such kind/extent as to be unfit for:
  - Marriage, AND
  - Procreation of children

#### **2(c): Recurrent Insanity**
- Subject to recurrent attacks of insanity
- Pattern of repeated episodes

**Proof Required:**
Medical evidence essential.

**Time Limit:**
NO specific time limit (unlike impotence).

**Bars to Relief [Section 12(2)]:**
- Petitioner knew of mental condition at time of marriage
- Parties cohabited with free consent after knowledge of condition

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A marries B. After marriage, discovers B suffers from schizophrenia making B unfit for marriage and procreation. A can seek annulment.

**Illustration 2:**
A marries B knowing B has recurrent insanity. A cannot later seek annulment on this ground.

---

#### **Case Law:**

**Lila Gupta v. Laxmi Narain** AIR 1978 Del 32
**Held:**
- Medical evidence essential
- Mere eccentric behavior insufficient
- Pattern of insanity must be established

---

### **Ground 3: UNDERAGE [Section 12(1)(c)]**

**Provision:**
"That the consent of the petitioner, or where the consent of the guardian in marriage of the petitioner was required under section 5 as it stood immediately before the commencement of the Child Marriage Restraint (Amendment) Act, 1978 (2 of 1978), the consent of such guardian was obtained by force or by fraud as to the nature of the ceremony or as to any material fact or circumstance concerning the respondent"

**Requirements:**
Marriage contravenes Section 5(iii) - age requirement:
- Bridegroom not completed 21 years, OR
- Bride not completed 18 years

**Who Can Petition:**
Only the **minor party** (party who was underage at marriage).

**Time Limit:**
Within **TWO YEARS** of attaining majority [Section 12(2)(c)].

**Consequence:**
Marriage voidable at option of minor party.

**Important:**
Also governed by **Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006** which gives additional rights.

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male, 20 years) marries B (female, 17 years). B can seek annulment within 2 years of turning 18 (i.e., before turning 20).

**Illustration 2:**
A (female, 16 years) marries B (male, 25 years). A can seek annulment before turning 20. After 20, right lapses.

---

### **Ground 4: CONSENT BY FORCE OR FRAUD [Section 12(1)(c)]**

**Provision:**
"That the consent of the petitioner... was obtained by force or by fraud as to the nature of the ceremony or as to any material fact or circumstance concerning the respondent"

**Two Types:**

#### **4(a): Consent by Force**
- Consent obtained through force, coercion, or threat
- No free will in giving consent
- Physical or mental force

#### **4(b): Consent by Fraud**
- Fraud as to **nature of ceremony**, OR
- Fraud as to **material fact or circumstance** concerning respondent

**Material Fraud Includes:**
- Concealment of previous marriage
- Concealment of serious disease
- Concealment of impotence
- False representation about character, status, health

**Not Material Fraud:**
- False claims about wealth or property (generally)
- Minor misrepresentations

**Time Limit:**
Within **ONE YEAR** of force ceasing or fraud being discovered [Section 12(2)(b)].

**Bars to Relief [Section 12(2)]:**
- Parties lived together as husband and wife with free consent after force ceased or fraud discovered

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (female) forced to marry B at gunpoint. She can seek annulment within 1 year after force ceases.

**Illustration 2:**
B conceals fact that he is HIV positive. A marries B. After discovery, A can seek annulment within 1 year.

**Illustration 3:**
B falsely claims to be engineer when actually unemployed. This may not be material fraud sufficient for annulment.

---

#### **Important Case Laws:**

**1. Vimlaben Ajitlal Patel v. Vatslaben Ashokbhai Patel** AIR 2008 Guj 21
**Held:**
- Concealment of material fact like previous marriage amounts to fraud
- Consent obtained by fraud vitiates marriage

**2. A. v. B** (1939) ILR 18 Pat 497
**Held:**
- Fraud must relate to material fact
- Must go to root of consent

---

### **Ground 5: PREGNANCY BY ANOTHER MAN [Section 12(1)(d)]**

**Provision:**
"That the respondent was at the time of the marriage pregnant by some person other than the petitioner"

**Requirements:**
1. Respondent (wife) was **pregnant** at time of marriage
2. Pregnancy by **person other than petitioner** (husband)
3. Petitioner was **unaware** of pregnancy

**Who Can Petition:**
Only **husband** (petitioner must be husband).

**Time Limit:**
Within **ONE YEAR** from knowledge of pregnancy [Section 12(2)(d)].

**Bars to Relief [Section 12(2)]:**
- Husband knew of pregnancy before marriage
- Parties lived together as husband and wife with husband's free consent after he had knowledge of pregnancy

**Proof:**
Medical evidence may be required.

**Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male) marries B (female). At time of marriage, B was pregnant by C. A was unaware. A discovers pregnancy 2 months after marriage. A can seek annulment within 1 year of discovery.

**Illustration 2:**
A marries B knowing B is pregnant by C. A accepts the situation. A cannot later seek annulment on this ground.

**Illustration 3:**
A marries B. Discovers B was pregnant by C at time of marriage. They continue living together for 6 months. A may be barred from relief due to approbation.

---

#### **Case Law:**

**T. Sareetha v. T. Venkata Subbaiah** AIR 1983 AP 356
Deals with privacy and dignity in matrimonial proceedings.

| 2 | Mental incapacity | 12(1)(b) | None specified |
| 3 | Underage | 12(1)(c) | 2 years after attaining majority |

## ‚öñÔ∏è BARS TO RELIEF [Section 12(2)]

Courts shall dismiss petition for voidable marriage if:

### **Bar 1: Time Limit [Section 12(2)(a), (b), (c), (d)]**
- Specific time limits prescribed for each ground
- Petition after time limit is barred

### **Bar 2: Approbation of Marriage [Section 12(2)]**
If petitioner:
- Knew of condition giving right to annulment, AND
- Led respondent reasonably to believe he/she would not seek annulment, AND
- It would be unjust to respondent to grant decree

**Principle:** Delay, acquiescence, or approval bars relief.

### **Bar 3: Death of Either Party**
After death of either party, petition for voidable marriage cannot be instituted.

## üîÑ COMPARATIVE TABLE: VOID vs VOIDABLE

| **Aspect** | **Void Marriage** | **Voidable Marriage** |
|------------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Legal Status** | Null from beginning | Valid until annulled |
| **Decree Necessity** | Not essential (declaratory) | Essential (constitutive) |
| **Grounds** | 3 grounds (Sec 5(i), (iv), (v)) | 5 grounds (Sec 12(1)) |
| **Who Can Petition** | Either party, even strangers | Only aggrieved spouse |
| **Time Limit** | None (any time) | Specific limits for each ground |
| **After Death** | Can be declared void even after death | Cannot be annulled after death |
| **Rights Created** | None (except Sec 16 children) | Full rights until annulment |
| **Remarriage** | No need for decree | Need decree before remarriage |
| **Cohabitation** | Does not validate | May bar relief (approbation) |
| **Bars to Relief** | None | Yes [Sec 12(2)] |

## üìú SECTION 16: LEGITIMACY OF CHILDREN

### **Section 16: Legitimacy of children of void and voidable marriages**

**Provision:**
Children of void or voidable marriages are **legitimate** if:
1. Marriage was null or annulled, AND
2. Child conceived before decree of nullity, AND  
3. At time of intercourse:
   - Parties did not know of facts making marriage void, OR
   - In case of voidable marriage, before decree

**Effect:**
Children have rights to:
- Maintenance
- Succession  
- Property

**Limitation:**
Children have rights only to property of **parents**, not other relatives.

**Illustration:**
A marries B. Marriage is void (A had living spouse). Child C born from this marriage. C is legitimate and has rights to A's and B's property (not property of A's other relatives).

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è LANDMARK CASE LAWS

### **1. Satya v. Teja Singh** AIR 1975 SC 105
**Held:**
- Conditions under Section 5 mandatory
- Violation makes marriage void or voidable as specified
- Clear distinction between void and voidable marriages

### **2. Chandra Mohini Srivastava v. Avinash Prasad Srivastava** AIR 1967 All 407
**Held:**
- Void marriage non-existent in law
- No decree needed for it to be void
- Decree merely declaratory

### **3. Yamunabai Anantrao Adhav v. Anantrao Shivram Adhav** AIR 1988 SC 644
**Held:**
- Voidable marriage valid until decree of nullity
- Bars under Section 12(2) must be considered

## üéì KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

### **Void Marriages - "LiSPro"**
- **Li**ving spouse (bigamy)
- **S**apinda relationship
- **Pro**hibited relationship (Schedule I)

### **Voidable Marriages - "IMPUF"**
- **I**mpotence
- **M**ental incapacity
- **P**regnancy by other
- **U**nderage
- **F**orce/Fraud

### **Critical Differences:**
1. Void = null from start; Voidable = valid until annulled
2. Void = no time limit; Voidable = time limits apply
3. Void = anyone can question; Voidable = only parties
4. Void = no bars; Voidable = bars apply

### **Section 16:**
Children of void/voidable marriages are legitimate (with conditions).
