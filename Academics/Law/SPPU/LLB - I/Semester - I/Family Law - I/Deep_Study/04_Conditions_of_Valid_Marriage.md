# üìò CONDITIONS OF VALID HINDU MARRIAGE (Deep Study)

## üîç Introduction

Section 5 of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 lays down **SEVEN essential conditions** that must be fulfilled for a valid Hindu marriage. These conditions are **mandatory** and their violation renders the marriage either void or voidable.

---

## üìú SECTION 5: CONDITIONS FOR A HINDU MARRIAGE

**Complete Text:**
"A marriage may be solemnized between any two Hindus, if the following conditions are fulfilled, namely:‚Äî"

### **THE 7 ESSENTIAL CONDITIONS:**

---

## 1Ô∏è‚É£ CONDITION 1: NO LIVING SPOUSE [Section 5(i)]

### **Text of Provision:**
"Neither party has a spouse living at the time of marriage"

### **Explanation:**
This condition mandates **monogamy** in Hindu marriage.

### **Legal Requirements:**
- Both parties must be **unmarried** at time of marriage, OR
- If previously married, prior marriage must have been dissolved by:
  - **Divorce**, OR
  - **Death** of former spouse, OR
  - **Decree of nullity**

### **Consequence of Violation:**
Marriage is **VOID** under Section 11.

### **Criminal Consequences:**
- Violation attracts punishment under **IPC Section 494** (Bigamy)
- Punishment: **7 years imprisonment** and/or fine
- **IPC Section 495** if fraud involved (concealing former marriage)

### **Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male) marries B (female). Without divorcing B, A marries C. Marriage between A and C is **void** as A has living spouse B.

**Illustration 2:**
A marries B. B dies. A then marries C. This marriage is **valid** as B is no longer living.

**Illustration 3:**
A marries B. They get divorced by decree. A marries C. This marriage is **valid** as first marriage dissolved.

### **Exception:**
**Custom may permit polygamy** - If parties are governed by custom permitting polygamy, second marriage may be valid under customary law (though this is extremely rare and courts are reluctant to recognize such customs in modern times).

### **Important Case Laws:**

#### **Sarla Mudgal v. Union of India** (1995) 3 SCC 635
**Facts:** Hindu husband converted to Islam and married second wife without divorcing first wife.
**Held:** Conversion merely to contract second marriage is fraud on law. First marriage remains valid under Hindu Law. Second marriage is void.

#### **Lily Thomas v. Union of India** (2000) 6 SCC 224
**Held:** Person remains Hindu for purposes of Hindu Marriage Act even after conversion if married under Hindu law. Cannot escape first marriage by conversion.

#### **Bhaurao Shankar Lokhande v. State of Maharashtra** AIR 1965 SC 1564
**Held:** Burden of proving first marriage has been dissolved lies on person contracting second marriage.

---

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ CONDITION 2: MENTAL CAPACITY [Section 5(ii)]

### **Text of Provision:**
"Neither party‚Äî
(a) is incapable of giving a valid consent to it in consequence of unsoundness of mind; or
(b) though capable of giving valid consent, has been suffering from mental disorder of such a kind or to such an extent as to be unfit for marriage and the procreation of children; or
(c) has been subject to recurrent attacks of insanity"

### **THREE Sub-Conditions:**

---

### **2(a): Incapable of Giving Valid Consent**

**Meaning:**
Party must be capable of understanding:
- Nature of marriage
- Duties and responsibilities
- Consequences of entering into marriage

**Examples of Incapacity:**
- Severe mental illness
- Intellectual disability preventing comprehension
- Unsoundness of mind at time of marriage

**Consequence:**
Marriage is **VOIDABLE** at option of other party [Section 12(1)(b)].

**Case Law:**
**Bimala Bose v. Satyendra Nath Bose** AIR 1952 Cal 396
**Held:** Person of unsound mind cannot give valid consent. Marriage voidable.

---

### **2(b): Mental Disorder Unfit for Marriage and Procreation**

**Requirements:**
- Party has mental disorder
- Disorder makes party unfit for:
  - Marriage, AND
  - Procreation of children
- Must be of such kind and extent

**Examples:**
- Severe schizophrenia
- Psychotic disorders
- Conditions preventing ability to discharge marital obligations

**Consequence:**
Marriage is **VOIDABLE** at option of other party [Section 12(1)(b)].

**Important:**
Both unfitness for marriage AND procreation must be established.

---

### **2(c): Recurrent Attacks of Insanity**

**Requirements:**
- Party has suffered recurrent attacks of insanity
- Pattern of repeated episodes
- Insanity must be established medically

**Consequence:**
Marriage is **VOIDABLE** at option of other party [Section 12(1)(b)].

**Case Law:**
**Lila Gupta v. Laxmi Narain** AIR 1978 Del 32
**Held:** Medical evidence essential to prove recurrent insanity. Mere eccentric behavior insufficient.

---

### **Important Principles on Mental Capacity:**

#### **Time of Assessment:**
Mental capacity must exist at the **time of marriage**.

#### **Burden of Proof:**
Party alleging incapacity must prove it.

#### **Medical Evidence:**
Courts require credible medical evidence.

#### **Temporary vs Permanent:**
Even temporary insanity at time of marriage sufficient to make marriage voidable.

---

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ CONDITION 3: AGE REQUIREMENT [Section 5(iii)]

### **Text of Provision:**
"The bridegroom has completed the age of twenty-one years and the bride, the age of eighteen years at the time of marriage"

### **Age Requirements:**

#### **Bridegroom (Male):**
- Must have **completed 21 years**
- Means attained 21st birthday

#### **Bride (Female):**
- Must have **completed 18 years**  
- Means attained 18th birthday

**Note:** Recent legislative proposals aim to increase bride's age to 21 years to bring parity. Check latest amendment status.

### **Consequence of Violation:**

Marriage is **NOT void** but:
- Marriage is **VOIDABLE** at option of minor party [Section 12(1)(c)]
- Minor can seek annulment within **2 years** of attaining majority

### **Additional Consequences:**

#### **1. Child Marriage Restraint:**
**Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006:**
- Child marriage is voidable at option of contracting party who was child
- Can be annulled before attaining majority
- **Within 2 years** after attaining majority

#### **2. Penal Provisions:**
Under Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006:
- Male adult above 18 marrying minor: **2 years imprisonment** and/or fine up to ‚Çπ1 lakh
- Person performing/conducting/directing child marriage: **2 years imprisonment** and/or fine up to ‚Çπ1 lakh

#### **3. Registration Difficulty:**
Many states refuse to register marriages where age requirement not met.

### **Proof of Age:**
- Birth certificate
- School leaving certificate
- Passport
- Affidavit
- Medical examination (in case of dispute)

### **Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male, 20 years) marries B (female, 19 years). Marriage is voidable. Either party can get it annulled within 2 years of attaining prescribed age.

**Illustration 2:**
A (male, 22 years) marries B (female, 17 years). Marriage voidable at option of B within 2 years after B turns 18.

### **Important Case Laws:**

#### **Jitendra Kumar v. State of Haryana** (2012) 11 SCC 493
**Held:** Penal provisions under Prohibition of Child Marriage Act are in addition to provisions under Hindu Marriage Act.

#### **Smt. Shewanti v. State of Haryana** 2014 Cr LJ 3399
**Held:** Medical test (ossification test) can determine age when documents unavailable.

## 4Ô∏è‚É£ CONDITION 4: NO SAPINDA RELATIONSHIP [Section 5(iv)]

### **Text of Provision:**
"The parties are not within the degrees of prohibited relationship unless the custom or usage governing each of them permits of a marriage between the two"

### **Explanation - Sapinda:**

**Definition [Section 3(f)]:**
Two persons are sapindas of each other if one is lineal ascendant of other within:
- **3 degrees** (through father), OR
- **5 degrees** (through mother)

### **How to Calculate Degrees:**

**Rule:** Exclude the first generation, then count upward.

**Example (Father's Line):**
```
Self ‚Üí Father (excluded) ‚Üí Grandfather (1) ‚Üí Great-grandfather (2) ‚Üí GG-Grandfather (3)
= 3 degrees through father
```

**Example (Mother's Line):**
```
Self ‚Üí Mother (excluded) ‚Üí Maternal Grandfather (1) ‚Üí Mat. Great-GF (2) ‚Üí Mat. GG-GF (3) ‚Üí Mat. GGG-GF (4) ‚Üí Mat. GGGG-GF (5)
= 5 degrees through mother
```

### **Prohibited Sapinda Relationships:**

Marriage between following sapindas is **prohibited**:

#### **Through Father (within 3 degrees):**
- Self and Father's sister (Aunt)
- Self and Father's mother (Grandmother)
- Self and Father's father's sister
- Self and Father's mother's sister

#### **Through Mother (within 5 degrees):**
- Self and Mother's sister (Aunt)
- Self and Mother's mother (Grandmother)
- Self and any female ascendant within 5 degrees on mother's side

### **Consequence of Violation:**
Marriage is **VOID** under Section 11.

### **Exception - Custom:**
"Unless the custom or usage governing each of them permits of a marriage between the two"

**Application:**
- In South India, cross-cousin marriages customary
- Marriage with mother's brother's daughter permitted by custom
- Custom must be proved

**Burden of Proof:**
Party claiming benefit of custom must prove:
- Custom exists
- Custom applies to both parties
- Custom is valid

### **Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male) wants to marry his father's sister B. This is within sapinda relationship (within 3 degrees through father). Marriage is **void** unless custom permits.

**Illustration 2:**
A (male from Tamil Nadu) marries B (his maternal uncle's daughter). This is valid if South Indian custom permits cross-cousin marriage.

### **Important Case Laws:**

#### **Madhavrao v. Raghavendrarao** AIR 1946 SC 33
**Held:** Sapinda relationship calculated according to Section 3(f). Marriage within prohibited degrees void.

#### **Veerappa Setty v. Veeramma** AIR 1955 Mad 291
**Held:** Custom permitting marriage within sapinda degree must be strictly proved.

## 5Ô∏è‚É£ CONDITION 5: NO PROHIBITED RELATIONSHIP [Section 5(v)]

### **Text of Provision:**
"The parties are not sapindas of each other, unless the custom or usage governing each of them permits of a marriage between the two"

**Note:** This repeats sapinda prohibition from Condition 4. Additionally refers to **Schedule I** for prohibited relationships.

### **Prohibited Relationships (Schedule I):**

Schedule I lists relationships within which marriage is absolutely prohibited.

#### **Relationships Prohibited:**

**A. Through Blood:**

1. If a man, with:
   - Mother
   - Daughter
   - Sister
   - Father's sister
   - Mother's sister
   - Sister's daughter
   - Brother's daughter
   - Father's brother's daughter
   - Father's sister's daughter
   - Mother's brother's daughter
   - Mother's sister's daughter

2. If a woman, with:
   - Father
   - Son
   - Brother
   - Father's brother
   - Mother's brother
   - Sister's son
   - Brother's son
   - Father's sister's son
   - Mother's brother's son
   - Mother's sister's son
   - Father's brother's son

**B. Through Adoption:**
Same prohibitions apply to relationships created through valid adoption.

### **Consequence of Violation:**
Marriage is **VOID** under Section 11.

### **Exception - Custom:**
Custom may permit marriage within these degrees (rare in practice).

### **Difference: Sapinda vs Prohibited Relationship:**

| **Sapinda** | **Prohibited Relationship** |
|-------------|----------------------------|
| Defined by degrees (3/5) | Listed in Schedule I |
| Lineal ascendants/descendants | Includes collateral relatives also |
| Religious concept | Legal specification |
| Custom can permit | Custom can permit (rarely) |

### **Illustrations:**

**Illustration 1:**
A (male) wants to marry his sister B. This is **prohibited relationship** under Schedule I. Marriage absolutely void.

**Illustration 2:**
A (male) wants to marry his niece (brother's daughter). This is prohibited under Schedule I. Marriage void.

## 7Ô∏è‚É£ CONDITION 7: CEREMONIES AND CUSTOMS [Section 7]

**Note:** While Section 5 lists substantive conditions, **Section 7** prescribes procedural requirement.

### **Text of Section 7:**
"(1) A Hindu marriage may be solemnized in accordance with the customary rites and ceremonies of either party thereto.

(2) Where such rites and ceremonies include the *Saptapadi* (that is, the taking of seven steps by the bridegroom and the bride jointly before the sacred fire), the marriage becomes complete and binding when the seventh step is taken."

### **Requirements:**

#### **1. Customary Rites and Ceremonies:**
- Marriage must be solemnized according to customary rites
- Custom of either party sufficient
- No particular form mandatory

#### **2. Saptapadi (Seven Steps):**
If ceremony includes Saptapadi:
- Marriage complete when **7th step taken**
- Before 7th step, marriage not complete
- Sacred fire (Agni) as witness

### **Saptapadi - The Seven Vows:**

Each step represents a vow:
1. **First Step:** Nourishment and food
2. **Second Step:** Strength and vitality
3. **Third Step:** Prosperity and wealth
4. **Fourth Step:** Happiness and harmony
5. **Fifth Step:** Progeny and children
6. **Sixth Step:** Longevity and seasons
7. **Seventh Step:** Friendship and companionship

**Completion:**
Marriage complete and binding at 7th step.

### **Variations in Customs:**

Different communities have different ceremonies:
- Some perform Saptapadi
- Some perform Saptapadi with variations
- Some don't perform Saptapadi at all

**All Valid:** As long as ceremony is according to custom of either party.

### **Consequence of Non-Performance:**

If essential ceremonies not performed:
- Marriage may be **void**
- Question of fact whether ceremony essential to custom

### **Case Laws:**

#### **Smt. Shanti v. Ram Kishan** AIR 1984 Raj 59
**Held:** If Saptapadi not performed and it is essential custom, marriage void.

#### **Chhaganlal Govindlal v. Municipal Corporation of Delhi** AIR 1964 SC 1411
**Held:** Saptapadi is completion point of marriage where it is part of ceremony.

## ‚öñÔ∏è VOID vs VOIDABLE - Quick Reference

### **VOID Conditions:**
- Living spouse [5(i)]
- Sapinda relationship [5(iv)]
- Prohibited relationship [5(v)]
- Essential ceremonies not performed [Sec 7]

### **VOIDABLE Conditions:**
- Mental incapacity [5(ii)]
- Underage [5(iii)]

### **Important Numbers:**
- **7 conditions** in Section 5
- **21 years** (bridegroom)

1. Conditions are **mandatory**
2. Some violations = **void**, some = **voidable**
3. Custom can save certain violations
4. Burden of proving custom on party claiming it
