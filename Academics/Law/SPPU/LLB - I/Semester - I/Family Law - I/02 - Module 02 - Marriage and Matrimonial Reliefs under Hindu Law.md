**ğŸ“‹ Section 5(i): Bigamy**
- Neither party has spouse living
- **Effect**: Contravention = **VOID** (Section 11)
- **Punishment**: Section 494-495 IPC
- **Case**: G.S. Raj v. Bandaru Pavani (AIR 2009 SC 1085)

**ğŸ“‹ Section 5(ii): Mental Capacity**
- Not incapable of valid consent (unsoundness)
- Not suffering from mental disorder (unfit for marriage/procreation)
- Not subject to recurrent attacks of insanity/epilepsy
- **Effect**: Contravention = **VOIDABLE** (Section 12(1)(b))

**ğŸ“‹ Section 5(iii): Age**
- Male: 21 years, Female: 18 years
- **Effect**: Child Marriage = Voidable (PCMA 2006)
- **Case**: Arunkumar and Sreeja v. Inspector General (2019) - trans woman included as "bride"

**ğŸ“‹ Section 5(iv): Prohibited Relationship**
- Not within degrees of prohibited relationship
- **Exception**: Custom permits (Section 3(a))
- **Effect**: Contravention = **VOID** (Section 11)
- **Includes**: Half blood, uterine blood, illegitimate, adoption relationships

**ğŸ“‹ Section 5(v): Sapinda Relationship**
- Not sapindas of each other
- **Limits**: 3 generations (maternal), 5 generations (paternal)
- **Exception**: Custom permits
- **Effect**: Contravention = **VOID** (Section 11)
- **Theories**: Oblation theory, Particles of same body theory

**ğŸ“‹ Section 7: Ceremonies**
- Customary rites and ceremonies of either party
- **Saptapadi**: Marriage complete when 7th step taken
- **Case**: Sitabai v. Vithabai (1958) - presumption of valid ceremonies
- **Special**: Section 7-A (Tamil Nadu) - simple declaration valid

**ğŸ“‹ Section 8: Registration**
- Optional but recommended
- **Case**: Seema v. Ashwani Kumar (2006) - SC directed compulsory registration

#### 2. Grounds of Divorce

**ğŸ“‹ Section 13(1): Both Husband and Wife (9 Grounds)**

1. **Adultery** (Section 13(1)(i))
   - Voluntary sexual intercourse with person other than spouse
   - **Case**: Joseph Shine v. Union of India (2018) - adultery not offence under IPC, but still ground for divorce

2. **Cruelty** (Section 13(1)(ia))
   - Treated with cruelty after marriage
   - Can be physical or mental
   - Courts interpret liberally

3. **Desertion** (Section 13(1)(ib))
   - Deserted for continuous period of not less than 2 years
   - Must be immediately preceding presentation of petition

4. **Conversion** (Section 13(1)(ii))
   - Ceased to be Hindu by conversion to another religion

5. **Mental disorder** (Section 13(1)(iii))
   - Incurably of unsound mind OR
   - Mental disorder of such kind/extent that petitioner cannot reasonably be expected to live with respondent
   - Includes: Schizophrenia, psychopathic disorder

6. **Leprosy** (Section 13(1)(iv))
   - Virulent and incurable form

7. **Venereal disease** (Section 13(1)(v))
   - In communicable form

8. **Renunciation** (Section 13(1)(vi))
   - Renounced the world by entering religious order

9. **Not heard of** (Section 13(1)(vii))
   - Not been heard of as being alive for 7 years or more

**ğŸ“‹ Section 13(1-A): Breakdown Grounds (2 Grounds)**

1. **No resumption after judicial separation** (Section 13(1-A)(i))
   - No resumption of cohabitation for 1 year or more
   - After decree of judicial separation

2. **No restitution after decree** (Section 13(1-A)(ii))
   - No restitution of conjugal rights for 1 year or more
   - After decree of restitution

**ğŸ“‹ Section 13(2): Wife Only (4 Grounds)**

1. **Bigamy** (pre-1955 marriages) (Section 13(2)(i))
   - Husband had married again before commencement of Act
   - Other wife must be alive at time of petition

2. **Rape, sodomy, bestiality** (Section 13(2)(ii))
   - Husband guilty of such offence since marriage

3. **Non-resumption after maintenance order** (Section 13(2)(iii))
   - Order for maintenance passed
   - No resumption of cohabitation for 1 year or more

4. **Repudiation** (Section 13(2)(iv))
   - Married before attaining 15 years
   - Repudiated after 15 but before 18 years

**ğŸ“‹ Section 13-B: Mutual Consent**

**Requirements**:
1. Living separately for 1 year or more
2. Not able to live together
3. Mutually agreed to dissolve

**Procedure**:
- Joint petition
- Wait at least 6 months
- Make joint motion before 18 months
- Court passes decree after inquiry

**Key Cases**:
- **Sureshta Devi v. Om Prakash (1991)**: Mutual consent must continue till decree
- **Amardeep Singh v. Harveen Kaur (2017)**: 6 months can be waived in exceptional cases

**ğŸ“‹ Section 14: Restrictions**
- No divorce within 1 year of marriage
- **Exception**: Exceptional hardship or depravity

**ğŸ“‹ Section 15: Remarriage**
- Can remarry when no right of appeal OR appeal dismissed

## ğŸ“Œ UNIT 3: NULLITY OF MARRIAGE

### ğŸ§  MASTER STRUCTURED NOTES

#### 1. Void Marriage (Section 11)

**âŒ Characteristics**
1. Void marriage is NO marriage
2. Does not exist from beginning
3. No legal consequences flow from it

**ğŸ“‹ Grounds (Section 11)**
1. Bigamy (Section 5(i))
2. Prohibited relationship (Section 5(iv))
3. Sapinda relationship (Section 5(v))

**âš ï¸ Important Points**
- No decree necessary (but can be declared)
- Who can petition: Either party to the marriage
- Special case: First wife can bring declaratory suit

---

#### 2. Voidable Marriage (Section 12)

**âš ï¸ Characteristics**
1. Valid until avoided
2. Can be avoided only by party
3. Remains valid if not avoided
4. Cannot challenge after death
5. Legal consequences flow until avoided
6. Retrospective effect when avoided

**ğŸ“‹ Grounds (Section 12(1))**

1. **Impotency** (Section 12(1)(a))
   - Marriage not consummated
   - **Case**: Digvijay Singh v. Pratap Kumari (1970)

2. **Mental Capacity** (Section 12(1)(b))
   - Contravention of Section 5(ii)

3. **Consent by Force or Fraud** (Section 12(1)(c))
   - Within 1 year of discovery
   - Must not have lived together after discovery

4. **Pre-marriage Pregnancy** (Section 12(1)(d))
   - Pregnant by person other than petitioner
   - Within 1 year of marriage
   - No marital intercourse since discovery

**ğŸ“‹ Section 16(1): Void Marriage**

**ğŸ“‹ Section 16(2): Voidable Marriage**

**ğŸ“‹ Section 16(3): Property Rights**
