# Case Law - Very Brief Gist

## üìã Quick Case Law Reference

### 1. **Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co. (1893)**

**Gist:** 
- Company advertised reward for using product; woman used it, got flu, claimed reward
- **Principle:** Offer can be made to the world (unilateral contract)
- **Rule:** Acceptance by conduct is valid; no need to communicate acceptance for unilateral contracts
- **Relevance:** Section 2(a), 2(b) - Offer & Acceptance; Section 9 - Implied acceptance

---

### 2. **Hadley v. Baxendale (1854)**

**Gist:**
- Mill shaft delayed in delivery; mill closed; claimed lost profits
- **Principle:** Damages must be foreseeable at time of contract
- **Rule:** Recoverable damages are:
  - Arising naturally from breach, OR
  - Reasonably supposed to be in contemplation of parties
- **Relevance:** Section 73 - Remoteness of damages

---

### 3. **Nash v. Inman (1908)**

**Gist:**
- Minor bought clothes, later refused to pay
- **Principle:** Minor's contract is void (not voidable)
- **Rule:** Contracts with minors are void ab initio; cannot be enforced against minor
- **Relevance:** Section 11 - Capacity to contract

---

### 4. **Lalman Shukla v. Gauri Dutt (1913)**

**Gist:**
- Employee found employer's missing nephew; employer had offered reward
- **Principle:** Acceptance must be communicated to offeror
- **Rule:** Mere performance of act without knowledge of offer does not constitute acceptance
- **Relevance:** Section 4 - Communication of acceptance

---

### 5. **Balfour v. Balfour (1919)**

**Gist:**
- Husband promised wife monthly allowance; they separated; wife sued
- **Principle:** Domestic/social agreements lack intention to create legal relations
- **Rule:** Agreements between spouses/family members presumed not to create legal obligations
- **Relevance:** Agreement vs Contract distinction; Intention to create legal relations

---
