### 3. OFFER & ACCEPTANCE (Sections 2(a), 2(b), 3-9) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

#### Key Rules to Remember:

**OFFER:**
- Must be definite and certain
- Must be communicated (Section 3)
- Can be revoked before acceptance (Section 5)
- Lapses by rejection, counter-offer, time, death

**ACCEPTANCE:**
- Must be absolute and unqualified (Section 7)
- Must be communicated (Section 4)
- Can be express or implied (Section 9)
- Postal rule: Acceptance complete when posted

**Communication Rules:**
- **Offer**: When it comes to knowledge of offeree
- **Acceptance**: When posted (postal) or communicated
- **Revocation**: Before acceptance reaches offeror

**Common Exam Questions:**
- When is offer/acceptance complete?
- Can offer be revoked?
- What is counter-offer?

---

### 4. CONSIDERATION (Section 2(d), 25) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

#### Rules of Consideration:
1. ‚úÖ Must move from promisee
2. ‚úÖ May be past, present, or future
3. ‚úÖ Need not be adequate
4. ‚úÖ Must be real, not illusory
5. ‚úÖ Must be lawful

#### Exceptions (No Consideration Needed):
- **Section 25(1)**: Natural love and affection
- **Section 25(2)**: Compensation for past voluntary act
- **Section 25(3)**: Promise to pay time-barred debt
- Completed gift

**Common Exam Questions:**
- Is consideration necessary?
- What are the exceptions?
- Is past consideration valid?

### 5. FREE CONSENT (Sections 14-22) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

#### Vitiating Factors (Makes Contract Voidable):

| Factor | Section | Key Point |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Coercion** | 15 | Physical/legal threat |
| **Undue Influence** | 16 | Mental pressure, dominant position |
| **Fraud** | 17 | Intentional deception |
| **Misrepresentation** | 18 | Innocent false statement |
| **Mistake** | 20-22 | Bilateral = Void, Unilateral = Valid |

**Important Distinctions:**
- **Coercion vs Undue Influence**: Threat vs Mental pressure
- **Fraud vs Misrepresentation**: Intentional vs Innocent
- **Bilateral vs Unilateral Mistake**: Both parties vs One party

**Common Exam Questions:**
- What vitiates free consent?
- Distinguish between fraud and misrepresentation
- When is mistake fatal to contract?

### 6. CAPACITY TO CONTRACT (Sections 11-13) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

#### Who Can Contract?

**COMPETENT (Section 11):**
- ‚úÖ Age of majority (18 years)
- ‚úÖ Sound mind (Section 12)
- ‚úÖ Not disqualified by law

**NOT COMPETENT:**
- ‚ùå Minors (Contract is VOID)
- ‚ùå Persons of unsound mind
- ‚ùå Persons disqualified (alien enemies, convicts, insolvents)

**Key Case:** Nash v. Inman - Minor's contract is void

**Common Exam Questions:**
- Can a minor contract?
- What is the effect of minor's contract?
- Who is of sound mind?

### 7. VOID vs VOIDABLE CONTRACTS ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

#### VOID CONTRACT (Section 2(g)):
- ‚ùå No legal effect from beginning
- ‚ùå Cannot be enforced
- **Examples:**
  - Bilateral mistake (Section 20)
  - Unlawful object (Section 23)
  - Agreement without consideration (Section 25) [with exceptions]

#### VOIDABLE CONTRACT (Section 2(i)):
- ‚úÖ Valid until avoided
- ‚úÖ One party can avoid
- **Examples:**
  - Coercion (Section 15)
  - Undue influence (Section 16)
  - Fraud (Section 17)
  - Misrepresentation (Section 18)

**Critical Distinction:**
- **Void**: No effect from beginning
- **Voidable**: Valid until avoided

**Common Exam Questions:**
- Distinguish void and voidable
- When is contract void?
- When is contract voidable?

### 8. REMEDIES FOR BREACH (Sections 73-75) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

2. **PENALTY & LIQUIDATED DAMAGES (Section 74)**

3. **QUANTUM MERUIT (Section 70)**

**Common Exam Questions:**

- What is the rule in Hadley v. Baxendale?
- What is quantum meruit?

- List available remedies (Section 73-75)

- Apply to facts
