# QUASI CONTRACTS – 5 MARKS QUESTIONS

## Q1: What are Quasi Contracts? Explain their nature.

**Quasi Contracts:**
"Certain relations resembling those created by contract" (Sections 68-72).

**Nature:**
- Not true contracts (no offer-acceptance)
- Obligations imposed by law
- Based on equity and justice
- Prevent unjust enrichment
- Restitutionary in nature

**Basis:** *"Nemo debet locupletari ex alterius jactura"* - No one should enrich unjustly at another's expense.

**Characteristics:**
1. No agreement between parties
2. Obligation created by law
3. One party must compensate other
4. Based on principles of equity
5. Remedy is restitution

**Five Types:** Sections 68-72 cover five situations.

## Q2: Explain Section 68 - Necessaries to incapable persons.

**Section 68:**
"If a person, incapable of entering into a contract, or any one whom he is legally bound to support, is supplied by another person with necessaries suited to his condition in life, the person who has furnished such supplies is entitled to be reimbursed from the property of such incapable person."

**Elements:**
1. Person incapable (minor, unsound mind, disqualified)
2. Necessaries supplied
3. Suited to condition in life
4. Reimbursement from PROPERTY only

**Necessaries:**
- Food, clothing, shelter
- Medical treatment, education
- Marriage expenses

**Case Law: Nash v. Inman (1908)**
11 fancy waistcoats - not necessaries. Cannot recover.

**Case Law: Chapple v. Cooper (1844)**
Funeral expenses - necessaries. Estate liable.

**Important:** Only property liable, not person.

## Q3: Explain Section 69 - Payment by interested person.

**Section 69:**
"A person who is interested in the payment of money which another is bound by law to pay, and who therefore pays it, is entitled to be reimbursed by the other."

**Elements:**
1. One person bound by law to pay
2. Another person interested in payment
3. Interested person pays
4. Entitled to reimbursement

**Who is Interested:**
- Co-surety paying debt
- Junior mortgagee paying senior mortgage
- Lessee paying rent to save property
- Co-owner paying property tax

**Not Interested:** Mere volunteer or stranger.

**Case Law: Exall v. Partridge (1799)**
Bailee paid warehouse rent to prevent goods' sale. Entitled to recover from bailor.

**Example:** A and B co-own property. A pays entire tax. A can recover half from B.

## Q4: Explain Section 70 - Obligation for non-gratuitous acts.

**Section 70:**
"Where a person lawfully does anything for another person, or delivers anything to him, not intending to do so gratuitously, and such other person enjoys the benefit thereof, the latter is bound to make compensation to the former in respect of, or to restore, the thing so done or delivered."

**Elements:**
1. Lawful act
2. For another person
3. Not gratuitously (expecting payment)
4. Benefit actually enjoyed
5. Must compensate

**Case Law: State of West Bengal v. Swastik Gases (1970)**
Oxygen cylinders supplied to hospital during emergency. Entitled to reasonable compensation.

**Example:**
Doctor provides emergency treatment to unconscious patient. Patient must pay reasonable charges.

**Limitation:**
If done gratuitously or benefit not enjoyed, no obligation.

## Q5: Explain Section 71 - Finder of goods.

**Section 71:**
"A person who finds goods belonging to another, and takes them into his custody, is subject to the same responsibility as a bailee."

**Position:** Finder becomes bailee of goods.

**Rights:**
1. **Lien** for expenses
2. **Sell goods** if:
   - Owner cannot be found
   - Owner refuses to pay charges
   - Goods perishable/value less than charges
3. **Sue for reward** if offered
4. **Compensation** for preservation

**Duties:**
1. Take reasonable care
2. Not use goods
3. Return to true owner
4. Make effort to find owner

**Case Law: Hirachand v. Temple (1911)**
Finder has title superior to everyone except true owner.

## Q6: Explain Section 72 - Money paid by mistake or under coercion.

**Section 72:**
"A person to whom money has been paid, or anything delivered by mistake or under coercion, must repay or return it."

**Two Situations:**

**1. By Mistake:**
- Payment by mistake of fact must be returned
- Mistake of law generally not recoverable (exceptions exist)

**Case Law: Kelly v. Solari (1841)**
Insurance company paid claim forgetting policy lapsed. Money recoverable.

**2. Under Coercion:**
- Money paid under threats/force
- Must be returned

**Case Law: Great Western Railway v. Sutton (1869)**
Money paid under illegal demand recoverable.

**Examples:**
- Duplicate payment by mistake
- Bank transfers to wrong account
- Payment under threat

**Note:** Payment under legal compulsion not recoverable.

## Q7: What is the doctrine of Quantum Meruit?

**Meaning:**
"As much as earned" or "In proportion to work done."

**Nature:**
- Quasi-contractual remedy
- Claim for reasonable remuneration
- Prevents unjust enrichment

**When Applicable:**
1. **Contract becomes void** - After partial performance
2. **Contract repudiated** - By other party
3. **Work under void agreement** - Believing it valid
4. **Divisible contract** - Partly performed
5. **Substantial performance** - Of indivisible contract
6. **Partial performance accepted** - By other party

**Case Law: Planché v. Colburn (1831)**
Author wrote partially, publisher abandoned series. Author entitled to quantum meruit.

**Case Law: Sumpter v. Hedges (1898)**
Builder abandoned work. Not entitled (in default). But entitled for materials used by owner.

**Not Available:**
- Complete performance (sue on contract)
- Party in default
- Gratuitous services

## Q8: Give examples of Quasi Contracts in daily life.

**1. Emergency Medical Treatment:**
Unconscious patient treated by doctor. Patient must pay reasonable charges. (Section 70)

**2. Finding Lost Property:**
A finds B's wallet. Must care for it, return to B. Can claim expenses. (Section 71)

**3. Duplicate Payment:**
A pays electricity bill twice by mistake. Utility must refund. (Section 72)

**4. Co-ownership:**
Two brothers co-own property. One pays property tax. Can recover half from other. (Section 69)

**5. Supplying Food to Minor:**
Restaurant supplies food to minor. Can claim reasonable price from minor's property. (Section 68)

**6. Bank Error:**
Bank transfers money to wrong account. Recipient must return. (Section 72)

**7. Property Protection:**
Tenant pays building insurance to prevent property loss. Can recover from landlord if landlord was obliged to pay. (Section 69)

**8. Emergency Services:**
Ambulance service to accident victim who cannot contract. Can claim reasonable charges. (Section 70)

**Common Thread:** All prevent unjust enrichment and ensure fairness.
