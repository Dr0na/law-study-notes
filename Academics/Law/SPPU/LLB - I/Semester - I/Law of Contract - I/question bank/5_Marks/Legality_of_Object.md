# LEGALITY OF OBJECT – 5 MARKS QUESTIONS

## Q1: When is consideration or object unlawful under Section 23?

**Section 23:** Consideration/object unlawful if:
1. **Forbidden by law** - Expressly prohibited
2. **Defeats provisions of law** - Indirectly circumvents law
3. **Fraudulent** - Involves fraud/deceit
4. **Injurious** - Involves injury to person/property
5. **Immoral** - Contrary to morality
6. **Opposed to public policy** - Harmful to public interest

**Effect:** Agreement void.

**Case Law: Gherulal Parakh v. Mahadeodas (1959)**
Sale beyond legally permitted quantity - void as forbidden by law.

## Q2: Explain agreements opposed to public policy.

**Public Policy:**
Agreements harmful to public welfare/interest.

**Categories:**

1. **Trading with enemy** - During war
2. **Restraint of trade** (Section 27) - Restraining profession
3. **Restraint of marriage** (Section 26) - Restraining marriage
4. **Restraint of legal proceedings** (Section 28) - Ousting courts
5. **Champerty & maintenance** - Supporting litigation for profit
6. **Creating monopoly** - Harmful monopolies
7. **Trafficking in public offices** - Corrupt practices

**Effect:** Void.

**Case Law: Gujarat Bottling v. Coca Cola (1995)**
Post-termination trade restraint - void as opposed to public policy.

## Q3: Explain Section 27 - Restraint of Trade.

**Section 27:**
"Every agreement by which any one is restrained from exercising a lawful profession, trade or business of any kind, is to that extent void."

**Principle:** Freedom of trade protected.

**Absolute in India:**
All restraints void except statutory exceptions. Reasonableness irrelevant.

**Three Exceptions:**
1. **Sale of goodwill** - Seller may be restrained within reasonable limits
2. **Partnership during** - Partners not to carry similar business while partnership continues
3. **Partnership after** - Restraint for specified period/area after dissolution

**Case Law: Niranjan Shankar v. Century Spinning (1967)**
Employment non-compete clause even after termination - void. Section 27 absolute.

---

## Q4: State the exceptions to Section 27 (Restraint of Trade).

**Exception 1: Sale of Goodwill [Proviso 1]**
- Seller may agree not to carry similar business
- Within specified local limits
- As long as buyer carries on business

**Example:** Doctor sells practice, agrees not to practice within 5 km.

**Exception 2: Partnership - During [Proviso 2]**
- Partners may agree not to carry similar business
- While partnership continues

**Exception 3: Partnership - After Dissolution [Proviso 3]**
- Agreement that partners not carry similar business
- Within specified local limits
- For specified period
- After dissolution

**Example:** After dissolving restaurant partnership, partners agree not to open similar restaurant within 10 km for 3 years.

**Indian vs. English:**
- India: Only these 3 exceptions
- England: Reasonable restraints also valid

## Q5: Explain Section 26 - Restraint of Marriage.

**Section 26:**
"Every agreement in restraint of the marriage of any person, other than a minor, is void."

**Principle:** Freedom to marry is fundamental right.

**Scope:**
- General restraint on marriage is void
- Exception: Restraint of minor's marriage may be valid (for minor's protection)

**Examples:**

**Void:**
- A agrees not to marry anyone for ₹10 lakhs - Void
- A promises not to marry during employment - Void

**May be Valid:**
- Agreement restraining marriage to particular person (if reasonable)
- Guardian's reasonable restriction on minor's marriage

**Rationale:**
- Marriage is social institution
- Public policy favors marriage
- Prevents interference with personal freedom

## Q6: Explain Section 28 - Restraint of Legal Proceedings.

**Section 28:**
"Every agreement, by which any party thereto is restricted absolutely from enforcing his rights under or in respect of any contract, by the usual legal proceedings in the ordinary tribunals, or which limits the time within which he may thus enforce his rights, is void to that extent."

**What is Void:**
- Absolute restriction on right to sue
- Shortening limitation period

**What is Valid:**
- Arbitration agreements (proviso)
- Extending limitation period
- Conditional restriction (reasonable conditions)

**Case Law: Nath v. Radha Kishan (1941)**
Agreement ousting court jurisdiction for all disputes - void.

**Exception:** Arbitration agreements for existing or future disputes - valid.

## Q7: What are immoral agreements? Give examples.

**Immoral Agreements:**
Agreements contrary to accepted moral standards.

**Examples:**
1. **Prostitution agreements** - Payment for sexual services
2. **Living together without marriage** - Cohabitation agreements
3. **Promoting immorality** - Facilitating immoral acts
4. **Sale of obscene materials** - Trading in prohibited content

**Case Law: Pearce v. Brooks (1866)**
Carriage hired to prostitute knowing purpose - void for immorality. Cannot recover hire charges.

**Case Law: Mst. Gulab Kumari v. Ramesh Chander (1965)**
Woman in immoral relationship promised maintenance - void as based on immoral consideration.

**Effect:** Agreement void. Money paid generally cannot be recovered.

**Rationale:** Courts won't enforce agreements promoting immorality.

## Q8: Explain Section 24 - When part of consideration/object is unlawful.

**Section 24:**
"If any part of a single consideration for one or more objects, or any one or any part of any one of several considerations for a single object, is unlawful, the agreement is void."

**Principle:** Illegal part taints entire agreement.

**Meaning:**
If even part of consideration or object unlawful, whole agreement becomes void.

**Illustration from Section 24:**
A promises to superintend legal manufacture and illegal traffic. B promises ₹10,000. Void - object partly unlawful.

**Severability:**
Sometimes legal part can be separated from illegal part and enforced if:
- Illegal part severable
- Removal doesn't alter nature
- Parties intended separate

**But General Rule:** Entire agreement void if part unlawful.

**Example:**
Agreement for ₹10,000 - ₹7,000 for legal work, ₹3,000 for illegal work. Entire agreement void.
