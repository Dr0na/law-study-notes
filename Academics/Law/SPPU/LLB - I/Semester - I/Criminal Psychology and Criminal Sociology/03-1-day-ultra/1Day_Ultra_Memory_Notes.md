## üìö **MODULE 06: SUBCULTURES**

## üìö **MODULE 07: SOCIAL PROCESS**

### **SUTHERLAND - 9 PROPOSITIONS**

1. Learned | 2. Communication | 3. **Intimate groups** | 4. Techniques + attitudes | 5. Definitions | 6. **EXCESS of favorable ‚Üí Crime** | 7. FDPI | 8. All learning | 9. Same needs

**FDPI**: Frequency, Duration, Priority, Intensity

**Proposition 6**: Most important (excess of favorable definitions)

---

### **AKERS**

Sutherland + Operant conditioning = Differential Reinforcement

**If rewarded ‚Üí Repeat | If punished ‚Üí Stop**

---

### **NEUTRALIZATION - "3D-CA"**

**Used BEFORE crime to overcome guilt**:

1. Denial of **Responsibility** ("Not my fault")
2. Denial of **Injury** ("No harm")
3. Denial of **Victim** ("Deserved it")
4. **Condemnation** of condemners ("They're corrupt")
5. **Appeal** to higher loyalties ("For my gang")

**Drift**: Youth drift between conventional and criminal (not fully committed to either)

---

### **HIRSCHI - "A-CAB"**

**4 Social Bonds** (prevent crime):

1. **A**ttachment (Emotional - Don't hurt them)
2. **C**ommitment (Investment - Too much to lose)
3. **I**nvolvement (Time - No time for crime)
4. **B**elief (Morality - Crime is wrong)

**All strong ‚Üí Conform | Any weak ‚Üí Crime**

---

### **LABELLING**

**Primary**: Initial minor deviance (not identity)

**Secondary**: After labeling, becomes identity (master status)

**Process**: Rule-break ‚Üí Caught ‚Üí **LABELED** ‚Üí Master status ‚Üí Self-fulfilling ‚Üí Secondary deviance

**Policy**: Avoid labeling, diversion programs

---

### **SELF-CONTROL**

**Low SC traits** (6): Impulsive, Simple tasks, Risk-seeking, Physical, Self-centered, Temper

**Origin**: Age 10 (childhood), stable

**Policy**: Early intervention

"Nothing is an offence which is done by a person who, at the time of doing it, by reason of unsoundness of mind, is incapable of knowing the nature of the act, or that he is doing what is either wrong or contrary to law."

"A person becomes delinquent because of an excess of definitions favorable to violation of law over definitions unfavorable to violation of law."
