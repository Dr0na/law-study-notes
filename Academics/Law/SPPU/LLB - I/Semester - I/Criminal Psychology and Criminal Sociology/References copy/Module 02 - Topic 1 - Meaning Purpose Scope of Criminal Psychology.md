## ðŸ“– **Meaning of Criminal Psychology**

Criminal psychology is a comparatively new science which developed during the 19th century. In India, it is still developing. In the early century, studies were done in relation to various aspects of criminal psychology.

### Definitions

The term criminal psychology has been defined in various ways:

1. **The study of the mind and its working in relation to crime**

2. **The study of the mental processes, motivational patterns, and behaviour of criminals**

3. **The study of the wills, thoughts, intentions, and reactions of criminals**

---

### Core Concept

**Criminal psychology** is a science which helps investigative institutions in fulfilling their mission more effectively by applying psychological knowledge to it.

---

### Etymology

The word **"psychology"** means the study of the soul:
- Derived from the Greek word **"psuchi"** (soul)
- Latin word **"psyche"** (soul)
- **"Logos"** (a rational course of study or science)

---

### Definition by L.R.C. Haward

According to Criminal psychologist **L.R.C. Haward**:

> Criminal psychology is that branch of applied psychology which is concerned with the **collection, examination, and presentation of evidence for judicial purposes**.

Thus, according to him, criminal psychology is concerned with **investigative and court processes**.

---

## ðŸŽ¯ **Purpose of Criminal Psychology**

With the help of criminal psychology, professionals in psychology like psychiatrists and psychologists can assist in understanding both the **mental and physical state of mind** of a person.

### Primary Objectives

Criminal psychology is the study of the **wills, thoughts, intentions, and reactions** of criminals and all that occurs during criminal behaviour. The study is done deeply relating to:

1. **Finding out the cause** - Why someone had committed a crime
2. **Understanding reactions** - Reactions after the crime

### Role in Legal System

**Criminal psychologists are often called as witnesses in courts of law** to help the judge understand the mind of the criminal.

---

## ðŸ” **Roles of Criminal Psychologists**

Criminal psychologists cover a wide range of roles such as:

1. **Researching evidence**

2. **Implementing treatment programs**

3. **Modifying offenders' behaviour**

4. **Advising parole boards**

5. **Responding to changing needs of prisoners and staff**

6. **Stress management techniques** for staff and prisoners

7. **Statistical analysis** used for prisoner profiling

8. **Using psychological testing** to assess offenders

9. **Providing psychological reports** to the court or representatives of parties

10. **Appearing in court as an expert witness** and giving opinion in courts of law

11. **Performing analysis of crime**

12. **Giving opinion** relating to a person to mental health tribunals

**Scope is wide:** Psychology studies, describes, and explains the behaviour of all living persons.

### 3. Politics

Politicians make use of psychology by applying:
- Group dynamics
- Social distance
- Propaganda
- Leadership techniques
- For gaining popularity, leading masses, and effective administration

---

### 4. Guidance and Counselling

The use of criminal psychology is important in **guidance and counselling** as it provides valuable guidance to persons seeking solutions to their problems of adjustment in:
- Education
- Employment
- Personal life

**Process:**
1. Counsellor assesses behaviour through various psychological measures and techniques
2. Suggests measures to solve difficulties
3. Helps individual know and assess own abilities, shortcomings, habits, temperaments
4. Develop well-balanced and integrated personality

---

### 5. Military

Psychology helps military officers in:
- Selection
- Training
- Promotion
- Classification of various candidates by analyzing their personalities

---

### 6. Criminal Investigation

**A criminal psychologist:**
- Performs research
- Advises police officers about how to proceed with investigation in particular cases

**Assessment Role:**
- Usually involved in assessment of individuals to provide clinical judgment
- Uses interviews, assessment tools, or psychometric tests (special questionnaires)
- These assessments inform:
  - Police
  - Courts
  - Prison officers
  - Parole boards
  - Probation services
- About psychological functioning of individuals
- Influences different sections of criminal justice system to function effectively

**Criminal Profiling:**
- Psychologists may be asked to create criminal profiles to aid investigation
