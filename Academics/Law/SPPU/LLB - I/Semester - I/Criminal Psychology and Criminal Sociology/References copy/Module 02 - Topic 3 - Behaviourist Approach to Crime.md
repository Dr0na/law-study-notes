## ðŸ“‹ **Characteristics of Behaviourist Approach**

In the behaviourist approach towards commission of crime, the following are important characteristics:

### 1. Individual or Group Participation

A crime may be committed by:
- **Individual**, OR
- **Group of individuals** who may be:
  - Directly participating in the commission of crime
  - Indirectly participating in the commission of crime

**Examples of group crimes:**
- Professional theft
- Drug addiction
- Fraudulent advertising

---

### 2. Common Behaviour Pattern

The behaviour which occurs in a behaviour system is **not unique to any particular individual** but is **common behaviour**.

**Characteristic:**
> It operates in the same way in a large number of persons.

---

### 3. Joint Participation

**Common and joint participation** in crime commission is the essential characteristic of the behaviourist approach towards crime.

---

### 4. Mutual Cooperation Among Criminals

**Criminals get mutual cooperation from each other because of:**
- **Sentimental coordination** between them
- **Common desire** for commission of crime

---

## ðŸ”‘ **Core Principle**

**Behaviourist approach towards crime takes into consideration the behavioural system which contributes towards commission of crime.**

This approach examines:
- How behaviour patterns facilitate crime
- How criminal behaviour is learned and reinforced
- How social and environmental factors shape criminal conduct
- How criminals coordinate and cooperate in criminal activities

---

## âœ… **Key Insights**

The behaviourist approach emphasizes:

1. **Collective nature** of many crimes
2. **Common behavioural patterns** among criminals
3. **Social learning** and mutual reinforcement
4. **Coordinated action** in criminal enterprises
5. **Environmental and situational factors** influencing behaviour

Unlike the psychoanalytic approach (which focuses on individual personality and unconscious drives), the behaviourist approach emphasizes:
- Observable behaviour
- Environmental influences
- Learning and conditioning
- Social cooperation and patterns
