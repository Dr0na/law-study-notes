## ðŸ”‘ **DEFINITIONS OF CRIMINAL BEHAVIOUR**

According to **Andrews and Bonta**, there are **4 definitions** of criminal behaviour which include the following areas:

### 1. Legal Definition

**Acts prohibited by law and are punished by the state**

---

### 2. Moral/Religious Definition

**Acts which are considered to be violation of moral or religious code** and are believed to be punishable by a supreme authority such as God

---

### 3. Social Definition

**Acts which violate norms of society or traditions** and are believed to be punishable by community

---

### 4. Psychological Definition

**Acts causing serious psychological stress or mental damage to a victim**

---

## ðŸ“ **Comprehensive Definition**

From the above definitions, **criminal behaviour** can be defined as:

> **Any kind of anti-social behaviour which is punishable by law or norms stated by community.**

**Note:** Therefore, it is very difficult to define criminal behaviour comprehensively.

## ðŸ” **CAUSES OF CRIMINAL BEHAVIOUR**

### 1. Financial Problems or Starvation

If a person struggles everyday for even getting food for survival, there are chances that such person may get involved in criminal activity.

---

### 2. Low Social Status

When one is having low social status, there are chances that such person may:
- Become aggressive
- Fight back against the whole society

---

### 3. Mental Illness

Some **mental disorders** may increase aggressive behaviour in such persons.

---

### 4. Anti-Social Values

Sometimes individuals are of the opinion that **criminal behaviour is justified** in certain cases.

**Individuals possessing this trait** may get involved in criminal activities.

---

### 5. Family Background

Because of **lack of family support** (emotionally, financially, morally):
- Individual lacks ability to solve problems
- Unable to communicate effectively
- Many times family members do not possess ability to solve problems of individual
- They may also get involved with criminal activity

---

### 6. Low Self-Control

**People who have low ability to:**
- Control temperament
- Control impulsivity

**Consequences:**
- Many times fail to think before acting
- Likely that such person may commit crime without any planning
- Without thinking about consequences of behaviour

## ðŸ§  **PSYCHODYNAMICS OF CRIMINAL BEHAVIOUR**

### Alternative Name

Psychodynamics of criminal behaviour is also known as **Psychoanalytic Theory of Freudian School**.

---

### Foundation

Based upon psychoanalytic theory of:
- **Sigmund Freud** (Austrian neurologist)
- **August Aichorn**

---

### Core Argument

The psychodynamics of criminal behaviour theories argue that:

> **Criminal behaviour originates primarily in the personalities of offenders rather than in their biology or situation.**

---

### Personality Concept

**Personality** refers to:
- Reasonably stable patterns of behaviour
- Including thoughts, feelings, and emotions
- That distinguish people from one another

**Role:**
- Personality controls a person's way of adopting to life experiences
- Therefore separates people on the basis of their behaviour

---

### Criminality Link

**Psycho-criminologists** link criminality to:
- Disturbances of the personality
- Persons with **abnormal personality** (whatever may be the reason)
- Will be more likely to engage in anti-social behaviour
- Because they view the world differently

---

### Summary of Psychoanalytic Model

In short, the psychoanalytic model of the criminal offender depicts:

> **An aggressive, frustrated person dominated by events that occurred early in childhood.**

## ðŸ‘¤ **KEY THEORISTS AND CONTRIBUTIONS**

### Alfred Adler (Founder of Individual Psychology)

**Main Theory:**
> **Inferiority complex** is the main cause of crime.

---

### August Aichorn

**Most important causes of crime according to Aichorn:**

#### 1. Faulty Methods of Child Training

**Argument:**
> Parents are responsible for making the child criminal in **both cases**:
> - When parents have **no time to give love**, OR
> - When parents **give love too much** to the child

**Implication:** Both extremes (neglect and over-indulgence) are harmful.

---

#### 2. Criminal Parents Influence

**Effect:**
- A child having criminal parents is generally upset
- Therefore gets involved in crime commission itself

**Solution:**
> A child should be given proper treatment to make their life happy and provide them pleasurable environment.

---

### Carl Jung

**Background:**
- Closest student of Sigmund Freud
- Developed **analytical psychology**

**Main Contribution:**
> An important aspect of human psychic structure is the **collective unconsciousness**.
