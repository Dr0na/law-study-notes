## üìñ **INTRODUCTION TO SUBCULTURAL THEORIES**

### **What is a Subculture?**

**Culture** = The values, norms, beliefs, and behaviors shared by members of society

**Subculture** = A group within larger society that has:
- Distinct values, norms, behaviors
- Different from mainstream culture
- But exists within larger society

---

### **Delinquent Subculture**

> A subculture where **criminal behavior is valued** and **conforming to law is rejected**.

**Characteristics:**
- Inverted values (what society condemns, subculture approves)
- Group support for delinquency
- Status gained through crime
- Opposition to mainstream culture

---

### **Why Study Subcultures?**

**Observation:** Crime is often **collective**, not individual
- Gangs
- Group delinquency
- Peer influence

**Question:** Why do certain youth form delinquent subcultures?

---

### **Core Premise**

> Subcultural theories argue that some youth commit crime because they are **conforming to the values of their subculture**, which are different from mainstream values.

**Key Insight:**
- Delinquents are not "rebels without a cause"
- They ARE conforming - but to **subcultural values**
- Group behavior, not individual pathology

<a name="cohen-theory"></a>
## üë• **COHEN'S THEORY OF DELINQUENT SUBCULTURE**

### **Albert Cohen (1918-2014)**

**Background:**
- American criminologist
- Book: **"Delinquent Boys: The Culture of the Gang"** (1955)
- Extended Merton's strain theory

---

### **Cohen's Research Question**

> Why do **lower-class boys** form **delinquent gangs** that commit **non-utilitarian crime**?

**Puzzle:**
- Most gang delinquency is NOT for money
- Vandalism, fighting, joyriding
- Seems purposeless, malicious
- Merton's theory doesn't explain this

---

### **The Problem: Status Frustration**

#### **Middle-Class Measuring Rod**

**Cohen's Core Concept:**

> Society (especially schools) evaluates everyone by **middle-class standards** - the "middle-class measuring rod".

---

**Middle-Class Values in Schools:**

| Value | Expectation | Lower-Class Reality |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Ambition** | Plan for future, delay gratification | Live in present, immediate needs |
| **Achievement** | Strive for excellence | Survival focus |
| **Self-Reliance** | Individual responsibility | Collective/family orientation |
| **Respect for Property** | Value possessions | Lack possessions |
| **Academic Skills** | Read, write, study | Poor preparation |
| **Verbal Skills** | Articulate, express well | Limited vocabulary |
| **Manners** | Politeness, etiquette | Direct, physical |
| **Non-Violence** | Use words, not fists | Toughness valued |

---

#### **Lower-Class Boys in School**

**Reality:**
- Family hasn't prepared them for middle-class school
- Lack middle-class values and skills
- Teachers judge them by middle-class standards
- **Result:** FAILURE

**Consequences:**
- Poor grades
- Teacher disapproval
- Peer rejection
- Feel inadequate

---

### **Status Frustration**

> **Status Frustration** = The frustration experienced by lower-class boys when they fail to achieve status in middle-class institutions (school).

**Process:**
```
Lower-class boys ‚Üí Middle-class school
             ‚¨áÔ∏è
        Judged by middle-class measuring rod
             ‚¨áÔ∏è
        Cannot meet standards
             ‚¨áÔ∏è
        FAILURE + Humiliation
             ‚¨áÔ∏è
        STATUS FRUSTRATION
```

---

### **The Solution: Delinquent Subculture**

**Formation Process:**

**Step 1: Common Problem**
- Many lower-class boys face same problem
- All experiencing status frustration

**Step 2: Come Together**
- Boys with similar problem find each other
- Form groups (gangs)

**Step 3: Reaction Formation**

> **Reaction Formation** = Overreaction in opposite direction to what frustrates you.

- Middle-class values frustrate them
- So they **invert** those values
- Create opposite value system

**Step 4: New Status System**
- Status now gained through delinquency
- Respect for breaking middle-class rules
- Group provides alternative status

---

### **Characteristics of Delinquent Subculture**

#### **1. Non-Utilitarian**

> Delinquency is **not for money** or material gain.

**Examples:**
- Vandalism (destroying property for no gain)
- Joyriding (stealing car just to drive, not sell)
- Fighting (for fun, not profit)

**Why?**
- Purpose is to reject middle-class values
- Gain status in subculture
- Express contempt for middle class

---

#### **2. Malicious**

> Acts seem **spiteful**, aimed at middle class.

**Examples:**
- Destroying school property
- Taunting teachers
- Deliberate misbehavior

**Why?**
- Revenge against middle class
- Express hostility
- Prove they don't care about middle-class approval

---

#### **3. Negativistic**

> Values are **opposite** of middle-class values.

**Inversions:**

| Middle-Class Value | Delinquent Subculture Value |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Respect property | Destroy property |
| Study and achieve | Reject education |
| Be polite | Be disrespectful |
| Plan for future | Live for now |
| Delay gratification | Immediate pleasure |
| Non-violence | Toughness, fighting |

---

#### **4. Versatile**

> Subculture engages in **variety** of delinquent acts, not specialization.

- Theft today
- Vandalism tomorrow
- Fighting next day
- No specific pattern

---

#### **5. Short-Run Hedonism**

> Focus on **immediate pleasure**, not long-term planning.

- Live for the moment
- No thought of consequences
- Spontaneous acts

---

#### **6. Group Autonomy**

> Strong **loyalty** to gang, **resistance** to outside control.

- Gang is family
- Intolerance of restrictions
- Anti-authority
- "Us vs Them" mentality

---

### **How Status is Achieved in Subculture**

**Middle-Class:**
- Good grades
- Academic achievement
- Career success
- Material possessions

**Delinquent Subculture:**
- Toughness
- Daring criminal acts
- Defying authority
- "Street cred"
- Loyalty to gang

---

### **Cohen's vs Merton's Theory**

| Aspect | Merton | Cohen |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Goal** | Monetary success | Status and respect |
| **Means** | Economic means | Middle-class values |
| **Crime Type** | Utilitarian (theft) | Non-utilitarian (vandalism) |
| **Class** | All classes | Lower-class boys |
| **Solution** | Individual (innovation) | Collective (gang subculture) |
| **Crime Purpose** | Achieve success | Reject middle-class values |

---

### **Policy Implications**

**To Prevent Delinquent Subcultures:**

1. **Change Schools**
   - Don't judge all by same standard
   - Multiple pathways to success
   - Vocational education
   - Respect diverse backgrounds

2. **Provide Alternative Status**
   - Sports, arts programs
   - Youth clubs
   - Mentorship
   - Legitimate ways to gain respect

3. **Bridge Class Gap**
   - Reduce inequality
   - Better preparation for school
   - Early childhood programs

4. **Community Programs**
   - After-school activities
   - Supervised recreation
   - Skill-building

---

### **Criticism of Cohen**

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Limitations**

1. **Overgeneralization**
   - Not all lower-class boys form gangs
   - Not all gangs are non-utilitarian

2. **Ignores Female Delinquency**
   - Theory only about boys
   - Girls' delinquency different

3. **Assumes Conscious Reaction**
   - Reaction formation may not be conscious
   - Boys may not deliberately invert values

4. **Middle-Class Delinquency**
   - Cannot explain middle-class delinquency
   - Focus only on lower class

5. **Empirical Support Weak**
   - Hard to measure status frustration
   - Some gang crime IS utilitarian

<a name="miller-theory"></a>
## üé≠ **MILLER'S LOWER-CLASS GANG DELINQUENCY**

### **Walter Miller (1920-2004)**

**Background:**
- American anthropologist
- Article: **"Lower Class Culture as a Generating Milieu of Gang Delinquency"** (1958)
- Different from Cohen

---

### **Miller's Core Argument**

> **Lower-class culture is distinct** from middle-class culture and **naturally produces delinquency** - No need for reaction formation.

**Key Difference from Cohen:**
- Cohen: Delinquent subculture is **reactive** (reaction against middle class)
- Miller: Lower-class culture is **independent** (exists on its own)

---

### **Six Focal Concerns of Lower-Class Culture**

> **Focal Concerns** = Values and issues of greatest concern to lower-class culture.

> üß† **Memory Hook - "T-S-S-E-F-A"**

---

#### **1. TROUBLE**

**Definition:** 
> Getting into and staying out of trouble is a dominant concern.

**Two Sides:**

**Getting Into Trouble:**
- Fighting, drinking, sexual adventures
- Way to show toughness
- Gain status and respect

**Staying Out of Trouble:**
- Avoid police, courts, prison
- Practical concern

**Ambivalence:**
- Trouble is bad (punishment)
- But also brings status (respect for toughness)

---

#### **2. TOUGHNESS**

**Definition:**
> Physical prowess, bravery, masculinity are highly valued.

**Emphasis on:**
- Physical strength
- Courage, fearlessness
- "Macho" behavior
- Not showing weakness
- Endurance of pain

**Why Important in Lower Class?**
- Often female-headed households
- Boys lack male role models
- Overcompensate by emphasizing masculinity
- Prove they are "real men"

**Criminal Relevance:**
- Fighting to show toughness
- Violence to prove masculinity
- Cannot back down from challenge

---

#### **3. SMARTNESS**

**Definition:**
> Ability to outsmart, outwit, con others; "street smarts".

**NOT Academic Intelligence:**
- Not book learning
- Practical intelligence
- Cunning, shrewdness
- Ability to con, hustle

**Skills Valued:**
- Deceiving others
- Fast talking
- Getting over on people
- Avoiding being conned yourself

**Criminal Relevance:**
- Fraud, con games
- Hustling
- Outwitting police
- Status from successful crimes

---

#### **4. EXCITEMENT**

**Definition:**
> Seeking thrills, taking risks, avoiding boredom.

**Contrast to Middle Class:**
- Middle class: Routine, stability, planning
- Lower class: Excitement, risk, spontaneity

**Activities:**
- Gambling
- Drinking
- Sexual adventures
- Fighting
- Fast driving

**Why?**
- Life is otherwise monotonous
- Low-skilled jobs are boring
- Excitement breaks monotony

**Criminal Relevance:**
- Crimes for thrill
- Joyriding
- Dangerous stunts
- Spontaneous crimes

---

#### **5. FATE**

**Definition:**
> Belief that life is controlled by fate, luck, forces beyond one's control.

**Attitude:**
- "What will be, will be"
- External locus of control
- Luck determines success
- Cannot control destiny

**Contrast:**
- Middle class: Plan, work hard, shape future
- Lower class: Accept fate, gamble on luck

**Why?**
- Experience of powerlessness
- Efforts don't pay off
- No control over opportunities

**Criminal Relevance:**
- Don't worry about consequences
- "If caught, it's fate"
- Risk-taking behavior

---

#### **6. AUTONOMY**

**Definition:**
> Desire to be free from external control and authority.

**Surface vs Depth:**

**Surface:** "I don't need anyone, I'm independent"

**Depth:** Actually NEED structure and rules (but won't admit it)

**Behavior:**
- Resentment of authority
- Resist bosses, teachers, police
- Want freedom from rules
- But also seek guidance (unconsciously)

**Criminal Relevance:**
- Defy authority
- Break rules to assert independence
- Resist control

---

### **Gang Delinquency as Normal Expression**

**Miller's Argument:**

Lower-class boys join gangs and commit delinquency NOT because of:
- ‚ùå Reaction to middle class (Cohen)
- ‚ùå Strain from blocked goals (Merton)

But because:
- ‚úÖ **Conforming to lower-class culture**
- ‚úÖ Expressing focal concerns
- ‚úÖ Normal socialization in their culture

---

### **Process of Gang Formation**

**Step 1:** Boys grow up in lower-class culture
- Learn focal concerns from family, peers
- Values: Trouble, toughness, smartness, etc.

**Step 2:** Form peer groups (gangs)
- Ages 12-16 most active
- Hang out on streets
- Gang is reference group

**Step 3:** Act According to Focal Concerns
- Fighting (toughness)
- Hustling (smartness)
- Joyriding (excitement)
- Defying police (autonomy)

**Step 4:** Behavior Defined as Delinquent
- Middle class calls it "crime"
- But boys see it as normal expression of values

---

### **Female Behavior in Lower Class**

**Miller's View:**
- Girls have different focal concerns
- Less emphasis on toughness
- More emphasis on:
  - Sexual attractiveness
  - Having children
  - Securing male partner

**Female Delinquency:**
- Less violent
- More status offenses
- Different from males

---

### **Policy Implications**

**Miller's Suggestions:**

1. **Don't Try to Change Lower-Class Culture**
   - It's stable, persistent
   - Futile to impose middle-class values

2. **Provide Legal Outlets**
   - Channels for expressing focal concerns legally
   - Sports for toughness
   - Business for smartness
   - Adventure programs for excitement

3. **Realistic Expectations**
   - Don't expect lower-class youth to adopt middle-class values
   - Work within their culture

4. **Economic Opportunities**
   - Jobs that value lower-class skills
   - Respect for non-academic intelligence

---

### **Criticism of Miller**

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Limitations**

1. **Stereotyping**
   - Portrays lower class negatively
   - Not all lower-class people share these values
   - Overgeneralization

2. **Cultural Determinism**
   - Makes crime seem inevitable in lower class
   - Removes individual responsibility
   - Fatalistic

3. **Ignores Within-Class Variation**
   - Lower class is not homogeneous
   - Many lower-class people hold middle-class values

4. **Dated Research**
   - Based on 1950s Boston gangs
   - May not apply to modern gangs
   - Different contexts now

5. **Class Bias**
   - Assumes middle-class values are "correct"
   - Lower-class values as "deviant"

6. **Ignores Structural Causes**
   - Focuses on culture
   - Doesn't explain WHY lower class has these values
   - Poverty, discrimination not central

## üìä **COHEN VS MILLER: COMPARISON**

### **Comprehensive Comparison Table**

| Aspect | Cohen | Miller |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Basic Cause** | Reaction to status frustration | Expression of lower-class culture |
| **Lower-Class Culture** | Same goals as middle class | Different, autonomous culture |
| **Delinquency** | Reactive (against middle class) | Proactive (conforming to subculture) |
| **Origin** | Status frustration in school | Normal lower-class socialization |
| **Values** | Inversion of middle-class values | Independent focal concerns |
| **Crime Type** | Non-utilitarian, malicious | Expression of focal concerns |
| **Consciousness** | Conscious reaction formation | Unconscious cultural conformity |
| **Solution** | Change schools, provide alternative status | Change culture (difficult) or provide outlets |
| **Tone** | Lower class WANTS middle-class success | Lower class has OWN distinct values |
| **Female Delinquency** | Not addressed | Mentioned (different pattern) |

---

### **Example Scenario**

**Vandalism of School:**

**Cohen's Explanation:**
- Boys frustrated by school failure
- School represents middle-class values that reject them
- Vandalism is revenge/rebellion against middle class
- Expression of hostility

**Miller's Explanation:**
- Boys seeking excitement (focal concern)
- Demonstrating toughness and daring
- Defying authority (autonomy)
- Normal expression of lower-class values
- Not necessarily hostile to middle class

## üîç **EMPIRICAL RESEARCH**

### **Support for Cohen**

**Evidence:**
- Gang delinquency often non-utilitarian
- Vandalism, fighting without clear purpose
- Status does matter in gangs
- School failure precedes gang membership

---

### **Support for Miller**

**Evidence:**
- Lower-class culture does emphasize different values
- Gangs do value toughness, smartness
- Not all gang members had school failure
- Some delinquency is thrilling, not reactive

---

### **Synthesis**

**Both May Be Partly Right:**
- Some gangs form due to status frustration (Cohen)
- Some gangs express cultural values (Miller)
- Different gangs, different causes
- Both theories explain some cases

---

## üåç **MODERN RELEVANCE**

### **Contemporary Gang Research**

**Modern Findings:**

**Support for Subcultural Theories:**
- Gangs do provide alternative status
- Group values differ from mainstream
- Peer influence is powerful

**Challenges to Theories:**
- Much modern gang crime IS utilitarian (drugs, weapons)
- Globalization changes culture
- Not just lower-class phenomenon
- Female gang membership increasing

---

### **Modern Gangs in India**

**Indian Context:**
- Street gangs in cities
- Caste-based groups
- Political gangs
- Different dynamics than Western gangs

**Applicability:**
- Status concerns still relevant
- Group values important
- But economic factors also crucial
- Cultural specificity needed

## ‚úÖ **SUMMARY & KEY TAKEAWAYS**

### **Core Concepts**

1. **Subculture** = Group with distinct values within larger society

2. **Delinquent Subculture** = Group where crime is valued

3. **Cohen's Theory**:
   - Lower-class boys face status frustration in middle-class schools
   - Form gangs with inverted values (reaction formation)
   - Non-utilitarian, malicious, negativistic delinquency
   - Solution: Alternative status opportunities

4. **Miller's Theory**:
   - Lower-class has distinct culture (not reactive)
   - Six focal concerns: Trouble, Toughness, Smartness, Excitement, Fate, Autonomy
   - Delinquency = Normal expression of lower-class values
   - Solution: Provide legal outlets for focal concerns

5. **Key Difference**:
   - Cohen: Lower class WANTS middle-class success (can't achieve)
   - Miller: Lower class has OWN values (different culture)
