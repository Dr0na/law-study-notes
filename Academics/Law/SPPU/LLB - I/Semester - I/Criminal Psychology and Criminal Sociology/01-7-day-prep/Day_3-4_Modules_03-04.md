## üîç **CRIMINAL PROFILING - MUST KNOW**

### **Definition**

Process of identifying personality traits, behavioral tendencies, and demographic characteristics of offender based on crime characteristics

---

### **Historical**

- **1888**: Jack the Ripper (Dr. Thomas Bond - first profile)
- **1970s**: FBI Behavioral Science Unit
- **Modern**: Evidence-based, AI-assisted

---

### **Types of Profiling**

1. **Crime Scene Analysis** - FBI approach
2. **Investigative Psychology** - David Canter (UK)
3. **Geographical Profiling** - Location patterns

---

## üìã **PROFILING PROCESS (6 Steps)**

> üß† **Mnemonic "AC-R-PIA"**:
> 1. **A**ssimilate (collect info)
> 2. **C**lassify (organized vs disorganized)
> 3. **R**econstruct (crime sequence)
> 4. **P**rofile (generate profile)
> 5. **I**nvestigate (use profile)
> 6. **A**pprehend (capture, interview)

---

## üìä **ORGANIZED VS DISORGANIZED - CRITICAL FOR EXAM**

### **Organized Offender**

**SMART mnemonic**:
- **S**ocially competent
- **M**ethodical planning
- **A**bove average IQ
- **R**emoves evidence
- **T**argeted victim

**Details**:
- Planned crime
- Brings weapon, takes it
- Little evidence left
- Body moved/hidden
- Good social skills
- Employed, lives with partner
- Follows media

---

### **Disorganized Offender**

**MESSY mnemonic**:
- **M**ental issues
- **E**vidence everywhere
- **S**pontaneous
- **S**ocially inadequate
- **Y**ields (leaves) body at scene

**Details**:
- Spontaneous crime
- Uses weapon at scene
- Much evidence
- Body left at scene
- Poor social skills
- Unemployed, lives alone
- May change behavior drastically

---

## üîë **MO VS SIGNATURE - EXAM FAVORITE**

### **Comparison**

| Aspect | MO (Modus Operandi) | Signature |
|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Definition** | Method to commit crime | Psychological need |
| **Purpose** | Functional (accomplish crime) | Emotional (gratification) |
| **Necessary?** | Yes (to commit crime) | No (unnecessary for crime) |
| **Changes?** | Yes (evolves, improves) | No (stays same) |
| **Example** | Wearing gloves, breaking window | Posing victim, taking trophy |

> üìå **Remember**: MO = Practical, Signature = Psychological

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è **MERITS vs DEMERITS - EXAM TABLE**

### **Merits (‚úÖ)**

1. **Narrows suspects** - Focuses investigation
2. **Guides investigation** - Interview strategies
3. **Links cases** - Identify serial crimes
4. **Generates leads** - New directions
5. **Court use** - Expert testimony
6. **Prevention** - Predict future crimes
7. **Interview strategies** - Tailor approach

---

### **Demerits (‚ùå)**

1. **Lack scientific rigor** - More art than science
2. **Inaccuracy** - Can be wrong
3. **Confirmation bias** - Ignore contradictory evidence
4. **Limited applicability** - Best for serial crimes only
5. **Resource intensive** - Expensive, time-consuming
6. **Ethical concerns** - Stereotyping, privacy
7. **Unrealistic expectations** - CSI effect
8. **Legal issues** - Not admissible as evidence

## üìñ **FORENSIC PSYCHOLOGY - CORE**

### **Definition**

Application of psychological knowledge and methods to criminal justice system

**Forensic** = Latin "forensis" (of the forum/court)

---

### **Scope - Three Main Areas**

1. **Investigation**: Profiling, interrogation, lie detection
2. **Court**: Expert testimony, competency, insanity
3. **Prison**: Classification, treatment, prevention

## üîç **THREE MAIN APPLICATIONS - EXAM FORMAT**

### **1. INVESTIGATION**

**Key Roles**:

**Criminal Profiling** (already covered Day 3)

**Cognitive Interview**:
- Enhance witness memory
- Context reinstatement
- Report everything
- Different perspectives/orders

**Reid Technique** (Interrogation):
- 9-step process
- Elicit confession
- **Criticism**: Can cause false confessions

**Polygraph**:
- Measures physiological responses
- **Not admissible** in Indian courts (Selvi case, 2010)
- Can be beaten

**Psychological Autopsy**:
- Determine suicide vs homicide
- Reconstruct mental state

---

### **2. COURT APPLICATIONS**

#### **Expert Witness**

**Areas of Testimony**:
- Mental state of accused
- Competency to stand trial
- Criminal responsibility (insanity)
- Risk assessment
- Mitigation at sentencing

**Requirements**:
- Advanced degree
- Specialized training
- Experience
- Registration (RCI in India)

---

#### **Competency to Stand Trial**

**Criteria** - Accused must be able to:
1. Understand proceedings
2. Understand consequences
3. Communicate with attorney
4. Assist in defense
5. Make rational decisions

**If Incompetent**: Treatment to restore competency

---

#### **Criminal Responsibility (Insanity Defense)**

**Section 84, IPC**:

> Nothing is an offense if done by person with unsoundness of mind, incapable of knowing:
> - Nature of act, OR
> - Act is wrong or contrary to law

**Assessment**:
- Mental status at time of offense
- Diagnosis
- Causal link between illness and crime

> üìå **Exam**: Always mention Section 84 IPC

---

#### **Eyewitness Testimony Issues**

**Factors Affecting Accuracy**:

**Encoding** (during event):
- Stress (weapon focus effect)
- Duration of exposure
- Lighting conditions

**Storage** (retention):
- Time delay (memory fades)
- Post-event information (contamination)

**Retrieval** (testimony):
- Leading questions
- Biased lineups
- Cross-race effect

> **Key Point**: Eyewitness confidence ‚â† Accuracy (Elizabeth Loftus research)

---

### **3. PRISON APPLICATIONS**

#### **Classification**

**Purpose**: Match inmates to appropriate facilities

**Factors**: Security risk, mental health needs, criminal history, behavior

**Levels**: Maximum, Medium, Minimum, Open

---

#### **Treatment Programs**

**Cognitive-Behavioral**:
- Change criminal thinking
- Reasoning & Rehabilitation (R&R)
- Moral Reconation Therapy (MRT)

**Anger Management**:
- Recognize triggers
- Relaxation techniques
- Assertiveness training

**Sex Offender Treatment**:
- CBT, relapse prevention
- Victim empathy
- Reduces recidivism 10-15%

**Substance Abuse**:
- Detox, therapeutic communities
- 60-80% inmates have substance issues

---

#### **Mental Health Services**

**Prevalence**: 3-4√ó higher than general population

**Services**:
- Screening at intake
- Individual/group therapy
- Medication management
- Crisis intervention

---

#### **Suicide Prevention**

**Risk Factors**:
- Mental illness (especially depression)
- First-time incarceration
- Solitary confinement
- Life sentence

**Prevention**:
- Risk screening
- Observation cells
- Remove hazards
- Mental health treatment

---

#### **Staff Support**

**Officer Stress**:
- Safety concerns
- Inmate manipulation
- Ethical dilemmas

**Support**:
- Stress management training
- Critical incident debriefing
- Counseling services
