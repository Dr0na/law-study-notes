## üìö **MODULE 01: SCHOOLS OF CRIMINOLOGY**

### **‚ö° SPEED TABLE - FOUR SCHOOLS**

| School | Time | Idea | Cause | Punishment | Key Figure |
|--------|------|------|-------|------------|------------|
| **Pre-Classical** | <1700s | Supernatural | Divine will | Cruel, arbitrary | - |
| **Classical** | 1764+ | Rational | Free choice | Proportionate | **Beccaria** |
| **Neo-Classical** | Late 1700s | Modified | Choice + circumstances | Flexible | - |
| **Positivist** | 1870s+ | Scientific | Determined | Treatment | **Lombroso** |

---

### **‚ö° CLASSICAL SCHOOL - 4 POINTS**

> üß† **"FREE"**

- **F**ree will
- **R**ational choice (pleasure vs pain)
- **E**qual punishment for equal crime
- **E**veryone equal before law

**Reforms**: Abolish torture, proportionality, certainty, swift, public

---

### **‚ö° POSITIVIST THINKERS - QUICK**

| Name | Theory | Key Points | Mnemonic |
|------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **LOMBROSO** | Born Criminal | Physical stigmata, atavism, 4 types | **L**ook for signs |
| **FERRI** | Multiple Factors | 3 causes (PAS), 5 types, penal substitutes | **F**actors (PAS) |
| **GAROFALO** | Natural Crime | Pity + Probity, harsh punishment | **G**uilty of being harsh |
| **TARDE** | Imitation | 3 laws (Contact, Superior, Insertion) | **T**arde teaches |

---

### **üìã LOMBROSO CHECKLIST**

**Stigmata** (any 5-6 for exam):
- ‚úÖ Sloping forehead
- ‚úÖ Large jaw
- ‚úÖ Long arms
- ‚úÖ Small eyes
- ‚úÖ Large ears
- ‚úÖ Asymmetrical face

**Types**: Born, Insane, Criminaloid, Passionate

---

### **üìã FERRI'S PAS**

**3 Causes**:
- **P**hysical: Climate, geography, seasons
- **A**nthropological: Age, sex, heredity, mental state
- **S**ocial: Economy, education, family, religion

**5 Types**: Born, Insane, Habitual, Occasional, Passionate

**Penal Substitutes**: Social reforms (free trade, housing, lighting, education) instead of punishment

---

### **üìã GAROFALO'S TWO P's**

**Natural Crime violates**:
1. **PITY** (respect for life)
2. **PROBITY** (respect for property)

**Punishment**: Elimination (death), Partial elimination (life), Reparation

---

### **üìã TARDE'S 3 LAWS**

1. **Close Contact**: Imitate those near us
2. **Superior ‚Üí Inferior**: Lower imitates upper
3. **Insertion**: New replaces old

**Anti-Lombroso**: Social learning, NOT biological

## üìö **MODULE 02: PSYCHOLOGY**

### **‚ö° FREUD'S IES**

| Structure | Principle | Function | Crime Link |
|-----------|-----------|----------|------------|
| **ID** | Pleasure | Immediate gratification | Overdeveloped = Impulsive crime |
| **EGO** | Reality | Mediates | Weak = Cannot control Id |
| **SUPEREGO** | Morality | Conscience | Underdeveloped = No guilt |

**Crime = Imbalance of IES**

---

### **‚ö° OTHER PSYCHOANALYTIC**

- **Adler**: Inferiority complex
- **Aichorn**: Faulty child training (neglect OR over-indulgence = BOTH bad)
- **Jung**: Collective unconscious

---

### **‚ö° 4 DEFINITIONS OF CRIMINAL BEHAVIOUR**

1. **Legal**: Prohibited by law, state punishes
2. **Moral**: Violates moral/religious code
3. **Social**: Violates social norms
4. **Psychological**: Causes psychological stress

---

### **‚ö° 6 CAUSES - "FAMILY"**

- **F**inancial problems / starvation
- **A**nti-social values
- **M**ental illness
- **I**nferiority / low social status
- **L**ow self-control
- **Y**oung age / family background

---

### **‚ö° MENTAL ILLNESS**

| Type | Characteristics | Crime Link |
|------|----------------|------------|
| **Psychosis** | Loss of reality (schizophrenia) | Violent crimes, Sec 84 IPC |
| **Neurosis** | Anxiety | Kleptomania, pyromania |
| **Antisocial PD** | No conscience, psychopathy | MOST CRIMINAL TYPE |
| **Mental Retardation** | Low IQ | Poor judgment, easily influenced |

**Section 84 IPC**: Act of insane person not an offense if cannot know nature/wrongness

---

### **‚ö° AGGRESSION - 2 TYPES**

| Type | Nature | Goal | Example |
|------|--------|------|---------|
| **Hostile** | Impulsive, emotional | Harm | Assault in fight |
| **Instrumental** | Planned, calculated | Gain | Armed robbery |

**3 Theories**: Instinct (Freud), Frustration-Aggression (Dollard), Social Learning (Bandura)

### **‚ö° DEFINITION & SCOPE**

**Definition**: Application of psychology to criminal justice

**3 Areas**: Investigation, Court, Prison

### **‚ö° INVESTIGATION TOOLS**

### **‚ö° COURT - KEY POINTS**

**Expert Witness**: Mental state, Competency, Insanity, Risk, Mitigation

**Eyewitness Issues**: Stress, time, leading questions, cross-race effect ‚Üí Confidence ‚â† Accuracy

**Classification**: Match to facility (max, medium, min, open)

**4 Treatment Programs**:
1. **CBT**: Criminal thinking (R&R, MRT)
2. **Anger Management**: Triggers, relaxation
3. **Sex Offender**: Reduces recidivism 10-15%
4. **Substance Abuse**: 60-80% inmates affected

**Mental Health**: 3-4√ó higher than general population

**Suicide Prevention**: Screen, observe, treat

## üìö **MODULE 05: SOCIAL STRUCTURE**

### **‚ö° SOCIAL DISORGANIZATION - 4 POINTS**

**Chicago School** (Shaw & McKay, 1920s):
- Concentric zones
- Zone II (Transition) = Highest crime
- Area characteristics cause crime (not ethnicity)
- Weak social control

**PREP factors**: Poverty, Residential instability, Ethnic heterogeneity, Population turnover

### **‚ö° MERTON'S STRAIN - ABSOLUTE MUST-KNOW**

**Formula**: Goals (Success) - Means (Education/Jobs) = STRAIN

### **‚ö° AGNEW'S 3 STRAINS - "FLN"**

All ‚Üí Anger ‚Üí Crime (if poor coping)

## üìö **MODULE 06: SUBCULTURES**

### **‚ö° COHEN VS MILLER - SIDE BY SIDE**

| Feature | COHEN (1955) | MILLER (1958) |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Basic Idea** | **REACTIVE** | **PROACTIVE** |

| **LC Culture** | Same as MC | Different from MC |

| **Crime Type** | Non-utilitarian, malicious | Express values |
| **Tone** | LC wants MC success (can't get) | LC has own values |

---

### **‚ö° COHEN - 3 CONCEPTS**

1. **Middle-Class Measuring Rod**: School judges all by MC standards
2. **Status Frustration**: LC boys fail, feel inadequate
3. **Reaction Formation**: Invert MC values ‚Üí Delinquent subculture

**6 Characteristics**: Non-utilitarian, Malicious, Negativistic, Versatile, Short-run hedonism, Group autonomy

---

### **‚ö° MILLER - 6 FOCAL CONCERNS**

> üß† **ABSOLUTELY MEMORIZE: "T-S-S-E-F-A"**

1. **TROUBLE**: Getting into and out of it
2. **TOUGHNESS**: Physical prowess, masculinity
3. **SMARTNESS**: Street smarts, con, hustle
4. **EXCITEMENT**: Thrills, risks, avoid boredom
5. **FATE**: Luck, external control
6. **AUTONOMY**: Freedom from authority

## üìö **MODULE 07: SOCIAL PROCESS**

### **‚ö° SUTHERLAND - 9 PROPOSITIONS (LIST)**

1. Crime **learned** (not inherited)
2. Through **communication**
3. In **intimate groups** (family, friends) ‚Üê MOST IMPORTANT SOURCE
4. Learn **techniques** + **attitudes**
5. Learn **definitions** (favorable/unfavorable)
6. **EXCESS of favorable** ‚Üí Crime ‚Üê **KEY PROPOSITION**
7. Vary in **FDPI** (Frequency, Duration, Priority, Intensity)
8. All **learning** mechanisms
9. Same **needs** (need doesn't explain method)

---

### **‚ö° NEUTRALIZATION - 5 TECHNIQUES**

> üß† **"3D-CA"** or **"D-D-D-C-A"**

| # | Technique | Example |
|---|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **Denial of RESPONSIBILITY** | "Not my fault, I was drunk" |
| 2 | **Denial of INJURY** | "Nobody hurt, they have insurance" |
| 3 | **Denial of VICTIM** | "They deserved it" |
| 4 | **CONDEMNATION of condemners** | "Everyone corrupt, who are they?" |
| 5 | **APPEAL to higher loyalties** | "For my gang/family" |

**Used BEFORE crime** to overcome guilt

---

### **‚ö° SOCIAL BOND - 4 ELEMENTS**

> üß† **"A-CAB"** or **"A-C-I-B"**

| Element | Meaning | If Strong | If Weak |
|---------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **ATTACHMENT** | Emotional bond | Don't hurt them | Don't care |
| **COMMITMENT** | Investment | Too much to lose | Nothing to lose |
| **INVOLVEMENT** | Time in activities | No time for crime | Idle, bored |
| **BELIEF** | Moral values | Crime is wrong | Law not important |

**ALL strong ‚Üí Conform | ANY weak ‚Üí Crime**

---

### **‚ö° LABELLING - QUICK**

**Question**: How are people LABELED as criminal?

**Primary Deviance**: Initial, minor, not identity

**Secondary Deviance**: After labeling, becomes identity, master status

**Process**: Rule-breaking ‚Üí Caught ‚Üí Labeled ‚Üí Master status ‚Üí Self-fulfilling ‚Üí Secondary deviance ‚Üí Career

**Policy**: Avoid labeling (diversion), reintegrative shaming

---

### **‚ö° SELF-CONTROL**

**Theory**: Low self-control ‚Üí Crime

**6 Traits** (CRISTA or list):
1. Impulsive
2. Simple tasks
3. Risk-seeking
4. Physical
5. Self-centered
6. Temper

**Origin**: Established by age 10, stable

**Policy**: Early intervention (parenting, before age 10)
