```
1. By accident/misfortune
2. Without criminal intention/knowledge
3. Lawful ACT
4. Lawful MEANS
5. Lawful MANNER
6. Proper CARE & CAUTION
```

### M'Naghten Rule (Section 84)

**2 Limbs (satisfy ANY ONE):**
1. Cannot know NATURE of act
2. Cannot know act is WRONG/contrary to law

**Burden:** On accused (preponderance of probabilities)
**Time:** At time of commission of act

## MODULE III: INCHOATE CRIMES (1 hour)

### Abetment (Section 107) - 3 Ways

```
I - Instigation (active incitement)
C - Conspiracy (agreement)
A - Aid (intentional help)
```

**Key Point:** Can punish abettor even if principal not caught/punished

---

### Conspiracy (Section 120A)

**Essential:** Agreement between 2+ persons to do:
- Illegal act, OR
- Legal act by illegal means

**No overt act needed** - Agreement itself is offense

---

### Attempt (Section 511)

**Preparation vs. Attempt:**

| **Preparation**  | **Attempt**      |
|------------------|------------------|
| Arranging means  | Using means      |
| Remote           | Proximate        |
| Not punishable   | Punishable       |

**Punishment:** Up to 1/2 of maximum for completed offense

## MODULE IV: OFFENSES AGAINST BODY (3 hours)

### Culpable Homicide vs. Murder

**Key Difference:** DEGREE of intention/knowledge

| **Aspect**          | **Culpable Homicide**            | **Murder**                       |
|---------------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Intention           | Injury LIKELY to cause death     | Injury SUFFICIENT to cause death |
| Knowledge           | Act LIKELY to cause death        | Act in ALL PROBABILITY will cause death |
| Degree              | PROBABILITY                      | CERTAINTY                        |
| IPC Section         | 304                              | 302                              |
| BNS Section         | 100-105                          | 101                              |

---

### Murder Exceptions (300 IPC) - 5 Exceptions

**P-P-P-F-C:**
1. **P**rovocation (grave & sudden)
2. **P**rivate defense (exceeded in good faith)
3. **P**ublic servant (exceeded power in good faith)
4. **F**ight (sudden, without premeditation)
5. **C**onsent (person 18+ consented to death risk)

---

### Death by Negligence (304A)

**Essential Ingredients:**
1. Death caused
2. By rash or negligent act
3. NOT amounting to culpable homicide

**Rash vs. Negligent:**
- **Rash:** Consciousness of risk but hoping no harm (higher degree)
- **Negligent:** Failure to exercise care (lower degree)

**Case:** *Jacob Mathew* - Guidelines for medical negligence

---

### Grievous Hurt (320) - 8 Types

**E-S-H-M-D-D-F-20:**
1. **E**masculation
2. **S**ight (permanent loss)
3. **H**earing (permanent loss)
4. **M**ember/joint (deprivation)
5. **D**estruction of powers
6. **D**isfigurement (head/face)
7. **F**racture/dislocation
8. **20** days severe pain/unable to work

### Dowry Death (304B)

**Ingredients:**
1. Death of woman within 7 years of marriage
2. By burns/bodily injury/otherwise than normal circumstances
3. Soon before death subjected to cruelty/harassment
4. For or in connection with dowry demand

**Presumption:** Section 113B Evidence Act - If above proved, court SHALL presume accused caused dowry death

**Burden shifts:** To accused to prove innocence

---

### Wrongful Restraint vs. Confinement

| **Restraint (339)**          | **Confinement (340)**        |
|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Prevents going in PARTICULAR direction | Prevents going in ALL directions |
| Partial restriction          | Total restriction            |
| Alternative routes available | No alternative               |

---

### Force & Assault (349-351)

**Force (349):** Causing motion, change of motion, cessation of motion

**Criminal Force (350):** Force used:
- Without consent, AND
- To commit offense OR cause injury/fear/annoyance

**Assault (351):** Gesture/preparation causing apprehension of criminal force

üß† **Key:** Assault = Preparation; Force = Actual application

---

### Kidnapping vs. Abduction

| **Kidnapping (359-361)**     | **Abduction (362)**          |
|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| From lawful guardianship     | From any place               |
| Consent of person immaterial | Consent of person material   |
| Specific to minors/unsound   | Any person                   |
| Age: Male <16, Female <18    | No age limit                 |
| Substantive offense          | Not substantive (auxiliary)  |

## MODULE V: OFFENSES AGAINST WOMEN (2 hours)

### Rape (375-376 IPC / 63-73 BNS)

**Criminal Law Amendment Act 2013** - Major changes

**Essential Ingredients:**
1. Sexual intercourse (penetration - vagina/mouth/urethra/anus)
2. Against will OR Without consent
3. Or in circumstances vitiating consent (7 circumstances)

**7 Circumstances:**
1. Against her will
2. Without her consent
3. Consent by fear of death/hurt
4. Consent believing man is husband
5. Consent due to unsoundness of mind/intoxication
6. Woman under 18 years
7. Unable to communicate consent

---

### Punishment for Rape (376)

| **Circumstance**             | **Punishment**          |
|------------------------------|-------------------------|
| Normal rape                  | 7 years (min) to life   |

| Gang rape                    | 20 years (min) to life  |
| Rape causing death/PVS       | 20 years (min) to death |

**Custodial Rape:** Police, public servant, jail staff, hospital staff, person in authority

## MODULE VI: OFFENSES AGAINST PROPERTY (2 hours)

### Theft (378-382)

**Essential Ingredients:**
1. Dishonest intention
2. Movable property
3. Taken out of possession
4. Without consent

**Punishment:**
- Simple theft: 3 years
- From dwelling: 7 years
- By servant: 7 years
- After preparation for death/hurt: 10 years

### Criminal Breach of Trust (405-409)

**Essential Ingredients:**
1. Entrustment of property
2. Dishonest misappropriation OR conversion OR disposal
3. To another's detriment

**Aggravated Forms:**
- By carrier/warehouse-keeper (407): 7 years
- By clerk/servant (408): 7 years
- By public servant/banker/agent (409): 10 years

---

### Cheating (415)

**Two Ways:**
1. Deception causing delivery of property
2. Fraudulent/dishonest inducement

**Punishment:**
- Simple cheating: 1 year
- Cheating by personation: 3 years
- Cheating with advance booking: 7 years

## MODULE VIII: OFFENSES AGAINST STATE (1 hour)

### Waging War (121)

**Essential Ingredients:**
1. Waging war against Government of India
2. OR attempting to wage
3. OR abetting waging

**Punishment:** Death OR Life imprisonment + fine

**What is "Waging War"?**
- Taking up arms
- Actual levying of war
- Not mere speeches/writings

---

### Sedition (124A - NOW UNDER REVIEW)

**Essential Ingredients:**
1. Words (spoken/written) OR signs OR visible representation
2. Brings or attempts to bring hatred/contempt/disaffection
3. Against Government established by law

**NOT Sedition:**
- Criticism of government policies
- Academic discussion
- Without incitement to violence

**Punishment:** Life imprisonment OR 3 years + fine

**Important Cases:**
- *Kedar Nath v. State of Bihar*: Sedition valid if incites violence/public disorder
- *Balwant Singh*: Mere slogans not sedition
