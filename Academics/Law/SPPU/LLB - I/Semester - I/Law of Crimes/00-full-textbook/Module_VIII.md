## âš”ï¸ WAGING WAR AGAINST GOVERNMENT (SECTION 121 IPC / 147 BNS)

### Definition

Whoever:
- Wages war against Government of India, OR
- Attempts to wage war, OR
- Abets waging of war

**Punishment:** Death OR Life imprisonment + Fine

---

### What is "Waging War"?

**Essential Elements:**
1. âœ… Levying war
2. âœ… Against Government of India
3. âœ… By taking up arms
4. âœ… With intention to:
   - Overawe government by force, OR
   - Resist its authority by force

### What Constitutes "War"?

âœ… **Yes - Waging War:**
- Armed insurrection
- Actual levying of war
- Taking up arms against government
- Organized armed resistance

âŒ **No - NOT Waging War:**
- Mere speeches/writings
- Criticism of government
- Single murder of official (may be murder, not war)
- Riot without intent to overawe government

---

### Related Provisions

**Section 121A IPC (148 BNS):** Conspiracy to wage war
- **Punishment:** Life OR 10 years + fine

**Section 122 IPC (149 BNS):** Collecting arms to wage war
- **Punishment:** Life OR 10 years + fine

**Section 123 IPC (150 BNS):** Concealing with intent to facilitate waging war
- **Punishment:** 10 years + fine

## ğŸ“¢ SEDITION (SECTION 124A IPC / 152 BNS)

### âš ï¸ STATUS: UNDER REVIEW (Supreme Court 2022)

### Definition

Whoever by words/signs/visible representation:
- Brings or attempts to bring
- **Hatred, contempt, or disaffection**
- Against Government established by law

**Commits SEDITION**

---

### Essential Ingredients

```
1. âœ… Words (spoken/written) OR signs OR visible representation
2. âœ… Brings/attempts to bring hatred/contempt/disaffection
3. âœ… Against Government of India established by law
4. âœ… Incites or tends to incite violence/public disorder
```

---

### What is NOT Sedition?

```
âŒ Criticism of government policies
âŒ Academic discussion
âŒ Disapproval of administrative measures
âŒ Attempt to change law by lawful means
âŒ Comments without incitement to violence
```

**Explanation to Section 124A:**
"Disapprobation of measures of Government with view to obtain their alteration by lawful means without exciting hatred, contempt or disaffection is not sedition."

---

### Leading Cases

**ğŸ“Œ Kedar Nath Singh v. State of Bihar (1962) AIR SC 955**

**Facts:** Accused made speeches against government with violent language

**Held:** Section 124A constitutionally valid BUT applies only when:
- Words incite violence, OR
- Create public disorder, OR
- Disturb public peace

**Principle:** 
- Sedition law valid but narrow interpretation needed
- Must incite violence/disorder
- Mere criticism not sedition

**Impact:** This case saved Section 124A from being struck down but narrowed its scope significantly

---

**ğŸ“Œ Balwant Singh v. State of Punjab (1995) SC**

**Facts:** Accused shouted pro-Khalistan slogans after Indira Gandhi's assassination

**Held:** NOT GUILTY of sedition

**Principle:** Mere slogans without incitement to violence don't amount to sedition

---

**ğŸ“Œ Recent Development (2022):**
Supreme Court put Section 124A in abeyance pending review of constitutional validity. Directed no fresh cases to be filed and pending cases to be kept in abeyance.

---

### Punishment

**Section 124A:** Life imprisonment OR 3 years + fine

**Consequences:** Also lose right to government employment
