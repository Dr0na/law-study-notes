## üíÄ CULPABLE HOMICIDE (SECTION 299 IPC / 100 BNS)

### Definition

Person commits culpable homicide if act causing death is done:
1. With **intention** of causing death, OR
2. With **intention** of causing such bodily injury as is **likely** to cause death, OR
3. With **knowledge** that likely by such act to cause death

---

## ‚ö∞Ô∏è MURDER (SECTION 300 IPC / 101 BNS)

### Relationship

**All murders are culpable homicide, but all culpable homicides are not murder.**

Murder is aggravated form of culpable homicide.

### When is CH Murder?

Culpable homicide is murder if:
1. Intention of causing **death**, OR
2. Intention of causing bodily injury offender **knows likely** to cause death, OR
3. Intention of causing bodily injury **sufficient in ordinary course** to cause death, OR
4. Knowledge act so **imminently dangerous** that in **all probability** will cause death

---

## üÜö DISTINCTION: CULPABLE HOMICIDE vs MURDER

### Key Difference: **DEGREE** of intention/knowledge

| Aspect | Culpable Homicide | Murder |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| **Intention** | Injury LIKELY to cause death | Injury SUFFICIENT to cause death |
| **Knowledge** | Act LIKELY to cause death | Act ALL PROBABILITY will cause death |
| **Degree** | PROBABILITY | CERTAINTY |
| **Example** | Single blow with stick | Multiple stabs in chest |
| **IPC** | Section 304 | Section 302 |
| **BNS** | Sections 100-105 | Section 101 |
| **Punishment** | Life/10 years | Death/Life |

> üß† **Remember:** Only DEGREE difference, not kind

### Leading Cases

**üìå Virsa Singh v. State of Punjab (SC)**
**Four-step test:**
1. Was there intention to cause bodily injury?
2. What was the bodily injury intended?
3. Was it sufficient in ordinary course to cause death?
4. Was it the intended injury of specified kind?

**üìå Kesar Singh v. State of Haryana (SC)**
Distinction between knowledge and intention is difference of degrees.

## üõ°Ô∏è MURDER EXCEPTIONS (SECTION 300 IPC)

### Five Exceptions (Murder ‚Üí Culpable Homicide)

> üß† **Mnemonic: "P-P-P-F-C"**

```
1. PROVOCATION (Grave & sudden)
2. PRIVATE DEFENSE (Exceeded in good faith)
3. PUBLIC SERVANT (Exceeded power in good faith)
4. FIGHT (Sudden, without premeditation)
5. CONSENT (Person 18+ consented to death risk)
```

### Exception 1: Grave & Sudden Provocation

**Requirements:**
1. ‚úÖ Provocation must be GRAVE
2. ‚úÖ Must be SUDDEN
3. ‚úÖ Loss of self-control
4. ‚úÖ Before cooling time
5. ‚úÖ Not self-invited

**üìå K.M. Nanavati v. State of Maharashtra:** Time lapse negates suddenness

### Exception 4: Sudden Fight

**Requirements:**
1. ‚úÖ Fight must be SUDDEN
2. ‚úÖ In heat of PASSION
3. ‚úÖ Without PREMEDITATION
4. ‚úÖ Without undue advantage
5. ‚úÖ Not cruel/unusual manner

**Difference from Provocation:**
- Fight = mutual combat
- Provocation = one-sided

## ‚ö†Ô∏è DEATH BY NEGLIGENCE (SECTION 304A IPC / 106 BNS)

### Definition

Causing death by **rash or negligent** act NOT amounting to culpable homicide

### Essential Ingredients

```
1. ‚úÖ Death of person
2. ‚úÖ By RASH or NEGLIGENT act
3. ‚úÖ NOT amounting to culpable homicide
   ‚îî‚îÄ> No intention to cause death
   ‚îî‚îÄ> No knowledge death would result
```

### Rash vs Negligent

| Rash | Negligent |
|------|-----------|
| Consciousness of risk | Failure to exercise care |
| Hoping won't happen | No foresight |
| HIGHER degree | LOWER degree |
| Example: Driving 100 km/h in city | Example: Not checking brakes |

**Punishment:** 2 years OR fine OR both

### Leading Cases

**üìå Jacob Mathew v. State of Punjab (SC)**
**Guidelines for Medical Negligence:**
1. Simple lack of care/error ‚â† negligence
2. Practice acceptable to profession = no negligence
3. Equipment not available = no negligence
4. Standard = ordinary competent person, not highest
5. Criminal negligence must be gross/very high degree
6. Private complaint needs evidence from another doctor
7. Doctor not arrested routinely

## ü§ï HURT AND GRIEVOUS HURT

### Hurt (Section 319 IPC / 114-120 BNS)

**Definition:** Causing bodily pain, disease, or infirmity

**Examples:** Punching, pulling hair, pricking with needle

**Punishment:** 1 year OR ‚Çπ1000 fine OR both (Section 323)

---

### Grievous Hurt (Section 320 IPC / 121-125 BNS)

**Eight Types:**

> üß† **Mnemonic: "E-S-H-M-D-D-F-20"**

```
1. EMASCULATION (masculine vigor)
2. SIGHT (permanent loss either eye)
3. HEARING (permanent loss either ear)
4. MEMBER/JOINT (deprivation)
5. DESTRUCTION of powers member/joint
6. DISFIGUREMENT head/face (permanent)
7. FRACTURE/dislocation bone/tooth
8. 20 DAYS severe pain OR unable to work
```

**Punishment:** 7 years + fine (Section 325)

## üíç DOWRY DEATH (SECTION 304B IPC / 80 BNS)

### Definition

Death of woman within 7 years of marriage by burns/bodily injury/otherwise + Soon before death subjected to cruelty/harassment for dowry = Dowry Death

### Ingredients

```
1. ‚úÖ Death of woman
2. ‚úÖ Within 7 years of marriage
3. ‚úÖ By burns/bodily injury/not normal circumstances
4. ‚úÖ Soon before death: cruelty/harassment
5. ‚úÖ For or in connection with dowry demand
```

### Presumption (Section 113B Evidence Act)

If above proved, court **SHALL presume** accused caused dowry death.
**Burden shifts to accused.**

**Punishment:** 7 years minimum to life imprisonment

**üìå State of Punjab v. Iqbal Singh:** Section 113B needed to curb dowry menace

## üö™ WRONGFUL RESTRAINT vs CONFINEMENT

| Restraint (339) | Confinement (340) |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Prevents going PARTICULAR direction | Prevents going ALL directions |
| Partial restriction | Total restriction |
| Alternative routes available | No alternative |
| Example: Blocking one road | Example: Locking in room |
| Punishment: 1 month/‚Çπ500 | Punishment: 1 year/‚Çπ1000 |

## üëä FORCE AND ASSAULT

### Force (Section 349)

Causing motion, change of motion, cessation of motion

### Criminal Force (Section 350)

Force used:
- WITHOUT consent, AND
- To commit offense OR cause injury/fear/annoyance

### Assault (Section 351)

Gesture/preparation causing **apprehension** of criminal force

> üß† **Key:** Assault = Preparation; Criminal Force = Actual

**Punishment:**
- Assault (351): 3 months/‚Çπ500
- Criminal Force (352): 3 months/‚Çπ500

## üö∏ KIDNAPPING vs ABDUCTION

| Kidnapping (361) | Abduction (362) |
|------------------|-----------------|
| From lawful guardianship | From any place |
| Consent of person IMMATERIAL | Consent MATERIAL |
| Minor (M<16, F<18) OR unsound | Any person |
| Taking/Enticing | Force OR Deceit |
| Substantive offense | Auxiliary offense |
| Complete when taken | Continuing offense |
| Punishment: 7 years + fine | Depends on purpose |

**üìå Vardarajan v. State of Madras:** Allowing to accompany ‚â† Taking

> ‚úÖ **KEY TAKEAWAYS**
