## ğŸ¯ INTRODUCTION TO GENERAL EXCEPTIONS

General Exceptions = Circumstances where an act which would ordinarily be a crime is NOT treated as such.

### Philosophy

```
WHY GENERAL EXCEPTIONS EXIST:
1. ABSENCE OF MENS REA (No guilty mind)
2. JUSTIFIED ACT (For greater good)
3. LACK OF CAPACITY (Cannot form intent)
4. ACT UNDER COMPULSION (No choice)
```

### Classification

**TYPE 1: EXCUSABLE DEFENSES** (No mens rea)
- Mistake of fact
- Accident  
- Infancy
- Insanity
- Involuntary intoxication

**TYPE 2: JUSTIFIABLE DEFENSES** (Act justified)
- Necessity
- Private defense
- Consent
- Judicial acts

> M-Mistake, I-Infancy, A-Accident, N-Necessity, I-Insanity, P-Private defense, A-Acts of judges, N-Not voluntary

## ğŸ” MISTAKE OF FACT AND LAW

### Latin Maxim
**"Ignorantia facti excusat, ignorantia juris non excusat"**
= Ignorance of fact excuses, ignorance of law does not excuse

### Sections 76 & 79 IPC (14 & 17 BNS)

**Section 76:** Act of person bound by law or by mistake believing himself bound
**Section 79:** Act justified or by mistake believing himself justified by law

#### Essential Ingredients
```
FOR PROTECTION UNDER 76 & 79:
1. âœ… Mistake of FACT (not law)
2. âœ… Made in GOOD FAITH
3. âœ… With DUE CARE and ATTENTION
4. âœ… Person believed he was bound/justified by law
```

#### Difference: Section 76 vs 79

| Section 76 | Section 79 |
|------------|-----------|
| Person believes **BOUND** by law | Person believes **JUSTIFIED** by law |
| Legal compulsion | Legal justification |

### Leading Cases

**ğŸ“Œ R v. Tolson (1889)**
- Woman remarried after 7 years believing husband dead
- **Held:** NOT GUILTY - Reasonable mistake of fact
- **Principle:** Reasonable mistake in good faith is defense

**ğŸ“Œ R v. Prince (1875)**
- Took girl under 16 believing she was 18+
- **Held:** GUILTY - Act itself unlawful even if facts as believed
- **Principle:** No defense if act wrong anyway

**ğŸ“Œ State of West Bengal v. Shew Mangal Singh**
- Police fired on mob under Commissioner's order
- **Held:** Protected if acted in good faith with due care

### Mistake of Law

**General Rule:** NO DEFENSE (ignorantia juris non excusat)

**Limited Exceptions:**
1. Law not published
2. Relied on statute later held unconstitutional
3. Relied on judicial decision later overruled
4. Relied on official interpretation

**ğŸ“Œ R v. Bailey:** Ignorance of law no excuse (even if impossible to know)

## ğŸ›¡ï¸ RIGHT OF PRIVATE DEFENSE (SECTIONS 96-106 IPC / 34-44 BNS)

### Fundamental Principle

**Section 96:** Nothing is offense done in exercise of right of private defense

**Philosophy:**
- Natural right of self-preservation
- When no time to seek state help
- Preventive, not punitive

### When Right Arises & Ends

**Commencement:** When reasonable apprehension of danger
**Continuation:** So long as danger continues
**Termination:** When danger ceases

> ğŸ§  **Key:** Right is PREVENTIVE, not PUNITIVE
