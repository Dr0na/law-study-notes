## ğŸ‘¥ ABETMENT (SECTION 107 IPC / 45 BNS)

### Definition

A person abets an offense when he:
1. **Instigates** any person to do that thing, OR
2. Engages in **Conspiracy** for doing of that thing, OR
3. Intentionally **Aids** by act/illegal omission

> ğŸ§  **Mnemonic: "I-C-A"** (Instigation-Conspiracy-Aid)

---

### 1. Abetment by INSTIGATION

**Instigation** = Active suggestion, incitement, encouragement

**Methods:**
- Direct incitement
- Goading, urging
- Creating atmosphere of encouragement

**NOT Instigation:**
- Mere advice
- Casual remark
- Reporting offense to authority

---

### 2. Abetment by CONSPIRACY

**Requirements:**
- Two or more persons
- Agreement to do illegal act OR legal act by illegal means
- Overt act NOT necessary for abetment liability

---

### 3. Abetment by INTENTIONAL AID

**Requirements:**
- Aid must be INTENTIONAL
- By ACT or ILLEGAL OMISSION
- Knowledge that act being aided is offense

**Examples:**
- Keeping watch during burglary
- Providing weapons
- Driving getaway vehicle

---

| 113 | Abettor absent when offense committed | Same as principal |
| 111 | Different offense committed | Liable if consequence of abetment |
| 115 | Abetment of death/life offense not committed | Up to 7 years + fine |

## ğŸ¯ ATTEMPT (SECTION 511 IPC / 62 BNS)

### Definition

**Attempt** = Direct movement towards commission after preparation

**Section 511:** Attempt to commit offense punishable with imprisonment for life/imprisonment

### Essential Ingredients

```
1. âœ… Intention to commit offense
2. âœ… Offense punishable with imprisonment
3. âœ… Act done towards commission
4. âœ… Falls SHORT of completion
5. âœ… No express provision for punishment
```

---

### Preparation vs. Attempt

| Preparation | Attempt |
|-------------|---------|
| Arranging means | Using means |
| Remote from crime | Proximate to crime |
| Equivocal act | Unequivocal act |
| NOT punishable | Punishable (511) |
| Example: Buying poison | Example: Administering poison |

### Tests to Distinguish

1. **Proximity Test:** How close to actual commission?
2. **Equivocality Test:** Does act unequivocally show criminal intent?
3. **Substantial Step Test:** Has accused taken substantial step?

---

### Impossibility

**Factual Impossibility:** NOT A DEFENSE
- A tries to steal from empty pocket â†’ Still liable

**Legal Impossibility:** MAY BE DEFENSE
- Act attempted not actually criminal â†’ No liability

### Punishment

**Section 511:**
- Imprisonment: Up to **1/2** of longest term for offense
- OR Fine provided for offense
- OR Both

**Example:** Murder punishable with death/life â†’ Attempt to murder: Life or less
